Lecture Transcription

karmaṇy evādhikāras te
mā phaleṣu kadācana 
mā karma-phala-hetur bhūr 
mā te saṅgo ’stv akarmaṇi 

[Bg. 2.47] 

[You have a right to perform your prescribed duty, but you are not entitled to the fruits of action. Never consider yourself the cause of the results of your activities, and never be attached to not doing your duty.]

This is a very famous verse in the Bhagavad-gita and one of the most misunderstood of all of the teachings of the scripture. It is describing herein by Lord Krishna to Arjuna, that you have a right to perform your actions your work or your duty but you are not entitled to the fruits of your action. Never think yourself to be the cause of the results of your activity and never be attached to not doing your duty. Some people are under the impression that work is worship but in the third chapter of Geetha there is further clarification.  

yajṣārthāt karmaṇo ’nyatra loko ’yaṁ karma-bandhanaḥ  [Bg.3.9]  That any work that is done with selfish motivation, that work is the cause of bondage. But work that is done in devotion to God, that work is the very basis of liberation. It is the very means of liberation. So, some people think that if I take to spiritual life, then I will have to become a very impractical man. ‘I will not be able to provide for my family, I will not be able to live within this society, but I will have to renounce all possessions, all social connections and live in the Himalayas or in an ashram in a holy place. Therefore, since this concept is intolerable to me, it is better that I keep away from religion and it is better that I keep away from spiritually minded people.’ 

Priyavarata’s Story

In regard to this, I would like to narrate is a beautiful story from the Shrimad Bhagavatam. It is the story of Maharaja Priyavrata. Since it is Priyavrata’s birthday today, we will describe the glories of Priyavrata. Priyavrata was the eldest son of Swayambhuva Manu. Swayambhuva Manu was the king of Bharata Varsha. According to the Vedic literatures in those days, all the world was under ultimately one supreme Emperor. Swayambhuva Manu had two sons, his elder son was Priyavrata and his younger son was of the name, Uttanapada. Now, Priyavrata had absolutely no inclination to power or money or fame or position. He simply wanted to worship God. So, he appealed to his father that, ‘my younger brother is very highly qualified to be the king. Well, let him be the king and let me do my bhajans, let me serve my guru’ So, his wish was granted. So, Priyavrata went to a mountain in the Himalayas called Gandamadana parvat and there he accepted as his spiritual guide, the great Narada Muni, and there Narada Muni was instructing him in the science of devotion to God. However great one is, if one is actually serious about making real progress, one must accept the guidance of the spiritual authority. You see, when we are talking about religion, we are not simply speaking about a sentimental form of faith, we are talking about the highest most sophisticated science in all of creation. Within our universities today, we are teaching so many sciences. There is a science of geology, geography, mathematics, science of linguistics, science of biology, physics psychiatry, psychology, engineering, architecture, medicine, law. Now there is an approach of how to learn any science. 

Scientific Approach In Religion

If you are in need of a surgical operation, and someone comes up to you on the street and says,’ you know surgical operations are very costly.’ You say, ‘yes I know.’ He says, ‘well, I happen to be a surgeon and you give me your money and I will perform surgery and here’s my knife. Let us begin.’ Will you accept? First of all, you will ask him, ‘what are your qualifications?’ and he will tell you that ‘I have read the proper books.’ A proper surgeon must have read the proper books. Now, if anybody just gets the books and buys them from the University and sit at home and reads them. It's a very inexpensive way of getting an education. But will anyone accept you as an authority? If the books were enough, why do you spend so much money going to college? Because, in order to understand the books properly you absolutely must study under an authority, who has studied under another authority, who has studied under another authority, who knows how to apply the teachings of those books in practical life. 

In other words, you must study the books under an authorized professor, and everyone accepts in their practical life that this is the process of learning any education or science. But when it comes to the highest science... why do I say the highest science? There is a science of biology and physics which teach you about the gross matter of this world, and then there is a science of psychiatry and psychology which teaches you about a subtle matter that cannot be seen or heard or touched or taste, the science of the mind. Religion is the science of the soul, the science of consciousness, the science which reveals to you who you are, what is your nature, what is your essence, what is God and what is your relationship. When religion is seen scientifically, then it transcends all sectarian boundaries.  

Why is there so much conflict amongst religions today? Because, there’s no scientific approach! There is no philosophical approach! It is all simply sentiment and fanaticism. Religion without philosophy is either sentimentalism or fanaticism. So, therefore, when it comes to the highest science, practically anywhere in the world you go, you’ll find that people say, ‘Well, I think God is like this, and I feel that God is like this, and I believe that God is like this, and your opinion is as good as my opinion, and my opinion is as good as his opinion, and her opinion is as good as his opinion. Yata mata, tata patha. All paths lead to the same goal.’ So, anything you think about God, that’s God and everyone says, ‘yes you are very liberal minded, you are very learned.’ And the result of such religion: people hate each other, people are envious of one other, people have endless material attachments, people have no peace of mind, people are constantly in anxiety.  

Accept an Authority

Th... the resources of this world are being horribly exploited on every level. Are these distinctly saintly people? Therefore, it is said in the Shastra tadvijṣānārthaṃ sa gurumevābhigacchet [Mu. 1.2.12] If you want to understand the truth, you must accept an authority, you must approach one who knows the truth.

tad viddhi praṇipātena 

paripraśnena sevayā 

upadekṣyanti te jṣānaḿ 

jṣāninas tattva-darśinaḥ 

[Bg. 4.34]

[Just try to learn the truth by approaching a spiritual master. Inquire from him submissively and render service unto him. The self-realized souls can impart knowledge unto you because they have seen the truth.]

If you want to know the truth, you must accept a knower of the truth, approach him, inquire submissively, render service to him, he can impart the truth to you, because he knows. Sometimes people asked me, ‘Radhanatha Swami, why did you become Hari Krishna devotee? After all there’s so many other religions in the world. You were born in the United States of America, you were born in a surrounding of Christianity and Judaism, that’s... your heritage and here you are talking about how all religions are essentially one. So why did you change? If everything’s one, why change?’ 

I can explain to you there’s no question of changing, the question is of knowing, the question is of understanding. Although all religions are essentially teaching love of God, whether one is a Christian or a Jew or a Buddhist or a Parsi or a Muslim or a Hindu or a Jain, the goal is to understand in truth through the realization - ‘who you are?’, ‘what is your relationship with God?’ and to serve God with love. That is the only religion: sanatana dharma. Why did I choose this way of life? Because, in all of my travels throughout the world and in all of my studies of the Scriptures and of all of my meeting with holy people, until I met His divine grace AC Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada, I never met anybody who truly revealed to me through his life and through his teachings, the means by... that the knowledge and the understanding and the realization by which I wanted to dedicate everything in the service of God. 

So, because he was like, this I am like this. This is not a question of this religion or that religion, it is a question of this is the person that inspired me to sacrifice my life in love for God, in love for all living beings. We are concerned with finding um...a person who can inspire us to love God.  

Priyavrata’s Story Continued

So Priyavrata as a young man, he found that person Shrila Narada Muni and Narada Muni was teaching him all about the principles of renunciation, tapasya, vairaghya. That ‘you stay up in his Mountain, you chant the names of God, you perform Tapasya in this way. Don’t entangle yourself with the things of this world. There’s no need! Let your brother do all that.’ So, of course Uttanapada he was a great king, and then he handed the throne over to his son, who was the great and celebrated Dhruva Maharaja. And the kingdom was passed down to these very qualified great saints, until after the great Prachetas, there was no son. So, then Swayambhuva Manu, who is still living... because in those days people live long life, he was thinking ‘what to do now? I’m the king again. There’s no one else. I want to dedicate my life and retire from all these worldly affairs.’ When you get old, you should retire, you should dedicate your life only for God, let the young people do all this work. 

So, where is Priyavrata? So, he went up to the mountain and Priyavrata saw him coming he thought, 'Oh my God! Narada muni, please save me. I have no desire to get married, I have no desire to have children, I have no desire to have a kingdom. It is all just so many headaches.’ Swayambhuva Manu began to explain that Priyavrata, 'it is your duty to become the king.' And Priyavrat was saying, 'but I am very afraid that my devotion to God will be spoiled if I'm associated with so much materialistic ways of life'.  

This is very much different than the politicians of today. Is it not? [devotees laugh] The politicians of today...they will beg, borrow, steal, kill, do anything to take to throne! Huh! Not all of them are so immoral, but most of the places the world they are. And when they attain the throne, my god! They never want to give it up! Just like in my country of the United States of America, the previous president was Ronald Reagan. How old was that man? My god, he was 75 years old. He should have taken sannyasa 25 years before [devotees laugh]. And he was still trying to just hold on to his position. And in Japan, how old is the emperor? [devotee says ‘85 years’] He just died, he died in the throne, correct 85 years, and he died in the throne! And he wanted to keep on living however invalid. ‘Let me keep my power, let me keep my position, let me keep my power to control!’ They'll do anything to get it and they'll do anything to keep it. Why? Because they are attached. They are attached to wealth, they are attached to prestige, they are attached to fame, they are attached to power.  

But here Swayambhuva Manu, he was the king, and he's begging his son, ‘take it! take it! I want to simply do bhajan, do kirtan associate with sadhus. I've had enough of this work! I've done my duty, now let me just chant ‘Hare Krishna’ and his son Priyavrata, it's not that he was canvassing votes, he was saying, 'please father! Keep the throne! I don't want it. Find somebody else'. These are men who are actually qualified. Why? Because they are attached to nothing for their own selfish interests but they are attached only to the service that they can render. So, therefore, Swambhuva Manu did not know what to do. At that time, the grandfather of Priyavrata, Lord Brahma, came to that place. Of course, Lord Brahma, as you know, he is the Guru for Narada Muni! Sometimes even in spiritual circles, there is this going to higher authorities. 

So, when Lord Brahma came, Narada Muni, Priyavrata ,they all offered obeisances. Along with Narada Muni...along with Lord Brahma, came Lord Shiva and so many great Saints with so many great Rishis. And then Lord Brahma began to speak. He said, 'Maharaja Priyavrata, you are afraid that if you accept a family and if you accept worldly responsibilities, then your spiritual life will be ruined'. He said, 'but if you are accepting it as a service to God, do you not think that God will protect you?' And then he used an example. He said that some people they leave their wife, and they leave their children, and their leave their houses to go to live in a forest, in the jungle. But when they go into the jungle, they take six wives with them! In the form of their mind and their five senses. As long as you are attached to the pleasures of your mind and your five senses, if you try to artificially renounce the world, you are actually going into the forest with six wives. You're better off staying at home with one wife, and being responsible. And then he began to explain that spiritual life is to be understood according to how we can please the Lord, how we can best serve the Lord. 

Perform Duty to Please Krishna

Bhagavatam instructs us: 

ataḥ pumbhir dvija-śreṣṭhā 
varṇāśrama-vibhāgaśaḥ 
svanuṣṭhitasya dharmasya 
saṁsiddhir hari-toṣaṇam

 [Sb 1.2.13] 

[O best among the twice-born, it is therefore concluded that the highest perfection one can achieve by discharging the duties prescribed for one’s own occupation according to caste divisions and orders of life is to please the Personality of Godhead.]

Whatever your position of life is. Whether you're a Brahmin-a priest, a ksyatria-a warrior, a vaishya-a mercantile or agricultural person or a sudra- a labourer. Whether you are a sanyasi in the renounced or a grihastha, a family man or woman, a brahmachaari, a student or a vanaprastha,retired. Whatever your particular position in society is; whether you are a doctor, a lawyer, an industrialist, a business, a housewife, a teacher, a professor, an architect, or a... whatever you may be if you perform your duty to please God your life is perfect. So, therefore, he told Priyavrata that 'I am not coming here to give you my opinion. As your guru, I am coming here to tell you, what is the will of God. The Supreme Lord Hari wants you to perform the great service of becoming king'. At that time Priyavrata, he bowed his head, and he said, 'Yes. I will accept this service and become king'. And then Lord Brahma left. And Priyavrata came down from that Gandamadana hill, and went into the great kingdom. And he became a very great and powerful king. But how did he rule the world? It is explained by Bhaktivinoda thakur  

grihe thako, vane thako, sada ‘hari’ bole’ dako, 
sukhe duhkhe bhulo na’ko, vadane hari-nam koro re 

gay gora madhur sware naam, gay gora madhur sware 

[Gaya Gaur Madhur Sware Verse 2] 

[Whether you are a householder or a sannyasi, constantly chant "Hari! Hari!" Do not forget this chanting, whether you are in a happy condition or a distressful one. Just fill your lips with the hari-nama.]

Whether you are a householder or a renunciant, if you sincerely dedicate your life to glorify God by chanting his name, your life is perfect. Krishna says in Gita: 

yatkaroshhi yadashnaasi 

yajjuhoshhi dadaasi yath | 

yattapasyasi kaunteya 

tatkurushhva madarpanamh 

[Bg. 9.27]

[Whatever you do, whatever you eat, whatever you offer or give away, and whatever austerities you perform – do that, O son of Kuntī, as an offering to Me.]

Whatever you do, whatever you think, whatever you offer and give away in charity, whatever you eat should be done as an offering to God. This is spiritual life, this is the essence of the Gita, this is the essence of all scriptures! You see, God does not see who you are or what you do. God sees the quality of your faith and the quality of your devotion. He is not considering; he does not consider if you have a PhD or if you are illiterate. It's all the same. If you have a PhD, use it in devotion and that is perfect. If you are illiterate, use it in devotion and your life is perfect... If you have crores and crores of rupees, use it in devotion and your life is perfect. If you have nothing but a begging pot and a stick, use it in devotion and your life is perfect. It is the quality of one's consciousness that is the singular factor in spiritual life. 

There is a story in the Ramayana when Lord Shri Ramchandra was banished from Ayodhya by his stepmother Kaikeyi. He and Sita, and Lakshmana, and the charioteer Sumantra, they began to proceed towards Dandakaranya forest. They came to the bank of the Ganges and at that place there was an old friend of Lord Rama his name was King Guha. And Guha was not an ordinary king. He was living in the jungle and his people were of a very, very low and untouchable class... by people who were very caste conscious. They were of the Nishada race, which means they were outcasts, living in the forest. Now, although King Guha by all social purposes was an outcast, he was very simple and very humble and he had such devotion and such a desire to please Lord Rama. When he saw Lord Shri Ramachandra with tears of love in his eyes, he bowed at his feet. he said, 'My dear Lord, this jungle is my kingdom and all of these people they are my subjects, but I am your servant. Please accept me as your servant, accept all my subjects as your servant, accept all of my Empire as your kingdom!’ You see materialistic people, they are afraid of God. Why are they afraid of God? Because they know that God is the proprietor of everything, and they do not want to give up their false sense of proprietorship, therefore they'd rather not believe in God. It makes life very easy. Or even if they believe in God, they want to keep him on the shelf somewhere, or they'll go to see him on Sundays. But the rest of the week I have my own life. But King Guha, he was a pure hearted servant, he was a real saint in the true sense, because he accepted that, ‘everything is yours my Lord, please take it, as I am your servant, enjoy.’

Higher in Position so Lower of a Servant

Maharaja Priyavrat, when he accepted the kingdom, he never thought himself to be the King. He considered himself the humble servant, not only of God but of every citizen in the kingdom. That ‘it is my duty to sacrifice my life even for the most insignificant creature in the kingdom if he needs my help.’ There's also the story of Maharaja Sibi. He was a King. One day a vulture was chasing after a pigeon, and the pigeon fell at the feet of Maharajah Sibi and said, 'please, you are the king. I am in your kingdom; therefore, I am your subject. It is your duty to protect me. This vulture is coming to kill me.' Maharaja Sibi said 'Yes, vulture will not harm you, I am here to protect you'. The vulture said, 'huh! But I am also your subject. I am also living in your kingdom and my food is pigeon. So, because of you, you're gonna starve me to death.' So, Maharaj Sibi said,' oh my god!’ See in this world you can please some of the people some of the time, but you can never please all the people all the time. But it's the duty of the King to try to please all the people all the time, even at the cost of his life. So, he said, 'Alright Mr. Vulture, whatever the weight of this pigeon is, I will give you out of my own flesh.' The vulture said, 'all right, very good'. They put the pigeon on a scale and then the King started cutting off his flesh and putting it on the other side of his scale. And however much he put; the pigeon always was a little heavier. And he was cutting piece after piece after piece of his own flesh and the pigeon was still more! So, finally Maharaja Sibi said, 'alright.' and he sat on the scale himself with his whole body and it was perfect even weight. And the King said, 'then you eat me'. If the king is not willing to give his life in the service to protect even the most insignificant service, he has no right being king. The king is the servant. In spiritual classification, the higher the position you take, the lower of a servant you become. In material calculation, the higher the position, the more you think yourself master. But in spiritual life the higher position, the more you consider yourself servant.  

So, at that time, the pigeon and the vulture turned into, their bodies were just completely transformed into beautiful celestial beings and they said King Sibi, 'we just wanted to test you... your glory, we wanted to show the world: what are the qualities of a devotee of God? How he is so compassionate'. So, Maharaja Guha, he was saying to Lord Rama, 'please take everything, it is all yours. You are the Lord of my life.' And then he began to offer the most menial services to Rama. 'It is getting close to dark, I will help you cross the Ganges tomorrow.' And with his own hands he began to collect leaves and soft grass, and he began to make a bed for Lord Ramachandra, and of course Lakshman was helping him. Then after the bed was made very nicely, he approached Sita and Rama 'please take rest, be happy.’ He fanned him, he fed him with fruits and whatever the best he could come... come up with from the forest. In this way, Lord Ramachandra was perfectly delighted. He was absolutely happy and pleased. The day before he was living in the kingdom of Ayodhya, in a palace of marble and gold and jewels. In the softest most comfortable bed. With servants and opulent foods all around him. And now he was living in the jungle and a bed of leaves. But because that bed of leaves was prepared by the devoted hands of Maharaj Guha, that bed of leaves was more luxurious, more pleasant, more enjoyable than even the most opulent expensive beds in the heavenly planet. And through the night Maharaja Guha did not even consider sleeping. He was simply guarding Lord Ramachandra and making sure everything was just right. And Sita Ram, they slept with great pleasure. And they awoke in the morning and Maharaja Guha helped them across the Ganges. 

Humility

In this way, he showed us, Lord Shri Ramachandra showed us, that he only sees the quality of our humility and the quality of our devotion. He does not see our position. [Maharaja coughs] So, therefore, there is no need to change our occupation. There is no need to change our caste or our position in society. There's no reason to change our religion! When our guru Maharaj AC Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada came to the West, some Christian people approached him and said, 'why you have come to our country? What do we need you Hindus for? We have our own religion. We're Christians.' You know, some Christians, they're very... of course, some people from every religion... they are very narrow-minded, 'we have our religion and it's the only religion'. Hindus like that too - Muslims like that too- there are Buddhist. Everywhere religion has it’s what they call fundamentalist huh, extremist. So, this person was a very, very important newspaper reporter from a very big newspaper. 'We are a Christian country; we don't need you. Go home'. 

Here he is a 70-year-old man, he's come humbly to try to render some service. Being a spiritual personality, I'll tell you right now, it is a thankless task. People think, 'oh! they're giving him flowers' but after they give them flowers, all they give them is all their problems, right? They give you a garland they touch your feet, they say 'oh you are very good' and everyone's thinking 'I just see he's just enjoying' but they don't see what comes after the garland and the feet touching. We have to work out all their problems… counselling... so much …. absolute sacrifice. Our guru Maharaja came all the way, three heart attacks, on a steamship, no money, 70 years old, didn't know anybody. He comes to this foreign land as the humble servant of God and here's this woman is telling him, 'Go home you rascal. We don't need you.' And how did he reply? He said,' I have not come to convert you, I have come to enlighten you. You are a Christian, be a real Christian. A real Christian is one who loves God! I am simply teaching love of God! Chant the name of God, follow the laws of God, you have your traditions, do not change your traditions, but understand the essence. We have our traditions; we will not change our traditions but we have the essence! What is the essence? That we can all come together to glorify God, to serve God, to sacrifice for God. There's one God. And Lord Ramachandra has showed us through his great ple...pleasurable reciprocation with Maharaja Guha, that it is this quality of devotion, not any sectarian affiliation, but it is the quality of devotion that actually brings us near and dear to the Supreme Lord. We should not see the things of this world with superficial eyes. We should understand what is really of value. The things of this world have a beginning and an end and yet we're wasting our entire life in pursuit of these things.

Attachment to the eternal 

kamala-dala-jala, jivana talamala  bhajahu hari-pada niti re , Govindadas has prayed like this: 

e dhana, yaubana, putra, parijana 

ithe ki āche paratīti re 

kamala-dala-jala, jīvana ṭalamala 

bhajahū hari-pada nīti re. 

[Bhaja Hu Re Mana Verse 3]

[What assurance of real happiness is there in all of one's wealth, youthfulness, sons, and family members? This life is tottering like a drop of water on a lotus petal; therefore you should always serve and worship the divine feet of Lord Hari.]

He says, ‘throughout my life I have been attached to my wife, my children, to my wealth and to my home, my position in society, to my prestige. So, now I'm an old man, and what do I find? That despite having all these things and working for all these things all my life, my life is full of frustration and anxiety. Kamala dala jala jivana talamala all these things that I am putting so much value in, they are like a drop of water on the leaf of a lotus flower. They're here today, tomorrow they slip away. Bhajahu hari pada niti re. Therefore, from this day on let me concentrate my mind, fix my mind on the feet of Lord Hari.

bhajahū re mana śrī-nanda-nandana 

abhaya-caraṇāravinda re 

durlabha mānava-janama sat-sańge

[Bhaja Hu Re Mana Verse 1]

[O mind just worship the lotus feet of the son of Nanda, which make one fearless. Having obtained this rare human birth, cross over this ocean of worldly existence through the association of saintly persons.]

  

My dear mind, please fix your mind, fix yourself on the feet of the Supreme Lord, the beautiful son of Nanda Maharaja, Shri Krishna. If you fix your mind anywhere else, you will simply be filled with fear. Why? Because the feet of God is the only thing eternal. Everything else will be taken away, and why do we fear in this world? Because we are always afraid of the inevitable, that everything we are dedicating our precious human energy to is going to be taken away today or tomorrow and you have no power to stop it. So, therefore, an intelligent person discriminates what actually is valuable! 

Story of Laksha Hira

Last night we told a nice story. I was requested to tell it again this evening and it is very instructive in this regard. This is a story that is found in one of the great ancient Puranas. And this story is also repeated by Srila Krishnadas Kaviraj Goswami, the author of Chaitanya Charitamrita. He refers to this beautiful narration. It is the story of a brahmin, and this brahman, he was suffering from the disease of leprosy. It was a very painful condition for him. Of course, you know, especially here in India, you know what is leprosy. You know how incapacitated it can make a person. Now this Brahmin had a very, very virtuous, and beautiful wife. She was coming from a very, very high family but she was married to this man. And after he had leprosy, she felt that it was her duty. and as her... her religious duty and service to always be true and faithful to help her husband through his miserable suffering life. She would cook for him, she would clean him, she would have to work for him in every respect. He could do nothing. So, one day she brought him to a holy river, and with her own hands she had to bathe him. While she was bathing him, there was a very beautiful prostitute. Her name was Laksha Hira, which means one whose beautiful lustre is like... is like hundreds and thousands of diamonds and precious jewels. 

So, after the bath, with great care and attention she took her husband home. But that evening as she was trying to feed him, she saw that he was very, very much dissatisfied at mind. She said, 'my dear husband! What is wrong with you? What can I do to make you happy? I will do anything. What is causing you distress?'. He said, 'I'm afr... I’m ashamed to tell you'. She said, 'No you must tell me. I am your wife'. He said, 'You remember that prostitute we saw today? I have become very attached to her. I cannot possibly get her out of my mind.' So, the wife with tears in her eyes she said,' my hu... my dear husband, do you want this prostitute?'. He said 'yes'. With folded palms she said, ‘I will do everything I can to keep you happy.’ 

 That family of Brahmins had no money. So, what she did is, she went to the house of that prostitute. There were so many other prostitutes living there, and every day she would clean the whole house from the ground floor to the top floor immaculately. The house was never so clean. So, this one, Laksha Hira, she asked one of her associates, ‘How is it that this house is always so immaculately clean? Who is doing this?' One of her associates said, 'It is this Brahmin lady. Although she's a Brahmin, she comes, she doesn't accept any payment. And even the workers who are supposed to clean this house... she works so hard, they have nothing to do.’ So Laksha Hira says, 'Please bring this lady to me. I do not understand'. So, the brahmins wife approached her. She said, 'Why are you doing like this? You are a Brahmin. Why are you serving an outcast like me?' She said, 'because my husband is very anxious to have you. My only desire is, you satisfy him'. Laksha Hira replied, 'Ah, I understand. You and your husband come to my home for dinner tonight.’ 

 So, they both came for dinner and she had prepared very, very, nice cuisine. She divided the meal into two sections. She said, 'Here on this side, there is very, very pure sattvic vegetarian prasad...Maha Prasad and it is sitting on a simple plantain leaf and in this simple clay cup there is the water from the Ganges. And on the other side there is solid gold and silver plates and cups and bowls with all decorative designs very costly and exquisite, fit for a king. And in it is the most opulent luxurious food cooked in the finest ghee, but each preparation is made out of meat.’ So, she said, ‘Now you take your choice'. The Brahmin said,' I will take the Mahaprasad'. So, the Brahmin began to eat the nice Prasad off the plantain leaf and the clay cup and after he was finished and he was very satisfied, the prostitute looks at him and she said, 'your wife is like this Prasad, and although she is very simple, not very attractive, very poor. She's like a clay cup and a plantain leaf, but in the inside, she is very sattvic – pure Prasad.’ She said, ‘I am like these gold and silver bowls. I may be very luxurious and very beautiful and very glorious on the outside but inside there is simply Rajasic and Tamasic passion and ignorant. So, why did you come here to see me, when you already have the best?’ The Brahmin, when he heard this, he became enlightened. Simply by hearing this instruction, he became completely detached from his sinful motivations in life and with folded hands he said, ‘today I have been enlightened, my dear. You are my guru' and from that day on, that Brahmin was in the highest planes of realization of what is really valuable in this world.  

Value of Time

Do you realize the value of every moment of human life? Even the wealthiest man in the entire creation, if he gives all of his money, can he buy back one second that is past? No. Therefore, every moment you would have is more valuable than any wealth. Each moment of your life is like a precious invaluable pearl. It is said in the Bible, 'you should not throw your pearls to the swine.’ Throw your pearls to the swine; swine means pig, hogs. Hogs like stool, right? A hog is very satisfied with eating your stool. So, why do you throw him your pearls? He does not require it. The time we waste seeking out the gold and silver bowls of this phantasmagoria of the illusory glitter of this temporary world, we are throwing the pearls of our precious time to the swine. Human life means to discriminate what is actually of value. 

āyur harati vai puṁsām udyann astaṁ ca yann asau. [Sb. 2.3.17] It is described in Bhagavatam, the time gone with every rising and setting of the Sun, you are one day closer to death. You're not getting any younger. You're getting older! Your time is limited! But for those who utilize their time to glorify God, to serve the... to serve the Lord of their heart, with every rising and setting of the Sun, they are one day closer to eternal. So, through the example of Maharaja Priyavrata, through the example of Maharaja Guha, through the example of this leper brahmin, and how many more examples do we need? Let us try to understand what is really the goal of life and what is valuable and what is worthy of my endeavor. Perform your duty, but understand how to perform your duty. Whatever you do should be an act of humble devotion to the Supreme Lord.  

Now, how is that possible? It is very simple. People are thinking perhaps what is all this theoretical information he's giving us? People in his world are very practical, very tangible. Spiritual life is the most practical, it is the most tangible. In fact, it is the only practical and only tangible alternative in life. First, we have to install the Lord within our heart, we have to recognize his presence in our heart and then whatever we do, we can do it as an offering to Him. The easiest way of recognizing the Lord within our life, is to be conscious of Him by chanting His name. It is so simple.

In this age of kali yuga, there is only one practical method, chant the name of God. God has come into this world as Shri Chaitanya Mahaprabhu to teach us this process. 

namnam akari bahudha nija-sarva-saktis 

tatrarpita niyamita smarane nakalah

[Cc. Antya. 20.16] 

[“ ‘My Lord, O Supreme Personality of Godhead, in Your holy name there is all good fortune for the living entity, and therefore You have many names, such as “Kṛṣṇa” and “Govinda,” by which You expand Yourself. You have invested all Your potencies in those names, and there are no hard and fast rules for remembering them. My dear Lord, although You bestow such mercy upon the fallen, conditioned souls by liberally teaching Your holy names, I am so unfortunate that I commit offenses while chanting the holy name, and therefore I do not achieve attachment for chanting.’]

You see, Lord Chaitanya. We are followers of Lord Chaitanya Mahaprabhu. He was very universal in his approach to religion. He explained in his prayer that ‘My Lord could have many, many names. We are not concerned with what religion you follow, or what name you chant, as long as it is an authorized name of God, which has been imparted by the Lord through the scripture. And anyone who chants any of your names with sincere...sincerity and humility, he will attain the supreme perfection of life. Because all of your powers, and all of your glories, and all of your opulences are within your name.’ But then the very under this sloka, he explains the proper disposition of mind in which you chant. He says, ‘Although you have so many names, and although you can attain all realization and you've empowered all your names of all your glories, I am so unfortunate that I have no attraction for any of your names.’

In other words, he is teaching us that unless you chant the names of God with humility, the result will be minimal. No one should think himself better than anyone else. If you do, you are the lowest of the low. Spiritual advancement is exclusively on the basis of humility. Jesus said, 'the meek shall inherit the earth.' The greater we think we are, the lower we are in the eyes of God. The lower we think we are, the higher we are under eyes of God. That is the proper disposition of a servant.
