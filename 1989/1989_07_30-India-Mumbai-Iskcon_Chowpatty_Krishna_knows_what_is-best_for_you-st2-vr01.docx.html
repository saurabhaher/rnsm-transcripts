Lecture Transcription

gopīnāṁ tat-patīnāṁ ca
sarveṣām eva dehinām
yo ’ntaś carati so ’dhyakṣaḥ
krīḍaneneha deha-bhāk

[Sb. 10.33.35]

[He who lives as the overseeing witness within the gopīs and their husbands, and indeed within all embodied living beings, assumes forms in this world to enjoy transcendental pastimes..]

Purport

We certainly do not assume our bodies to enjoy transcendental pastimes, as the Lord does. We eternal souls have accepted material bodies by force because of our foolish attempt to enjoy this material world. The Lord’s forms are all eternal, spiritual existence and cannot be reasonably equated with our temporary flesh.

Since Lord Kṛṣṇa is the Supreme Lord dwelling within the gopīs, their so-called husbands and all other living beings, what possible sin could there be on His part if He embraces some of the beings He Himself has created? What fault could there be if the Lord goes with the gopīs to a secret place, since He already dwells within the most secret part of every living being, the core of the heart?

nāsūyan khalu kṛṣṇāya
mohitās tasya māyayā
manyamānāḥ sva-pārśva-sthān
svān svān dārān vrajaukasaḥ

[Sb. 10.33.37]

[The cowherd men, bewildered by Kṛṣṇa’s illusory potency, thought their wives had remained home at their sides. Thus they did not harbor any jealous feelings against Him.]

Purport

Because the gopīs loved Kṛṣṇa exclusively, Yoga-māyā protected their relationship with the Lord at all times, even though they were married. Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī quotes from the Ujjvala-nīlamaṇi as follows:

māyā-kalpita-tādṛk-strī
śīlanenānusūyubhiḥ
na jātu vrajadevīnāṁ
patibhiḥ saha saṅgamaḥ

“The gopīs’ jealous husbands consorted not with their wives but with doubles manufactured by Māyā. Thus these men never actually had any intimate contact with the divine ladies of Vraja.” The gopīs are the internal energy of the Lord and can never belong to any other living being. Kṛṣṇa arranged their apparent marriage to other men simply to create the excitement of parakīya-rasa, the love between a married woman and her paramour. These activities are absolutely pure because they are the Lord’s pastimes, and saintly persons since time immemorial have relished these supreme spiritual events.

brahma-rātra upāvṛtte
vāsudevānumoditāḥ
anicchantyo yayur gopyaḥ
sva-gṛhān bhagavat-priyāḥ

[Sb. 10.33.38]

[ After an entire night of Brahmā had passed, Lord Krishna advised the gopīs to return to their homes. Although they did not wish to do so, the Lord’s beloved consorts complied with His command.]

Purport

In the Bhagavad-gītā (8.17) Lord Kṛṣṇa explains, “By human calculation, a thousand ages taken together is the duration of Brahmā’s one day. And such also is the duration of his night.” Thus one thousand ages entered within a single twelve-hour night when Lord Kṛṣṇa performed His rāsa dance. Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī compares this inconceivable impression of time to the fact that many universes fit neatly within the forty-mile range of earthly Vṛndāvana. Or one may consider that mother Yaśodā could not encircle the small abdomen of child Kṛṣṇa with numerous ropes, and that at another time He manifested many universes within His mouth. The transcendence of spiritual reality above and beyond mundane physics is concisely explained in Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī’s Laghu-bhagavatāmṛta:

evaṁ prabhoḥ priyāṇāṁ ca
dhāmnaś ca samayasya ca
avicintya-prabhāvatvād
atra kiṣcin na durghaṭam

“Nothing is impossible for the Lord, His dear devotees, His transcendental abode or the time of His pastimes, for all these entities are inconceivably powerful.”

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī further explains that the word vāsudevānumoditāḥ indicates that Lord Kṛṣṇa advised the gopīs, “To assure the success of these pastimes, you and I should keep them secret.” The word vāsudeva, a name of Kṛṣṇa, also indicates Lord Kṛṣṇa’s plenary expansion who acts as the presiding Deity of consciousness. When the word vāsudeva is understood in this context, the word vāsudevānumoditāḥ indicates that the presiding Deity of consciousness, Vāsudeva, manifested embarrassment and fear of their elders within the gopīs’ hearts, and therefore it was only with great reluctance that the young girls returned home.

[mangalacarana] hare kṛṣṇa hare kṛṣṇa kṛṣṇa kṛṣṇa hare hare/hare rāma hare rāma rāma rāma hare hare.

janma karma ca me divyam
evaṁ yo vetti tattvataḥ
tyaktvā dehaṁ punar janma
naiti mām eti so ’rjuna

[Bg. 4.9]

[One who knows the transcendental nature of My appearance and activities does not, upon leaving the body, take his birth again in this material world, but attains My eternal abode, O Arjuna.]

Krishna’s pastimes are transcendental and inconceivable

The transcendental nature of Krishna's birth and activities are not subjected to the limitations of material creation. They are purely spiritual before they are ananta they are unlimited without restriction. In this material world we are always frustrated because the soul is eternal and it is looking for eternal pleasure but due to the time factor all pleasures have a beginning and an end. The soul is part of Krishna who’s unlimited, the soul is seeking for the unlimited nature of Krishna, but it is constantly frustrated because in this creation everything is limited, limited by time and limited by space. But in the spiritual existence, all of these material qualities are conspicuous by its absence. na tad bhāsayate sūryo [Bg. 15.6] Krishna Says in Gita, that in the transcendental world there is no need for sun or moon or electricity because everything is by nature light sat-chit-ananda eternal, full of knowledge and full of bliss, knowledge is light, ignorance is darkness. This material world is a place of asat-acit -niranand it is a place that is temporary and it is full of ignorance, it is full of pain. That is why the whole material world is dark, nothing emanates its own light, our bodies are ignorant they are simply dark and everything around us. Therefore the sun which is a gift of God comes to bring light into the darkness of this world. But when the sun sets everything by its own nature is dark, but in the spiritual world every body of every living being, every element, all substances are cit, they're full of knowledge, therefore they emanate their own brilliant light. It is said that the soul itself is more radiant than ten thousand suns. But when that soul is covered by darkness due to forgetfulness, ignorance its qualities are hidden.

Description of Spiritual World and Material World

Krishna has created his own abode with pure spiritual elements, therefore there is no darkness. Time itself is conspicuous by its absence. Therefore ananta everything about the world of Vaikunta is unlimited without restriction and therefore it is a place of ananda, a place of bliss. It is our home. This world is a strange foreign land, this world is a place of terrible culture shock for the eternal soul. When we become…. we become habituated conditioned and addicted to the darkness of this world that is our great misfortune. Therefore Krishna descends into this world to reveal the pure joyful unlimited pastimes of the spiritual world.

Gopis the internal potency of the Lord Krishna

So what is said here in the Srimad-Bhagavatam we should have no doubt that it is a fact, it is true, how Krishna arranges his lila in the most wonderful exciting ways. Sukadeva Gosvami explains here that the husbands of the gopis, they were never although they loved their wives they were never jealous of Krishna, why? because their wives were always by their side. And Viswanath Chakravarthy Thakur explains that the gopis were the internal potency of the lord and they're exclusively meant only for Krishna's satisfaction, therefore there is no possibility of any other living being even touching the body of the gopis. And he is explaining that Krishna by his supreme yogamaya potency, he created maya form of every gopi he manufactured an exact duplicate of every gopi and that duplicate was living with the husband. But the the gopi herself, she was with Krishna during this rasa-lila. And whenever there was a time when any intimate dealing between a husband or wife would take place, it was the maya imitation gopi that was with the husband. But factually the husbands themselves in Vrindavan under the spell of yogamaya were simply thinking in terms of Krishna's pleasure and therefore whatever they did whether it be with the maya gopi or with the original gopi they did it only to increase the ecstasy of the gopis Lila with Krishna. In this way by the inconceivable unlimited potency of Krishna he maintained the absolute most purest position of the gopis and at the same time facilitated the highest ecstasy of divine love called parakiya-rasa, the love between the lord with the excitement of the danger and the surrender of having to break the laws of mundane morality.

Krishna knows what is best for you

In this material world, if we behave immorally we are simply immoral. Krishna, he can arrange the lila of Vrindavan in such a way that we can appear to be immoral just to create the special ecstasy of that excitement but remain perfectly moral on every level. And similarly because the gopis for so long cherished a desire to dance with Shri Krishna, Krishna condensed an entire night of Brahma, which is billions of our earthly years into twelve hours one evening how is this possible? For Krishna what is impossible?

Description of Dwaraka

We read in Srimad-Bhagavatam when Krishna is living in Dwaraka which was only a relatively small island, there were hundreds and millions and billions of people living in Dwarka and yet we do not read about traffic jams or overpopulation. We simply read about beautiful parks, beautiful lakes, fresh clean water, fresh air, beautiful wonderful palaces with wonderful wonderful gardens all around. And we read about how Krishna he took care in vraja bhoomi of Nanda Maharaja’s cows, nine hundred thousand cows. And [how they would] there was no problem controlling them, they would all go to the Yamuna to drink water together. Now by material calculations it would take all day to feed nine hundred thousand cows on the bank of a river but Krishna he did it all within a few minutes and went on with his pastimes. And how is it that Krishna manifested the entire universe within his mouth to show this wonderful darshan to mother Yashoda, all the mountains, all the trees and forests, all the planets all the oceans, all the abodes of the devatas. They were all seen in the tiny little mouth of Gopal. How is it, that mother Yashoda could get all the ropes in Vraj bhoomi tie it together and it was always two fingers too short from going around the waist of Krishna. This is Krishna's inconceivable potency achintya shakti, because he is Ananta, he is unlimited for the pleasure of his pastimes with his devotees he can do anything, he's the supreme controller.

Krishna’s inconceivable potency in the form of Kala and real opulence of Vrindavan

Krishna condensed an entire night of Brahma trillions of billions of years into twelve hours simply to satisfy his devotees. Because Krishna says in Gita “Time I am devouring all living beings.” Krishna's kala rupa, in this form he is the supreme controller, the supreme lord of this entire creation everything is under the control of time, but the time is under the control of Krishna. What is it that Krishna cannot do to give pleasure to his devotees? Krishna's bhaktavatsal simply to satisfy the heart's desires of the Vraj gopis. Krishna has made all of these incredible wonderful arrangements. Vrindavan lila, it was explained in a discussion between Srivasa Thakur and Swaroop Damodar. Srivasa pandit challenged Swaroop Damodar that “What is your Vrindavan? just some trees and some cows but my Lakshmi Devi in Vaikuntha, she lives in beautiful beautiful palaces and there is so much opulence, so much luxury, so much wealth, so much beauty. This Vrindavan is a very simple place how can we compare it to the abode of my Lakshmi?” He was speaking in this way just so that Swaroop Damodar Goswami would reveal to the world the truth, the essence of the opulence of Vrindavan. And Swaroop Damodar Goswami replied that, “Vrindavan is a place where every granule of dirt is chintamani dham. It possesses all opulence, all one's desires are fulfilled just by approaching any of the kalpa vriksha trees. Every grain of the dust is more valuable than the most… most precious of all jewels. It is a place where no one walks everyone dances, where no one speaks but everyone sings. It is the ultimate supreme most abode of opulence and love.[but the opulence of].
