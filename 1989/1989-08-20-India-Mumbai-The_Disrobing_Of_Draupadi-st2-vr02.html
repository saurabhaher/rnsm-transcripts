[mangalacarana]

ye yathā māṁ prapadyante
tāṁs tathaiva bhajāmy aham
mama vartmānuvartante
manuṣyāḥ pārtha sarvaśaḥ

[Bg. 4.11]

As all surrender unto Me, I reward them accordingly. Everyone follows My path in all respects, O son of Pṛthā.

Purport

Everyone is searching for Kṛṣṇa in the different aspects of His manifestations. Kṛṣṇa, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, is partially realized in His impersonal brahma-jyotir effulgence and as the all-pervading Supersoul dwelling within everything, including the particles of atoms. But Kṛṣṇa is fully realized only by His pure devotees. Consequently, Kṛṣṇa is the object of everyone’s realization, and thus anyone and everyone is satisfied according to one’s desire to have Him. In the transcendental world also, Kṛṣṇa reciprocates with His pure devotees in the transcendental attitude, just as the devotee wants Him. One devotee may want Kṛṣṇa as supreme master, another as his personal friend, another as his son and still another as his lover. Kṛṣṇa rewards all the devotees equally, according to their different intensities of love for Him. In the material world, the same reciprocations of feelings are there, and they are equally exchanged by the Lord with the different types of worshipers. The pure devotees both here and in the transcendental abode associate with Him in person and are able to render personal service to the Lord and thus derive transcendental bliss in His loving service. As for those who are impersonalists and who want to commit spiritual suicide by annihilating the individual existence of the living entity, Kṛṣṇa helps also by absorbing them into His effulgence. Such impersonalists do not agree to accept the eternal, blissful Personality of Godhead; consequently, they cannot relish the bliss of transcendental personal service to the Lord, having extinguished their individuality. Some of them, who are not firmly situated even in the impersonal existence, return to this material field to exhibit their dormant desires for activities. They are not admitted into the spiritual planets, but they are again given a chance to act on the material planets. For those who are fruitive workers, the Lord awards the desired results of their prescribed duties, as the yajṣeśvara; and those who are yogīs seeking mystic powers are awarded such powers. In other words, everyone is dependent for success upon His mercy alone, and all kinds of spiritual processes are but different degrees of success on the same path. Unless, therefore, one comes to the highest perfection of Kṛṣṇa consciousness, all attempts remain imperfect, as is stated in the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (2.3.10):

akāmaḥ sarva-kāmo vā
mokṣa-kāma udāra-dhīḥ
tīvreṇa bhakti-yogena
yajeta puruṣaṁ param

“Whether one is without desire [the condition of the devotees], or is desirous of all fruitive results or is after liberation, one should with all efforts try to worship the Supreme Personality of Godhead for complete perfection, culminating in Kṛṣṇa consciousness.”

Lecture Transcription

Material opulence brings spiritual complacency

In the Bhagavad-gita, Lord Sri Krishna instructs us,

ye yathā māṁ prapadyante
tāṁs tathaiva bhajāmy aham
mama vartmānuvartante
manuṣyāḥ pārtha sarvaśaḥ

[Bg. 4.11]

[As all surrender unto Me, I reward them accordingly. Everyone follows My path in all respects, O son of Pṛthā.]

[coughing] “That as you surrender unto me, I reward you accordingly.” The Supreme Personality of Godhead is within the heart of all of us. He also describes, sarvasya cāhaṁ hṛdi sanniviṣṭo mattaḥ smṛtir jṣānam apohanaṁ ca [Bg. 15.15]. That as the lord seated within the core of our heart, he is the source of our remembrance, our knowledge and our forgetfulness. In fact, he reciprocates with us exactly according to our desire in life. [coughing] Therefore, in essence, the path of yoga or the path of religion is the process taught by the lord and his great servitors by which we can learn how to properly approach God? How to approach him with the proper desire? You see, in this world, the tendency is to become very spiritually complacent. Especially, when our material necessities are very nicely provided. Because factually almost everyone is more or less completely materialistic, Ah… even they…though we may have some religion in our life. When it comes down to where our energy is directed, it's more or less with the motive of providing the necessities and the pleasures of our senses and the senses of those who are near and dear to us. And therefore, when the necessities of life, the pleasures of life, are being very nicely provided then there really is not so much need to ask much of God. We will casually request him to give us liberation at the time of death and to protect the situation that we have in this world. But there is not much intensity in our prayer to the lord.

Tests brings out best

Therefore, knowing this tendency to be so deeply rooted within our conditioned consciousness, the lord creates often times very very difficult tests for the aspiring devotees. And the purpose of these tests is to give the devotee the chance to cry out in helpless dependence for God's mercy. Sometimes, when a devotee is in a predicament, someone may ask why is God allowing that to happen to his devotee? The answer is, because he is very kind to his devotee. You see, the greatest obstacle in our spiritual life is false pride and everyone is a victim of false pride. Everyone is thinking,

prakṛteḥ kriyamāṇāni
guṇaiḥ karmāṇi sarvaśaḥ
ahaṅkāra-vimūḍhātmā
kartāham iti manyate

[Bg. 3.27]

[The spirit soul bewildered by the influence of false ego thinks himself the doer of activities that are in actuality carried out by the three modes of material nature.]

That I am the doer of my activities. Just see, what I have done? Just see, what I am doing? And wait and see what I will do? Literally everyone is a victim of this way of thinking. And when everything is going our way, we consider that we have everything very nicely under control. And therefore, what is the need of God? We may do our puja, we may chant our mantras. But for our practical purposes, what really is the need of God? By my good efforts, by my intelligence, everything is going on just according to plan. This consciousness is the worst enemy to one who wants to chant the name of God properly. When we chant the holy names of the lord, we must understand, that it is the quality in which we chant, which will be the cause of the result of our chanting. Some people chant mantras so many times everyday for so many years and they wonder - why I am not feeling the divine presence of God? Because this process is not mechanical. In the process of bhakti, nothing is mechanical. [coughing]

No formalities, only bhakti with qualities

Recently, we approached one very great sadhu and we were discussing formalities in the vaishnava tradition. And he was explaining, in the vaishnava tradition, there are no formalities. Every single relationship and every way we deal with every single person is not a formality. It is an expression of love, it is a means of purification. Someone asks.. Aha… “Is [coughing] relation of the diksha guru or the sannyas guru more important? Or is one just a formality?” He said, “What is this formality? There are no questions of formalities.” Every relationship with we have and every way we deal with every individual, is based on love and respect and service. You see, the path of bhakti casts out all empty formalities and all superficial mechanical processes. Everything we do, every dealing we have is meant to be done in an attitude of service, devotion and sacrifice. And of all, [coughing] to the principles, in the process of bhakti, there is nothing more emphasized, nothing more essential for our spiritual purification than the chanting of the holy names of God. So, how much we must guard against taking the chanting of the holy names as a formality, or as a mechanical process. When we chant the name of God, we are petitioning the lord who is non-different than his name to reciprocate with us by showering us with his mercy. And he will reciprocate [coughing] exactly according to the feeling in which we are chanting, to the sincerity in which we call out his name. Therefore, the lord describes to Maharaj Yudhishthir, yasyāham anugṛhṇāmi hariṣye tad-dhanaṁ śanaiḥ [SB. 10.88.8]. Sometimes, I purposely put my devotees in difficult situations so that my devotee has no one else and nothing else to turn to except me and in such a state of consciousness, he cries out my holy name with very very high quality of feeling.

Perfect questions, perfect answers

Today, many of you perhaps have witnessed on the Mahabharata series, the disrobing of Draupadi. And people with a philosophical mind often question, why did Krishna allow this to happen to his devotees? First of all, why Maharaj Yudhishthir, who's such a great great king, with such integrity as an honest man, why in the world would he take part in a gambling match? After all, there are four regulative principles no illicit sex, no intoxication, no meat-eating and no gambling, so why would he gamble? That is first question. Second is, why would Krishna allow this evil-minded Shakuni and Duryodhana to win the gambling match and take everything away? And why, if the Pandavas were such great heroes, such fearless devotees of the lord, why did they sit there on the floor and say nothing and do nothing to protect their own wife who is being stripped naked by unscrupulous personalities? These questions, they have come to your mind?

Qualities of a kshatriya

First of all, let us understand that whatever the lord does through his devotees, often has many many many purposes to fulfill. There are external and internal reasons for all the dealings of the dev… of lord with his devotees. First of all, Maharaj Yudhishthir was a kshatriya. Now, today there are no kshatriyas, there is practically no such thing as a kshatriya on earth anymore who has the qualities of a real kshatriya.

In the Bhagavad-gita, Krishna describes, what are the qualities of a real kshatriya. He is completely fearless, he is heroic, he is expert in combat, he is the protector of the innocent, he is chivalrous. In the days of the vedic age, the king, when he would declare war against another military force, he would be the first one on a battlefield with a sword in his hand to fight. So, you see, the king would not declare war unless there was something very important because his life was at stake. Therefore, he had to be very heroic and fearless.

Today’s self-protective and coward politicians

Today what kings, what prime ministers, what presidents, would even consider going anywhere near the battlefield. Ah? They usually remain thousands of miles away in a nice presidential mansion with so many security guards, at secret aids, secret service men all around, protecting them. And they are living in a plush room with soft furniture, drinking their liquor, watching their TV and making all sorts of political decisions. And what are those political decisions? To send the innocent youth to the battlefield, where they're being slaughtered wholesalely by the enemy. And he just keeps recruiting more and more and more and more men to go to the battlefield to shed their blood.

By the vedic standard that person is not a king, he is not kshatriya, he is a coward. He is a low-class man who has taken the role that he does not deserve to have. But we find that the political leaders of every country in the world are like this.

Real kshatriya are always ready to accept challenges

But in the vedic times, when kshatriya were kshatriyas, even the worst most heinous demons, if they were king, they would be first in the battlefield, what to speak of the righteous. Because that was the code of kshatriya. The code of kshatriya was that he would never ever ever refuse any challenge, whatever it was. Therefore, when Maharaj Yudhisthir was challenged to a game of dice, as a kshatriya, it was his duty, he was obliged that he could not refuse that challenge. Whether it was licit or illicit, it was a challenge, they had to be accepted. So, the question is, if somebody challenges us to gamble, does that mean we should gamble. Ah… Because sometimes people do like this, I bet you that this happens like this. So does that mean we should think ‘Well! Maharaj Yudhisthir could not refuse a challenge so neither can I! Ah…? if you are a brahman, a vaishya, a sudra or anything else, you are not allowed to gamble ever. But if you are a qualified kshatriya, then you have that right.

Kshatriya risk his life to protects all citizens

Now, as I say there are no kshatriyas on earth any more of that stature. So therefore, gambling is not justified for anyone today. It is simply illicit activity. If you are willing to give your life to protect any innocent creature on earth then you have a right to gamble like Maharaj Yudhisthir. Because Maharaj Yudhisthir was willing to give his life, risk his life to protect any innocent creature in the kingdom, even if it be an insignificant dog or a cow or a low-class person. [coughing] Maharaj Yudhisthir was the protector of all praja, all the… Aha… citizens of his kingdom. And if even the most insignificant personality came to him with a problem, he took it as his life and soul to solve the problem of that person, even if it meant risking his life. If we have that quality, then we can know that we are ksha… kshatriya who must accept all challenge. So therefore, for Maharaj Yudhisthir, there was absolutely nothing illicit about accepting gambling. It would have been illicit for him to refuse because it would have been below his integrity as a kshatriya to refuse any challenge. [coughing] Second of all, Krishna wanted to show how horribly dangerous gambling really is. He wanted to warm us… warn us through this episode that nobody should ever go near this process of gambling. Whoever you are, however great you are, when you come in the process of this activity, you become intoxicated and you lose your intelligence. And it is a fact.

Infatuation of gambling

Just like in America, there is, or in India too. All over the world there is alcoholics anonymous. [coughing] There is drug users anonymous. Now, the biggest growing organization of this type in the western world is gamblers anonymous. Because once you get gambling in your system, it's an addiction that impels you to gamble again and again and again. You cannot stop, it goes beyond your control. All of your rational thinking and intelligence is stolen away by this vice. And it is a fact, it is habit-forming. Therefore, even such a great soul as Maharaj Yudhisthir, if he lost his intelligence due to the infatuation of gambling, what is our position? How much we must be very careful to eve… never ever ever even consider. It is one of the four pillars of sinful life.

Integrity of Maharaj Yudhisthir

Krishna also wanted to show, the nobility of Maharaj Yudhisthir. How he was such a man of high honour that when he would give his word, there would be no condition possible that would divert his attention from fulfilling his promise. Today, words are so cheap. People just… their tongues are so uncontrolled, their minds are so uncontrolled, they will just say anything, not considering the consequence or the responsibility they have for each word they speak. Uha… People are very fond of gossiping, criticizing others, not knowing what a responsibility they have, and what the consequences that will come upon them for doing this. Not even knowing whether what they're saying is actually true or untrue, they speak. This is the sign of a very low class mentality.

Maharaj Yudhisthir, he would not speak anything that was not truth. He would not speak anything that was not beneficial for all persons involved. In whatever he would say, he would back up with his life. Aha… Now we say something and if we're challenged, you say like this then ‘Well!... I didn't mean it like that or I didn't actually say that.’ Aha… So easy. When Maharaj Yudhisthir would say something, he would back that word up with everything he had. This is called honor. Krishna describes that for kshatriya to be dishonored as worse than death. How this a bodily conception of life? Why are we so attached to honor? It is not simply a idea of false prestige that to be dishonored as worse than death. But for a kshatriya, because they had such high integrity that whatever they would say, whatever they would do in the service of God. If they would have backed down or be dishonest in any way shape or form, that would be worse than death.

Morality Vs Spirituality

You see in this age of Kali yuga, people are not very concerned with honor or truth or honesty. But Maharaj Yudhisthir, [coughing] when he vowed to gamble his own life, his brother's life, his kingdom, all his possessions, all of his wealth and even his wife, although he did it on the intoxication of gambling, still he was a man of such honor that he would not back down anything. He understood, how responsible he was for everything he said. That is the conduct of a civilized man or a civilized woman.

Some people may say ‘Well! This is not a very spiritual thing, this is simply mundane morality.’ But this is a spiritual thing. Because unless Krishna specifically and directly tells you to say something against the codes of honesty, you have absolutely no right to tell a lie or to be dishonest in any circumstance of life without the blessings of the great souls. So, Maharaj Yudhisthir taught the people of the world, how to be a man of your word even under the most difficult conditions of life. You see, it's easy to become a man of your word when everything is just the way you like it. But when it takes great sacrifice and pain to stand behind your word, it takes a man a very deep sincerity. Maharaj Yudhishtir was such a man.

Flickering nature of this world

Krishna also wanted to show the flickering nature of everything of this world. How you could be the king of earth in one second and five minutes later you can have nothing. That is the situation of every one of us here today. You can be a big big industrialist with billions of dollars, you can be a very very expert scholar who can give wonderful discourses, you can be a very wonderful cook or housewife, all abilities to satisfy others. Whatever your situation, know that in five minutes, you can have nothing. That is the nature of this world. Krishna, through material nature can take away all your wealth, all of your intelligence, all of your popularity, all of your prestige, all of your abilities, in a second. [coughing] What he took away from Maharaj Yudhishtir? He had millions and millions of times more than any of us will ever hope to have, he lost it all in a second.

In… in my country America some years ago, Uha… just forty years ago, there was a depression where the stock market crashed and within five minutes people who were multi multi-millionaires living in big big palaces, five minutes later they were on the streets begging for a potato, with no place to sleep. [coughing] Now if that can happen in such a country as America, you don't think it can happen in a country like India? Rogues, thieves can attack at any moment. Krishna wanted to show us not to be too attached to anything because the flickering nature of this world, kamala-dala-jala, jīvana ṭalamala [Bhajahū re mana śrī-nanda-nandana 3] It’s here today and tomorrow it may be gone.

So, what are you going to attach yourself to? These things or to that which is permanent. The only thing permanent in life is our relationship with God. An intelligent man wisely invests his human energy primarily in establishing that relationship with God knowing that all the things of this world, today may be here but tomorrow they may be gone. It is not within our control ultimately. [coughing]

Swayamvara ceremony of Draupadi

Perhaps the most important lesson to be learned in this condition is that, in the ultimate issue, we are all completely helpless. Draupadi was a great queen, she was not an ordinary human being. By the great sacrificial ceremony of King Drupada, Draupadi was born of fire along with her brother Drustadyumna. [coughing] She was the wife of the five Pandavas. It is described, when the swayamvara ceremony of Draupadi was taking place, it was specially designed so that nobody except Arjuna could possibly have her hand in marriage. What had to be done by the person to win her hand was impossible by any other warrior on earth except two people, Arjuna and Karna. And of course, Bhishma, but because Bhishma was a lifelong brahmachari, everyone knew that he wouldn't be coming. So, when Karna approached, where there was a.. Aha… there was a fish.. Auh?... behind a moving wheel, and you have to look down in a reflection of water and pierce the eye of the fish without looking directly.

So, when Karna came, everyone was very afraid, ‘Oh! my God, he could do it.’ So, she insulted him by saying, “You are a low-class man. You are not really kshatriyaite, you are not allowed to be in this competition. After all your father is only a chariot driver therefore your distinction does not qualify you.” Means, she was very very special in her qualifications. She was meant to be the queen of earth. Now being in such a condition, the normal person will become very proud, ‘Just see what I have.’ Every woman who has a good husband is proud of her husband. Auh… Even a woman who has a terrible husband is.. tries to be proud of her husband. Ah… After all, this is all I have. [laughter] In America, it's not like that you just get a new one. [laughter] [coughing] But in India, you're stuck with the one you have. So, you might as well be proud of him. Right!

A chaste wife always support husband

And there are stories in the vedic literatures, a very very high class woman who were very… who were very protective and proud of even men who were very fallen, always trying to uplift them and help them. Just like Srila Prabhupad’s sister. She was a very religious, very pious woman and her husband used to go out drinking. Chastity is a most beautiful, precious ornament of woman

In this way, a woman by nature is really proud of her husband and if a woman has a good husband, my God! she becomes very proud, ‘Just see my husband, how good he is.’ Now Draupadi, if you think, Um… matajis, you have good husband, she had five best husbands. Chastity is always respected

Our guru maharaj, Srila Prabhupad tells the story of how, when he was young, he was once went with his friends in Calcutta and there was a streetsweeper woman, bhangi. Now for a woman of such high consciousness, to lose chastity is worse than death. They would welcome death a thousand times before losing their chastity. They would accept the tortures of hell before losing their chastity.

Psychology of envious person

Now, Draupadi was a woman of such quality. And Duryodhana knew this. And because he was so envious, he wanted in front of the Pandavas, for her to give up all her chastity by force simply to humiliate and embarrass those who he was envious of. This is the mentality of an envious person. He loves to see the suffering of others, he loves to see the degradation of others. If you are envious of someone, you cannot tolerate seeing that person glorified, you cannot tolerate hearing that person's name esteemed. [coughing] But you'll do anything or everything by hook or by crook to somehow or other diminish the prestige of that person's good name and fame. Aha… And depending on the degree of your envy that will determine the degree of how malicious you will be. Aha.. [laughter] [coughing] He's not really so good after all, there's really many people so much better and I'm one of them. I may not say it directly, but indirectly eventually, you will see.” Aha… But if someone is just outright envious like Duryodhana, they just… he just wanted to utterly smash the most precious… just the most precious quality of Draupadi to pieces and make her into an untraced prostitute in front of all. [coughing]

Every action has equal and opposite reaction

You know, Duryodhana is living within the hearts of all of us. To some degree, we all have this quality. We must be very careful, not to respond to this voice of envy within us. Because we see the result of what happened to Duryodhana because he offended great souls. He died a miserable, suffering death and became infamous for the rest of history as an envious rascal. Aha… [coughing] That is the result, you lose everything when you offend great souls due to envy. It may not happen instantly. After all, Duryodhana offended the Pandavas and for the next fourteen years, he was the king of earth. So, one may think ‘Well! What's so bad about offending great souls? He was the king of earth during that fourteen years.’ Many people were probably talking like this. But it says that the ‘Mills of time, they grind slow but exceedingly fine.’ Whatever you deserve, you will get. Maybe not today but in due course of time. Aha…

Vedic culture honors woman's chastity

So, Duryodhana wanted to strip Draupadi naked in the assembly of great men. Now, for a woman to be undressed before anyone other than her husband is considered to be unchaste. Now, we're speaking about five thousand years ago in Bharata varsha. [coughing] Today this principle is not very well, not very popular. Draupadi in a helpless condition

So, understand the intensity of the condition that Draupadi was being… was in. She had.. Dushyasana who was a trained military warrior, who was powerful like no man in this age can ever accomplish.
