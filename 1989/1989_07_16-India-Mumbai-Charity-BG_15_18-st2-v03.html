Lecture Transcription

Om namo bhagavate vasudevaya UPPERCASE WILL HIGHLIGHT I

vidyā-vinaya-sampanne
brāhmaṇe gavi hastini
śuni caiva śva-pāke ca
paṇḍitāḥ sama-darśinaḥ

[The humble sages, by virtue of true knowledge, see with equal vision a learned and gentle brāhmaṇa, a cow, an elephant, a dog and a dog-eater [outcaste].]

[BG 5.18]

Purport

A Kṛṣṇa conscious person does not make any distinction between species or castes. The brāhmaṇa and the outcaste may be different from the social point of view, or a dog, a cow and an elephant may be different from the point of view of species, but these differences of body are meaningless from the viewpoint of a learned transcendentalist. This is due to their relationship to the supreme, for the supreme Lord, by his plenary portion as Paramātmā, is present in everyone’s heart. Such an understanding of the supreme is real knowledge. As far as the bodies are concerned in different castes or different species of life, the Lord is equally kind to everyone because he treats every living being as a friend yet maintains himself as Paramātmā regardless of the circumstances of the living entities. The Lord as Paramātmā is present both in the outcaste and in the brāhmaṇa, although the body of a brāhmaṇa and that of an outcaste are not the same. The bodies are material productions of different modes of material nature, but the soul and the supersoul within the body are of the same spiritual quality. The similarity in the quality of the soul and the supersoul, however, does not make them equal in quantity, for the individual soul is present only in that particular body whereas the paramātmā is present in each and every body. A Kṛṣṇa conscious person has full knowledge of this, and therefore he is truly learned and has equal vision. The similar characteristics of the soul and supersoul are that they are both conscious, eternal and blissful. But the difference is that the individual soul is conscious within the limited jurisdiction of the body whereas the supersoul is conscious of all bodies. The supersoul is present in all bodies without distinction.

This afternoon we are reading from the fifth chapter of the Bhagavad Gita entitled 'action in Krishna consciousness.’

oṁ ajṣāna-timirāndhasya
jṣānāṣjana-śalākayā
cakṣur unmīlitaṁ yena
tasmai śrī-gurave namaḥ[Sri Guru Pranama]

vidyā-vinaya-sampanne
brāhmaṇe gavi hastini
śuni caiva śva-pāke ca
paṇḍitāḥ sama-darśinaḥ

[BG 5.18]

[The humble sages, by virtue of true knowledge, see with equal vision a learned and gentle brāhmaṇa, a cow, an elephant, a dog and a dog-eater [outcaste].]

Humility, see in the light of knowledge

In this particular sloka Lord Krishna is describing the vision of one who has truly acquired the quality of humility and one who is seen in the light of knowledge.

In the darkness of ignorance we see everything according to how we can enjoy or suffer due to the existence of that. Therefore those persons who can help us in our pursuit of happiness in this world we consider them our friend and those persons who are for some reason or other against our better interest of sense gratification we consider them our enemy. In this way, the conditioned soul is constantly distinguishing and making a distinction between one living being and another.

Demoniac vision

When Prahlada Maharaja was at the gurukul of his father Sanda and Amarka they were teaching him just this: How to get the most from your friends to exploit them for more and more sense gratification and how to detect who is your enemy and always be suspicious and cautious and prepared to manipulate conquer and destroy them if necessary. So when Prahlada Maharaja was asked, “What have you learned in school? All they are teaching me is how to make distinctions between one living entity and another. But as far as I am concerned this is all a great illusion.”

Vision of a self-realized soul

A self-realized soul such as Prahlada Maharaja sees the soul which is part of God within the heart of every living being. Because he is not concerned with his own personal material interests, he is able to see to the spiritual welfare of himself in all others.

Therefore as Krishna is describing here for such a learned soul who has acquired true knowledge for him there is no friend and there is no enemies. He simply sees the beloved Lord of his life within everyone and everywhere. Krishna says,

[For one who sees Me everywhere and sees everything in Me, I am never lost, nor is he ever lost to Me.]

[BG 6.30]

Application, a devotee is never anxious for his own sense gratification, but to please Krishna

Of course it is very easy to theoretically discuss these matters but to actually put it into practice in our life that takes a great deal of sincerity. Talk is cheap. Anyone can talk like I am a very great soul, I am very benevolent. But when one is tested and there is risk, risk of life, inconvenience, suffering then we could understand what is the real quality of our consciousness and when we have understood the truth.

In this regard, in the Srimad-Bhagavatam ninth canto there is a beautiful example of this virtue. That is in this story of the great king Rantideva. He was so selfless that he would do nothing for himself but his every breath his every action was for the welfare of others. In fact his family because of their great love for him and their great respect for his devotion without ever complaining or criticizing they followed his path in all respects.

So it is described that for the benefit of his kingdom and for the purification of his own heart Rantideva underwent a fast for forty eight days where he did not take any food or any water. After forty eight days, when he and his family who also shared this fast along with him, they were on the verge of death due to hunger they were given nice fresh water and very opulent and succulent foods made with milk and ghee. So they offered this food to their beloved Lord of their life Sri Krishna.

A devotee is never anxious for his own sense gratification. A devotee is always anxious to please Krishna. A devotee understands that lord Sri Krishna is the root of all existence and if the root is satisfied automatically everything coming from that root will also be nourished.

ahaṁ sarvasya prabhavo

mattaḥ sarvaṁ pravartate

iti matvā bhajante māṁ

budhā bhāva-samanvitāḥ

[BG 10.8]

[I am the source of all spiritual and material worlds. Everything emanates from Me. The wise who perfectly know this engage in My devotional service and worship Me with all their hearts.]

So Rantideva offered his food to Lord Hari and then sat down with his family and was about to partake to break this great forty eight day fast. Just at the moment when they were about to begin a learned brahman came to their door. I have come to beg alms from you. Rantideva was so happy to see the brahmana. He was thinking how wonderful, God is so kind that he has sent this man just to give me an opportunity to render service. Just as an opportunity to make a further sacrifice in my life. So with great love in his heart, Rantideva divided a portion of the food and sumptuously fed that brahmana. After the brahmin had completed and was fully satisfied then he and his family sat down to partake of the prasad. Just at that moment a sudra a lowborn laborer by caste distinction, came to his door and said: "My dear sir, I am very hungry. Please do you have some food?" So Rantideva took another portion of the food, sat down the sudra and fed him very sumptuously to his heart's delight. When the sudra left, Rantideva again sat down with his family whatever little was left, they decided we will break our fast. At that time another stranger came to the door with several dogs and he said: "My dear king, my dogs are very hungry and so am I, can you spare us some food?" So Rantideva, with great happiness, thinking another opportunity to render service. Whatever was left of the food, he fed to the hungry dogs and their master. All that was left was one little portion of water which was barely enough for one person to drink. And Rantideva sat down and was about to drink that water and at that time an untouchable chandala who's considered of the most forbidden caste in the Hindu culture this chandala or dog eater came to the door of Rantideva and said: "I am very thirsty, please grant me some water." And when Rantideva heard this he became very pleased at his heart and he began to offer prayers to the supreme Lord sri Krishna. He said my dear Lord Krishna almighty Vishnu, lord of the world, master of the devotees, supreme reservoir of all beauty and opulence. I do not desire in this life the eight mystic perfections nor do I desire in this life liberation from the sufferings of material existence nor do I desire any form of elevation in the field of rigid religiosity. I only have one desire. Please fulfil this one desire. And that is, let me remain with the most suffering people within this humanity and let me suffer for them so that they could be happy. Grant me this one benediction. And then with this prayer in heart, with a heart full of love he took that last portion of water and he offered it through the chandala to the outcasts.

At that point Rantideva, his limbs very weak, his organs in a condition of starvation. He was about to drop dead and just at the moment before his death, the brahmana, the sudra, the stranger and the chandala they all appeared and assumed their original forms of Brahma, Shiva and the various great devatas. And they were so pleased with Rantideva. They explained that we have tested you, just to see how genuine your compassion to others really is.

After all, anyone can be very compassionate, very charitable and very benevolent when you're not really sacrificing yourself. In the Christian world, they say that real charity is when you give till it hurts. If it doesn't hurt that means you haven't given enough.

So they were so pleased with Rantideva, that they offered him any benediction he desired of this material world and when he offered his benediction, Rantideva simply fixed his mind on the lotus feet of the supreme lord Sri Krishna. Because Rantideva was fully aware that the real treasure, the real wealth, is the remembrance of the lotus feet of the lord. The wealth of this world and the benedictions provided by the demigods as Krishna describes in Gita

kāmais tais tair hṛta-jṣānāḥ

prapadyante ’nya-devatāḥ

taṁ taṁ niyamam āsthāya

prakṛtyā niyatāḥ svayā

[BG 7.20]

[Those whose intelligence has been stolen by material desires surrender unto demigods and follow the particular rules and regulations of worship according to their own natures.]

They are temporary. They could only nourish the senses and the mind. But the great treasure, the great wealth of the divine remembrance of Lord Hari is the fulfilment of all the desires of the soul. Therefore Rantideva was not interested in any benediction other than the remembrance of Krishna. And in that divine remembrance my Lord let me suffer for the sake of others. In this way Rantideva due to his pure devotion to the Lord is celebrated eternally for his transcendental quality of compassion.

Now we notice that when the brahman came to Rantideva, he did not say you are a brahman, you can get food anywhere let me wait for the poor man to come. At the same time when the poor man the chandala, the outcast came, he didn't say what benefit am I going to give by giving to an outcast, why don't I give to the brahman. He made no distinction for that matter. He was as anxious to give to the dogs as he was to the brahman. Because he was seeing the sacred duty of service to the Lord in all circumstances of life. Sometimes there is this idea of Daridra Narayana. That the poor man is Narayana because Narayana is within the poor man's heart. But the fact is Narayana is also in the rich man's heart. He is also in the heart of the dog and the hog. So therefore, a saintly person doesn't simply think in terms of helping the downtrodden and poor. Because factually, from the spiritual point of view most everyone in society is downtrodden and poor.

In our community in West Virginia new Vrindavan there is a beautiful monument that was constructed as a memorial or samadhi for our guru maharaj his divine grace AC Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada.

So sometimes we are challenged why you put so much energy into erecting this building when that same money could be feeding the poor and clothing the people in these streets who have nothing. So the answer is very simple. So actually, the devotees are very anxious to feed the poor with nice Krishna prasad and are very anxious to clothe the poor in the street. But what about the rich and the middle class, why should we neglect them? Spiritually, they are usually more poverty-stricken than the poor. The greatest threat to real spiritual life is false pride. Usually, the poor people have very little to be proud of. This is described by Sukadeva Goswami in the Srimad-Bhagavatam. He says, when one is very poor, one will allow anyone into their house because they have nothing to lose. So when the great saints and sages come begging for food or begging for whatever they let them in. They'll hear from them. They have nothing to lose. They have nothing to be proud of. But often times, wealthy people they are suspicious of everyone. They are always in fear. Why have you come? What do you want? What is he trying to get from me? What is this real motive? I don't want him in here. Often times, they have in our country, they have walls around their houses and gates and barking dogs just to save their fortune. So in this way often times the wealthy class of men are more spiritually poverty-stricken than those living on the streets. So what about them?

vidyā-vinaya-sampanne

brāhmaṇe gavi hastini

śuni caiva śva-pāke ca

paṇḍitāḥ sama-darśinaḥ

[BG 5.18]

[The humble sages, by virtue of true knowledge, see with equal vision a learned and gentle brāhmaṇa, a cow, an elephant, a dog and a dog-eater [outcaste].]

A real devotee of the lord sees everyone with equal vision. He wants to uplift and help everyone. Whatever he can do for the poor or the rich he will do it.

So this beautiful monument for our guru maharaj, Srila Prabhupada's palace of gold, it did not take much to build. It was done by the hands, the devoted hands of the devotees themselves and in the meanwhile it is attracting rich, poor, middle class, all classes of people to come to see and to hear the message of the real goal of life.

Greatest wealth

In Bhagavad-Gita, Lord Sri Krishna describes that the greatest wealth is transcendental knowledge. He says to Arjuna, that there is nothing so pure and nothing so sublime as transcendental knowledge. Because transcendental knowledge opens the doors by which we can become free from all distinctions based in the bodily concept of life. Which means we become free from all the sufferings of the actions and reactions of life.

So Rantideva he was equal to all. He was a well-wisher and a friend to all. Even at the cost of his own life because he understood that to please god is the real purpose of my existence.

ataḥ pumbhir dvija-śreṣṭhā

varṇāśrama-vibhāgaśaḥ

svanuṣṭhitasya dharmasya

saṁsiddhir hari-toṣaṇam

[SB 1.2.13]

[O best among the twice-born, it is therefore concluded that the highest perfection one can achieve by discharging the duties prescribed for one’s own occupation according to caste divisions and orders of life is to please the Personality of Godhead.]

One goal in life

In Bhagavatam it is described that whatever one's occupation, whatever one's duty, whatever one's responsibilities in this world, there's only one goal and that is to please Krishna. If Krishna is pleased our life is a success. If Krishna is displeased our life will soon be revealed to be a miserable failure, a waste. It is described that by pleasing the Lord and by pleasing his devotees, real light is shed upon all living beings.

Compassion

There are many examples throughout history of persons who were willing to endure pain and suffering for others. Para-dukha-dukhī. This is the natural quality of a self-realized soul. That his only pain is to see the pain of others. That his only inconvenience is the inconvenience of others. That he sees the lord in the heart of every living being therefore he becomes the servant of every living being. This is the definition of love. Real love is not on the basis that you are mine to enjoy. Love in this world is based on bodily attachment. That because you are of me or I am of you and because you are giving me happiness and I am giving you happiness therefore I love you. But real love is causeless. Real love is the love of god. The love of god makes no distinction.

samo ’haṁ sarva-bhūteṣu

na me dveṣyo ’sti na priyaḥ

[BG 9.29]

[I envy no one, nor am I partial to anyone. I am equal to all. But whoever renders service unto Me in devotion is a friend, is in Me, and I am also a friend to him.]

Krishna is equal to all

Krishna says, “I favor no one. I'm equal to all.” Krishna loves every living entity, it is his nature. ahaṁ bīja-pradaḥ pitā. He is the father of all living entities. Whether that father is punishing the child or whether the father is rewarding the child, the father equally loves the child. His punishment and his reward are simply expressions of the same divine love. So sometimes Krishna smashes the sinners and casts him into abominable conditions of life and sometimes he rewards the pious with great prosperity and sometimes he elevates his pure devotees to the spiritual world Vaikuntha. According to how you were approaching Krishna he is giving you just what you need. Just what you require out of love but the love is equal to all. So when we become Krishna conscious, when we always remember Krishna, when we realize the divine presence of Krishna within our hearts and we surrender to him there then Krishna allows the empty vessel of our pure heart to be the instrument of his love. He allows us to be the medium by which he can show his love to the world. That is the real meaning of surrender.

Instrument in Krishna’s hand

We approach our spiritual master as his divine grace but whose divine grace are we speaking of? We are speaking of Krsna's divine grace. Krishna's divine grace is manifesting to this world through the hearts of the sadhus and as soon as you think it is my mercy, it is my compassion, immediately the circuit is cut. No longer is that connection there. Just like the television it is receiving the television waves from the television station and it is simply manifesting in each and every house but as soon as the circuit is cut the television is blank. So similarly the pure soul because of his connection to Krishna within his heart through devotion and surrender the circuit of the divine mercy of god is manifesting himself to the world through such a sadhu. But as soon as we think it is mine, I am great, I am merciful, I am charitable, I am benevolent, I am not so bad after all then immediately the circuit is cut and then whatever little you have is all you can give. Whatever little you think you have

(00:30:52-00:33:58)

Krishna is the proprietor of everything. He is the proprietor of whatever qualities you have, whatever wealth you have, whatever knowledge you have. One who is willing to accept this principle, one who is willing to be an instrument on behalf of Krishna, one who is willing to accept suffering for the sake of others, one who is willing to always remember Krishna, such a person is blessed with the greatest fortune. Therefore lord Sri Krishna concludes the Bhagavad-Gita with a simple instruction

man-manā bhava mad-bhakto

mad-yājī māṁ namaskuru

mām evaiṣyasi satyaṁ te

pratijāne priyo ’si me

[BG 18.65]

[Always think of Me, become My devotee, worship Me and offer your homage unto Me. Thus you will come to Me without fail. I promise you this because you are My very dear friend.]

Every living being is the dear friend of God. And how to always think of Krishna? The supreme Lord himself has descended into this world to show us the simple process of how we can always remember him, how we can think of him constantly. And that is through glorifying his holy name. Nama sankirtana om visnur.

Holy Name

The Vishnu Purana describes that there is nothing more auspicious than the congregational chanting of the name of God. It describes, even the most utterly inauspicious condition of life becomes completely auspicious as soon as the name of God is chanted.

(00:33:10-00:33:19)

Caitanya-caritāmṛta describes that the most difficult obstacle imaginable, is easily overcome if one remembers in the core of his heart the name of God. And even the simplest thing becomes impossible for one who forgets the supreme Lord of one's life. Therefore the real standard of our elevation or degradation has nothing to do with the material conditions of our life. It is determined by the internal quality of our consciousness. How much and how we are remembering Krishna. When we cry out for Krishna with sincere devotion and helpless dependence, that moment you are the wealthiest person in all of creation. And when you forget Krishna and think how nice I have everything under control, you are spiritually in poverty. Therefore Rantideva demonstrated by his life not simply by words. Anybody could give a nice lecture, describing these virtues but then when the test comes, we run away. We hide somewhere. Rantideva was not like this. He practiced what he preached. This was based on faith. Faith in the mercy of God and faith in the holy name. So you know as described in the Srimad Bhagavatam

yasyāsti bhaktir bhagavaty akiṣcanā

sarvair guṇais tatra samāsate surāḥ

harāv abhaktasya kuto mahad-guṇā

manorathenāsati dhāvato bahiḥ

[SB 5.18.12]

[All the demigods and their exalted qualities, such as religion, knowledge and renunciation, become manifest in the body of one who has developed unalloyed devotion for the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Vāsudeva. On the other hand, a person devoid of devotional service and engaged in material activities has no good qualities. Even if he is adept at the practice of mystic yoga or the honest endeavour of maintaining his family and relatives, he must be driven by his own mental speculations and must engage in the service of the Lord’s external energy. How can there be any good qualities in such a man?]

This means that all the qualities of God, the sadhus and the demigods are all manifested to one who surrenders to the Supreme Personality of Godhead. This quality of Rantideva, the great king, this glorious quality of Prahlada Maharaja, that very quality is your very essence because you are as much part of god as they are. By chanting god's name with a sincere vow of determination and with a sincere desire to please the lord, these natural qualities awaken from within us. So therefore there is no more sublime benediction in all of creation than the opportunity to chant Hare Kṛṣṇa, Hare Kṛṣṇa, Kṛṣṇa Kṛṣṇa, Hare Hare/ Hare Rāma, Hare Rāma, Rāma Rāma, Hare Hare. For, by vibrating the sacred syllables of the holy name of Krishna, the great treasurer of divine love is opened and revealed from within the core of our heart and then we could learn how to enjoy the real riches of the mercy of god. And we can share those riches with everyone and everyone and bring real happiness to the world. Thank you very much. Hare Krishna

Question: "Do we need to discriminate in giving charity?" [devotee]

You have to know how to give charity. Bhagavad-Gita describes there is charity in the mode of passion, charity in the mode of ignorance and charity in the mode of goodness and there's also transcendental charity, divine charity. So therefore, through study of shastra and through inquiring from the great sadhus you must understand how to give charity and to whom.

Charity in the mode of goodness is with consideration of the proper place, the proper person, or the proper method. If you give prasad to someone who you know will eat it, that is divine and spiritual. You are giving him not only food to nourish his body but food to nourish his soul. If you give a person something where you know that that person is going to take that and use it for illicit destructive activities, that is charity in the mode of ignorance. You are not elevated by such charity, that is not the service of God. We cannot be sentimental. Everything we do, has to be based on knowledge, not sentiment. Otherwise our so-called service can actually be doing damage. Just like if your child is sick and the child is not supposed to eat a particular type of food but she wants it. Give me this food. So you say oh it'll make her happy let me give it to her and then she gets ten times more sick. Is that charity? Is that compassion? No. Charity means you give the child what the child actually needs, not necessarily what she wants. Love means with a genuine concern for the person's welfare. We don't discriminate who we give mercy to but we must discriminate whether it is mercy. Whether it is the will of God. In this matter we must be very discriminant. Just like a doctor may have a free medical clinic doesn't discriminate who he treats. He'll treat anybody. Rich or poor, educated or uneducated, Hindu, Christian, Muslim or Jew. He'll treat anybody but he'll discriminate how he treats each person. It's not that he'll give everyone the same injection and say I'm equal to all. That is called ignorance. You give every person exactly what they need to uplift them and make them healthy. That is like the doctor who's not willing to diagnose the patient just give everyone the same thing. Is that compassion? Whatever we do should be based on knowledge.

Real charity, Krishna makes a distinction, charity in the mode of ignorance does not elevate you, it degrades you. Charity in the mode of passion, further binds you. Charity in the mode of goodness uplifts your soul, your consciousness and when you give God when you give Krishna, that charity liberates your soul and liberates the soul of those persons you are sharing your life with. So therefore if we really are truly concerned with helping persons we must have knowledge and we must discriminate. It is not a very compassionate concept to simply give without understanding what we're giving or how it will benefit another. Bhagavad-Gita describes, should I read? This is from the s chapter of Bhagavad-Gita.

[Charity given out of duty, without expectation of return, at the proper time and place, and to a worthy person is considered to be in the mode of goodness.]

[BG 17.20]

Purport: In the Vedic literature charity given to a person engaged in spiritual activities is recommended as the highest charity of course. But charity performed with the expectation of some return or with the desire for fruitive results or in a grudging mood is said to be charity in the mode of passion and charity performed in an improper place and time and given to unworthy persons without respect and with contempt is charity in the mode of ignorance. I thank you very much
