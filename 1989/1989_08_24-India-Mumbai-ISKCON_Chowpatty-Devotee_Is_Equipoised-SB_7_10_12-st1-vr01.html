Lecture Transcription

kathā madīyā juṣamāṇaḥ priyās tvam
āveśya mām ātmani santam ekam
sarveṣu bhūteṣv adhiyajṣam īśaṁ
yajasva yogena ca karma hinvan

[ŚB 7.10.12]

[“It does not matter that you are in the material world. You should always, continuously, hear the instructions and messages given by Me and always be absorbed in thought of Me, for I am the Supersoul existing in the core of everyone’s heart. Therefore, give up fruitive activities and worship Me.”]

Purport

When a devotee becomes materially very opulent, one should not think that he is enjoying the results of his fruitive activities. A devotee in this material world uses all material opulences for the service of the Lord because he is planning how to serve the Lord with these opulences, as advised by the Lord Himself. Whatever material opulence is within his possession he engages to expand the glories and service of the Lord. A devotee never performs any fruitive or ritualistic ceremony to enjoy the results of such karma. Rather, a devotee knows that karma-kāṇḍa is meant for the less intelligent man. Narottama dāsa Ṭhākura says in his Prema-bhakti-candrikā, karma-kāṇḍa, jṣāna-kāṇḍa, kevala viṣera bhāṇḍa: both karma-kāṇḍa and jṣāna-kāṇḍa — fruitive activities and speculation about the Supreme Lord — are like pots of poison. One who is attracted to karma-kāṇḍa and jṣāna-kāṇḍa spoils his existence as a human being. Therefore a devotee is never interested in karma-kāṇḍa or jṣāna-kāṇḍa, but is simply interested in favorable service to the Lord (ānukūlyena kṛṣṇānuśīlanam), or cultivation of spiritual activities in devotional service.

Lord Narshinghdev continues in his words to Prahlada Maharaja, it does not matter that you are in the material world, you should always continuously hear the instructions and messages given by me and always be absorbed and thought of me, for I am the supreme super-soul existing in the core of everyone's heart therefore give up fruitive activities and worship Me.

Purport by Srila Prabhupada

When a devotee becomes materially very opulent one should not think that he is enjoying the results of his fruitative activities. A devotee in this material world uses all material opulences for the service of the Lord because he is planning how to serve the Lord with these opulences as advised by the Lord himself whatever material opulence is within his possession, he engages to expand the glories and service of the Lord. A devotee never performs any fruitative or ritualistic ceremony to enjoy the results of such Karma rather a devotee knows that karma kanda is meant for the less intelligent man. Narottam Das Thakura says in his Prema-bhakti-candrikā, karma-kāṇḍa, jṣāna-kāṇḍa, kevala viṣera bhāṇḍa: both karma-kāṇḍa and jṣāna-kāṇḍa  Fruitative activities and speculation about the Lord are like pots of poison, one who is attracted to karma kanda and gyana kanda spoils his existence as a human being therefore a devotee is never interested in karma kanda or gyan kanda but is simply interested in favourable service to the Lord, (ānukūlyena kṛṣṇānuśīlanam), the cultivation of spiritual activities in devotional service.

Translation- it does not matter that you are in the material world, you should always continuously hear the instructions and messages given by me and always be absorbed in thought of me for I am the super-soul existing in the core of everyone's heart therefore give up fruitative activities and worship me.

Fortune teller story by Srila Prabhupada

There is that story that Srila Prabhupada tells, about the fortune teller who came to one kingdom and was giving benedictions to everyone in the kingdom according to their respective position. First, he was approached by the prince and the benediction he gave the prince was better you live. Then he was approached by a brahmacari and he told the brahmachari the benediction better you die. Then he was approached by a butcher the slaughterer of innocent animals and he gave him the benediction better you neither live nor die and then he was approached by a pure hearted devotee of Lord Krishna and he gave the benediction either you live or die. So the people in general wanted an explanation why he had given these particular benedictions to the respective persons and he began to explain that this prince, he is enjoying a very pleasurable life at the moment. He has all material opulences prestige ,fame, all sorts of sense gratification, no inconveniences but because in his opulent condition he is engaging in sinful activities after he exhausts all his previous piety he's going to have to suffer in his next birth all of the sins of his committed. Therefore, better he live, for a death he will suffer the torments of hell. As far as the brahmacari he is living quite the opposite life [sniffs]he has no material opulence he is no material pleasures he is forsaking everything pleasing in this world [sniffs] he is performing hardships austerities sleeping on the hard floor, eating very simple Prasad he is the obedient servant of all others, simply carrying out the order of his superiors. In this way he is living a very hard life full of pain and suffering but the fruits of this great tapasya is when he leaves this body he will go back home back to Godhead. So for the brahma…. Brahamachari the benediction I have given is better you die and go back to Godhead finish up all these austerities and hardships. In regard to the butcher the Prophet described that this butcher every day during his life he is surrounded by bloodshed and the screaming of horror from the mouths of murdered animals. Therefore his existence is full of the stench of death and full of the sound of pain therefore he is living a hellish condition and when he has to give up his body he is going to have to suffer exactly whatever pain he has caused for all these animals therefore he is in an abominable painful condition now and it will only get worse and continue after death so for such a person better he neither live nor die, and then the people asked but what about this benediction you have given to this great devotee of Lord Shri Krishna and he replied ,” That this devotee his body his mind his words are constantly engaged in the loving service of Lord Shri Krishna at every moment and he is finally the greatest happiness the greatest pleasure in remembering the Lord and offering his devotion to the Lord. At the time of death it is the Lord's unconditional guarantee that his devotee will go back home back to Godhead and what will he do there? he will do the same thing he's doing here he will be dedicating his body mind words everything in the loving service of the Lord and always remember him . So therefore whether he lives or dies it doesn't matter because he is always experiencing the divine happiness of remembering Krishna.”

Krishna is there, where devotees chant His glory

So, Lord Narshingha Dev is here giving the same benediction to Prahalad Maharaja. He says,” My dear Lord it does not matter that you are in the material world because you are constantly hearing my glories and you are constantly engaged in chanting the holy names. Wherever the presence of the Lord is that is Vaikuntha. The Lord has spoken to Narada muni tatra tiṣṭhāmi nārada yatra gāyanti mad-bhaktāḥ [Padma Purāṇa] that wherever my devotees are chanting my glories you should know that I am in that place and wherever Krishna is, he also manifests his supreme abode of Goloka Vrindavan. So therefore, the spiritual world is factually wherever the devotee is praising the glories of the Lord and this was confirmed by Srila Advaita Acharya, when Shri Chaitanya Mahaprabhu was on his way to Vrindavan and Lord Nityananda by a very clever device rerouted Lord Gaur Hari to Shantipur. Advaitya Acharya explained to Shri Chaitanya Mahaprabhu that really you are not in Vrindavan bathing in Yamuna but you are in Shantipur bathing in Ganga and Sri Chaitanya Maharprabhu said,” oh Lord Nityananda he has tricked me. “ Chaitanya Mahaprabhu became quite angry but Advaitya archarya being very grateful to Lord Nityananda for having tricked the Lord wanted to protect him so he said ,”no no no he did not trick you what he was saying was perfectly honest because he told you that this is the Yamuna but factually at the place called Prayag where the kumbha mela takes place every 12 years these Sri Ganga mai and Jamuna mai they come together into one river and from that point it is understood that on the eastern bank that is the Ganga and on the western bank it is Yamuna and because you are now on the western bank you are actually bathing in the Yamuna and as far as your being in Vrindavan wherever the lotus feet of those pure devotees are who are constantly glorifying the Lord that place is Vrindavan
“ we could analyse this logic.

There is a verse that describes that Krishna in his divine form of Govinda never takes one step out of Vrindavan .When Govinda ji, Shyamsundar leaves Vrindavan it appears that he leaves but factually Shyamsundar remains in Vrindavan and the Yamuna which is the border of Vrindaban whenever he goes that comes he goes in Yamuna and at that time the Govinda ji feature of Goloka remains in Vrindavan and he takes on his Vasudev feature to go to Mathura and Dwarka but Govinda never leaves Vrindavan. It is also described by Vishwanath Chakravarthi Thakura, about the birth of Lord Shri Krishna it is described that in the tenth canto of Bhagwatam that Vasudeva Shri Krishna appeared in the prison cell of Kamsa as the child of Devaki and Vasudev but Vishwanath chakravarti thakura describes in the Puranas there is information given that mother Yashoda had twin children. So factually Krishna is Devkinandan but he's also directly the son of Yashodanandan. He is known by the people in general as the foster child of Yasoda, but he is both he is simultaneously the foster child and he is also the real child. Yasoda nandan braja jana ranjana and he is the giver of pleasure to all the inhabitants of Vrindavan. So, Lord Shyamsundar he never ever ever leaves Vrindavan and at the same time he tells us kali-kāle nāma-rūpe kṛṣṇa-avatāra [ Cc. Adi 17.22] that he is nondifferent than his name.

tatra tiṣṭhāmi nārada yatra gāyanti mad-bhaktāḥ [Padma Purāṇa] wherever his name is being chanted he is there so we are coming to a logical conclusion if he is wherever his name is and Shyamsundar never leaves Vrindavan that means wherever his name is being chanted that place is Vrindavan dham ki jai [Laughs]…and this is the essence of what is being spoken by Srila Advaita Acharya yet my dear Lord because you are always praising the glories of the Lord therefore wherever whatever tract of land your lotus feet are touching that place is Vrindavan dham and that is why the great souls are said to have lotus feet. vande guroḥ śrī-caraṇāravindam [Sri Gurvastakam] charan- feet, Aravind that means his feet are like the Lotus because a Lotus is protected by the leaf of the Lotus and the leaf of the Lotus has a natural repellent against water or any type of dirt, anything that touches the leaf of the Lotus immediately rolls off of it , like a ball if you put water on the leaf of the Lotus it becomes like a ball and instantly rolls off and any dirt or any debris anything it is such of a slippery substance that it just repels it instantly. This material world is considered a dirty place because the three modes of material nature are in all directions influencing people to materialistic activities and therefore for a devotee of the Lord as we have explained many times the difference between piety and impiety is like the difference between wet stool and dry stool. It's all stool, it's all material. A hog like sweat because it's very tasteful doesn't like dry but a human being to him wet or dry, it is abominable. So similarly a materialistic person likes happiness in the mode of goodness, doesn't like distress to him he discriminates between happiness and distress between the three modes of material nature but for a devotee it's all material it's all temporary it's all dirt duḥkhālayam aśāśvatam [Bg. 8.15]

For a devotee Brhama Loka and Patala Loka is same

For a devotee the happiness of Brahma loka and the suffering of Patala loka there's not much difference. To materialist it's a great amount of minutes just examine our own life we're talking about the broad difference in the spectrum between the billions and billions of years of a unimaginable pleasure of Brahmaloka and the excruciating unthinkable suffering of Patala loka .It's quite a difference , huh , look at us we get a little boil in our body and we think ahaha so horrible so painful how will I live and then when the pus comes out with a ha how nice how nice what a wonderful difference. There is so such an insignificant thing at this we're thinking there's such a difference. But a devotee he doesn't even see a difference between Brahma loka and Patala loka what to speak of having a boil or not having a boil, this is the nature of this world we're making such a difference between happiness distress pleasure pain but a devotee makes no distinction because he knows it's all temporary it's all ultimately a miserable condition.

So, in this way the devotees of the Lord they are constantly situated in Vrindavan, the spiritual world for a very simple reason because they no longer take pleasure in the objects of the senses but they take pleasure in glorifying Krishna. As soon as you take pleasure in the objects of the senses you are in the material world and as soon as you take pleasure and glorifying Krishna you are in Vrindavan. So therefore our life should be organized in such a way that we minimize as far as possible how much we are gratifying our senses and we keep our senses constantly immersed in the service of the Lord of the senses and of all the senses the most important is the tongue - by always discussing the glories of the Lord and chanting his name and by always hearing his glories then we can always be situated in Vrindavan dham. And we can create the atmosphere of Vrindaban for others to take shelter of. Shri Shri Radha Gopinath has appeared in this very extraordinary city of Bombay simply to create the divine atmosphere of Vrindavan, how much is his mystic power the city full of dirt, crime, rats, poverty, noise, pollution and just about everything else that he has descended into this city to create the atmosphere of the pure eternal abode of Vrindaban. This temple room actually is Vrindavan it is just the question of our vision if we are always thinking of Krishna then Vrindaban will be revealed to us right here but if we are even if we are sitting in this temple before Radha Gopinath if we are thinking nonsense talking nonsense thinking about our own sense gratification talking about our own sense gratification then we are in the material world. For that matter even if you go to the holy land of Vrindavan, if you are thinking in terms of your sense gratification factually you are not in Vrindavan, you are in the material world even though your body may physically be in Vrindavan dham which is non-different than the spiritual world your consciousness is not.That is why Srila Narottam Das has a song

Pesaya chadi haka be, sudha habe nana, kabeihar mohiba sri Vridavana”(song in Bengali)

He wrote this song while living in Vrindavan and he's praying when will that day be when I relinquish all desire for material sense gratification, when I forget all about enjoying this world only then will I be able to see Vrindavan.

So, Lord Narsingh Dev is here explaining to Prahalad that it does not matter whether you are in this material world you should always continuously hear the instructions and messages given by me and always be absorbed in thought of me, for I am the super-soul existing in the core of everyone's heart therefore give up fruitive activities and worship Me.
