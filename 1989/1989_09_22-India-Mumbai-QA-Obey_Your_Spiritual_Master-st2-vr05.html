Who is Neophyte?

We have to accept the fact that we are neophytes. A devotee who thinks he's advanced is a neophyte. A devotee who thinks he's so advanced that he could transcend that order of the spiritual master, he is a neophyte. And a devotee who humbly realizes that ‘I'm the most fallen. I better be very strict about what guru Maharaja says,’ he is actually on the path, on the royal road back home, back to Godhead.

You see, this is due to false pride. No matter how advanced we are, we are never too advanced to surrender to the order of the senior sadhus. Otherwise, it is false pride and that false pride is the seed which will fructify in a spiritual catastrophe. So, we must be very… we must always consider ourselves to be the most fallen. We should never think ourselves to be advanced. As soon as you think yourself to be advanced, that should be the signal that you're in Maya. Ah? That is a fact, just like in this mouth speaker, there's a little red light that says ‘battery’ and as soon as the battery starts getting low, the red light goes on, so you know ‘Now the battery's going dead.’ Ah? So similarly, we have so many spiritual credits. We've done so much to make advancement in Krishna consciousness, but what our Krishna consciousness starts going down and we start succumbing to pride then the little red light comes on ‘I'm very advanced.’ Ah? As soon as you think you're an advanced devotee take that to be that little red light, this warning that your battery is about to burn out, you better plug yourself back in. Ah?
