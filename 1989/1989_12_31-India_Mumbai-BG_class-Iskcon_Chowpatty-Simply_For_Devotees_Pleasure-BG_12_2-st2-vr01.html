Translation

The Supreme Personality of Godhead said: Those who fix their minds on My personal form and are always engaged in worshiping Me with great and transcendental faith are considered by Me to be most perfect.

Purport

In answer to Arjuna’s question, Kṛṣṇa clearly says that he who concentrates upon His personal form and who worships Him with faith and devotion is to be considered most perfect in yoga. For one in such Kṛṣṇa consciousness there are no material activities, because everything is done for Kṛṣṇa. A pure devotee is constantly engaged. Sometimes he chants, sometimes he hears or reads books about Kṛṣṇa, or sometimes he cooks prasādam or goes to the marketplace to purchase something for Kṛṣṇa, or sometimes he washes the temple or the dishes – whatever he does, he does not let a single moment pass without devoting his activities to Kṛṣṇa. Such action is in full samādhi.

Arjuna enquires from Lord Krishna: Should one worship Lord’s personal form or the impersonal Brahman?

We are reading from the Bhagavad Gita, chapter twelve, which is entitled ‘Devotional service.’ Today we are reading from the second verse of the twelfth chapter. In this verse Bhagavan, Sri Krishna is answering the most relevant question of Arjuna. The first verse, Arjuna inquires. And in this inquiry, he is clearing a great doubt which from time immemorial, has been within the people's minds, who have been on the path of yoga or self-realization.

You see, the Absolute Truth can… can be understood according to different perspectives, as he reveals himself differently, according to how you approach him. Some approach the Absolute Truth, as the impersonal, unmanifest, all-perver… all-pervading energy. And that, at this realization, all sense of personality, all sense of variety, and all sense of form, is transcended, and they're longer no longer exists. Others worship the personal form of God through devotion. And according to their understanding, the impersonal, all-pervading energy of the Lord the Brahman, is simply the… like the sunlight that is emanating from the form of the Almighty Sun, in the form of Krishna. So, since time immemorial, there has been great debates, over which is a higher and more perfect realization of God; his impersonal aspect or his personal aspect; Brahman or Krishna. So Arjuna, he is inquiring from Krishna. And there is no higher authority than Krishna himself. Amongst the munis, or the impersonal philosophers, sometimes known to be following the path of Jnana yoga, the foremost and most influential was Sripad Shankaracharya. Now Shankaracharya, he accepted Bhagavad Gita as a supreme word of God. In fact, he himself, wrote beautiful poems in glorification of the Personality of Godhead, describing that [Narayana parobyatat ?? 4.45 unclear] that Narayana is completely transcendental, and is the supreme. So Shankaracharya, as well as all the great acharyas, Ramanujacharya, Madhvacharya, Vishnu Swami, Nimbarkacharya, Vallabhacharya, Chaitanya Mahaprabhu, they all accepted Bhagavad Gita as the supreme word of God. We can know the Lord only by bhakti

The blessed Lord said he whose mind is fixed on my personal form, always engaged in worshiping me with great and transcendental faith and devotion, he is considered by me to be the most perfect. So, the process of bhakti, Krishna explains in this twelfth chapter, is the only means by which we can know him in his perfect and complete manifestation, bhaktyā tv ananyayā śakya aham evaṁ-vidho ’rjuna [Bg 11.54] In the twelfth chapter Krishna says, only by undivided devotion can I be understood as I am. Through the process of jnana or mental discrimination, intellectual prowess, we can understand how the Absolute Truth is beyond all the relative senses and the perceptions of this world. He is beyond the three modes of material nature, and he is the impersonal all-pervading Brahman, which is existing everywhere.

Through the process of the astanga yoga system, we can understand how the Lord is seated within the heart of every living being, but to… to enter into the supreme realization of God and eternal loving relationship with him in the spiritual world, this is only po… possible through the process of bhakti or unalloyed devotion. Vrindavan residents enjoy discussing Krishna’s lilas

There is a place in Vrindavan. This is an example that I will give. There is a place in Vrindavan, it is near Nandagaon. Try to understand Nanda Maharaj, who your son is, he is not an ordinary man. He's not an ordinary child. He must, we have decided, either he is Vishnu, or at the least he's Brahma or Shiva. After all, so many demons who are totally unconquerable by anyone, when they came into Vrindavan, your son effortlessly killed them. When he was just a baby child, he killed Putana, he killed Trinavarta. He killed some Gadasura, Aghasura, Bakasura, Vatsasura. Krishna is a very great person, you must just let him do anything he wants.’’ Nanda Maharaj looked at Upananda and the other cowherd men and he said, ‘’You may think like this, but I do not see things this way. As far as I am concerned, Krishna is my child. What kind of illusion are all of you in? You are in a grand illusion. You are saying he's some great personality, Vishnu, Mahesh, unnh… Brahma, some demigod or some yogi. When it's raining outside and there's loud thunder or lightning in the middle of the night, this great yogi, you say the fearless God of all, he comes running into our bed completely scared to death, saying ‘Save me, save me.’ And unless we embrace him and put him into our arms and say ‘We will protect you,’ he just cries and cries and cries and trembles in fear.’’ Unnnh? In this way, the residents of Vrindavan are teaching us, that the supreme perfection of our life, is to always take great pleasure in hearing the glories of the supreme Absolute Truth, the Personality of Godhead. In fact, this is the ultimate yoga. Sitting in silent meditation, in the Himalayas, controlling the breath, performing asanas, studying the scriptures scrutinizingly; these are very important processes of purification. But these are all considered very kindergarten fundamental types of yoga processes. The ultimate yoga process, according to the sastra, is to simply take great ecstasy in hearing and chanting the glories of the Lord.

Perfection of life can be attained by constantly hearing and chanting the glories of the Lord

Suka dev Goswami told Maharaj Parikshit,

tasmād bhārata sarvātmā
bhagavān īśvaro hariḥ
śrotavyaḥ kīrtitavyaś ca
smartavyaś cecchatābhayam

[SB 2.01.05]

[O descendant of King Bharata, one who desires to be free from all miseries must hear about, glorify and also remember the Personality of Godhead, who is the Supersoul, the controller and the savior from all miseries.]

That the state of ultimate perfection the goal of life, free of all fear, can only be attained by constantly hearing and chanting the glories of the Lord. This is considered to be the perfection of samadhi. It is only through this process, that loving devotion can awaken within our heart. And according to Bhagavad Gita, twelfth chapter, Krishna is explaining, that there is no higher realization of the truth than pure devotion to his personal form.

So, of course, some people misunderstand ‘How is it that Krishna is behaving in such a way? How is it that he appears just like a frightened little child who cannot be happy unless he has some prasadam in his mouth?’ Ah, actually, that same Krishna when he expands himself, he becomes Narayan, Maha Vishnu, Shankar. But in the spiritual world, his only business, is giving pleasure to those who have surrendered their lives to him.

Krishna’s supreme enjoyment is to please his devotees

You're saying that everything is meant for his enjoyment? Annnh? If everything is meant for his enjoyment, and he's created things, so he's the enjoyer of everything. Isn't that kind of selfish? It may appear that way. But we must understand what is his enjoyment. His ultimate enjoyment is to give pleasure to his devotees to reciprocate love with his devotees. It is explained in Vrindavan, sometimes the Gopis, they would gather together when Krishna was just a little child. And they would clap their hands and they would sing. [Maharaj claps] And Krishna, he would just start dancing around the house. And they would all be laughing. He was dancing like a puppet in the hands of his devotees, simply to give them pleasure. By hearing Krishna’s pastimes we are attracted to loving him and serving him

But we have to understand that Krishna wanted to struggle and to kill the demons was nothing for him. He didn't have to struggle on strain. But to show how much he wanted to please his devotee, he showed he's willing to struggle and strain for that. Aaanh? So he would come to the house and Nanda Maharaj would come to the door, and Krishna would be standing there with the shoes of Nanda Maharaj on his head, and he would say, ‘’Father, please accept your shoes.’’ And Nanda Maharaj, tears would flow from his eyes in love, ‘Aaanh… how much my son loves me.’ So in this way, understand that Krishna’s supreme enjoyment, is giving pleasure to his devotees.

And when Krishna acts in a mischievous way, I just narrated to you one story of Nanda Maharaj, describing all the mischievous lilas of Lord Sri Krishna. There are thousands, millions of such mischievous lilas and Nanda Maharaj was only describing just a little, just a little sample of what goes on in the house every day. Aaanh? And just see how all the residents of Vrindavan, they were finding such pleasure and such joy. They were laughing, they were crying, they forgot everything else in the entire existence. They were completely immersed in loving Krishna by hearing these wonderful past times. So why does Krishna behave like this, simply for the pleasure of his devotees. Simply so that people will be attracted to loving him and serving him. For those who are addicted to material sense enjoyment, the hearing of the past times in the philosophy of the Personality of Godhead, is the most powerful medicine, that completely like an inoculation, it immunes you from all the influences of suffering and the… the enjoyment of this material world. And for those who are liberated, for those who have who are pure in heart, for them, there is no greater ecstasy. There is no higher state of divine samadhi, than hearing the glories of the Lord.

Hearing Srimad Bhagavatam clears inauspiciousness of the heart and awakens our taste for Krishna katha

Srimad Bhagavatam is specifically that scripture which describes in the tenth canto, the wonderful pastimes of the Supreme Lord as he appeared on this earth. And therefore Srimad Bhagavatam is called the Paramhamsa samhita. Which means this scripture is especially designed for the Paramhamsas, for the top most yogis, for the top most transcendentalists, who have pierced through all lower realizations of the Absolute Truth. This scripture is especially meant simply to be enjoyed by the greatest of all spiritual personalities. But the Srimad Bhagavatam is also the most powerful medicine for those who are under the influence of the three modes of material nature and are struggling hard in this material existence, undergoing the repetition of birth and death. Therefore, it is described in Bhagavata Purana

naṣṭa-prāyeṣv abhadreṣu

Aaanh… that anyone who hears bhagavat philosophy every day, regularly, all inauspicious things within his heart are cleansed away.

śṛṇvatāṁ sva-kathāḥ kṛṣṇaḥ

Aaanh… it is described that by hearing Bhagavad regularly and rendering service to the person Bhagavatams, to those who are exemplifying the principles of the Bhagavatam in their life, these two are inseparable, Simply by hearing Srimad Bhagavatam, you will never understand the essence of what it is saying, unless you render service to those great souls who are living by the principles of Bhagavatam. One wants to hear more and more and more, one becomes eager, anxious, enthusiastic.

The more we are eager to hear about Krishna, the more we lose our greedy propensities

Just like Maharaj Parikshit, when he was… when he was hearing Srimad Bhagavatam from unnh… from Suka dev Goswami, seven days, all day, no eating, no sleeping, no speaking anything, only hearing Bhagavat. We may be thinking how’s this possible? Everyone would be outside eating prasad, talking about how it was a nice lecture, but it was too long. [laughter] But Maharaj Parikshit, seven days!! He didn't even think of eating or sleeping. Sometimes when we're giving class, people are whispering amongst one another. It was so quiet in that assembly of sages, that not a single person made a sound. Why? Because they were so anxious, so eager to hear. And Maharaj Parikshit was describing to Suka dev Goswami, that the more you speak, the more I want to hear. Every word only increases my anxiety to hear the next word. Every syllable makes me more enthusiastic to hear the next syllable, and it's ever increasing. And Suka dev Goswami said, ‘’This is the nature of spiritual subject matter.’’ Material subject matter becomes very hackneyed, very old, very dry, after some time. But spiritual subject matter, for one who is in the proper state of consciousness, if he is eager to hear; it constantly awakens the presence of the Lord within one's heart. And Krishna says ‘For one who is eager to hear my message within the heart, I personally myself, cleanse all misgivings, all lust, all anger, all greed, all pride, all illusion. I establish myself, I establish the truth from within, as an irrevocable fact, to one who takes great pleasure in hearing about me.’ The more we learn to take pleasure in hearing the subject matter of Krishna, the more we lose our desire to enjoy the greedy propensities of the senses in the mind. Aaanh? Krishna says in Gita, paraṁ dṛṣṭvā nivartate [Bg 2.59]. That means that, you can give up the lower tendencies of material sense enjoyment, only when we experience something higher, something superior. And that begins by drinking through our ears, the immortal nectar of the divine subject matter of Krishna’s holy lila, and Krishna’s holy names.

And, of course, the more we approach Krishna, as supreme divine person, not a person like you and me. Before we can understand the truth in the glory of Krishna stealing butter, and stealing Gopi’s clothes, these things sometimes we think, aaanh… I can do these things too. First we have to understand in the first nine cantos of Bhagavatam, the greatness and the glory of God. We have to understand how by his impersonal manifestation, he is pervading everything and everything is within him alone. How through his purusha avatars, Karanodaksayi Vishnu, Kshirodaksayi Vishnu, Garbhodaksayi Vishnu, the supreme Absolute truth is creating everything within this cosmic manifestation. When we understand the greatness in the glory, of the supreme power of God, then when we hear that this most magnificent, powerful supreme of all beings; how he is scared of the lightning, running to mother Yashoda to give him protection from fear. Then when you hear this story, how the Absolute Truth the Almighty, Bhagawan is behaving like this, just to give loving sentiments to his devotees, just to give pleasure to his devotees. Then tears flow from your eyes, your heart melts and you lose all your attraction to everything of this world. You simply want to become the humble servant of this most wonderful munificent, and glorious of all personalities. You simply want to become the servant of his servants.

By understanding the greatness of the Lord, bhakti can be awakened This is the power of hearing. This is the power of service to the Vaishnavas, those who are attracted to hearing the past times of the Lord, those who are attracted only to glorifying the past times of the Lord. Such persons, they are like touchstone. Their association is the greatest treasure, the greatest benediction, the greatest wealth in all of creation, to become the servant of such persons, and to hear from them, and to follow their instructions, which is non-different than the instruction of the Lord himself. Then this quality of bhakti will naturally, not something mechanical, but it will naturally awaken from within our consciousness. Chaitanya Mahaprabhu has said:

nitya-siddha kṛṣṇa-prema ‘sādhya’ kabhu naya
śravaṇādi-śuddha-citte karaye udaya

[“Pure love for Kṛṣṇa is eternally established in the hearts of the living entities. It is not something to be gained from another source. When the heart is purified by hearing and chanting, this love naturally awakens.]
