[mangalacarana]

In the sixth chapter of Bhagavad-gita, Lord Sri Krishna instructs us,

bandhur ātmātmanas tasya
yenātmaivātmanā jitaḥ
anātmanas tu śatrutve
vartetātmaiva śatru-vat

[Bg. 6.6]

[For him who has conquered the mind, the mind is the best of friends; but for one who has failed to do so, his mind will remain the greatest enemy.]

Lecture Transcription

The mind is the best friend of the living entity and potentially the mind is the worst enemy. For the mind that is controlled, the mind is the ones best, most well-wishing friend. But for the mind that is uncontrolled, the mind is one’s worst enemy. Infact this very essential philosophical understanding is amplified in the Srimad Bhagavatam where in Lord Rishabha Deva tells us that our mind is in fact our only enemy. There is no other enemy but our own mind. Living in this world, we have so much opposition coming our way. There may be people who are trying to even destroy us, whether it be individually whether it be in business or the nation. So in this way this is the world of so many friends and enemies. We make friends with those persons who either we can exploit very nicely or who agree with us in our principle of how to enjoy in this world. And someone who is opposed to our conception of enjoyment in this world, they become our enemy. So, really, life, in one sense, is a path by which we meet so many friends and so many enemies. This is the way of the world. Prahalad Maharaj, he explains to us in the Srimad Bhagavatam that when he was going to school this is exactly what he was being taught. How to get the most sense enjoyment, how to get the most benefits from those who are your friends and how to defeat your enemy and defend yourself against your enemy. But he explained that I do not care for this education because as far as I am concerned, no one is my friend and no one is my enemy. Because these are all mental conceptions. These are all the results of the uncontrolled mind. Tonight's lecture is entitled "Self-Management." Of course being a sanyasi in the religious order, please forgive me if I do not give you an academic course in the process of managing your business. But on behalf of Lord Sri Krishana an on behalf of all the great saints, sadhus, and Acharyas, I would like to humbly present to you perhaps the most essential understanding of how to manage your life with knowledge of the true needs of the self. Prahalad Maharaj went on and he explained that we are not this material body. This material body is real. It is not an illusion. There is a certain class of philosophers that say “Brahma satyam jagan mithya “that the spirit is truth, brahma. But everything of this temporary world is simply an illusion. It is a dream. It doesn't really exist. But the fact is that this material world is real. This material body is real. But the illusion is that we are identifying ourselves with this body. As soon as the eternal, immortal soul, which is the real self, the real person, the real emanating force in this body....as soon as it comes under the spell of the dream that I am this material body, then our mind becomes our worst enemy. Because immediately our mind begins to judge everything according to how it can enjoy the senses and the body and it completely neglects the real needs of the soul. So therefore, before there can be self-management there must be knowledge. Without knowledge every aspect of our life is a waste. Let us take the example of one great king. His name was Prataparudra. He was the king of Orissa. A very, very qualified man with great riches and great power. Now at the time Lord Caitanya who was a great incarnation of God, He appeared in that very town of Jagannath Puri. Lord Caitanya following the principles of the sanyas order at that time was not found of meeting king because kings are usually very much political by nature. So when Maharaj Prataparudra found that Mahaprabhu will not meet me if I am king then what is the use of this-kingdom. Yes, I have wealth worth billions of dollars. I have a beautiful family. I have wonderful influence over everyone. He wasn't that kind of politician like today. Everyone liked him. Today every country I travel, practically nobody really likes and trusts their political leaders. Ah.. In most countries it’s not a question of who can best serve us.Ah..Its a question of who is going to do the least damage while he is in office. Ah In America, last year there was a selection between Mr. Dicacus and Mr. Bush and there was hardly anyone of the hundreds and millions of people in America that liked either one of them .The question is let’s hear what they have to say and let’s try to figure out who is going to make things worst then the other and we will vote for the other. Ah.. There is hardly any trust for the political leaders because there is so much corruption. People aspire for political office for control, for power, for influence, for fame, and for money. Ah...But a real king, a real political leader, he only aspires to be the servant of every single person in his kingdom. And when he has that mentality, then everyone respects him. Then everyone trusts him. There is the story of that famous king in the Vedic times. His name was Maharaj Sibhi. It’s the duty of the king to protect, maintain, and keep everyone satisfied. Not only the rich people but the poor people. Not only the poor people but even the animals. It is described that one time a pigeon, how many pigeons are in Bombay? Who cares about the pigeons? The king should care about each pigeon and he does, if he is a good king. One time a hawk was chasing after a pigeon to eat him. So the pigeon came to Maharaj Sibhi. He was the-king living in big palace. He could have easily said, "go talk to one of my secretaries. Talk to one of my ministers. I am busy." But this pigeon came before the king very humbly and said, " you are the king. You are responsible for everyone's welfare. This hawk is about to eat me. I am coming to take shelter of you. Please protect me.” So king said, “all right. Have no fear. Ah...” So he said to the hawk,” you cannot touch this pigeon as long I am here. He has come to take shelter of me.” And the hawk said,” what this is? Don’t you know that just as you are meant to protect this pigeon, you are also meant to protect me? And my food is pigeon. That is my natural food. You are neglecting to give me what I require. So how will I eat if you don’t let me kill this pigeon?" Please understand that Maharaj Sibhi had millions of dollars, he had a beautiful family, and he had power over practically all the world. But he was such a humble servant even of the most insignificant creature. Then he said that I will give you anything you want. And the hawk said that you will have to give me, from your own body, the equal amount of weight of this pigeon, of flesh. So Maharaj Sibhi said, “Yes Of course. I will it is my duty. I must satisfy both of you. I am your servant. I am your humble servant." So he cut off a big piece of his own flesh and put it on a scale and the pigeon was on the other side of the scale. But the pigeon outweighed. He put another flesh, but the pigeon was so heavy even though he was only like this big. Finally Maharaja Sibhi sat on the scale himself and it was a completely equal balance.

And he said, "all right hawk, eat me. That is what I am for. The kings purpose is meant only for this purpose, to serve. If it means giving my life to honorably serve, then I will give my life. Eat me." At that time the pigeon and the hawk, they transformed into the demigods. And they said," my dear king, we just wanted to show the world what is a real king. How the king is not exploitative? He is not taking his office for the purpose of any aggrandizement or material purpose for his own benefit. He is simply the humble servant of every creature within his kingdom. So Maharaja Prataparudra was a king like that. He was a real servant of society. Why? Because he was a servant of God. When you become a servant of God, you realise that God is present everywhere and in everything. Mamaivamso jiva loke, jiva bhuta sanatana [Bg. 15.7] That every living being is part of God. To become the servant of God means to become the servant of every living creature. To love God means to love every living creature. So Maharaja Prataparudra said that if I cannot attain the mercy of Lord Caitanya because of my position then I will give it up. There is no value of my riches. There is no value of my kingdom if the very purpose of my human life which is self- realisation is being defeated, yo vā etad akṣaraṁ gārgy aviditvāsmāḻ lokāt praiti sa kṛpaṇaḥ [Bṛhad-āraṇyaka Upaniṣad 3.8.10] It is said in the Garga Upanisad by Srila Garaga Muni to his wife that one who takes this rare human birth and quits his body like the cats and dogs without understanding the science of self-realization is the most unfortunate of all persons. Sometimes it is said that man is a rational animal. Ah.. Now if you take away the rational, man is an animal. Correct? Now, what does it mean to be rational? Rational means to be able to discriminate what is truth. If we do not live for the truth, on the basis of the truth, then our whole life is a waste. It is an illusion. There would be no fruit of happiness. There is no real purpose to our life. Rational animal means man has the power to discriminate what is the truth, what is knowledge, who am I, and what is the purpose of life. The animal is working hard eating, sleeping, mating, and defending. Simply maintaining himself, his family, and his little society, his herd, contributing forward to his little herd. But the truth, Krishna says, when you have base on the truth you will know that all living beings are my parts and parcels, they are in Me and they are Mine. Someone may ask that the kind of life you are talking about, this religious life, is very impractical in today’s society. It is a society of competition and might is right. But let us examine within our hearts what do we really want in life. There is nothing wrong with competition. After all, in the Bhagavad Gita Arjuna was being ordered by Krishna to compete. But He wanted him to compete in pursuance of the truth. Duryodhana was competing on the principle of illusion of ignorance. So whatever your field of activity is in this world that is not bad. But we must act in this world on the basis of truth. There is nothing more glorious than truth. And there is only one problem in this world, ignorance. Every other problem in this world is only due to the presence of ignorance, because human society is in ignorance. Even in the name of religion we are attached to so many traditions, so many rituals, but as far as our real duty to God, the real goal of life, practically everyone is ignorant. They may come to a pravachan, come to a lecture, hear so many nice things, but the goal of life is still based on the principle of ignorance. Therefore, what happiness, what purpose, what goal have we accomplished. Someone who works very hard in this world without knowledge of the truth, according to Bhagavad Gita he is called mudha. namam duskritanah mudha. He is like an ass. Is this the way we want to manage our life? Like an ass? The ass works very hard. They put big, big loads on his back. He foams at the mouth. He works ten hours a day. For what? a little grass, a little sex life, a nice place to stay. Sound familiar. Human society is the society of asses. It is mudha society. And when someone takes to spiritual life people become very offended, 'why you are not a good ass like the rest of us? Why you don’t want to work like an ass simply for a little grass, and a little place to stay, and a little family life? Why? That’s normal. That what everyone is doing.' But the purpose of human life is not to be an ass. The ass is the ass. The human being is meant exclusively for self-realization. And our time is getting very short. Vishnu Purana says that the greatest misfortune in all of the creation is to have the human life and to waste one moment not in pursuance of the truth. When we pursue the truth, when we pursue the truth which is that we are the eternal servant of Krishna or God and whatever we do must be for the pleasure of God. He is the proprietor of everything that exists. He is the proprietor of me. He is the proprietor of my mind, my body, my family, my home, everything. And everything is meant for his satisfaction. That is truth. To think anything is mine is ignorance. So we can work very hard. What is the difference between a mudha and a saint? Not that one works and one doesn’t. It’s that one is working but his goal, his ambition is simply sense enjoyment. Therefore, such a human being is not a rational animal. He is an animal however sophisticated he may dress himself. In human life if you work very hard according to your duty but in pursuance of the truth, in the service of the God with knowledge of who you are, what is the purpose of life, and what is real pleasure. You see, the mind is very difficult to manage because the mind is by nature very envious. Ah.. Because of ignorance, envy takes its seat within our mind. What is the source of that envy? Very simple. God is the proprietor of everything; God is the enjoyer of everything. When I want to be the enjoyer I become envious of Gods position. Ah.. Whether you are pious or impious. Let us understand what Krishna is teaching in Bhagavad Gita. traigunya visaya Veda nistrai gunya bhayarjuna [Bg. 2.45]

Krishna says that this teaching of mine is not to make you pious. It's to establish you on the truth. It’s to give you the platform of real peace which is situated in knowledge of the soul, self-realization. You are part of God. You are meant for his enjoyment. Everything is meant for his enjoyment. Therefore, Krishna is called in Gita, Purusottama. The word, purusa, is generally translated from Sanskrit to mean enjoyer and uttama means the ultimate, the supreme. So Purusottama literally means the Supreme Person, the Supreme enjoyer. Now the living entity wants to become Purusottama. He wants to become enjoyer. But factually he has no right to enjoy the property of God. So as soon as we want to be the enjoyer, we are expressing envy towards Gods position. Now try to understand. When you are envious of God who is the complete whole you are potentially envious of every living being who are his parts and parcels. Because every creature on earth is a reflection of God. Every creature on earth is a sample of God. God is present within everyone's heart. We are qualitatively one with God. Krishna says, "I am the ability in man. I am the intelligence of the intelligent. I am the strength of the strong. All mighty and great things you see is but a spark of my splendor." So whatever beauty, whatever knowledge, whatever wealth, whatever fame, whatever renunciation a person has, understand that that is just a tiny little sample of Gods opulence shining through him. And when we are envious of someone, we are envious of God. We are not envious of that person. We are envious of God manifesting himself through that person. When a woman sees that someone is more beautiful than her, she becomes envious. I want to be like that.' But Krishna says that I am the beauty of the beautiful. If we see that, oh this is a little shining example of the beauty of God. How beautiful. How nice is God. Imagine his complete beauty and there is no envy. But as soon as you want to be the beautiful one and then there is competition. There is envy. Fame in this world people are very much competing over fame. But all fame is the opulence of God. To be specific, all these opulence’s are fortune and Laxmi Devi is the goddess of fortune. All the beautiful and wonderful things of this world that we see in others that we want, it is all the blessing of Laxmi. And laxmi is the energy of Narayan. So a devotee who sees with this vision, he is non envious of anyone. He is equal to all. He doesn't discriminate. He is the friend and the well-wisher of all living beings. Krishna says in Bhagavad Gita that if you simply have one quality you are qualified to go back to the spiritual world. And that one quality is that you are non-envious. Krishna tells Arjuna that because you are not envious, you can understand this message. Who is the total opposite counter part of Arjuna? Duryodhana. Duryodhana was the personification of envy. Ah.. He saw that Yudhistir, Bheema, Arjuna, they were more powerful then him. They were more popular then him. They were more wise then him and not only that. They had the inherited right to have all the wealth of the kingdom of Hasthinapur. So he was envious. He wanted it for himself. He wanted to enjoy. Now Arjuna, he was thinking that let Duryodhana have it all. Why should I fight and cause this disturbance? Krishna told him that you must fight because Duryodhana will exploit everyone and it is the duty of a Kshatriya to actually protect from these type of people the innocent. But you should not fight like an envious rascal, like Duryodhana. You should fight simply for my enjoyment. Know me to be the Purusa and you are simply the servant. Whatever activity we perform, should be done in this spirit without envy toward God. And that is the essence of Bhagavad Gita. People have this very, very gross misunderstanding what Gita means. Many people think that Gita means work is worship. Gita does teach that work is worship but it does not teach that all work is worship. It teaches how to make your work into worship. And it is a very simple formula,

bhoktāraṁ yajṣa-tapasāṁ
sarva-loka-maheśvaram
suhṛdaṁ sarva-bhūtānāṁ
jṣātvā māṁ śāntim ṛcchati

[Bg. 5.29]

[A person in full consciousness of Me, knowing Me to be the ultimate beneficiary of all sacrifices and austerities, the Supreme Lord of all planets and demigods, and the benefactor and well-wisher of all living entities, attains peace from the pangs of material miseries.]

Very simple. Know that I am the proprietor of everything that exists, everything is meant for my enjoyment, and I am the real friend of all living beings. If you work in this spirit, then work is worship and you will achieve real peace. But if you are working on the basis of envy, then your work is not worship. yajṣārthāt karmaṇo ’nyatra, loko ’yaṁ karma-bandhanaḥ [Bg. 3.9] Your work will only be a cause of bondage. It may be bondage in the mode of ignorance or in the mode of passion or even in the mode of goodness. But Krishna says,

ā-brahma-bhuvanāl lokāḥ
punar āvartino ’rjuna
mām upetya tu kaunteya
punar janma na vidyate

[Bg. 8.16]

[From the highest planet in the material world down to the lowest, all are places of misery wherein repeated birth and death take place. But one who attains to My abode, O son of Kuntī, never takes birth again.]

From Brahma loka the highest heavenly planet where the most pious beings take birth after they leave this body to patala loka where the most evil creatures are cast, they are all places of misery. They are all places of birth and death. So when Krishna says that work is worship yajṣārthāt karmaṇo ’nyatra, loko ’yaṁ karma-bandhanaḥ [Bg. 3.9] He means that work which is done without envy. That is worship. And work without envy means work in knowledge. Without knowledge there cannot be non enviousness in your life. Knowledge is that Krishna, Bhagavan, God, is Purusottama. He is the supreme enjoyer and we are meant for his enjoyment and everything is meant for his enjoyment. And when we work in that spirit, that non envious activity is worship. And when we work in that spirit we become the friend of all living being. Then we understand the essence of what Prahalad was speaking to his classmates that I am not concerned with this subject of friends and enemies. To me, I love everyone. Why? Because I can perceive Krishna in everything, everywhere nothing can change that love. Just like a good parent the child may be completely misbehave and be a rascal and the parent may be very disappointed with the child, very disgusted with the child, the parent may be very much hurt by the child but the parent, a good parent will never stop loving the child. The love will never change. I remember my father he was a very good man. He still is a very good man. He told me this. Because you know when you are a teenager, especially in the western world, the inclination is to become quite mischievous and that means that in India it’s not so much like this. But India is becoming like this. The young generation historically is very fond of trying to pursue whatever is the opposite of the older generation. "Whatever you say is right must be wrong because you are old and I am young." Therefore, there is a sense of rebellion, a sense of freedom. I do not have to do what my parents say and therefore to exercise my freedom I will not do anything they say.' In India this is very subdued but I see amongst the teenage people this is very much coming. I have seen from my own experience that in America where people are very materialistic some of the children...they want to become devotees of God because it’s completely against what their parents believe. And sometimes when parents are very religious, the children want to be very sinful because then they don't feel that they are under the control of their parents. Here in India I have seen that parents who are very religious but at the same time they have a mature responsible understanding of what their religion is and they want to be sober and accept the responsibilities of this world and it’s still at the same time, you know, be devoted to their Guru and Krishna. But the children, what they want, just to rebel against their parents. They don't know that they are doing like this. It's just a psychological situation that takes place in the teenage years. What they do is they want to completely surrender everything. They want to completely just “zuuuuuuuuu”[Maharaja making sound], just become sanyasis tomorrow. Why? Because my parents, they are not like that. So this is the natural tendency. So when I was young of course, you start becoming a little mischievous. So I was going to college. I was just leaving for college. Which means, in America, you are just leaving home? And usually you never come back [Maharaja laughs]. Once you get out, you never go back. That’s the standard there. So I was just leaving and my father was driving me, I believe to a place where I was to catch a bus to go to that college. Now, I chose to go to college the furthest point from where my home was in the country of America, one of the furthest points, couple of thousand miles away. That way nice freedom. My Father told me, he said he was crying. Like a father he cries out of love. He said, I want you to know this, my son that you are my son and I love you and nothing can ever change that. Nothing in this world can change that. If you become a drug addict, if you become an alcoholic, if you become a thief, if you become a murderer, if the police call me tomorrow and tell me that you have murdered someone, even if you completely reject me, even if you steal all my property, my love for you cannot change. It's not possible. I will be disappointed, I will be hurt, but you are my son and I love you and nothing can change that. Of course, this is just a tiny little reflection of the pure love that God has for every living being. God’s love for us is unconditional. We are his children. However much we run away and out of envy for him we try to usurp and take the credit for what is meant to him, He does not change his love for us. And similarly a saintly person who is non envious has the exact same merciful quality as God himself. A saintly person sees every living being as part of God. Therefore, there is unconditional love for God and for all living creatures within' his heart. That person is non envious. So know. As soon as you start envying anyone in this world know that that envy is not for that person. It is a reflection of that stored envy within your heart that is meant for God himself. And self-management means to learn to control our mind to become non envious of God, to learn to manage our life in such a way that ‘we are living according to the principle that I am meant for the enjoyment of the supreme, that everything is his property, that self-realization, the purification of the heart, the realization of this is the goal of my life. That is why we say that for the mind that is uncontrolled, in truth, that mind is one’s worst enemy. But due to ignorance, due to that exploitative disease that I am the enjoyer, we make so many friends and enemies. We are cheating ourselves. Human life that is not made with the priority of self-realization designed accordingly, the self-destruction. We are cheating ourselves. Therefore, how to make our mind our friend? How to control our mind? How to manage our life so that we are pursuing the truth? It is simultaneously very simple but it is also very difficult. How is this? The principle is very simple but the application entails one quality and that is sincerity. The principle is: by associating with saintly persons, by hearing from them submissively, and taking what they say into our heart then we can understand exactly what truth is. It’s very simple. Arjuna was bewildered, he was confused. He simply opened his heart to his guru, Lord Sri Krishna and simply by opening his ears, his heart, and hearing he understood exactly what is to be done, what is not to be done, what is the truth, and what is ignorance, and how to live a God conscious life. It is very simple. The first thing is we must have association of saintly persons. Without that association self-realization is practically speaking impossible. Without the association of saintly persons, factually, according to all the shastras, our human life its real purpose will be defeated. Rishabh Dev says, mahat-sevāṁ dvāram āhur vimuktes, tamo-dvāraṁ yoṣitāṁ saṅgi-saṅgam [SB 5.5.2] By serving the great souls, by hearing from them, and associating with them, the doors to enlightenment are open wide. But by associating with envious persons who are attached to material sense enjoyment and sinful life especially, the doors to ignorance and bondage are wide open in our life. Now, in one sense it is very easy to associate with saintly persons but in another sense it is very difficult. Because to associate with saintly person…. You are associating with someone who loves you, someone who cares about you, someone who is manifesting the qualities of God before your eyes. That makes it easy. But also the saintly person, as the representatives of God, they are exposing to you your own false ego. When you hear them speak; when you see them, you realize what kind of false ego you have within yourself. It is very difficult to put that aside and be humble before them. Humble means to carry out their order. Humble means to become their servant. Pride is always there to convince us otherwise. Ah.. It’s very easy to an arm chair religious person who is simply 'yes, yes. I agree, I agree.' But to actually pursue the path of self-realization, to actually accept the path of the great souls means tad viddhi praṇipātena, paripraśnena sevayā [Bg. 4.34]

It means you have to become submissive. But by becoming submissive and hearing from the great souls, our life is blessed with the supreme treasure of Gods mercy. Now when we speak about great souls we are not considering necessarily from this sampradaya, that sampradaya or this religious sect or that religious sect. Sanatan dharma is the essence of the Vedic knowledge, it is the teaching of Bhagavad Gita, and it is the essence of all religion. Sanatan dharma means to simply become non envious. Sanatan dharma means that Krishna is the supreme enjoyer, God is the supreme enjoyer, and our natural function is to serve him, to live for his pleasure. That is Sanatan dharma in a nutshell. Anyone who is purely teaching Sanatan dharma, in essence, the path of Bhakti, devotion to God, he is saintly or she is saintly. In this age of kaliyuga there is one process which is taught by the Scriptures and the great saints. And by strictly with the conviction, dedication, accepting this process, all the impurities, all the unwanted qualities within our heart are removed and that process is 'harinam sankirtan', the chanting of Gods names. It is said in the Vedic literatures that what was available in Satyayuga through thousands and thousands of years of meditation, what was available in Tretayuga by performing massive, massive sacrifices headed by the most highly qualified priests and Brahmans, and what was available in Dwapara Yuga by worshiping the Supreme Personality of Godhead directly or in his deity form with great devotion, the same goal can easily be achieved in Kaliyuga by chanting the holy names of God. When we chant hare kṛṣṇa hare kṛṣṇa kṛṣṇa kṛṣṇa hare hare/hare rāma hare rāma rāma rāma hare hare,

We are accepting the most authorized, scientific process of purifying our heart. That purification is a process of bringing our heart back to its natural, healthy condition. Tonight in this room I see we have several doctors. According to the medical science the body is by nature supposed to be healthy. But the natural conditions of this world is that there are always enemy forces that are attacking the natural health of the body. And therefor medication is meant to destroy or remove the enemy forces, the germs, the diseases and allow the natural health of our body to continue. So the holy name is like medicine. The soul is pure. The soul is part of God. The soul is full of love. The soul has no envy. But our mind is very disturbed due to the bad qualities that are constantly attacking. The chanting of the holy name brings about a revolution of our heart. It creates an atmosphere within us where only love and peace and mercy and compassion can exist. Everything else is removed. Now do you not think that if you had these qualities you would certainly be a much better business man? Whether you are an industrialist or a doctor or a lawyer or a house wife or a professor or an administrator or a manager, if your motivation is based on love, trust in God, faith in God, if it is based on mercy and compassion towards all, living beings, do you think that you will be less effective? You will be the most effective. But your purpose will bring about real satisfaction in your life and you can contribute real satisfaction, real happiness to the world. You can help alleviate the suffering of humanity. Humanity is suffering. So this is the greatest need in human society, a change of consciousness, a purification of consciousness. Let us not be a part of the problem but let us be a part of the solution to the problem. This is real self-management. Learn to control your mind. Learn to make your mind your friend by following spiritual discipline in your life and keeping holy association. And then the treasure of peace, the treasure of real happiness, we can enjoy, and we can distribute the contents of that treasure to our loved ones and to the world. That is the goal of life. So let us take this very seriously. At least let us consider it very seriously because as I have said that with every minute your life's duration is reduced. Nobody gets younger in this world. Everyone is getting older. This world is like a river. The river is constantly flowing towards the ocean. So similarly physical nature means our life is constantly flowing towards the ocean of death. That is a fact that can never be changed because that is physical nature. But spiritual nature is also compared to a river. Because the soul is like a river that is constantly flowing towards the ocean of love of God. The nature of the soul is like a river. It is constantly flowing. It is constantly meant to flow towards the all blissful all loving ocean of Krishna. Now the question is that which river do we want our consciousness, to dive into. We have a choice. Do we want to dive into the river that was inevitably going to the ocean of birth, old age, disease, and death? That is the ocean that this body is destined for. There is no other place that this body can go except to the ocean of death. And as we coasting along the river, every moment you are closer and closer and closer to the ocean. Or do we choose the river of the soul? Human life is meant to learn how to become attracted to the ocean of qualities of Krishna. That attraction is our nature. Queen Kunti prayed,

[O Lord of Madhu, as the Ganges forever flows to the sea without hindrance, let my attraction be constantly drawn unto You, without being diverted to anyone else.]

You are watching Mahabharat in the mornings Sundays so you have some familiarity with the great dilemma that queen Kunti was facing in her life. After the battle of Kuruksetra, after all the sufferings and test and trails and tribulations that her and her children had to undergo by living in the forest for fourteen years, by being exploited, by being cheated, by being persecuted, attempts of assassination for both her and her children in places like varnataka, finally all the enemies were killed. All oppositions were removed and they were put in their proper place as the kings of the world. Queen Kunti prayed that, “My dear lord, as the Ganges ever flows to the sea without hindrance, let my attraction be forever drawn unto you without being diverted to anything or anyone else”. Now this may sound selfish. Why a mother wants to be only attracted to Krishna? What about her children? What about everyone else? Queen Kunti was a wise woman. She knew that if you water the root of the tree, the whole tree is nourished. If you love Krishna, you love everything that is coming from Krishna. And Vedanta sutra says: janmadyasya yathah, everything is emanating from the absolute truth. When you love Krishna, you are attracted to Krishna, your love, your devotion, your attitude of service will awaken for everything and everyone. But as long as our minds are diverted to love this person or that person and we neglect Krishna it’s like putting water on the different parts of the tree but neglecting the root. Ultimately you cannot really love anyone. It's superficial. It's empty. That love is simply floating in the river that is quickly approaching the ocean of death. So human life in essence means to learn how to be attracted to the all glorious qualities of God. By hearing about him, by chanting his names, our consciousness is submerged into the divine river of the soul and it will ever increasingly come closer and closer and closer to that infinite ocean of bliss, Lord Sri Krishna. This is our choice in life. Which river will we choose. This is the difference between a human being and an animal. This is the great benediction that we have in our life. Let us not take this great treasure or benediction and put it on our shelf. Let us keep it close to us at all times and get the full benefits. Some people are given the jewel of the highest benediction of the way to God and they put it on their shelf and every now and then they take it out. But this jewel is the most precious thing.
