[Bg. 2.47]

Translation

You have a right to perform your prescribed duty, but you are not entitled to the fruits of action. Never consider yourself the cause of the results of your activities, and never be attached to not doing your duty.

Lecture Transcription

This is a very famous verse in the Bhagavad-gita and one of the most misunderstood of all of the teachings of the scripture. It is describing herein by Lord Krishna to Arjuna , that you have a right to perform your actions, your work or your duty but you are not entitled to the fruits of your action. Never think yourself to be the cause of the results of your activity and never be attached to not doing your duty. Some people are under the impression that work is worship but in the third chapter of Geetha there is further clarification - Yajnarthat karmano 'nyatra loko 'yam karma-bandhanah that any work that is done with selfish motivation, that work is the cause of bondage. But work that is done in devotion to God, that work is the very basis of liberation. It is the very means of liberation.

Some people think that if “I take to spiritual life, then I will have to become a very impractical man. I will not be able to provide for my family, I will not be able to live within this society, but I will have to renounce all possessions, all social connections and live in the Himalayas or in an ashram in a holy place. therefore, since this concept is intolerable to me, it is better that I keep away from religion and it is better that I keep away from spiritually minded people”.

Story of Priyavrat and Uttanpada

In regard to this, I would like to narrate a beautiful story from the Srimad Bhagavatam. It is the story of Maharaja Priyavrat. Since it is Priyavrat’s birthday today, we will describe the glories of Priyavrat. Priyavrat was the eldest son of Swayambhuva Manu. Swayambhuva Manu was the king of Bharatvarsha . According to the Vedic literatures in those days, all the world was under ultimately, one supreme Emperor. Swayambhuva Manu had two sons, his elder son was Priyavrat and his younger son was of the name, Uttanapada. Now, Priyavrat had absolutely no inclination to power or money or fame or position. He simply wanted to worship God. So he appealed to his father that," my younger brother is very highly qualified to be the king. Well, let him be the king and let me do my bhajans, let me serve my guru Maharaja". Ok, his wish was granted. So Priyavrat went to a mountain in the Himalayas called Gandamadana parvat and there he accepted as his spiritual guide, the great Narada Muni and there Narada Muni was instructing him in the science of devotion to God. However great one is, if one is actually serious about making real progress, one must accept the guidance of the spiritual authority. You see, when we are talking about religion, we are not simply speaking about a sentimental form of faith, we are talking about the highest most sophisticated science in all of creation.

Within our universities today, we are teaching so many sciences. There is a science of geology, geography, mathematics, science of linguistics, science of biology, physics, psychiatry, psychology, engineering, architecture, medicine, law. Now there is an approach of how to learn any science. If you are in need of a surgical operation, and someone comes up to you on the street and says, "you know surgical operations are very costly", you say, " yes I know". He says, "well, I happen to be a surgeon and you give me your money and I will perform surgery and here's my knife let us begin". Will you accept? First of all, you will ask him, "what are your qualifications" and he will tell you that I have read the proper books. A proper surgeon must have read the proper books. Now if anybody just gets the books and buys them from the University and sits home and reads them. it's a very inexpensive way of getting an education. Well anyone accept you as an authority if the books were enough, why do you spend so much money going to college, because, in order to understand the books properly you absolutely must study under an authority , who has studied under another authority , who has studied under another authority, who knows how to apply the teachings of those books in practical life. In other words, you must study the books under an authorised professor and everyone accepts in their practical life that this is the process of learning any education or science. But when it comes to the highest science, why do I say the highest science, there is a science of biology and physics which teach you about the gross matter of this world, and then there is a science of psychiatry and psychology which teaches you about a subtle matter that cannot be seen or heard or touched or taste, the science of the mind.

God, Religion and Guru

Religion is the science of the soul, the science of consciousness, the science which reveals to you who you are, what is your nature, what is your essence, what is God and what is your relationship . When we lead you to see it scientifically, then it transcends all sectarian boundaries. Why is there so much conflict amongst religions today, because, there's no scientific approach, there is no philosophical approach. It is all simply sentiment and fanaticism. Religion without philosophy is either sentimentalism or fanaticism. So, therefore, when it comes to the highest science, practically anywhere in the world you go, you'll find that people say, "well ! I think God is like this and I feel that God is like this and I believe that God is like this and your opinion is as good as my opinion and my opinion is good as his opinion and her opinion is as good as his opinion ". Yato mat tato path all paths lead to the same goal, so anything you think about God, that's God and everyone says, yes you are very liberal minded, you are very learned, and the result of such religion, people hate each other, people are envious of one other, people have endless material attachments, people have no peace of mind, people are constantly in anxiety. The resources of this world are being horribly exploited on every level. Are these distinctly saintly people? Therefore, it is said in the Shastra ‘Tat vigyan artham su gurum eb abhi gachyate’ If you want to understand the truth, you must accept an authority, you must approach one who knows the truth.

If you want to know the truth, you must accept a knower of the truth, approach him, inquire submissively, render service to him, he can impart the truth to you because, he knows. Sometimes people asked me, "Radhanath Swami, why did you become Hare Krishna devotee? After all there are so many other religions in the world. You were born in the United States of America, you were born in a surrounding of Christianity and Judaism, that's your heritage and here you are talking about how all religions are essentially one. So why did you change? If everything's one , why change?". I can explain to you, there's no question of changing, the question of knowing, the question of understanding. Although all religions are essentially teaching love of God, whether one is a Christian or a Jew or a Buddhist or a Parsi or a Muslim or a Hindu or a Jain, the goal is to understand in truth through the realization - who you are, what is your relationship with God and to serve God with love. That is the only religion - Sanatana dharma. Why did I choose this way of life because, in all of my travels throughout the world and in all of my studies of the Scriptures and of all of my meeting with holy people, until I met His divine grace A C Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada, I never met anybody who truly revealed to me through his life and through his teachings, the means by that the knowledge and the understanding and the realization by which I wanted to dedicate everything in the service of God. Because he was like this, I am like this. This is not a question of this religion or that religion, it is a question of this is the person that inspired me to sacrifice my life in love for God, in love for all living beings. We are concerned with finding a person who can inspire us to love God.

So Priyavrat as a young man, he found that person Srila Narada Muni and Narada Muni was teaching him all about the principles of renunciation, tapasya, vairaghya, then "you stay up in his mountain you chant the names of God you perform tapasya in this way, don't entangle yourself with the things of this world. There's no need. Let your brother do all that". So of course Uttanapada he was a great king, and then he handed the throne over to his son who was the great and celebrated Dhruva Maharaja and the kingdom was passed down to these very qualified great saints until after the great Prachetas, there was no son. So then Swayambhuva Manu who was still living, because in those days people live long life, he was thinking “what to do now, I'm the king again there's no one else. I want to dedicate my life and retire from all these worldly affairs” when you get old you should retire you should dedicate your life only for God, let the young people do all this work. So where is Priyavrat? So he went up to the mountain and Priyavrat saw him coming he thought, "Oh my God! Narada muni, please save me". I have no desire to get married, I have no desire to have children, I have no desire to have a kingdom, it is all just so much headaches. Swayambhuva Manu who began to explain to Priyavrat, "it is your duty to become the king" and Priyavrat was saying, "but I am very afraid that my devotion to God will be spoiled if I'm associated with so much materialistic ways of life".

This is very much different than the politicians of today. Is it not? The politicians of today - they will beg, borrow, steal, kill, do anything to take to throne. Huh ! Not all of them are so immoral, but most of the places the world they are and when they attain the throne, My god!, they never want to give it up. Just like in my country of the United States of America, the previous president was Ronald Reagan. How old was that man, my god, he was 75 years old. He should have taken sanyasa 25 years before and he was still trying to just hold on to his position and in Japan, how old is the Emperor? he just died, he died in the throne, correct? 85 years, and he died in the throne and he wanted to keep on living however invalid, let me keep my power, let me keep my position, let me keep my power to control. They'll do anything to get it and they'll do anything to keep it, why? Because they are attached, they're attached to wealth, they are attached to prestige, they are attached to fame, they are attached to power .

But here Swambhuvamanu, who he was the king and he's begging his son, take it - take it, “I want to simply do bhajan, do kirtan associate with sadhus. I've had enough of this work. I've done my duty now, let me just chant HareKrishna” and his son Priyavrata, it's not that he was canvassing votes, he was saying, "please father! Keep the throne. I don't want it, find somebody else". These are men who are actually qualified, why? because they are attached, nothing for their own selfish interests but they are attached only to the service that they can render. So therefore, SwambhuvaManu did not know what to do. At that time the grandfather of Priyavrat, Lord Brahma, came to that place. Of course Lord Brahma, as you know, he is the Guru for Narada Muni. Sometimes even in spiritual circles, there is this going to higher authorities. So when Lord Brahma came, Naradamuni, Priyavrata, they all offered obeisance’s. Along with (Narada Muni) Lord Brahma, came Lord Shiva and so many great Saints with so many great Rishi's and then Lord Brahma began to speak. He said, " Maharaja Priyavrata! You are afraid that if you accept a family and if you accept worldly responsibilities, then your spiritual life will be ruined ". He said, "but if you are accepting it as a service to God, do you not think that God will protect you?" And then he used an example. He said that some people they leave their wife and they leave their children and their leave their houses to go to live in a forest, in the jungle. But when they go into the jungle, they take six wives with them, in the form of their mind and their five senses. As long as you are attached to the pleasures of your mind and your five senses, if you try to artificially renounce the world, you are actually going into the forest with six wives. You're better off stay at home with one wife and being responsible and then he began to explain that spiritual life is to be understood according to how we can please the Lord, how we can best serve the Lord. The real duty

Whatever your position of life is, whether you're a Brahmin, a priest, a Kshatriya (a warrior), a vaishya (a mercantile or agricultural person) or a sudra (a laborer), whether you are a sanyasi in the renounced or a grihastha, a family man or woman, a brahmachaari, a student or a vanaprastha ( retired) whatever your particular position in society is whether you are a doctor, a lawyer, an industrialist, a business, a housewife, a teacher, a professor, an architect or a.. whatever you may be if you perform your duty to please God your life is perfect. So therefore he told Priyavrat that "I am not coming here to give you my opinion. As your guru, I am coming here to tell you, what is the will of God. The Supreme Lord Hari wants you to perform the great service of becoming king". At that time Priyavrat he bowed his head and he said, " Yes. I will accept this service and become king". And then Lord Brahma left and Priyavrat came down from that Gandamadana hill and went into the great kingdom. He became a very great and powerful king. But how did he rule the world ? It is explained by Bhaktivinoda thakur

grihe thako, vane thako, sada ‘hari’ bole’ dako,

[Gay Gaur Madhur Sware]

Whether you are a householder or a renunciant, if you sincerely dedicate your life to glorify God by chanting his name, your life is perfect.

Krishna says in Gita

[Bg. 9.27]

[Whatever you do, whatever you eat, whatever you offer or give away, and whatever austerities you perform – do that, O son of Kuntī, as an offering to Me.]

Whatever you do, whatever you think, whatever you offer and give away in charity, whatever you eat should be done as an offering to the Lord. This is spiritual life, this is the essence of the Gita, this is the essence of all scriptures. You see, God does not see who you are or what you do. God sees the quality of your faith and the quality of your devotion. He is not considering, he does not consider if you have a PhD or a few at your illiterate. It's all the same to him. If you have a PhD use it in devotion and that is perfect, if you are illiterate use it in devotion and your life is perfect, if you have crores and crores of rupees use it in devotion and your life is perfect, if you have nothing but a begging pot and a stick use it in devotion and your life is perfect. It is the quality of one's consciousness that is the singular factor in spiritual life.

Story of Lord Ram and King Guha

There is a story in the Ramayana, when Lord Shri Ramchandra was banished from Ayodhya by his stepmother Kaikeyi, He and Sita and Lakshmana and the charioteer Sumantra, they began to proceed towards Dandakaranya forest. They came to the bank of the Ganges and at that place there was an old friend of Lord Rama, his name was king Guha and Guha was not an ordinary king. He was living in the jungle and his people were of a very-very low and untouchable class, by people who were very caste conscious. They were of the Naishad race, which means they were outcasts, living in the forest. Now although King Guha by all social purposes was an outcast, he was very simple and very humble and he had such devotion and such a desire to please Lord Rama. When he saw Lord Shri Ramachandra with tears of love in his eyes, he bowed at his feet he said, "My dear Lord, this jungle is my kingdom and all of these people they are my subjects, but I am your servant. Please accept me as your servant, accept all my subjects as your servant, accept all of my Empire as your kingdom". You see materialistic people, they are afraid of God. Why are they afraid of God because they know that God is the proprietor of everything and they do not want to give up their false sense of proprietorship therefore they'd rather not believe in God, it makes life very easy or even if they believe in God they want to keep him on the shelf somewhere and they'll go to see him on Sundays but the rest of the week I have my own life. But King Guha, he was a pure hearted servant, he was a real saint in the true sense because he accepted that, everything is yours my lord, please take it, as I am your servant, enjoy.

Maharaja Priyavrat, when he accepted the kingdom, he never thought himself to be the King he considered himself the humble servant not only of God but of every citizen in the kingdom - that it is my duty to sacrifice my life even for the most insignificant creature in the kingdom if he needs my help.

Story of Maharaja Shibi

There's also the story of Maharaja Shibi. He was a King. One day a vulture was chasing after a pigeon and the pigeon fell at the feet of Maharajah Shibi and said, "please, you are the king. I am in your kingdom, therefore I am your subject It is your duty to protect me. This vulture is coming to kill me." Maharaja Shibi said "Yes, vulture will not harm you, I am here to protect you". The vulture said, "hunn! but I am also your subject. I am also living in your kingdom and my food is pigeon. So because of you, you're gonna starve me to death. So Maharaj Shibisaid, "oh my god!” See in this world you can please some of the people some of the time, but you can never please all the people all the time". But it's the duty of the King to try to please all the people all the time, even at the cost of his life. So he said, "Alright Mr. Vulture, whatever the weight of this pigeon is, I will give you out of my own flesh ". The vulture said, "all right, very good". They put the pigeon on a scale and then the King started cutting off his flesh and putting it on the other side of his scale and however much he put the pigeon always was a little heavier and he was cutting piece after piece after piece of his own flesh and the pigeon was still more. So, finally Maharaja Shibi said, "all right" and he sat on the scale himself with his whole body and it was perfect even weight and the King said " then you eat me". If the king is not willing to give his life in the service to protect even the most insignificant service, he has no right being king. The king is the servant. In spiritual classification, the higher the position you take, the lower of a servant you become. In material calculation the higher the position, the more you think yourself master but in spiritual life the higher position, the more you consider yourself servant. So at that time, the pigeon and the vulture turned into, their bodies were just completely transformed into beautiful celestial beings and they said King Sibi, "we just wanted to test your (your) glory, we wanted to show the world what are the qualities of a devotee of God, how he is so compassionate".

So Maharaja Guha, he was saying to Lord Rama, "please take everything it is all yours. You are the Lord of my life" and then he began to offer the most menial services to Rama. "It is getting close to dark, I will help you cross the Ganges tomorrow" and with his own hands he began to collect leaves and soft grass and he began to make a bed for Lord Ramachandra and of course Lakshman was helping him. Then after the bed was made very nicely, he approached Sita and Rama "please take rest be happy". He fanned him, he fed him with fruits and whatever the best he could come come up with, from the forest. In this way Lord Ramachandra was perfectly delighted. He was absolutely happy and pleased. The day before he was living in the kingdom of Ayodhya, in a palace of marble and gold and jewels, in the softest most comfortable bed, with servants and opulent foods all around him and now he was living in the jungle and a bed of leaves. But because that bed of leaves was prepared by the devoted hands of Maharaj Guha, that bed of leaves was more luxurious, more pleasant, more enjoyable than even the most opulent expensive bed than in the heavenly planet and through the night Maharaja did not even consider sleeping. He was simply guarding Lord Ramachandra and making sure everything was just right and Sita, Ram they slept with great pleasure and they awoke in the morning and Maharaja Guha helped them across the Ganges. In this way, he showed us, Lord Shri Ramachandra showed us, that he only sees the quality of our humility and the quality of our devotion, he does not see our position.

So, therefore, there is no need to change our occupation, there is no need to change our caste or our position in society, there's no reason to change our religion.

Story of the leper Brahman

Last night we told a nice story. I was requested to tell it again this evening and it is very instructive in this regard. This is a story that is found in one of the great ancient Puranas and this story is also repeated by Srila Krishnadas Kaviraj Goswami, the author of Chaitanya Charitamrita. He refers to this beautiful narration. It is the story of a brahman and this brahman he was suffering from the disease of leprosy. It was a very painful condition for him. Of course, you know especially, here in India, you know what is leprosy, you know how incapacitated it can make a person. Now this Brahmin had a very-very virtuous and beautiful wife. She was coming from a very-very high family but she was married to this man and after he had leprosy she felt that it was her duty and it was her religious duty and service to always be true and faithful to help her husband through his miserable suffering life. She would cook for him, she would clean him, she would have to work for him in every respect, he could do nothing. So one day she brought him to a holy river and with her own hands she had to bathe him. While she was bathing him, there was a very beautiful prostitute, her name was Laksha Hira, which means one whose beautiful luster is like is like hundreds and thousands of diamonds and precious jewels. So after the bath, with great care and attention she took her husband home. But that evening as she was trying to feed him, she saw that he was very-very much dissatisfied at mind. She said, "my dear husband! What is wrong with you? What can I do to make you happy? I will do anything. What is causing you distress?". He said," I'm ashamed to tell you". She said, "no you must tell me. I am your wife". He said," you remember that prostitute we saw today? I have become very attached to her. I cannot possibly get her out of my mind." Well, the wife with tears in her eyes she said," my dear husband, do you want this prostitute?". He said "yes". With folded palms she said I will do everything I can to make you happy. That family of Brahmins had no money. So, what she did is, she went to the house of that prostitute, there were so many other prostitutes living there, and every day she would clean the whole house from the ground floor to the top floor immaculately. The house was never so clean. So this one, Laksha Hira, she asked one of her associates, "how is it that this house is always so immaculately clean? who is doing this?" One of her associates said, "it is this Brahmin lady. Although she's a Brahmin, she comes, she doesn't accept any payment and even the workers who are supposed to clean this house; she works so hard; they have nothing to do.”

So, Laksha Hira says, "please bring this lady to me. I do not understand". So the brahmins wife approached her. She said, "Why are you doing like this? You are a Brahmin. Why are you serving an outcast like me?" She said ,"because my husband is very anxious to have you. My only desire is, you satisfy him". Laksha Hira replied," I understand. You and your husband come to my home for dinner tonight". So, they both came for dinner and she had prepared very-very nice cuisine. She divided the meal into two sections.
