atrāpy udāharantīmam
itihāsaṁ purātanam
prahrādasya ca saṁvādaṁ
muner ājagarasya ca

[SB. 7.13.11]

[As a historical example of this, learned sages recite the story of an ancient discussion between Prahlāda Mahārāja and a great saintly person who was feeding himself like a python.]

Purport by His Divine Grace A.C.Bhaktivedant Swami Srila Prabhupada;

Purport

The saintly person met by Prahlāda Mahārāja was undergoing ājagara-vṛtti, the living conditions of a python, which does not go anywhere but sits in one place for years and eats whatever is automatically available. Prahlāda Mahārāja, along with his associates, met this great saint and spoke to him as follows.

taṁ śayānaṁ dharopasthe
kāveryāṁ sahya-sānuni
rajas-valais tanū-deśair
nigūḍhāmala-tejasam

dadarśa lokān vicaran
loka-tattva-vivitsayā
vṛto ’mātyaiḥ katipayaiḥ
prahrādo bhagavat-priyaḥ

[SB. 7.13. 12-13]

[Prahlāda Mahārāja, the most dear servitor of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, once went out touring the universe with some of his confidential associates just to study the nature of saintly persons. Thus he arrived at the bank of the Kāverī, where there was a mountain known as Sahya. There he found a great saintly person who was lying on the ground, covered with dirt and dust, but who was deeply spiritually advanced.]

karmaṇākṛtibhir vācā
liṅgair varṇāśramādibhiḥ
na vidanti janā yaṁ vai
so ’sāv iti na veti ca

[SB. 7.13.14

[Neither by that saintly person’s activities, by his bodily features, by his words nor by the symptoms of his varṇāśrama status could people understand whether he was the same person they had known.]

Purport

The inhabitants of that particular place on the bank of the Kāverī in the valley of the mountain known as Sahya were unable to understand whether that saint was the same man they had known. It is therefore said, vaiṣṇavera kriyā mudrā vijṣe nā bhujhaya. A highly advanced Vaiṣṇava lives in such a way that no one can understand what he is or what he was. Nor should attempts be made to understand the past of a Vaiṣṇava. Without asking the saintly person about his previous life, Prahlāda Mahārāja immediately offered him respectful obeisances.

taṁ natvābhyarcya vidhivat
pādayoḥ śirasā spṛśan
vivitsur idam aprākṣīn
mahā-bhāgavato ’suraḥ

[SB. 7.13.15]

[The advanced devotee Prahlāda Mahārāja duly worshiped and offered obeisances to the saintly person who had adopted a python’s means of livelihood. After thus worshiping the saintly person and touching his own head to the saint’s lotus feet, Prahlāda Mahārāja, in order to understand him, inquired very submissively as follows.]

bibharṣi kāyaṁ pīvānaṁ
sodyamo bhogavān yathā

vittaṁ caivodyamavatāṁ
bhogo vittavatām iha
bhogināṁ khalu deho ’yaṁ
pīvā bhavati nānyathā

[SB. 7.13.16-17]

[Seeing the saintly person to be quite fat, Prahlāda Mahārāja said: My dear sir, you undergo no endeavor to earn your livelihood, but you have a stout body, exactly like that of a materialistic enjoyer. I know that if one is very rich and has nothing to do, he becomes extremely fat by eating and sleeping and performing no work.]

Purport

Śrīla Bhaktisiddhānta Sarasvatī Ṭhākura did not like his disciples to become very fat in the course of time. He would become very anxious upon seeing his fat disciples becoming bhogīs, or enjoyers of the senses. This attitude is herewith confirmed by Prahlāda Mahārāja, who was surprised to see a saintly person adopting ājagara-vṛtti and becoming very fat. In the material world also, we generally see that when a man who is poor and skinny gradually endeavors to earn money through business or some other means and he then gets the money, he enjoys the senses to his satisfaction. By enjoying the senses one becomes fat. Therefore in spiritual advancement becoming fat is not at all satisfactory.

[Mangalacharan]

Lecture Transcription

Narada Muni tells the story of Prahlada Maharaj to Yudhistir Maharaj

This is a story narrated by Srila Narada muni to the great Maharaj Yudhistir, where he is further describing the glories of Prahlada Maharaj. This incidence is taking place after Prahlada was put on throne by Lord Narsimha dev as the king of entire universe. Prahlada although a Mahabhagvat, a devotee of the highest and purest nature, wanted to set the proper example for all human beings to follow, and also wanted to exhibit the natural quality of humility of great souls. He decided to take his confidential associates, and travel throughout the universe meeting saintly persons. You see, the more saintly you become, the more you can appreciate the saintliness in another, association of saintly persons for a neophyte devotee, is is only means of making spiritual advancement. Association of saintly devotees for a non-devotee is the only means of beginning in truth, the process of bhakti. So therefore, those who are either struggling hard in this material world, or struggling hard in the path of devotional service, it is most essential and relevant in their lives to seek out the association of saintly persons. As described by Maharaj Rishabh dev, ‘mahat-sevāṁ dvāram āhur vimuktes tamo-dvāraṁ yoṣitāṁ saṅgi-saṅgam’ [SB. 5.5.2] by associating with saintly persons and rendering service to them, only through this process are the doors to liberation are open wide. But by associating with people who are too much attached to enjoying the material senses, the doors to ignorance are open wide.

Associating with devotees through transcendental sound vibrations

Now for those great souls, who have attained by the mercy of God, and the vaishnavas, that state of pure devotion, to them there is nothing more pleasing and wonderful in their life, than the association of saintly persons. After all Prahlada Maharaj was a Mahabhagvat, and Narad Muni was also Mahabhagvat, and Narad Muni’s greatest pleasure as he is explaining, is to describe the glories of Prahlada, who is his own disciple, but because of Prahlad’s pure devotion to the Lord, Narad Muni found the greatest satisfaction, pleasure and inspiration, by associating with Prahlada through transcendental sound vibration. You see, when you hear about these great souls, you are associating with them, through transcendental sound. Transcendental sound is identical to the substance in which it is describing. Factually, right here, this morning, for those who are submissive, with open hearts, to allow this transcendental narration to enter into their hearts. Factually you are associating directly with Narada Muni and Prahlada Maharaj right now. It is not historical, although on one level it is. Transcendental sound transcends all the boundaries of time and space, because it is absolute, therefore when we hears the glories of Prahlada Maharaj, we are factually with Prahlada Maharaj, we are in his divine presence, he is factually living in this room at this time, and those persons can perceive him as much as they could in Satya yuga, if they simply hears submissively. Bhaktivinod Thakur, he wrote in the inscription on the Samadhi Mandir of Srila Haridasa Thakur, that vaishnavas, they live and die to spread the holy name around. He reasons ill who says that the, “vaishnavas die”, because they are living in sound. Through the sound vibration of the instructions, through the sound vibration of lila, they live forever, in the hearts of those who are simply willing to hear, and this is, one of the great characteristics we must understand, to understand the importance of hearing. When we all are deeply submissive, in the sacrifice of hearing, then our entire consciousness is factually transported to the transcendental platform, and we are in the presence of God when we chant his holy name, and we are in the presence of his eternal associates when we hearing their glories, just as, we are meant to hear attentively, when we are chanting japa, with the same attention we must hear Srimad Bhagavatam, how every… every aspect of our being, should simply be immersed in grasping on to each syllable of the sound, of the sacred name of God. With same intensity, and with that same sincerity, we should be hearing Srimad-Bhagavatam, and the great souls, this is how they heard it. When Sukdeva Goswami originally spoke Srimad Bhagavatam on the bank of the Ganges, it is described Maharaj Parikshit, was so immersed in utter concentration and hearing the sound vibration, that he completely forgot eating, sleeping, mating or defending. There was only one thing in his consciousness and that was that sound vibration, he was in rapt attention. He was in Samadhi. And it is described that everyone in the assembly who was there, was just as much intensely hearing the sound vibration of the narration of Bhagavatam. It is described that as Sukdeva Goswami was speaking, for seven days continuously, it was so quite, because everyone was in so much enraptured, that even if a pin, or a blade of grass, would fall to the ground, it would have created a tremendous disturbance. There was nobody moving around, there was nobody talking to amongst each other, there was no one falling asleep, everyone was simply on the edge of their seats, as we say, just anticipating the next word. Ah, Everyone, not like in our Bhagvatam classes, where we are wishing when the will it be over, they never wanted it to end, they wanted it to go on hearing forever, ‘nityaṁ bhāgavata-sevayā’ [SB.1.2.18] they were transported, because factually, as it is described in Bhagvatam, ‘kṛṣṇe sva-dhāmopagate’ [SB. 1.3.43] that this Srimad-Bhagavatam is the literary incarnation of Krishna, just as the holy name is non-different then Krishna in every respect, so similarly the Bhagavata-Purana is absolutely in every speck non-different than Krishna, and by hearing this narration we are actually in the divine presence of all these great souls like Dhruv Maharaj, Prahlada Maharaj. Sometimes we think, why am I living in this day and age, my God, why I couldn’t I be living, when Chaitanya Mahaprabhu is dancing in the streets of Navdweep, with Lord Nityananda, Haridasa Thakur and Advaita acharya, Vakreshwar Pandita and Srivasa Thakur all these great souls, Svarupa Damodar Goswami, why was I not there? Why was I not there in Vrindavan, when Rupa Goswami, Sanatana Goswami, Jiva Goswami, Raghunath dasa Goswami, Raghunath Bhatt Goswami, Gopal Bhatt Goswami, Lokanath Goswami, Narottam dasa Thakur, Krsnadasa Kaviraja Goswami, Srinivas acharya, they were all there practically at the same time, where was I? Where was I, when Prahlada Maharaja was king of the Earth, ruling everyone with compassionate religious principles? Where was I, when other great sages headed by Parikshit Maharaj, was hearing for seven days, the original transmission of Bhagavatam by Sukdeva Goswami? The fact is every morning you are on all these places, when you hear. Right now, what we are doing, factually we are simultaneously, if we are receptive, we are on the bank of the Ganges, hearing from Sukadeva Goswami. There is absolutely no difference. We are factually, in the presence of Narada Muni, and Maharaja Yuddhisthir, hearing the transcendental glories of Prahlada, ah, it’s wonderful.

Importance of attentive hearing

We are very lucky, but we don’t know it. Why don’t we know it? Because we are inattentive, you see inattentive hearing, and we…we deprive ourselves of the ultimate fortune that is ours, that is right before us in the transcendental sound. It’s like this story of a man who had a jewel, was a Chintamani jewel, but he didn’t know, it’s was a Chintamani jewel, he thought it was a jewel, nice jewel, but he did not know it’s a Chintamani jewel. You could have all your desires fulfilled.” The man was so angry, “I had the most precious thing, but I didn’t know what it was, so I neglected it and lost it.” This is our position. We have the most precious thing right in our hands, the association of devotees, the Srimad-Bhagavatam pure spiritual sound vibration, which will transport you to the spiritual world immediately; we have the holy name which is the Krishna himself, ‘kali-kāle nāma-rūpe kṛṣṇa-avatāra’ [Cc. Adi .17.22] Krishna with all his glories and opulence is right before us, in his holy name, but because we are inattentive, we do not even know what we have, and we neglect it, and due to neglect, we are risking loosing it, and then when we realize what we had, we think ‘my god, how foolish.’ So you see, inattentiveness means, losing the opportunity of the greatest benediction, that is at hand. So, here, it is being described that Prahlada Maharaj, although such a great saint, he understood the glories of the great saints, there is no greater pleasure, there is no greater satisfaction, for a great personality to humble himself before another great personality, see amongst great personalities, there is not supposed to be competition, there is supposed to be ‘Dasoasmi’, that I’m your servant. So, umm… in this situation, Prahlada Maharaj, was anxious to hear from this great saintly person, he wanted to hear more and more and more, he was thirsty to hear the glories of the Lord, the glories of renunciation from this great souls. So his tour of the universe, seeking out saints, to associate with them, to hear from them, took him to the bank of the Kaveri river, which is in South India, near the Sahya mountain, and there he met a person, nobody understood, he was a saintly person, perhaps most of the persons thought, he was a mad man. Actually in America it is said, “It takes one to know one.” Have you ever heard that saying? “It takes one to know one.” Factually, only person who knows who a pure devotee is? Is a pure devotee, we can have some idea, but the persons who really know, are those who are… who are on that platform. A Mahabhagvat can really understand who a Mahabhagvat is. We can have some idea based on faith and scriptural reference, but the actual knowing, comes, when you are on that platform. So Prahlada Maharaj saw this man, laying on the ground, on his belly, in the jungle, a dirty mess, covered with dirt and dust and weeds and grass, it looked like, he hadn’t even gotten up in years, he was just laying there. Prahlada Maharaj could understand he took the vow of a python, ah. Do you really know, what is the vow of a python? A python simply sits in one place, and when it’s time to eat, he simply opens his mouth, and whatever happens to go into his mouth he eats, and whatever doesn’t come into his mouth, he doesn’t eat. Ah, and of course, it takes a lot of faith, please understand, how many things come into your mouth, plus he is a vegetarian,[not clear 23.48] he can’t eat anything that crawled into his mouth. It had to be vegetarian food that just entered into his mouth. But this was his vow.

Fearlessness of a devotee comes from faith in the Lord

So he just laid there with his mouth open, and Prahlada Maharaj understanding from his vow, “Oh my God, this man should be very skinny, but he is fat. How is this possible?” [laughter] This is the power of faith. God is everywhere.

It is said in Bhagavad-Gita, that the Supreme Lord, for one who surrenders to him with faith, he provides what he…he …he protects what he has preserves, and he carries what he lacks. Another ways a devotee, who has faith in God, for his own bodily maintenance, he doesn’t worry. He knows God will provide. This is the quality, especially of those, who are in renounced order of life. Srila Prabhupada would often explained it in his books, that the quality of a sanyasi that is most prominent is that, he is fearless, if a person is not fearless he cannot be proper, in the ashram of sanyas. And what does it mean to be fearless? It means that you have so much faith in God, that, you just do not bother for…for your physical bodily maintenance, you simply accept what God provides, and you know that he will provide. You know in most dangerous condition of life he will provide protection. A sanyasi if he is living in the darkest, deepest, jungles, with snakes, scorpions, lions, tigers, leopards, panthers, pythons, ah, elephants, rhinoceroses they are all around him and he is not afraid, because he knows, Krishna is in their hearts ‘rakhe krsna mare ke, mare krsna rakhe ke’ If Krishna wants to kill me then, I’m supposed to die, what should I, why am I afraid? I’m not this body, and if Krishna wants to protect me, than these animals they all are my friends, ah, because he is completely depending on Krishna, and he has faith in Krishna, he is fearless. As far as where to sleep, where to eat, Jesus Christ in the Bible, when he was preaching to his disciples, his original disciples, he was telling them, “You leave your homes, you leave your occupations, you just follow me and preach,” they said, “Oh, will leave our occupations.” One man is a fisherman, his first disciple Peter. It was not like that he was laying there like a lazy man, but because he knew, he was totally immersed in the thought of Krishna. He was just laying there, and purifying the universe simply by immersing himself in loving thoughts of Krishna, He was just laying there, and purifying the universe simply by immersing himself in loving thoughts of Krishna, ah, and because he was in thought of Krishna he had complete faith, that Krishna will provide and if he doesn’t provide, who needs this body anyway, and if he does provide I’ll go on living in this body. So he opened his mouth, and Prahlada was struck with wonder, because he was fat. Usually, as… as Prabhupada says in this purports, “Bhaktivedanta Sarasvati Thakur did not like fat disciples, he would chastise, he would sometimes severely reprimand his disciples, when he saw them getting fat, because it means, they are too much enjoying sense gratification, and sense gratification means, no Krishna consciousness, where there is sense gratification, there cannot be Krishna consciousness, and where there is Krishna consciousness, there cannot be sense gratification, they go ill together.

Prahlada Maharaj shows exemplary behavior by worshipping the devotee

But this man he was, he had no sense gratification, but Krishna made him fat, just to show the world, how if one has faith in him, he don’t, doesn’t only give him what he needs, he give him much more even, if one has that faith. There is nothing to fear. They are worship able by the king of the universe. What is the difference? This man had nothing. He was living like a serpent. Blind Faith

[Devotee:] When do you call faith as blind faith? [not clear] Ok!

[Maharaj:] Real faith is faith that is based on the scriptures and the words of the sadhus. What they say, we should have faith in. When the guru or sadhu, give us an instruction or give some information, we should have complete faith in that, ah. When we read scriptures we should have complete faith in that, because it comes, it is coming from God. But if the source, of all information is our own mental concoction or somebody else’s mental concoction, that is blind faith. The difference between real faith and blind faith is one that the source of the information is one it is coming from God and other is coming from maya, ‘guru, sadhu, shastra’, when we have faith in guru, sadhu and shastra, this is how God speaks to us, in this world through Guru, Sadhu and Shastra. So, to have faith in God is real faith. Any other faith is blind. And when blind lead the blind, they both fall into the ditch, and suffer, ah.
