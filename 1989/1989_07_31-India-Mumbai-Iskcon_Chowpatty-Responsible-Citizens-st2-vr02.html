Lecture Transcription

Religious fights to show rigorous might

Recently, [clears the throat] I was giving a lecture on the philosophy of Bhagavad-Gita in one of the major universities of the United States of America. And one very intelligent, graduate student, at the end of my class, became very disturbed in mind and he raised his hand. And he inquired in a very challenging spirit, “You are talking about the solutions to the problems of the world and there are so many problems in this world. And you are trying to convince us that the real cure to the diseases in society is religion. But as far as I am concerned, the greatest disease and the impetus that is causing the worst problems in the world is religion. I am a practical man.” He acclaimed, “I have to see what's before my eyes, look at the condition of the world today. In Sri Lanka, there is bloodshed, violence and hate between the Buddhists and the Hindus. In India, we find bloodshed, violence and hate between the Hindus and the Sikhs. If you move a little west toward that border of Pakistan, we find hate, bloodshed and violence between the Hindus and the Muslims.”

“Let us move slightly west to the Middle East, Iran and Iraq, where there is violence and murders and massive bloodshed every day between two different sects of Muslims. Let us proceed slightly further west, in Lebanon there's bombings, there's terrorism taking place every day between the Muslims and the Christians. We can proceed slightly west to Israel where every day, there's terrorism and taginism and violence between the Muslims and the Jews. We go a little west, the European countries where for generations and generations and generations that has been massive hate, turmoil and violence between the Christians and the Jews. Let us procee… proceed a little further west to Ireland, where two different Christian sects with slightly different understandings, the Catholics and the protestants who read the same exact scripture, they are killing one another on the streets because of their religious differences. And if you go into the ocean to America, my God! everyone's against everyone.” Aha…

Communities with immunities

Recently, I was at a ministerial meeting, I was invited as a guest and there was the chief ministers and priests from all the churches, monasteries, mosques within the community. And the purpose of these ministers coming together is to treac… try to… try to create religious unity within the community of that particular city. So, they were speaking all lofty Um… ideals. But because I happened to be there, I was very controversial because I'm from a very small minority in that part of the world.

So, they began to challenge, what is the validity of your religion? After all, we are large, established religions that are acceptable by everyone, what is the validity of your religion? Why should we accept you? Aha… So, I was just listening at the beginning. Before I even have to say anything, the baptist looked at the methodists and said, “Why should we accept you?” And then I spoke, I said, “What is religion? Please, define to me. What is the basis of your understanding of how to approach God?”

And the baptist man said, “Unless you are baptized by being dunked in water, you cannot go to heaven but you will go to hell eternally.” And the methodists looked at the baptist and said, “Unless you're sprinkled in baptism, you're going to hell forever and cannot enter into the kingdom of God.” And then another man from another church began to explain his method of baptism and, “It's the only way, if you are not baptized in that particular ritual, in that process, you are condemned to hell forever. What to speak of your Hare Krishna? [laughter] You're mainline Christian with just a different process of ritual, you're condemned.” Auh… This is factually how religion is.

Forgetting spirit and essence

So, the student was challenging me that, “If you want to solve the problems of the world, the first thing you have to do is kick out religion, kick out God and let's try to make things practical, tangible and work together to do something good for humanity.” From a superficial point of view, what he was saying, that's the validity. But the question is what is religion? When the Lord Jesus Christ appeared in this world, at that time the hierarchy of the religious system was very corrupt and he was very very straightforward and bold. He said, “You have a form of religion but you have forgotten the spirit and power behind it.”

It is a fact that the various religions of the world appeared to differ, in so many ways. Different languages, different names of God, different forms of ritual, different quantities of philosophical understanding. But the essence, the goal of every religion for anyone who opens their heart to study is the same. You see, when we take the external forms to be the religion then we become sectarian and fanatical. We have to understand that the external forms are only a means to approach the end. But the goal and the focus of our mind must constantly be fixed on the end, the goal, the essence.

Root of fanaticism

In the Vedas, it is described that religion without philosophy is either sentimentalism or fanaticism. And philosophy without religion is simply mental speculation. Aha… What does this mean in our practical life, when people have a sense of belief in devotion but no philosophical explanation of why, and how, and what is the very essence… and people become fanatics. All of the war and bloodshed in the name of God has absolutely nothing to do with God, it has nothing to do with religion, it's simply fanaticism. What is everyone fighting for? We cited all these examples of all these different wars, they are all fighting for land and control. Right, that's all. It's not a question, if you believe this and I believe that, it's essentially you can question ‘I want control, our side, our religion, our sect wants the control in the property.’ And the other people say ‘No, we want.’

Principle of proprietorship

Now what is Krishna say in Bhagavad-gita, in the fifth chapter,

bhoktāraṁ yajṣa-tapasāṁ
sarva-loka-maheśvaram
suhṛdaṁ sarva-bhūtānāṁ
jṣātvā māṁ śāntim ṛcchati

[Bg. 5.29]

[A person in full consciousness of Me, knowing Me to be the ultimate beneficiary of all sacrifices and austerities, the Supreme Lord of all planets and demigods, and the benefactor and well-wisher of all living entities, attains peace from the pangs of material miseries.]

He says that, ‘Real religion is to simply understand that everything is the property of God, nothing belongs to you. And therefore, everything is meant for the enjoyment of God. And he is the well-wishing friend of everyone. One who attains this knowledge, achieves lasting peace.’ So, in the name of religion, people are simply fighting over - ‘What is mine? I am of this religion and this is mine.’ This is the basic evil behind all the problems of the world, is this illusion of I and mine. janasya moho ’yam ahaṁ mameti [SB. 5.5.8]. Forgetful that everything is the property of God. Everything is meant to be used in the service of God.

This principle is taught by Mohammed, this principle is taught by Jesus Christ and all the great apostles and teachers that have come in his line. This is the essential principles taught by Lord Ramachandra, by Lord Sri Krishna, by Lord Buddha. What is religion?

Definition of dharma

In the Vedic literatures, the word given for the goal of life for all human beings to achieve is Sanatan dharma. Now let us understand this philosophically. Dharma is a Sanskrit word which literally means, reflects the constitutional position of a person. Dharma means that which can never be separated from one's existence. The dharma of fire is heat. Can you have fire without heat? The dharma of water is liquidity. In its natural condition, water will always be liquid. If you put it in a totally foreign unnatural condition then it will become solid. But as soon as its natural environment again is there, it melts into liquid. What is the dharma? What is the nature of every living being?

The Vedas describe that everyone must render service. No one can live without serving. We find the husband is serving his family, the wife is serving her family, the student is serving the teacher, the teachers serving the students. The government officials are supposed to be serving the people, and the people are supposed to be serving the government. Essentially everyone is serving their mind and their senses without exception.

Sanatan dharma solve identity crises

Now Sanatan means eternal. What is the eternal occupation of every living being? Before we can understand, what is our eternal occupation, first, we must have a clear idea of who we are. Ninety-nine point nine percent of the people in this world have no idea who they are. If you ask someone, who are you? ‘My name is Raj Patel’ Aha… my name is Radhanath Swami. I may be Radhanath Swami now but factually twenty years ago, I had another name. Auh… Does that mean, I'm a different person and I could change my name tomorrow. I am an American. The fact is, if I stay in India long enough, I could possibly get Indian citizenship, then I'm an Indian. There are so many Indians who have gone to America who are citizens, they are no longer Indians. But I am the same person. You can change your name, you can change your nationality. You may say, ‘I am a man.’ Auh… But you know modern science has become very advanced these days. [laughter] You could even change that now, clinically, biologically, legally. Auh… I had practical experience.

Can I share with you one short story? I was in a place called Cleveland, Ohio. Auh… I was… I would come there regularly to give lectures. And there was once, a very… Aum… very sincere young girl who was coming, teen-age girl, she was coming every day and listening very carefully to the philosophy of Bhagavad-gita.

Modern science creates identy crises

And then for about a year, no one ever heard anything from her again. And then one day, about twelve noon, there was a ringing at the doorbell. So, I went down the stairs, I opened the door and to my surprise, he came back. [laughter] Auh… I said, “What happened?” he said with a very deep voice, “I'd like to start attending your services again.” Aha… I was not very interested in asking too much personal questions until, it, whatever you want to call - he or she, wanted to move in with the brahmacharis. Auh… the brahmacharis said, “No, no, no, no way. [laughter] We're conditioned to the impressions of our past.” So, I said, “Better you live outside and just come.” So the next day, he came with his girlfriend who previously was somebody else's boyfriend. [laughter] I do not want to expound on these matters in too much detail.

But the point I want to make is science has become so advanced. My God! It has achieved such heights that now you could biologically completely change from a man to a woman. So, if you say ‘I'm a man.’ That's not even necessarily perfect. So who are you? Through all the changes of this body, you remain the same. You can change your religion, change your sex, change your nationality, change the color of your skin, but you remain the same.

A missing department - ‘Science of soul’

According to Bhagavad-gita, the science of understanding who you are, is very very clearly and succinctly explained. Krishna says in Gita,

dehino ’smin yathā dehe
kaumāraṁ yauvanaṁ jarā
tathā dehāntara-prāptir
dhīras tatra na muhyati

[Bg. 2.13]

[As the embodied soul continuously passes, in this body, from boyhood to youth to old age, the soul similarly passes into another body at death. A sober person is not bewildered by such a change.]

‘As the embodied soul continually passes in this body from boyhood to youth to old age, the soul similarly passes into another body in death.’ na jāyate mriyate vā kadācin nāyaṁ bhūtvā bhavitā vā na bhūyaḥ [Bg. 2.20] ‘The soul is eternal it is imperishable, it is unborn, it is undying.’ You are that soul. Now everyone is very expert at understanding the needs of the body. As I say, my principal service in America is to lecture in universities. There are universities that have thousands of courses biology, geology, computer technology, engineering, political sciences, mathematics. There is not a single course in a single university that I have seen, that discusses the science of who you are. And the purpose of life.

Soul’s quality and quantity

According to the Gita, we are that consciousness, that life force that animates the mind in the body. Now what is the nature of that consciousness?

mamaivāṁśo jīva-loke
jīva-bhūtaḥ sanātanaḥ
manaḥ-ṣaṣṭhānīndriyāṇi
prakṛti-sthāni karṣati

[Bg. 15.7]

[The living entities in this conditioned world are My eternal fragmental parts. Due to conditioned life, they are struggling very hard with the six senses, which include the mind.]

Lord Sri Krishna describes in the Gita, that ‘All living beings are part of me, in that they are in me and they are mine.’ We are all part of God, qualitatively we are one with God but quantitatively he is great and we are infinitesimal. The Upanishad describes, nityo nityānāṁ cetanaś cetanānām eko bahūnāṁ yo vidadhāti kāmān [Kaṭha Upaniṣad 2.2.13]. That there is one supreme eternal being, God. And there are innumerable living beings, who are always subordinate and depending on him.

Soul’s duty towards whole

Now what is the nature of the part? To serve the interest of the whole. As soon as the part fails to serve the interest of the whole, that part becomes useless. Aha… Just like in a machine, one little screw, what is the value of a screw? When it's in the machine, it's keeping the whole machine running. Its worth the value of the entire machine. Aha… But when that screw is separated from the machine, it's worth nothing. It… It has no value whatsoever. Similarly, all the parts of the body, they are meant to serve the whole of the body.

There's a very interesting little story in one of the Puranas to illustrate allegorically the position of the rebellious living entities in this world. It is described that all the limbs of the body the mouth, the eyes, the ears, the hands, the feet, they all met together. They had a conference. And they all began to complain that, “We are working so hard simply to give food to the belly. Aha… And the belly that lazy fool, all he just sit there, enjoy. We were doing all the work. So let's go on strike.” What do they call it in Bombay when everyone goes on strike? [devotees replies “Bund”] Bunda. Aha… They declared bunda. “We are going to enjoy ourselves, what do we need this lazy loafer belly for.” So, the hand began to try to enjoy food by its own invention, and the feet, and the eyes, and the mouth, and no one would give anything to the belly. And after some time, what happened is every limb of the body began to become weaker and weaker and weaker until they all had an emergency urgent conference, because they were all about to die. And they decided, “I think, we should call an end to this bunda and we should all work to serve the belly and then we'll all be nourished and happy.” Aha… It is the nature of the part, it must serve the interest of the whole.

Watering roots of the tree

Krishna says in Gita,

ahaṁ sarvasya prabhavo
mattaḥ sarvaṁ pravartate
iti matvā bhajante māṁ
budhā bhāva-samanvitāḥ

[Bg. 10.8]

[I am the source of all spiritual and material worlds. Everything emanates from Me. The wise who know this perfectly engage in My devotional service and worship Me with all their hearts.]

He says ‘I am the source of all spiritual and material worlds, everything emanates from me. I am the root of everything that exists.’ He is the root. Now if you have a tree, there are so many parts to the tree. But every part of the tree is getting its nourishment from the root. If you water the leaves, and water the branches, and water the twigs, and water the bark, and water the trunk with billions of gallons of water but you don't put anything on the root, what happens to the tree? It dwindles and it dies. But if you simply put water on the root, automatically the whole tree is nourished. So, it is the dharma, it is the eternal occupation of every spirit soul to engage in the loving service of the supreme soul.

Great acharyas focus on essence

Krishna describes in Gita,

bhaktyā mām abhijānāti
yāvān yaś cāsmi tattvataḥ
tato māṁ tattvato jṣātvā
viśate tad-anantaram

[Bg. 18.55]

[One can understand Me as I am, as the Supreme Personality of Godhead, only by devotional service. And when one is in full consciousness of Me by such devotion, he can enter into the kingdom of God.]

That it is only by this loving service, that God reveals himself to his devotee. In the old, in.. in the New Testament Lord Jesus Christ was asked, “You have given us so many instructions, so many regulative principles to follow. What is the first and greatest of all commandments?” And he declared, “The first and great of all commandments is to love God with all your heart, all your mind and all your soul.” And generations before Lord Jesus Christ, Moses, the leader of the Jews, the prophet of the Jews, God's chosen representative for that people. He explained that, “The foremost purpose of all religion is to love God with all your heart, all your mind and all your soul.”

In the Islamic faith, what does Islam mean in Arabic? The word literally means to submit yourself with humility before God. And Krishna, he concludes the Bhagavad-gita with the ultimate instruction,

sarva-dharmān parityajya
mām ekaṁ śaraṇaṁ vraja
ahaṁ tvāṁ sarva-pāpebhyo
mokṣayiṣyāmi mā śucaḥ

[Bg. 18.66]

[Abandon all varieties of religion and just surrender unto Me. I shall deliver you from all sinful reactions. Do not fear.]

‘Abandon all varieties of religion and just surrender to me, I shall protect you from all sinful reactions, do not fear.’ And Srimad-Bhagavatam echoes the same conclusion of all the great seers of truth.

dharmaḥ svanuṣṭhitaḥ puṁsāṁ
viṣvaksena-kathāsu yaḥ
notpādayed yadi ratiṁ
śrama eva hi kevalam

[SB. 1.2.8]

[The occupational activities a man performs according to his own position are only so much useless labor if they do not provoke attraction for the message of the Personality of Godhead.]

Whatever religion you have, whatever occupation you perform, if in the end, it does not culminate in pure loving devotion to the Supreme Lord, śrama eva hi kevalam [SB. 1.2.8] you have wasted your life.

Qualities of lovers of God

When we are speaking of religion, we are speaking of Sanatan dharma which means our nature, our inherent essence is to know God, to love God and to serve God in that spirit of love. So, one may ask, what about the rest of humanity? If we're all sitting in the temple and just doing our puja to God, what about the problems of the world? What about everybody else? Aha… But Bhagavad-gita describes, what is the consciousness of a person, who knows and love God,

Because he sees the lovable object of his life, that part of that beautif… of God is within the heart of everyone.

Real problem is lack of compassion

Theoretical to practical

There is a saying, ‘If you are not a part of the solution, you are a part of the problem.’ Aha… Now every one of us, as human beings must sincerely question within our heart, the integrity of our existence. According to the Brahma-Sutra, the summary study of all the Vedic literatures. The first aphorism is athāto brahma jijṣāsā [Vedānta-sūtra 1.1.1]. That now that you have attained this valuable human form of life, you have one particular and specific duty that must be accomplished. And that is to realize God, to inquire into the nature of brahman, the Supreme Lord. Religion is not simply a theoretical subject matter to discuss. Religion is not a matter of argument to see who can defeat the other by word-jugglery and philosophical gymnastics. God, indifferent to language

One time, when I was in America giving a lecture, one Christian, very faithful Christian, he was very angry,
“Why you say the name Krishna?” I asked him, “In your Christian religion, do you accept that God is all attractive.” What does it mean to be all attractive? Parasara Muni describes,

aiśvaryasya samagrasya
