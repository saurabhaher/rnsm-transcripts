nāhaṁ vedair na tapasā
na dānena na cejyayā
śakya evaṁ-vidho draṣṭuṁ
dṛṣṭavān asi māṁ yathā

[Bg. 11.53]

The form you are seeing with your transcendental eyes cannot be understood simply by studying the Vedas, nor by undergoing serious penances, nor by charity, nor by worship. It is not by these means that one can see Me as I am.

This shloka is from the eleventh chapter, text 53 from Bhagavad-gita

Purport by His Divine Grace Abhaycharanarvrinda Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada

Kṛṣṇa first appeared before His parents Devakī and Vasudeva in a four-handed form, and then He transformed Himself into the two-handed form. This mystery is very difficult to understand for those who are atheists or who are devoid of devotional service. For scholars who have simply studied Vedic literature by way of grammatical knowledge or mere academic qualifications, Kṛṣṇa is not possible to understand. Nor is He to be understood by persons who officially go to the temple to offer worship. They make their visit, but they cannot understand Kṛṣṇa as He is. Kṛṣṇa can be understood only through the path of devotional service, as explained by Kṛṣṇa Himself in the next verse.

bhaktyā tv ananyayā śakya
aham evaṁ-vidho ’rjuna
jṣātuṁ draṣṭuṁ ca tattvena
praveṣṭuṁ ca paran-tapa

[Bg. 11.54]

My dear Arjuna, only by undivided devotional service can I be understood as I am, standing before you, and can thus be seen directly. Only in this way can you enter into the mysteries of My understanding.

Lecture Transcription

A temple is a very sanctified place

This coming Tuesday, we will be celebrating for the pleasure of the Lordships Shri Shri Radha Gopinath. The most blessed and sacred occasion of Janamashtami. We would like all of you to kindly come, Monday and Tuesday in the evenings. We will have very beautiful ceremonies. And very kind guests have also agreed to come to help to make this a wonderful occasion. Some people, they wonder; if God is everywhere and if God is in everything, then why do you celebrate his appearance? He is always appearing. Krishna says in Gita, īśvaraḥ sarva-bhūtānāṁ
hṛd-deśe ’rjuna tiṣṭhati [Bg. 18.61] That, ‘I am seated in the heart of every living being.’ It is also explained that, the super soul exists everywhere. For one who sees me everywhere and everything in me, for him I am never lost and he is never lost to me. So just from the question of logic, here, I am sitting, if I’m sitting here before you, if someone says, “Well Radhanath Swami will appear at 2 O’clock.” You will think what is this appear, he is already sitting here. Does it make sense? So if God is always everywhere. He is always standing before us, why do we celebrate his appearance? This verse of Bhagavad-gita explains that Janmashtami is not simply a celebration to commemorate the historical appearance of Shri Krishna, when he incarnated on this earth to display his divine pastimes five thousand years ago. It is a fact, that five thousand years back on the occasion of Janmashtami, Shri Krishna appeared in his original transcendental form as the son of Devaki and Vasudev, to display his divine pastimes for the purification of all the world. This spirit of Janmashtami, why did Krishna appear? He did not appear simply to umm… establish a particular historical event to be celebrated. Everyone is stuck, go through the altar. Haa.. You see in Kaliyuga our mind is always being distracted. Ha? So therefore we should try to create as far as possible a peaceful and sacred atmosphere in which there is as little distraction as possible. That is why we come to the temple. After all, in our own home we have a temple, but in our home there are so many distractions. Everything is reminding us of our body. Right? This belongs to my baby, this belongs to my child, this belongs to my husband. Maybe I should cook now, maybe I should do this. Ha? And when we leave our home there’s traffic, there’s business, there’s all sorts of everything. So it is necessary to have a beautiful temple by which we can come to hear the glories of the lord; and to chant the glories of the lord without any distraction. Ha? The temple is meant to be a very, very sanctified atmosphere where the whole world can come, and very easily in the association of devotees, because if everyone comes for the common purpose of glorifying God there is no distraction amongst themselves. So therefore it is very important as a service to all of you that we create an atmosphere where there are not drills going and fans blowing and all kinds of distractions to take your mind away from the message of God, of Krishna consciousness.

The real spirit of Janmashtami

So we are talking about this most beautiful appearance of Shri Krishna. We have the birthday of the President; Ha? Just like in America we have so many birthdays of Presidents. There’s so many Presidents, there’s George Washingtons’ birthday, there’s Abraham Lincolns’ birthday, so many birthdays. And people just celebrate-yes he historically appeared at this time. And it ended up there was so many Presidents and there were so many days off school, because we have to celebrate, that they just decided, that let’s just lump them all into one and call it Presidents Day; and then we’ll just have one day off for your school for all the Presidents. Ha? Such a personal gesture of love for each and every one of these souls. Now it’s just a day off where you say, “Yes, this is the day when all the Presidents were celebrated.” So historically Krishna did appear. Yes, but why did he appear? He appeared for the purpose of establishing the goal of life, in the hearts of all living beings. Although he is everywhere and he is in everything, still he specially took birth in this world, to attract our minds back to his lotus feet, so that we could see him everywhere and in everything. So that we could serve him, and love him everywhere and in everything. The real spirit of Janmashtami, is to come together to learn the science of purifying our hearts, so that Krishna will actually appear to us. The fact is, he has appeared. In the form of Shri Shri Radha Gopinath, Krishna is personally standing before us. Then why is it, that when we look at them our hairs do not stand on end? Tears do not flow from our eyes. Our heart does not swell and overflow with an incessant volcano of love. It is because we are not seeing Krishna, although he is right there. Theoretically with our good sentiments we are accepting and they stand philosophy that this is God standing before me. But are we actually seeing the full beauty that is being manifested? Are we able to do so? Krishna reveals himself according to how you approach him. According to the attitude of service- devotional service, Krishna reveals himself. We do not approach Krishna for our pleasure, we approach Krishna for his pleasure. To the extent that is our consciousness, to that extent Krishna reveals himself to us.

Devaki’s service to Krishna

Look at the example of Devaki and Vasudev. In order to have Krishna as their child, Krishna put them through a situation where they had to suffer the loss of six consecutive children, who they were very much as a mother and father, loving with great care. Sometimes we see how when a mother or father loses a child, even the most great devotees, there’s tears of love that are umm.. shed due to the loss of that dear and precious child. Mother Devaki nursed these children nine months each, within her womb, with great expectation of raising, caring for and loving these children. And each one of them right before her own eyes was brutally murdered by Kamsa. But Devaki, she continued to have children. Why? If you knew your child would be killed, would you have another one? Has anyone ever asked, why she kept having children? She saw the first one killed and yet she went and had a second one, knowing it would be killed. She had the second one killed, she saw with her own eyes and the third and the fourth. Do you know what an incredible agonizing tragedy it was in her life, to nurse this child for nine months and then see it killed? After the fifth child, she went and had another one. Do you think she was having all these children because she could not control her senses and there was no family planning in those days? Ha? What do you think? No. She had perfect control of her senses. She was not the slightest bit into asking the pleasures of coming together with a man. She knew the plan of God. She knew that Krishna the Supreme Personality of Godhead would appear as her eighth son. Therefore, as a service to Krishna she had to have all these children, even though she had to physically, emotionally, suffer the loss of these children. Don’t think she did not cry and weep. She did. Bhaktas are not hard hearted people, bhaktas are very sensitive and soft hearted people. But she was willing to go through all this pain and agony to prepare the way for Krishna’s divine appearance in this world. It was the prophecy and the will of God that the eighth son, so she had to have child after child after child. Another words she manifested total selflessness, a total giving of herself for the pleasure and the mission in the will of God. Most people approach God- what can you do for me? Most people approach the guru and the sadhus- what can you do for me? And even when they offer something to Krishna, or offer something to the guru or the sadhu, they’re thinking, ‘I will enjoy giving it to you.’ Ha? But Devaki was not at all thinking like this. For the will of Krishna to serve him, she was willing to selflessly go through what is, I think you will all agree, the most painful conditions of life. You who are parents, tell me, is there anything that you can imagine more painful than seeing your child brutally murdered right before your eyes? If you lose your business, if you lose your reputation, if you lose your home, does that even compare to such a painful condition of life? What do you think? There’s nothing more painful. Devaki was willing to willingly accept this pain six times consecutively, because this was her service to Krishna.

We should go to the temple in proper consciousness

Therefore, Devaki exemplified to us what is really Janmashtami meaning. Ha? Did you ever think of Janmashtami in these terms? You were just thinking of Oh wow! We will see the abhishek and have a nice feast at midnight and enjoy. Ha? But let us see Janmashtami from the point of view of Devaki, because it was from her womb that Krishna appeared. And the spirit of Janmashtami is to invite Krishna to appear within our own life; within the womb of our own consciousness. Is it not? In order for Krishna to appear within the womb of our consciousness, we have to become like Devaki. We have to be willing to be the selfless servant of the lord. This is the meaning of bhakti.

Srila Prabhupada explains in his purport from the eleventh chapter of Bhagavad-gita. Hmmm!! Nor is Krishna to be understood by persons who officially go to the temple to offer worship. They make their visit, but they cannot understand Krishna as he is. Krishna can be understood only through the path of devotional service, as explained by Krishna himself in the next verse. So when we come to the temple, we will receive what we put into our visit. Krishna reveals himself as we approach him. If we come because it’s fun, then we will get some fun and we will also get purified little by little. If we come because we are afraid not to come, because people will think why did he not come. Ha? Then people will not think why he did not come, that will be the result [Maharaja laughs] And there will be some purification. But why should we really come to the temple if we want to maximumly utilize our human form of life. After all we only have a few minutes to live. You may say, “Why a few minutes? I have so many years.” Ha.. All of our years of life, they pass in what appears to be only a few minutes. Think back, now we are, some of us are seventy-five years old here today. How old is your father? [devotee:] Seventy-four [Maharaja:] Seventy-four. Some of us are fifty, sixty, forty. If you look back at your life, it appears that everything passed in a few minutes. Does it not? And as you grow older your life goes faster and faster and faster. So how to utilize these precious moments. When we come to the temple, we should come to the temple in proper consciousness. We should come in the consciousness with Devaki, illustrated by her life. We should be coming in the consciousness that, Krishna and Krishna’s devotees. I am coming to offer, offer my services to them.

When we are chanting the holy name, we are chanting as an offering of service for the pleasure of the lord; and for the pleasure of the lord’s devotees. When we sit and hear the class, we should not simply be thinking, ‘Yes, I enjoyed the class today.’ We should be sacrificing the precious time of our life as an offering to Krishna, by offering our ears, our mind, our intelligence, our heart, to hear his transcendental glories for his pleasure, and also for the pleasure of the devotees. Because each one of you, who is attentive contributes to the sanctity of the atmosphere which purifies everyone else in the room. When your mind is wandering, roaming, dancing from place to place, you are creating a turmoil within the atmosphere. But better you stay and do that. But if you really want to offer service as hard as you possibly can, you will open your heart, your ears and try to listen. Not because it’s fun; but as an offering of service to Krishna and to his devotees. And when we come before the beautiful form of the Archavigraha- the deity, Shri Shri Radha Gopinath, we should not simply be thinking, ‘Oh Krishna! you are enjoying, you are so beautiful today. And Srimati Radharani, how much I am enjoying how beautiful you are today.’ We should come before the deities to be enjoyed by them by offering our love to them, by offering our prayers, by offering our service, whatever we have, our mind, intelligence, whatever we can give. If we approach the deity in this way, Krishna does not need our money, he needs our love. Krishna does not need our intelligence. He does not need our managerial capacity. He does not need our influence over others. He has everything, but he needs our love and these are simply forms in which we can express our love to the Lord. This is the real spirit of Janmashtami. And when we are willing to dedicate our lives for the pleasure of Krishna, in the service of Krishna, then we have captured the great lesson of Devaki. And then, Janmashtami will have a real meaning to our life. Janmashtami will not just be a function that we attend, but it will be a experience of enlightenment, spiritual enrichment. So we should prepare ourselves in this consciousness.

Service attitude is the essence of spiritual life

bhaktyā tv ananyayā śakya
aham evaṁ-vidho ’rjuna
jṣātuṁ draṣṭuṁ ca tattvena
praveṣṭuṁ ca paran-tapa

[Bg. 11.54]

[My dear Arjuna, only by undivided devotional service can I be understood as I am, standing before you, and can thus be seen directly. Only in this way can you enter into the mysteries of My understanding.]

Krishna says that, only by undivided devotion can I be understood as I am, standing here before you. Krishna is standing before all of us right now. But he can only be understood as he is when he reveals himself by his own causeless mercy, in reciprocation with our devotional service. So this service attitude is the essence of spiritual life.

bahūnāṁ janmanām ante
jṣānavān māṁ prapadyate
vāsudevaḥ sarvam iti
sa mahātmā su-durlabhaḥ

[Bg. 7.19]

[After many births and deaths, he who is actually in knowledge surrenders unto Me, knowing Me to be the cause of all causes and all that is. Such a great soul is very rare.]

After many births and deaths of practicing the various systems of yoga, the various religions of studying the various philosophies of the world. After many births and deaths of such endeavors, one when finally comes to the point of real knowledge, one surrenders to Vasudev, knowing him to be the cause of all causes and all that is. This verse is very interesting. Because Krishna himself is speaking and he says vāsudevaḥ sarvam iti, sa mahātmā su-durlabhaḥ After many births and deaths of passing through all situations of life, one when finally comes to the point of knowledge, he surrenders to Vasudev, knowing him to be the cause of all causes and all that is sa mahātmā su-durlabhaḥ such a great soul is very rare. Krishna specifically calls himself by the name Vasudev. Vasudev is Krishna’s father. Krishna is named Vasudev because he took birth from Vasudev. And one who realizes Krishna, it is said that he has attained the Vasudev platform. Krishna appeared to Vasudev, why? Vasudev shared the same spirit as Devaki. What was the great sacrifice of Vasudev? We have discussed Devaki. Most people want a child so that they can enjoy it. Ha? Vasudev willingly arranged six children that he had to see killed. And each one was a male. And you know how much fathers are attached to male children here in India. Ha? This is Mathura and Mathura is part of India [Maharaja laughs]. So you can understand the natural affection Vasudev had for these children. And he was willing to experience all this pain because it was a service to Krishna, to help arrange for Krishna to appear for the rest of the world to enjoy, to be benefited by and to be enlightened by.

Sometimes you are willing to undergo tremendous amount of austerity, if you know that you will get something in the future. Right? If I know that I could have such a good son, then I’m willing to watch all these other things take place. But Vasudev, even after going through all of that, when Krishna finally did appear, he didn’t even, he wasn’t even attached to keeping Krishna. Krishna said, “The moment I appear Vasudev, take me across the Yamuna, bring me to the house of Nand Maharaja, there I will stay. You will be childless, living in the prison for the rest of the time.” Did Vasudev complain? Did he say, ‘My dear Krishna, do you know what I went through to have you as a child? Do you know all the suffering and pain? If it wasn’t for you coming as my child, I wouldn’t even have to be in prison today. It is only because of the prophecy of you, that I am here. It is only because of you that I lost my six children. And now, I finally have you and you are not even going to allow me to keep you for one hour. You want me to immediately take you to somebody else?’ Did he complain like that? No. When he was given the order by Narayan – that when I am born you take me to Nanda, then you come back and live in this jail. When he was given that order, Vasudev, with folded palms, he bowed his head, “Yes Krishna – Let your will be done. My life and soul is for your pleasure.” Ha? When we come to that consciousness, where we are willing to give everything for Krishna and expect nothing in return, then we are in the Vasudev platform. Then Krishna appears to us. So Krishna says vāsudevaḥ sarvam iti
sa mahātmā su-durlabhaḥ [Bg. 7.19] After many births and deaths, one when finally comes to the point of knowledge he surrenders to Vasudev. He surrenders to the mood of Vasudev. So he may be thinking, Vasudev is suffering. Externally it appears, yes, he is suffering. But because he is accepting suffering in the service for the lord. Actually internally, you should know that Krishna is reciprocating with him and giving him the purest and highest state of transcendental ecastcy with him, because he was willing to go through all of this. And he loved Krishna as his own child. After Krishna was brought to Gokul, he had to come back with the baby girl Yoga Maya. Then she flew away. He wasn’t even allowed to keep a daughter. Ha? And how much he was always worrying about Krishna. Kamsa was sending very powerful mystic Asuras through out to kill all the babies. He was always trying to make whatever enquiries –How is this little baby son of Nand Maharaja? How is he doing? What is he doing? Ha? How is he growing up? He could not even see his own child being brought up. What a pleasure it is for a father, the one son that was born, he wasn’t even allowed to see for ten years. But he was willing to accept that position because Krishna was enjoying. And when he would hear how Krishna was enjoying in Vrindavan, Gokul, he was so happy.

Unconditional trust and faith in the divine will of God

So how much are we willing to give for the pleasure of God. That is the spirit of Janmashtami. It is when we capture this spirit, do not think it is simply all pain, but this is the extreme in the extent that Vasudev and Devaki gave us an example of what it means to be a selfless servant, what it means, what is the qualification of Krishna to appear within our life? Ha? It is not that you may necessarily have to go through all of that. Actually Krishna consciousness is simply blissful. Chanting, dancing, feasting. It is wonderful. But we have to capture that same spirit. We have to be willing to go through all those things for the pleasure of Krishna, if it is his will. He may not make you go. He may or may not, that is his will. He may have everything go just your way, very nice. If that is his will we accept. Unconditional trust and faith in the divine will of God, that is what we learn from Vasudev and Devaki. Ha? When we come to the temple, with this feeling, ‘Krishna how can I serve you? Krishna how can I make you happy?’ Of course Krishna says mad-bhakta-pūjābhyadhikā [SB 11.19.21] If you want to make me happy, you have to make my devotees happy. Lord Krishna told Udava “He who calls himself my devotee is not my devotee”. Hmm? Sometimes we are very proud, ‘I am Krishna’s devotee.’ If you’re saying like that, according to Krishna you’re not his devotee. Krishna says he who calls himself the devotee of my devotee, he is my devotee. So by coming to please Shri Radha Gopinath, we must come with a desire to please the lord’s devotees, to serve the lord’s devotees. We should not come to the temple for our pleasure. We should come to the temple for Guru and Krishna’s pleasure. Then we will come whether we feel like it or not. Ha? That is the proper consciousness by which we approach Krishna. Janmashtami is most glorious. Actually it is the most illuminating and enlightening night of the year, before you approach Krishna as his divine, as his humble servant. Ha? Then Krishna will not only appear as a historical reference to our mind; but he will actually appear within our life on that night. He is everywhere, he is in everything and he is anxious to reveal himself, though we have to open our hearts in order to accept his divine mercy. Is there any questions?

Why does Krishna come to this world?

So I would like to announce, that by the divine mercy of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, he has appeared in the form of Shreenathji. Beautiful murti of Shreenathji and beautiful murti of Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu and Lord Nityananda Prabhu. They have come to our temple here, Radha Gopinath mandir and I cannot even try to explain the beauty and the glory of the mercy of Krishna in these wonderful forms. Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu and Lord Nityananda Prabhu, they are the most merciful of all incarnations, because they are freely giving love of Krishna, without discrimination to everyone, through the divine process of chanting the holy names. And Shri Gopal, Shreenathji is the most celebrated lifter of Govardhan hill. Krishna lifted the Govardhan hill to protect his devotees from otherwise destruction; and also to exhibit to the world, that this beautiful little cowherd boy, playing with his friends is the supreme absolute truth, the God of all Gods. So they will be appearing on October nineteenth, which is the day of Govardhan Puja, Annakuta. So it’s the most auspicious day of the whole year for Shreenathji Gopal, because he was the lifter of Govardhan. So on the day that he lifts the Govardhan hill he will appear to lift the hill right before our very eyes. So we’re making very great preparations for this wonderful ceremony. The preparation is both internally as well as externally, so please keep this on your calendars. I know it is close to Diwali and it is the time you visit your relatives and everything else, but your most important relative is your eternal father. Ha.. So you should also visit the house of your eternal father on that day, especially since it’s his birthday haa..[Maharaja laughs] He is appearing on that day. Krishna is so kind, he appears in so many wonderful ways out of his causeless mercy, only to attract our minds back to his loving service hmmm. That is why he comes to this world.

paritrāṇāya sādhūnāṁ
vināśāya ca duṣkṛtām
dharma-saṁsthāpanārthāya
sambhavāmi yuge yuge

[Bg. 4.8]

[To deliver the pious and to annihilate the miscreants, as well as to reestablish the principles of religion, I Myself appear, millennium after millennium.]

Krishna says I appear to annihilate the miscreants, to deliver the pious, to re-establish the principles of religion. All he has to do is take his sudarshan chakra and just cut a few veins in their hearts and they will die. Right? No problem for him. To re-establish the principles of religion, he can do that through his devotees. That’s usually how he does it anyway. To deliver the pious - He can deliver the pious through the agency of material nature if he wants. mayādhyakṣeṇa prakṛtiḥ, sūyate sa-carācaram [Bg. 9.10]. He is the controller of this nature. Well, he can deliver the pious through his empowered representatives, his devotees. Or he could deliver the pious invisibly just by controlling things in such a way. So why really does he come to this world? These are all the external reasons. The internal reason is to attract the minds of the world to his lotus feet and to give pleasure to the minds of his devotees. Ha?

So Krishna is appearing in so many wonderful ways. Let us allow our minds to be attracted to his lotus feet. That is the greatest perfection of our life. Now our minds are being so much obsessed with so many of the dualities and the duplicities of this world. You only get so caught up in these things- this person hates me. Oh! My God what am I? This person is trying to destroy me. This person’s trying to help me. Aw! I love this person; this person I don’t love. Ha? And how will I make this money and how will I maintain my family and how will I keep my health and what about at any mid-day there might be a war. Ha? Our minds are constantly being distracted to the temporary illusory forms of this world. And then, in this way we waste our whole life for naught, for nothing. Therefore, Krishna appears within his name. He appears within his devotees, he appears within the deity, in so many beautiful and wonderful ways. It’s like Krishna is in his name and yet there are so many wonderful melodies and so many wonderful songs, so many wonderful kirtans, so many wonderful bhajans. In this way his name takes so many forms just to attract our minds. Krishna appears in his devotees and there are so many different types of devotees. Some are heavy, ah! surrender, some are very sweet, some are very different types. All of them according to you he is appearing in different devotees just to attract our minds. And Krishna appears in so many wonderful forms as Lord Chaitanya, Lord Nityananda – dancing. As Radha and Krishna playing on their flutes, standing before in a most loving benevolent manner. He appears as Shreenathji, in his beautiful blackish form, as the lifter of Govardhan hill. In the form of just a little tiny baby, not a baby but just a little child of seven years old. Krishna is so mercifully coming in so many ways just to attract our minds to his lotus feet. He also appears as Prasad. Ha? Prasad is non-different than Krishna – Mahaprasadam. And how prasadam appears in so many ways. Some people like sweets, some people like sabji, some people like samosas haa.. He appears in all these ways just to attract your mind back to him. Haa!! So let us take this prasadam for full satisfaction and glorify the Lord Shri Shri Radha and Krishna and in love cry upon the help of Lord Chaitanya and Lord Nityananada. Thank you very much.
