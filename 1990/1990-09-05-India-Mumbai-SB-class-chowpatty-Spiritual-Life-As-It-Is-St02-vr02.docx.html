1990 - 095 (SB.8.10.1-6)

Srimad Bhagavatam (8.10.1-6)

TAGS: Spiritual life as it is (Conflict Resolution, Peace Formula, Complacency, and Seriousness)

(1)Sri Suka uvaca

Iti danava daiteya

Navindann amrtam nrpa

Yuktah karmani yattas ca

Vasudeva paranmukhah

[Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: O King, the demons and Daityas all engaged with full attention and effort in churning the ocean, but because they were not devotees of Vāsudeva, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Kṛṣṇa, they were not able to drink the nectar.]

(2) Sadhayitvamrtam rajan

payayitva svakan suran

pasyatam sarva bhutanam

yayan garuda vahanah

[O King, after the Supreme Personality of Godhead had brought to completion the affairs of churning the ocean and feeding the nectar to the demigods, who are His dear devotees, He left the presence of them all and was carried by Garuḍa to His own abode.]

(3) Sapatnanam param rddhim

drstva te diti-nandanah,

amrsyamana utpetur

devan pratyudyatayudhah

[Seeing the victory of the demigods, the demons became intolerant of their superior opulence. Thus they began to march toward the demigods with raised weapons.]

(4) tatah sura-ganah sarve

sudhaya pitayaidhitah

pratisamyuyudhuh sastrair

narayana padasrayah

[Thereafter, being enlivened because of drinking the nectar, the demigods, who are always at the shelter of the lotus feet of Nārāyaṇa, used their various weapons to counterattack the demons in a fighting spirit.]

(5) tatra daivasuro nama

ranah parama-darunah

rodhasy udanvato rajams

tumulo roma-harsanah

[O King, a fierce battle on the beach of the Ocean of Milk ensued between the demigods and the demons. The fighting was so terrible that simply hearing about it would make the hair on one’s body stand on end.]

(6) tatranyonyam sapatnas te

samrabdha-manaso rane

samasadyasibhir banair

nijaghnur vividhayudhaih

[Both parties in that fight were extremely angry at heart, and in enmity they beat one another with swords, arrows and varieties of other weapons.]

PURPORT

There are always two kinds of men in this universe, not only on this planet but also in higher planetary systems. All the kings dominating planets like the sun and moon also have enemies like Rahu. It is because of occasional attacks upon the sun and moon by Rahu that eclipses take place. The fighting between the demigods and the demons is perpetual; it cannot be stopped unless intelligent persons from both sides take to Krishna consciousness.

Krishna - The Supreme Enjoyer

There is one Supreme God, and that is Krishna and peace within this world can only come about when we accept this simple fact

bhoktāraṁ yajṣa-tapasāṁ

sarva-loka-maheśvaram

suhṛdaṁ sarva-bhūtānāṁ

jṣātvā māṁ śāntim ṛcchati

[A person in full consciousness of Me, knowing Me to be the ultimate beneficiary of all sacrifices and austerities, the Supreme Lord of all planets and demigods, and the benefactor and well-wisher of all living entities, attains peace from the pangs of material miseries.] [BG 5.29]

In the fifth chapter of Bhagavad Gita Krishna explains that this is the peace formula that he is the one Supreme Lord, who is the proprietor of everything and everything is meant for his enjoyment. He is our friend. That faith is the basic principle of peace. Without that faith, without putting this into action, there is no possibility of peace.

Proprietorship - Cause of all Conflicts

We find in this world, not only in this world but even in the world of upper and lower planets, that there is always conflict and enmity within the same family. Amongst children there is never peace. Children are always thinking this is mine. No, no this is mine. I want to do that, no, I want to do that. Whether India, America, I travelled all over the world many times, and one thing I found was very consistent that brothers and sisters are always fighting and husband and wife are always fighting. Friends and friends potentially at any time they are fighting. What to speak of competitors and people of different religions, different nations, different isms. Look at the wars in the word today practically everyone is fighting. In the Middle East there is so much fighting. In South East Asia there is so much fighting. In America there is so much fighting. In Africa there is so much fighting. In Russia there is so much fighting. In India there is so much fighting. In China there is so much fighting. Is there any place in the world where there is not such fighting? Can you think of anywhere? Ha..Ha.., doesn’t exist! Because everyone wants to be the enjoyer. Everyone wants to be the controller. Everyone wants to be the proprietor. Therefore, the only solution is to surrender to the fact that I am simply the servant. Krishna is proprietor of everything. That is Krishna Consciousness. Krishna Consciousness is very simple. Krishna Consciousness means to simply admit and accept that everything belongs to Krishna, and everything is meant for his satisfaction. Nothing is mine – ‘nirmam’ nothing is mine. As soon as we claim proprietorship over anything, we create potential conflict in our lives between other living beings. That is why Krishna says in Bhagavad Gita that one who is detached from material enjoyment and attached to the service of his lotus feet, he is the friend of every one and everyone is the friend of him.

But we see even amongst devotees this conflict takes place. Amongst neophyte devotees, even amongst apparently very advanced devotees. This material existence is a place of conflict and unless we become extremely transcendental, extremely selfless, it is very difficult to be aloof from the greed of this world and here we are finding Srila Prabhupada is explaining that there are always two classes of men. There is always the demons and there is always the devotees. In the spiritual world they do not exist, demons. But in this material world, from the highest planet to the lowest planet there is always the conflict of demons and devotees. Sometimes we are thinking, when the war will end, then there will be some relief. The war will never end until you are out of this material world altogether. There may be some retreat of the enemy for some time. But you should know that when the enemy is retreating – Maya is the enemy – she is simply remobilizing her forces for a bigger and better attack.

Always Remember Krishna Never Forget Krishna

Sometimes we think everything is alright now. Just see there is no problem, very nice, I am Krishna Conscious. Now the demons have subsided their attack so I can live peacefully. Do not fall into this illusion. It may appear peaceful now but that only means Maya is figuring out a better way to attack. Right? Just like the demons and the demigods. Whenever the demigods conquer the demons, the demons are always in the back somewhere invisible where they are make…. planning a bigger and better attack and this been going on from the beginning of creation and doesn’t end until the destruction of the entire universe by Lord Shiva. Even look at the Pandavas, even after they defeated the Kurus in the battlefield of Kurukshetra, they had such a massive victory; Yudhishtira was coronated as king and everyone was very happy and thinking that ah, alas, finally everything is alright and then Ashvatthama throws a Brahmastra to kill the only surviving heir of the throne, Maharaj Parikshit, another attack even after their victory. So you see, victory in Krishna consciousness means not that the war ends. Victory in Krishna consciousness means that moment after moment we are turning to Krishna. Moment after moment we are surrendering our lives to the will of our spiritual master, that is victory.

Greatest Enemy- Complacency

The greatest illusion is to think that we are safe, to think that everything is alright. Srila Prabhupada, he explains like a goat who is eating grasses but he is on his way into the slaughter house. He thinks everything is very nice but he doesn’t know that the nice green grasses are only leading him closer and closer and closer to his death. So the pleasures of this world are like that, only leading us closer and closer to pain, misery and suffering. So therefore, a devotee never becomes complacent, that is our worst enemy. Krishna tells Arjuna in the third chapter of Gita, “give up this lethargy and fight.” One of the greatest enemies for Krishna consciousness is complacency. To think that things are alright and then we become very casual in our relationship with Krishna. Yes Krishna you are very nice. You are very kind. You are giving me so many nice things.

Urgency to become Serious

So many times, people come to me and say, “Krishna is so kind to me, he is giving me everything I like.” But Krishna says –

yasyāham anugṛhṇāmi hariṣye tad-dhanaṁ śanaiḥ

[Srimad Bhagavatam 10.88.08]

[The Personality of Godhead said: If I especially favor someone, I gradually deprive him of his wealth. Then the relatives and friends of such a poverty-stricken man abandon him. In this way he suffers one distress after another.]

‘If you really want to see how kind I can be, wait till I take everything away….hahaha…. Then you will really understand my kindness. Now you do not understand what is my kindness, you do not understand what is kindness.’ Because this is the tendency of every conditioned soul. The tendency is that we want to be the enjoyer. That we want to just…even in Krishna consciousness we just want to say yes, yes, everything is pretty good now, now I can enjoy. Sometimes we see devotees, when everything is going pretty well, they take the advantage, they take undue advantage of their own God brothers and God sisters. In complacency sometimes they even criticize. They find fault. They are not so enthusiastic to surrender and obey their authority. But they just feel, Krishna Conscious, ‘I know who is Krishna, I know the books, I will do what I like to do. My authority is telling me to do this, but I know what it means to be Krishna conscious. I do what I want to do. I will go where I want to go. It is not such an important thing to surrender. After all I am pretty happy; I am pretty stable in my spiritual life.’ But then when some disasters, when some crises come into one’s life, and it’s beyond our control, there is pain, there is suffering, something dear to us is taken away, ah, and then we become very serious and we understand that I cannot make it on my own. Like a leaf separated from a tree I am simply being pushed by the strong wind of the material energy this way and that way. So in such a situation, although pain in the heart is there, we become very serious. I want to surrender. I want to obey. I want to glorify and serve my God brothers and God sisters. I… worse than death itself committing any offence to these devotees. That is the great mercy of the Lord. So, this great conflict we are reading about in the pages of Srimad Bhagavatam this morning. The demigods ha…just see, they had just gone through the great great sacrifice. The painful efforts of churning the ocean of milk and the goal of churning the ocean of milk was to get nectar, because with that nectar they will become very powerful and very happy. So they were victorious. They churned the ocean and by the mystical arrangement of Mohini Murty – the incarnation of Godhead – She gave all the nectar to them not to demons. So the demigods were celebrating, how wonderful. But how long that celebration last? Almost immediately after the demons marched upon them with all their raised weapons and it describes; very very severe war took place and it is described here, that such a war, that if anybody hears about it their hairs will stand on end. So, the demigods their celebration did not last very long. But because they drank the nectar, they are going to win this war and they are going to get their whole kingdom back and they are going to enjoy like anything. But we are going to read little later in Bhagavatam about how Bali Maharaj, he performs great sacrifice, and he pleases the Brahmans and, in this way, he gets tremendous potency and they will march again on the demigods and conquer them and take everything away and again demigods were without any thing. So, it is always back and forth, there is always this conflict, there is always this war.

So do not pray for the war to end. Pray that we may end the war within our own hearts. We may make peace with God within our own hearts. We may be his messenger of peace within this world. That is all that is important. You cannot change the world but you can change yourself. You can change individuals of this world, as did Prabhupada, as did Chaitanya Mahaprabhu. But the principle of the nature of this world of the demons and the devotees conflict can never be changed. Material existence is eternal in its principle of conflict but you can change your own heart and out of great compassion on behalf of Gurudev you can reach out to change the hearts of others and victory in life simply means to surrender to this principle alone. In preaching Krishna consciousness whether you are successful or not successful, you are successful. The victory is simply in the attempt, the victory is in the effort, the victory is in the devotion, not the result. So, we must become very sincere and understand the real battle is this internal battle against our own lethargy and our own pride. Pride that we are the enjoyer and the laziness, the complacency, that is such a strong force within us. Therefore, devotees must work very very hard, day and night, this is the real battle. The real battle is to always be engaged in Krishna’s service. If you are always engaged in Gurudev’s service and Krishna’s service, then you are victorious, moment after moment for your whole life through.

Real Victory in Krishna Consciousness

Just like Socrates, when they caught Socrates, they apprehended him. They imprisoned him. They made a special poison potion out of hemlock leaves and they told him, you drink this and very soon you will die. He said, “before you can kill me, first you have to catch me.” They were looking at him like what is this crazy – catch me – you are imprisoned in our chain, what are you talking of catching. Because he understood ‘I am not this body. You cannot catch my soul.’ The soul cannot be cut into pieces by any weapon, cannot be withered by the wind, cannot be moistened by water. The soul is transcendental. So the real battle is the battle between the bodily conception and the spiritual conception. This is the battle within our own hearts. The battle between the senses saying ‘serve me’ and Krishna saying ‘serve me’ and Krishna speaks through Guru, Sadhu and Shastra. Through the spiritual master, through the sadhu and through the scriptures He is saying, “this is how you should serve me” and our mind and senses are saying no, no, no, like this.

If you always engage faithfully in Krishna’s service, in the Spiritual Master’s service, then you are always victorious. Whatever happens externally is not so important. The real important thing is that our faith and our devotion – are always loyal to Krishna - that is the real victory. That is why it is said ‘die to live’. We must put aside every other conception in life. We must forget about changing the world, waiting for the world to change and we must be very aggressive, very enthusiastic to change our own lives, to change our own hearts, to help our spiritual master to change the hearts of each and every individual we come in contact with. That is victory. Ultimately you have to die. Does that mean that you have been conquered? ‘Die to live’ means if you are in this consciousness – that we are speaking Krishna Consciousness – then dying means entering into eternal life. If you are not in this Consciousness, dying means the ultimate defeat in the hands of material nature.

Therefore, let us take advantage and let us take shelter of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who is the Paramatma seated within our heart. Who is the witness, knower and friend? Who is arranging our life in such a way that through happiness, distress, honor, dishonor, pleasure, pain, we are always given the perfect opportunity to be victorious by remembering him and enthusiastically engaging in his loving service? So let us take shelter of the Lord and let us take shelter of his devotees. Let us create a Vaikuntha atmosphere, by avoiding by all means, the potential conflict that is between us. This is what it means to be the humble servant that when the conflicting forces of Maya come between God brothers or God sisters, we are willing to say, ‘I am your servant. You are right I am wrong. Let us continue on for the sake of our guru’s pleasure.’ Can you do that? That is what we mean by ‘die to live’. We have to let our ego die and then with great great enthusiasm we must work very hard in our spiritual master’s mission. That is our victory.

Is there any question? Do you like to add anything Maharaj? It’s like Rohininandan Maharaj and…he has just… and Radha Gopinath they have just attained victory in one battle, although by all material standards it seems like a total defeat. They lost everything that they put so much energy and time into but there is no defeat, this is all material conception. Because they were enthusiastic to serve the order of Guru, they are victorious. They have gained something millions and billions and trillions of times greater than some property, han? So, this is real victory. Actually, we should have, when they came, when they came home, we should have had a …in America they have ticker tape parades when the soldiers come home after winning a war. Everyone greets them and with all sorts of honorable reception. So, when they came home, we should have actually had a nice parade for them. They have come home victorious. Victorious means that they never gave up their enthusiasm and their determination to serve the Guru. That is real victory, real victory, victory over Maya, victory over illusion.

Sometimes we become very successful and we win over our opponents in devotional service and we become proud and we become complacent that just see I have done very good. That means, is you won a battle but lost the war, han? Better to lose the battle and win the war. All of the little skirmishes that we have to confront in this world are like the little battles but the ultimate war is whether we remain enthusiastic in the service of Krishna under all circumstances. Then we have won the war against Maya.

So do not see things superficially. Do not see things externally. We must always keep our minds and our vision focused on the essence – My dear spiritual master how can I serve you now? If we always live according to that principle we are always with Guru and we are always with Krishna and Sanjaya has said, “wherever there is Krishna and wherever there is Arjuna, there is certain to be victory.” [BG 18.78]

Hare Krishna! Thank you very much. Is there any questions?

I would like to thank all of you very much for making this festival such a wonderful festival yesterday. All of you put so much time, energy, great sacrifice and hard work for the pleasure of Sri Sri Gaur Nitai, Gaurachandra, Sri Gopal Nath ji and of course all of our guests and this great enthusiasm and sacrifice that you have all offered at the lotus feet of the Lordships will never be forgotten. It is only by the mercy of all of you Vaishnavas, that the Lord has so kindly agreed to appear for the purification of the entire universe. They have appeared in such beautiful, beautiful forms, such beauty no one has ever seen and this is the Lord’s reciprocation to all of your earnest and honest efforts to serve them and please them. As we make more and more sacrifice in our life, the Lord reveals himself in more and more beautiful and wonderful ways. So, continue on in this way. More and more and more we should be giving up for Krishna. Less and less and less should we be asking for ourselves and Krishna is just giving us a little little sample of how merciful he is when he reciprocates with our sacrifices in his loving service. So, thank you very very much. I consider it was a very wonderful and auspicious festival and let us have festivals like this every day in our..if not may be so elaborate but in quality we should have festivals like this every day for the pleasure of their Lordships. Is that possible Devamrit? Is that possible? Han? Qualitatively we should have festivals like yesterday, every day. Not quantitative…elaboration is something else. But just as we worked so hard to make the arrangements for the bathing ceremony, to decorate the Temple, to take care of the guests. Just as we worked so hard with such intensity, with that same intensity we should be doing our Food for life program to serve the poor nice Prasad. With that same intensity, the medicos should go out everyday and doing their seva. With that same intensity the Pujaris should be taking nice care of the Deities and making nice offerings for them. With the same intensity the college preachers should be going out and just battling against all odds to reach even one person’s heart. With the same intensity the Sankirtan devotees and the book distributors should be struggling, working, fighting to deliver this divine message to the hearts of the conditioned souls.

So actually, Krishna consciousness, Srila Prabhupada used to say that everyday is a festival because everyday the devotees are working so much, so much sacrifice simply to please the Lord and that is this festival. That is the real festival, that we are working hard to please the Lord and the enthusiasm to execute even our most ordinary duties under the instruction of authorities, that enthusiasm is the decoration that beautifies the Temple of the heart. Just like our Matajis, they work day and night. They didn’t even sleep at night, all day, all evening, all night they were simply working, working, working to decorate the Temple. In whatever service we have been given we should work that hard to decorate the Temple of our heart with that enthusiasm. Enthusiasm is what makes our devotional service beautiful in the eyes of Krishna, whatever it maybe. Hare Krishna!
