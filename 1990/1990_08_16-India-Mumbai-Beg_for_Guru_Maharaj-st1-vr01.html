[SB 8.6.27]

Then Lord Brahmā and Lord Śiva, after offering their respectful obeisances to the Lord, returned to their abodes. All the demigods then approached Mahārāja Bali.

dṛṣṭvārīn apy asaṁyattāṣ

[SB 8.6.28]

Mahārāja Bali, a most celebrated king of the demons, knew very well when to make peace and when to fight. Thus although his commanders and captains were agitated and were about to kill the demigods, Mahārāja Bali, seeing that the demigods were coming to him without a militant attitude, forbade his commanders to kill them.

Purport

Vedic etiquette enjoins: gṛhe śatrum api prāptaṁ viśvastam akutobhayam. When enemies come to their opponent’s place, they should be received in such a way that they will forget that there is animosity between the two parties. Bali Mahārāja was well conversant with the arts of peacemaking and fighting. Thus he received the demigods very well, although his commanders and captains were agitated. This kind of treatment was prevalent even during the fight between the Pāṇḍavas and the Kurus. During the day, the Pāṇḍavas and Kurus would fight with the utmost strength, and when the day was over they would go to each other’s camps as friends and be received as such. During such friendly meetings, one enemy would offer anything the other enemy wanted. That was the system.

“Mahārāja Bali, a most celebrated king of the demons, knew very well when to make peace and when to fight. Thus although his commanders and captains were agitated and were about to kill the demigods, Mahārāja Bali, seeing that the demigods were coming to him without a militant attitude, forbade his commanders to kill them.”

Lecture Transcription

Krishna tells in the Gita that for a kshatriya dishonor is worst than death. And in the great vedic histories, we find this most highly esteemed qualities in many many great rulers of the world. Such great souls as Maharaj Kshibi, Maharaj Yudhisthir, Bhishma dev, even Duryodhana. Powerful kings who understood the great importance of dealing in the battlefield and amongst their subjects with the high sense of honor.

Dishonourable wars in kaliyuga

Of course, this sentiment is practically lost as kaliyuga progresses. The battles today are fought least in the battle field. In the battle of Kurukshetra and even in regard to this great battle between demigods and demons, we find that those who are fighting will fight with very very high sense of honour. They will fai… they would only fight with someone who is equally match, who has equal weapon. And for them to die in such a battle is far more glorious and desirable than to live on the battle field as a cheater and loosing once sense of honour. If you look at the wars going on over the past tweenty or thirty years, most of it is based on the principles of terrorism which is the most utterly dishonorable act of conduct for one who is in a kshatriya role.

During the Vietnam rom… war, most of the killing was not done soldiers facing one another and shooting but rather they would plant various traps. People would be hiding in the jungles and they… they put bombs on the road and they would… they would be have their detonators and one person would be there and then a troop of several hundred soldiers would come walking and the person in the bushes will simply press the electrical charge of the detonator and bomb will go off and all soldiers would be killed, unexpecting, unarmed, not even a fight. Or they would put various bamboo shoots in the ground, person will step on it, leg would be all torn up and they would.. would… when they would be laying there in pain. People would just come out of bushes with gun and shoot them dead. This is how most of the killing was done. When somebody was sleeping, people would…urr.. some spy would secretly plant different bombs under different buildings and then someone would push the button and every thing would blow up. And as those who survive for as they were running away, there would be people in the bushes you couldn’t even see shooting them all dead. What to speak of the innocent civilians, They would come into villages and burnt the entire village down, thinking that there might be a spy was in the village, they will kill entire villages of people.

Vedic warfare, fair warfare

We see, in the battle of Kurukshetra that there wasn’t a particular civilian who was even slightest been involved. There were only great warriors who assembled in a place completely away from any of the common people of the society. And the war was between them, no one else would involved. And they agreed on.. on terms before the war and they all agree on all written law of kshatriya honour. Of course, at the end of the battle of Kurukshetra, adharma became very prevalent and Ashvatthama, we read what he had done? While all the survivors of the wars in the Pandava army save and except five Pandavas, Krishna, Sat... Satyaki and few others they had gone to Hastinapur. The others being very much tired from fighting day and night were sleeping. At that time Ashvathamma, Kritvarma and Kripacharya killed them all in their sleep. And This was considered the most utterly dishonorable and disgraceful activity for a kshatriya. And this incident was so disgraceful, was so utterly dishonorable that even to this day five thousand years ago, we cannot forget it because such things were unheard off in the days of the real kshatriya king.

Bali Maharaj’s high sense of honour for truthfulness

But today, the wars that are going on, this is all they do. Political parties plant bombs in airplane to murder the innocent people just to prove their political position. They plant bombs in buses, they plant bombs in trains, they plant bombs in movie theatres. This way people who have nothing to do with anything women, children, old men and ladies, they destroyed, they killed and they wounded for life. So, we find as kaliyuga progresses the sense of truthfulness which is the last leg of… of religion within this age is dwindling day by day. Austerity, mercy, cleanliness and truthfulness. These are the four legs of dharma. And in kaliyuga, only one remains that is truthfulness and as we see that is becoming weaker and weaker and weaker. But here we find Bali Maharaj, although he was king of the demons, the asuras. Even so as a kshatriya, he had a very very high sense of honour. The demigods came to his place and they came in a peaceful state, with a desire to make peace. Now of course, Bali Maharaja’s associates, they were thinking that ‘This is the perfect opportunity to get rid of these demigods once and for all. They do not have a chance, they are not [indistinct] which is slaughter them all right now, never have to see their faces again.’ But Bali Maharaj forbade them to do so because for Bali Maharaj dishonor was worse than death. For Bali Maharaj, truth was higher than any other acquisition that one can attain in this world.

Extra ordinary power of the lord

Later on this story we find, toward the end of this beautiful narration that Bali Maharaj ultimately defeats all the demigods in battle and ultimately, the demigods have no one else to turn to except Krishna. At that time, Krishna appears as Lord Vaman dev and for the sake of protecting his devotees. Just see, what the Lord is willing to undergo? The Lord is willing to take the position of a beggar. Why would God beg anything from anyone? He is the proprietor of everything that exist. sarva-loka-maheśvaram [Bg. 5.29], everything is the property of Krishna, he is the supreme controller. it is he who is the source of Brahma, Vishnu, Maheshwar. We read in the Srimad-Bhagvatam of how Lord Brahma create the entire universe that we are living with such miraculous powers. We read of how Lord Narayana is maintaining everything perfectly and we read of how Lord Shankar in his form of Rudra, destroys the entire cosmic creation. Such potencies can never be acquired by an ordinary man.

Modern scientist can’t harm mother earth but harm themselves

Our scientists and many of our political leaders in this world, they are very proud of great power that they have acquired through their good intelligence by building big big bombs. But with all of their biggest bombs, they would never have the power to even destroy this tiny little earth planet. By the arrangement of nature, they will destroy themselves before they will be able to destroy the earth. They could cause great imbalance in the earth that they have the power.

But ultimately, it is they that suffer due to imbalance of the earth due to… to exploitation. But they cannot destroy because the earth is very ancient, very old and will live a long time. And all this big big egomaniacs that are creating all these grand plans for destruction, they will cause some scars in the earth, their own mother. And ultimately because they are utterly depending on her for their survival, they will all die because she can no longer, she will no longer provide for them what is required. And after that whole civilization dies off. Gradually, the earth will heal itself, come back to its perfect and normal state. But for the mother earth, she is justs thinks all these crazy rascals just keep coming and going, coming and going, coming and going, killing one another. They cannot destroy the earth. With all of their devices and all their grand grand plans and all their nuclear bombs and radiations and so on and so forth they cannot harm practically even this one earth. Whatever harm they inflict upon on mother, ultimately, they are only harming themselves, mother earth will survive and go on.

Lord Brahma and Lord Shiva seek shelter of Lord Narayan

But Lord Shiva, when he get the indication from Narayan, he has the potency to destroy all the universe. That is his infinite power. And we read in Bhagvatam, how the great creator Lord Brahma, after he stole the calves and the cowherd boys from Sri Krishna, when Krishna expanded himself to reveal his divine opulence, Lord Brahma in utter humility had to fall at the feet of Lord Krishna, beg forne… forgiveness and surrender that, “Before you my Lord, all of my power to create is insignificant.” And we also read in the tenth canto, of Lord Shiva how sometime he is put in such a dangerous situation by giving benediction to demons like Vrikasura and those demons come back to him to kill him. How he was running for his life? and ultimately, he had to take shelter of Lord Narayan to save him.

Although Lord Shiva is the destroyer of all destroyers in this world. Brahma is the creator of all creators. Shiva is dest… destroyer of all the destroyers. Even Shiva on a practical poi… level, he has to run to Lord Narayan to be protected from being destroyed. And we find that all the demigods, the most powerful suppliers of all the benedictions upon humanity and all living beings in this earth, they are totally depending like beggars under divine provisions of Supreme Personality of Godhead, Narayan who is supplying all facilities for all living beings. And the same Lord Narayan out of his love for his devotees, in order to protect them, comes to this world as a beggar. Wearing the robe of a simple medicant with a begging pot, he came before the king of the demons Bali Maharaj, begging him for a donation.

Greatness of Lord Buddha

Now of course, the profession of a beggar is the most humbling of all occupation. Infact, this is the what reason why brahmacharis, sannyasis or monks of all traditions, they are sent out to become beggars. The reason is not that they need to be provided the bare necessities of life. That has the little or nothing to do with begging. It is not that they are in need so they must beg. Because we find some of the great wealthy people of the world even Kings, they become beggars after leaving their kingdoms behind. A perfect example is Gautam Buddha. Lord Buddha was a prince, his father was king and he had best wealth. And absolutely everything he had, his greatest desire was to just give it all over to his eldest son to enjoy. Lord Buddha was given his own beautiful beautiful palace beyond description. It is explained in the Buddhist literature that the… that the quality of the craftsmenship of his palace is such that we cannot even imagine in this day and age. And he had innumerable servants. He was given the most beautiful beautiful beautiful wife who was so faithful, kind and mild mannered in all circumstances. He was given great power, power over the entire kingdom. His parents had only one desire that he be satisfied with his material life and remain home. Because at his birth, one brahmana astrologer predicted that he would renounce everything and become a mendicant and this was unbearable thought for the parents.

So, they just tried to spoil him with every type of material pleasure and they hid from him every type of material inconvenience. So, he didn’t even know what the inconvenience was? He never even had a conception that there was such thing as suffering or pain. But then on that great and faithful chariot ride amongst the common people. When he saw suffering, when he saw old age, disease and death, he felt such compassion for the fallen soul that he left everything to become a beggar. With a begging bowl, he would go from door to door.

Lord Buddha inspires everyone to beg

And some of the greatest scholars, some people from the highest royal families, they were giving up all their worldly connections to join Lord Buddha and he would send them out with begging bowls to go door to door as beggar. To be a beggar means to be insulted, to be humiliated, to be in a subordinate position of even the lowest man of mankind. Ahh… To be seen by ignorant people and accused as being a parasite and somebody who is lazy. Why does such a person become a beggar? Why does Buddha send his people to be beggar? Even in the Buddhist countries today. Recently I was in Thailand, in Thailand if you go driving on train, go through villages, even the cities you will find every morning, every evening [Maharaj clears the throat] just herds and herds of monks are just going door to door. You see them with their begging pots, people give them some little some.. sometime they give nothing. And even in our own vaisanava tradition, we find traditionally even Rupa and Sanatan. Although they were prime ministers of the king of Bengal, they renounced it all and even though they had disciples who were willing to provide money to built beautiful temples like Madanmohan. Such unbelievable cost that must have been to build such a beautiful hand carved stone temple. And all the money was in the control of Sanatan Gosvami and yet what did he do? He would go out with his begging bowl and beg for some dry chappatis just to show example to us.

Sankirtan means to beg for other sake

So, this begging is meant for one purpose. It is meant to humble the heart of the aspiring devotee. Sankirtan is begging but actually a devotee feels that he is giving in begging, would giving this person the opportunity to make spiritual progress by connecting their consciousness with Krishna. We are not begging for food, we are not begging for money. We are begging a condition soul to have mercy upon his own life and offer something to Krishna for his spiritual welfare. We are beggars but we are not beggars for our sake. Externally it appears that way but at our hearts, we are actually begging a person to save himself by offering something to Krishna.

There is that wonderful story that Srila Prabhupad tells of one sannyasi who was begging door to door and he had a little food that some people had given some previous homes in his bowl and he came to a house of very materialistic grihastha and the man answered the door and he stood there with his begging bowl and the wife saw him. And she said, “This nonsense man! we work so hard for our money, this man is simply a lazy bum. Why doesn’t he get a job? you want something, I will give you a donation.” She went to the kitchen and got a handful of ashes and threw it all over his food and begging bowl and said, “Here, here is your donation.” The man lit up with delight, he said, “Thank you very much. Your spiritual life has begun. You have given something to Krishna.” So, this is… this is the sweetness of the beggars of the Lord.

Purpose of begging

They are begging for two reasons. One, they are begging to help that person make spiritual advancement by begging them to render some service because afterall the disciple is simply begging on behalf of the guru. Whatever the person gives is actually offering service to your guru. You have no claim to anything. [Maharaj clears the throat] Just like in New Vrindavan, there is a policy that when you go on sankirtan, you are not entitled to one penny. It is sinful to take one penny for yourself or one paisa. When you are begging, you are begging on the behalf of your spiritual master and for you to take one paisa is sinful. Its like a bank teller. When you go to a bank and you deposit [Maharaj clears the throat] a thousand rupees, does the teller has the right to think, ‘Well!, the thousand, if I take a hundred, not so bad, nine hundred goes to the bank’. Uhh… Criminal activity, sinful. Such a person must be put in prison, right. So, similarly when we go out begging on behalf of guru, we are doing the sankirtan, we are like bank teller. We are simply there approaching the spiritual… on the behalf of guru. We are simply begging people ‘Engage in service of my guru, make your life perfect.’ Lord Chaitanya, Lord Nityanand, they would go door to door begging people ‘Take the holyname. We are beggars begging you take the holyname. Give something to Krishna by chanting his name, make your life perfect.’ That is the mood.

So, in spiritual life, [Maharaj clears the throat] a beggar has two purposes. [Maharaj clears the throat] One is, he is begging on the behalf of guru, begging that person to make spiritual progress by enganging in devotional service. And two, he is taking a position which is the most humbling of all professions. There is nothing more humbling than being a beggar because it is the lowest position on the material scale of society.

Lord Vaman dev’s love for his devotees

Now, here is Lord Vaman dev who is the protector and maintainer of Brahma and Shiva, he is taking the position of a beggar, wearing the robe of a beggar, carrying the pot of a beggar and he is approaching a demon Bali Maharaj to beg. This is the love of God. This is the extent that God loves his devotees that to help his devotees, he is even willing to take the most humiliating position, the lowest position of a beggar and he is even willing to go and beg from a demon. Ahha… This is the extent of God’s love for his devotees.

Bali Maharaj willing to loose everything to honour the truth

And we find that Bali Maharaj who had taken a vow of charity. When Vaman dev came to beg from him, Sukracharya was saying, “No! no!! Bali, this is actually a… a cheater, this is God in disguise. He will steal everything from you. If you agree to give him these three steps of land, he will take everything you possess. You will be left with nothing.” And Bali Maharaj understood that what Sukracharya was saying was true but his sense of honour was of such integrity that he felt ‘Even if I loose everything, its not as bad as loosing my selfrespect, as loosing my truthfulness.’ And besides that he told Sukracharya, “If this is actually Krishna like you are saying. He is proprietor of everything and we are supposed to surrender everything to him anyway. And guru is supposed to tell us to surrender to Krishna and you are telling me not to surrender to Krishna therefore you are a bogus guru, get out of my palace.” Sukracharya cursed him that, “You will lose everything.” and then he left. And Bali Maharaj knew he would loose everything. But for the sake of truth, for the sake of the honour of preserving truth, he was willing to loose everything.

Even Duryodhana who acted in so many demoniac ways against the pandavas. During the battle of Kurukshetra, Bhisma gave him those five arrows to kill the pandavas. He was… he was simply holding those arrows till the next morning when Bhisma made a vow that these arrows will kill the Pandavas. He knew its true and he knew without Pandavas the whole world was his. But when Arjuna came to his home, Duryodhana understood ‘When a guest comes to my home, one must give him whatever he wants.’ Arjuna asked for the five arrows. Duryodhana knew ‘This means that I cannot win this war’ but he still gave him the arrows.

Devotees willing to sacrifice everything for truth

So, Aurr… of course as devotees of the Lord, we are not kshatriyas. We are bra… we are being trained by the grace of our guru Maharaj more in the line of being brahmans and therefore we must know that real honour and integrity of our life is how much we are willing to live for the truth at all costs, at any sacrifice? And Krishna explains ‘When you have thus learned the truth, you will know that all living beings are part of me, they are in me and they are mine.’ The truth is to understand that everything is the property of Krishna that everything is meant for Krishna pleasure that I am nothing but his servant and the servant of the servant. And therefore, to live for this truth, a devotee is willing to sacrifice anything and everything.
