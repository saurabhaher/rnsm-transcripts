Lecture Transcription

Verse: ŚB 7.15.72

ahaṁ ca gāyaṁs tad-vidvān

strībhiḥ parivṛto gataḥ

jṣātvā viśva-sṛjas tan me

helanaṁ śepur ojasā

yāhi tvaṁ śūdratām āśu

naṣṭa-śrīḥ kṛta-helanaḥ

Synonyms

aham — I myself; ca — and; gāyan — singing the glories of other demigods rather than those of the Lord; tat-vidvān — knowing very well the art of singing; strībhiḥ — by women; parivṛtaḥ — being surrounded; gataḥ — went there; jṣātvā — knowing well; viśva-sṛjaḥ — the prajāpatis, to whom the management of universal affairs was entrusted; tat — the attitude of my singing; me — my; helanam — negligence; śepuḥ — cursed; ojasā — with great force; yāhi — become; tvam — you; śūdratām — a śūdra; āśu — immediately; naṣṭa — devoid of; śrīḥ — beauty; kṛta-helanaḥ — because of transgressing the etiquette.

Translation

Nārada Muni continued: Being invited to that festival, I also joined, and, surrounded by women, I began musically singing the glories of the demigods. Because of this, the prajāpatis, the great demigods in charge of the affairs of the universe, forcefully cursed me with these words: “Because you have committed an offense, may you immediately become a śūdra, devoid of beauty.”

As far as kīrtana is concerned, the śāstras say, śravaṇaṁ kīrtanaṁ viṣṇoḥ: one should chant the glories of the Supreme Lord and the holy name of the Supreme Lord. This is clearly stated. Śravaṇaṁ kīrtanaṁ viṣṇoḥ: one should chant about and glorify Lord Viṣṇu, not any demigod. Unfortunately, there are foolish persons who invent some process of kīrtana on the basis of a demigod’s name. This is an offense. Kīrtana means glorifying the Supreme Lord, not any demigod. Sometimes people invent Kālī-kīrtana or Śiva-kīrtana, and even big sannyāsīs in the Māyāvāda school say that one may chant any name and still get the same result. But here we find that millions and millions of years ago, when Nārada Muni was a Gandharva, he neglected the order to glorify the Lord, and being mad in the association of women, he began to chant otherwise. Thus he was cursed to become a śūdra. His first offense was that he went to join the saṅkīrtana party in the company of lusty women, and another offense was that he considered ordinary songs, like cinema songs and other such songs, to be equal to saṅkīrtana. For this offense he was punished with becoming a śūdra.

Lecture by His Holiness Radhanath Swami Maharaja.

Story Of Sri Narada & relevance in our life.

We are continuing this beautiful story, which is narration of Narada Muni, of how he came to the Krishna Consciousness movement. Generally, for a conditioned soul, to come to Krishna Consciousness, the Supreme Lord, seeing the sincerity in that person’s heart, gradually creates turns of events in that person’s life, that brings that aspiring devotee to the lotus feet of the Lord. And in the lives of the great souls, sometimes the Supreme Lord casts them under the influence of Yoga Maya, to appear to be conditioned souls, just to show by their transcendental example, how all classes of men coming from all levels of society can become perfect in devotional service. Therefore, these wonderful stories are most purifying and educating for all sincere seekers of the truth. Generally, in our life turn of events take place and we cannot understand why.

Most of us, who have come to Krishna Consciousness, we were basically very ignorant in our previous days of life and so many series of events of sorrows, catastrophes, bewilderments, and successes, they came and went, and we really did not know what was happening or why? But after we come to the lotus feet of the spiritual master and Lord Sri Krishna, and we look back, we could see how everything in our life was very very carefully orchestrated by the Supreme Lord Sri Krishna to ultimately bring us to the point of associating with the devotees, approaching a bona fide spiritual master and surrendering to Sri Krishna. How it is happening, we do not know.

Personal story

I remember in my own life, right here in Bombay, I was sincerely seeking out a spiritual master and the spiritual path. In that process, I came all the way from Gaya to Bombay just to take a Buddhist Meditation course. I had no other reason for being here. The ten-day course ended and there was ten days break between the next ten days course. So, during that ten days break, on the first day, I left the ashram and I happened to be walking. This was the first time, I ever walked in the city of Bombay, and I came upon a large billboard advertising -‘Hare Krishna festival’, and I asked, “Where is this place, Cross Maidan?” and someone said, “it is just right here. Please turn right and you go straight.” So, I walked few minutes. And, there was the first day of the Pandal. The first pandal ever performed by Srila Prabhupada in Hare Krishna movement in India, actually in the history of the movement. It was the first pandal program, it was the first day and that evening Srila Prabhupada was speaking. How Krishna lead me to the feet of my guru maharaja, I do not understand, and it just happened that, the program was for ten days. Of course, the seed was planted in my heart. I did not understand that Srila Prabhupada was my guru, but I thought he was another great saint. But there was something very special. The seed was very deeply planted in my heart, hearing from him morning and evening for ten days, sitting at his lotus feet. Touching his feet, getting the dust on my head, I was thinking he is another great sadhu.

After that I went to Himalayas, and I went to Nepal to live with Himalayas, the other yogis on the bank of Ganges. Then there was a festival at Ambarnath, the big pilgrimage that takes place every summer. So I was in a third class train going to Ambarnath. I was just thinking I will go and worship Lord Shiva in Ambarnath. In one station, there was a large mass of saddhus. So I was thinking “so many sadhus!” and I was curious, “what is happening?” So I got up, just to ask, ‘why there are so many saddhus here’, and then the train left without me. I was thinking, “Oh my God! Now I have to wait till the next train comes, in one or two days.” Well I am here, I should find out where I am, and what all these people are doing here. So I asked one sadhu, “Where am I?” Now I asked them, “What is the occasion, that there are so many sadhus here?” They said, “Today is janmastami, Lord Krishna’s birth day.” I said, “Where am I?” They said, “This is Mathura, Krishna’s birth place.” So then they took me to Krishna’s birth place. I did not know, what was happening, I had no intention. And then after being in Mathura, they told me, “Vrindavana is the place where Krishna had lila, you should go there, it is a very charming place.” So I started walking to Vrindavana, after the Janmastami celebration, the Janmasthan, and Dwarakadish mandir at mid night. So then I went to Vrindavana and I decided, I’ll stay here for four or five days and then I will go onto Ambarnath. I have just an off time to make the pilgrimage. So after living in Vrindavana for four days, the next day I was ready to leave in the early morning. I was going to get off, to go to Mathura, to get the train. And what happened in the next morning, I could not stand up because I had typhoid fever, I could not leave Vrindavana, although I wanted so badly to leave Vrindavana, I could not leave Vrindavana. I was so sick. I could not travel for two months, and by the time I got better and I was a devotee. You know what happens, when you live in Vrindavana for two months, you become Krishna conscious. So then I decided, I am just going to stay in Vrindavana for the rest of my life. And I was thinking. But I have to find out my guru, so I was going to the different babajis, the different missions, visiting the different gurus and sadhus, living in Gaudiya matha, Nimbarka sampradaya, Ramanuja sampradaya, Charak sampradaya, just meeting all these people. And one day I was praying to Radharani, “Please guide me. Here, I am living like a vrajavasi, sleeping besides the Yamuna. But according to the sashtra and in my heart, I know, I need a guru.” So, I was praying. And while I was praying, all of a sudden a big bus come by, and I see all these Hare Krishna devotees in the bus, about 20 of them , and then a car drives by, and Srila Prabhupada was in that car. So, I want to visit Srila Prabhupada. After sitting at his lotus feet in Vrindavana, I could understand what my mission of life was to be.

So I had nothing to do with any of these things. While it was happening I did not know what was going on. But now, after attaining the shelter of the lotus feet, by the causeless mercy of our guru maharaj, we can look back and see how Krishna arranged disease, disappointment, longings, traveling here and there, missing trains, how he arranged everything just to bring me to the point, of having the opportunity to be his devotee. You see, no one comes to the lotus feet of the spiritual master without the mercy of Krishna. You cannot find your spiritual master. It is said,

brahmāṇḍa bhramite kona bhāgyavān jīva

guru-kṛṣṇa-prasāde pāya bhakti-latā-bīja

Caitanya-caritamrita Madhya 19.151

Which means that after wandering in this material universe for these countless births one is most fortunate, when one comes in contact with a bona fide spiritual master. And the spiritual master plants the seed of devotion in your heart. By the mercy of Krishna, one gets guru and by the mercy of guru, one gets Krishna.

śvaraḥ sarva-bhūtānāṁ

hṛd-deśe ’rjuna tiṣṭhati

bhrāmayan sarva-bhūtāni

yantrārūḍhāni māyayā

Bhagavad-Gita 18.61

Krishna is in the heart of every living being and he is directing everyone’s wanderings. He is directing you from within your heart and only by his direction and by his causeless mercy you can meet the great sadhus, his pure devotees. That is the greatest gift. That is the ultimate benediction given by god. And how wonderful it is - that Krishna puts his devotees through so many lessons in life to bring them to the point of accepting the shelter of the lotus feet of his pure devotees. In the eleventh canto of Srimad Bhagavatam we read about a brāhmana from Avanti desh. It is described there, that this brāhmana was a very wealthy landowner, very materialistic business-man. One-day thieves came, and stole all of his property and all of his wealth. And in a complete state of poverty, even his own family members rejected him because he could no longer provide for their sense gratification, and he was in complete anxiety. You know the brahmacaries, you do not know what it is like, to really be attached to material wealth and prestige and family life. But it is such a hard knot in the heart, that it’s just unimaginable how much pain and anxiety, when those things are separated from our existence.

Story of Avanti Brahmana.

So that brāhmana he was practically dead in anxiety, just lying in the ground crying. He was in a critical stage of lamentation and distress. He had lost his family, he had lost his wealth, he had lost his prestige, he had nothing left yet, he had nothing else in life. It is a pain worse than death. And in that condition, he remembers the paramatma in his heart. And he surrenders to the paramatma in his heart, and he became a pure devotee of the lord. Such a great pure devotee of the lord, that even god himself in the form of Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu would regularly recite his beautiful prayers of devotion to Sri Krishna. So, how Krishna puts his children in very perfect conditions, to bring us to the point of surrender! And factually we should know that whatever position we are in we have the opportunity to surrender. It is Krishna’s perfect plan that we surrender. The question is whether we take it or not.

Narada Muni’s story.

Here, we see the condition of Narada Muni. He is describing in his previous life, he was a beautiful gandharva. A gandharva means he had beautiful bodily features. He had a very strong healthy body. He was very very wealthy and long life. Do you not what is it like to be a gandharva? Such incredible opportunity to enjoy this world. A human being cannot even begin to comprehend, the quantity and quality of enjoyment that is available for a gandharva. The gandharvas are expert at everything they do. They are expert at fighting. They are expert in music. They are expert at dance. They are very very learned. Their consorts are apsaras, who are the most beautiful heavenly damsels in all over the cosmic creation. In gandharva loka there is no question of poverty. There is only wealth, riches and opulence. It is simply a comfortable life of song and dance. And they are also the devotees of the lord, but generally they are not very austere devotees. The devotees should not like to enjoy, but they are thankful to Krishna for all the sense enjoyment that they have. So he was such an exalted position but he committed an offence. He actually did commit an offence. Of course, we understand such great soul; this is under the influence of yoga maya, just to teach us lessons. But still the example is there for us to learn.

He committed the offence; Srila Prabhupada is explaining that he was considering the names of the demigods to be equal to the name of Vishnu. And he was chanting kirtans to the demigods, rather than the supreme lord of the heart. And, because of this the Prajapati had seen that he was not only doing this but he was also whimsically engaging in kirtan. He was dancing with women, instead of dancing with the other gandharvas. Because after all when you are very beautiful like this, you have all facilities to enjoy illicit activities. Although he was not engaging directly in illicit activities, he was taking advantage of his position and he was dancing with women and he was chanting the names of the demigods, which means his heart is full of material desires.

Because of this, the prajapatis, they cursed him. They cursed him to become a sudra, which means, he lost all his bodily beauty, he lost all his wealth, he lost his big strong healthy body, which had a life span of millions of years. He lost all of his fame and distinction. A sudra does not have nice looking bodily features, he has a very short life, he is not very expert in anything, and he is looked upon as being the lowest in the society. Can you imagine? What a blow that must have been! From a gandharva in the heavenly planets to a sudra on the earth, living in the jungles. Sometimes we think that why Krishna is doing this to me. He took away all his distinction, all his wealth, all his beauty, all of his physical health, everything he took away. But in the body of a sudra in that completely fallen condition, he was very humble, because he had nothing to be proud of.

Of course, even if you have nothing to be proud of, the nature of the false ego is to find something (to be proud of). But it is not easy,

janmaiśvarya-śruta-śrībhir

edhamāna-madaḥ pumān -SB.1.8.26

Kunti devi explains that the greatest spiritual disqualifications are high birth, physical beauty, wealth, and a good education. So Narada Muni had all these things in his gandharva body. And in a sudra body, he had no wealth, he had no bodily beauty, he had no education, and he had no distinction. In that situation, you really have to use your imagination to become proud. So he was basically in very humble condition of life. In that humble condition of life, in the association of great saints and sages, he could understand their glory. He could understand their divine position and he was fully aware of how he was so dependent on their mercy in his life. Generally, a person, who is affluent, takes advantage, takes for granted the association of saintly persons.

They associate with sadhus, either as a fashion, just to show people that they are very religious or just to make sure they are covered from all sides, just to make sure that by my own expertise and opulence in making my life something very great but if i do have my limitations, and just to make sure, beyond my limitations everything goes alright, I’ll get the the blessings of the sadhus, that is generally the condition of why a rich man approaches a sadhu. But when you approach a sadhu, you should feel that I have nothing, I have no one before God and his representatives, then one should fall at these great souls feet, and hear from them, what is the goal of life and how to attain it?

So Narada Muni although he had to experienced such a painful downfall. In that downfall he was perfectly situated to take full shelter of the lotus feet of the great saints. The great devotees of the lord and thus everyday he would take remnants of those great souls and he would render menial service to them and hear their message. This is the process of self-realization.

Srimad Bhagavatam 1.2.18

By hearing Srimad Bhagavatam regularly and by rendering service to the great souls then Krishna who is within our heart cleanses us of all the impurities and directs us to His lotus feet. So we should not ever complain, when things go wrong in our life. We should never complain, when things go wrong in our life. We should never blame this person or that person; we should see that everything is happening by the sweet will of the lord to give me the perfect opportunity to become his pure devotee. And it is the reverse in our life. There are the greatest acts of mercy of God in our life. A foolish devotee, a neophyte devotee, he thinks Krishna when everything is very nice. When everything is going on his way, when he is in good health, when people are properly respecting him, he is thinking oh, yes Krishna you are very kind upon me. But a really intelligent devotee, when he is disrespected, when he misunderstood, when things has been taken away from him, when he is losing everything, when he has being battered and smashed and kicked by the modes of the material nature. He thinks ‘ah, Krishna! It is very nice gift you are giving me!’ This is just the opportunity that I have been waiting for to surrender. And, in that condition the devotee takes the opportunity and increases his humble devotion to the supreme Lord. And this is the qualification of a sincere devotee, one, who even in the most negative conditions of life can see the divine, merciful hand of Krishna, behind the whole scene. Seeing that this is the just opportunity I need to become the humble servant of the servant of the servant of the Krishna and his devotees. And this is the very glorious lesson that is being illustrated by Krishna through His most magnificent devotee.

So Narada Muni in his gandharva body he was a sense enjoyer, and then he came to a body of a sudra, been taken away of sense gratification really having no sense enjoyment. There is nothing. In that condition he took shelter of the great souls and he understood the goal of life. But even throughout his life he was striving for Krishna Consciousness, he never attained it. He never gave up, even after going through all these falling situations from his position, being put in a completely humiliating condition of a sudra, taking shelter of the great souls, worshiping their lotus feet, serving them, eating their mahaprasad. You would think, “Alright Krishna, I fed enough. Make me your pure devotee”. But Krishna did not. He strived. He struggled for his whole life. His mother was bitten by a serpent. She was a widow. He had no parents. He was on his own. He was a young boy, living in the jungles. And he was striving for Krishna and Krishna only.

Krishna gave him one glimpse. It is explained that after he left home, just wandering through the cities, through the villages, through the towns, through the agricultural fields, through the forests, and into the deepest jungles, he was just looking for Krishna, everywhere trying to experience what is the goal of life? everywhere. But he could not find it. But the words of the great sages were constantly echoing within his heart. He was keeping it going on and on and on with faith. The words of the great souls give us faith and direction in our lives. Then, at last Narada Muni after drinking some clear water, he sat in meditation and Vishnu appeared to him. He was so much captivated by the beauty of this beautiful form, then Vishnu disappeared and he was in very great anxiety and he began to perform the same process of meditation, but Krishna does not appear by any mechanical process. He only appears by His own sweet will, when He feels that His devotee is ready to see Him. But he could not find that beautiful form again. Then Narayan spoke to him from within the heart that in this life you will never see me again. You must strive and struggle throughout the rest of your life to purify yourself.

So this sudra, he began to just strive and struggle to purify his life. He never gave up. Because of the words of the great souls, he maintained great faith and determination, enthusiasm and such patience and then he lived his whole life just struggling and striving but not finding, and Krishna was so pleased by his sincere endeavour, then in his next birth he took birth in the form of Narada Muni. And Narada Muni never for a second from the time of his birth was not experiencing the divine mercy of Sri Krishna in his life. Never was not he experiencing the sweetness of pure bhakti and he became the intimate associate of the Lord, why? Because his only business was preaching the glories of the Lord throughout the world, throughout the universe; playing upon his Veena. So this is very very great lesson that we must learn.

The Journey of our lives.

Unless we hear very strictly with great faith to the order of our spiritual master, we will not understand what the mercy of Krishna is and how it is coming upon us. Therefore Viswanath Chakrbarti Thakur has said,

Because, in the face of that experience, we have the greatest opportunity to become Krishna Conscious, if we simply remain fixed in our service, if we remain fixed in our service in this condition of humility. We should always be looking for our own faults. We should never ever look for the faults in others. In looking for the faults in others, you simply become further incriminated in your false ego and whims. Thus we become further incriminated in forgetfulness of Krishna. When we look for our own faults and see how Krishna is revealing to me my own faults. Krishna is revealing to me my own lack of Krishna Consciousness, my own lack of humility, my own lack of faith. Q&A Transcription

Q: In this, devine lila everything seems to be completely predicted, while in material world whatever is happening with devotees, non-devotees, is that also completely fixed?

A: It is not fixed, but Krishna is so great, he even knows what is not fixed. You only know, what is fixed. But Krishna knows, even what is not fixed. You can act any way you like. It is not fixed. But Krishna knows how you will act. He will give you the opportunity to go up or down. And he does not interfere with your independent choice. But He knows how you will act. But He has to give you the chance. Even if He knows, you can fall down. But even in falling down ultimately that will help you go up. But this is the longer road. Just like Narada Muni in his gandharva body, he made a mistake. He was committing an offence. But even the reaction to that an offence was the perfect arrangement for him to be Krishna Conscious. Whether you do the right thing or the wrong thing, you can get a result. In either case, you can have the perfect opportunity to be Krishna Conscious. But you have to in order, to actually become Krishna Conscious, you have to give up doing the wrong thing once and for all. Sometimes if you make a mistake, the result shatters you shatters you so badly. Shatters your sense of security in this world so badly. It is the perfect opportunity to give up making mistakes. Sometimes you need that, it is said that experience is the best teacher, but a fool learns by no other way. An intelligent man hears the sashtra, hears from the devotees, understands what is right, what is wrong and follows and goes to back to godhead. Another person even though he believes it,he is not willing to do it, he is not willing to follow. And therefore he has to become smashed brutally by Krishna. Brutally smashed, mental or physical, whatever it takes. In that condition, finally he comes to the realization “I am going to stop, making these mistakes”. I am going to live according to guru’s order.

But, because you are a devotee and you have surrendered to Krishna. Nothing I say, necessarily will affect your life. Because when you surrender to Krishna “daivi prakrit”,

mahātmānas tu māṁ pārtha

daivīṁ prakṛtim āśritāḥ

Bhagavad-Gita 9.13

You come out of the influence of the divine energy. Although you have due to your past activities, a set destiny, due to your karma. When you surrender to Krishna, you no longer are under the laws of karma. Krishna can let you go through, the reactions of your karma, if he likes, then everything will act according to your horoscope. He can intervene at any time he wants, and separate you from that condition. Just like our guru maharaja Srila Prabhupada in 1966, he had a very major heart attack. They took him to the hospital. Srila Prabhupada said, get me out of the hospital, as soon as he gained his consciousness, that I cannot tolerate the doctors in hospital. Bring me to the temple. Then Srila Prabhupada says, that was my destined moment to die. But Krishna told me, at that time that He has more work for me in His mission. So, when you become a devotee, Krishna could intervene in your destiny in any way he wants. Even if your destiny is to be prosperous and successful, Krishna can bring complete calamity in your life and take everything away. And that is what you need. And even if you are supposed to be diseased and die young, Krishna can give you a healthy body to preach through old age, if he wants. But, unless we utilize our independence to surrender to Krishna, then we are simply victimized by the results of our previous activities.

Q: Can we change our destiny?

A: The system has been created by the divine. But within the system you create your destiny. The divine hand of God has created the laws of Karma. And then the divine hand of god, has cast you, into the material existence, by your own sweet will. Because you wanted to come here, and while you are here you have choice. If you act sinfully then your destiny will be to suffer. If you act piously then your destiny will be to enjoy. Now what we have done in our previous life is what we are experiencing now. Nothing is on just. This is the divine arrangement. Everything that comes into our life, is we deserved due to our past activities, and what we are doing now will determine what are our future will be. So in this way our destiny has been set by our own previous lives and our own life at this time. But if you surrender to Krishna, Krishna says

sarva-dharmān parityajya

mām ekaṁ śaraṇaṁ vraja

ahaṁ tvāṁ sarva-pāpebhyo

mokṣayiṣyāmi mā śucaḥ

Bhagavad-Gita 18.66

Then, you are no longer under the imprisonment of the laws of Karma. Then, Krishna will simply do whatever He wants to do to help you to become a pure devotee. It may appear like karma, he may give you happiness and he may give you distress. This is just like a person, who is experiencing happiness and distress by the laws of Karma. But it is not because, necessarily you deserve it. You may deserve it or you may not deserve it. But you need it. If you act sinfully, if Krishna feels that the sinful reaction is not good for your spiritual progress, He can take it away. And if you acted very piously and Krishna sees that the affluence, that you deserve according to your karma is not good for your spiritual life, He will not give it to you. A karmi, one who is frutitive worker in this world, he gets what he deserves and nothing else. He gets the reactions of his own activities. But a devotee, he gets or she gets, what Krishna knows that devotee needs to make spiritual advancement. There is maya prakriti and there is daivi prakriti. Maya prakriti is this material nature, which is the three modes of material natures, directed by maya, you are simply enjoying or suffering the fruits of your actions, that is all going on in this material world. But daivi prakriti means the divine energy of the lord, where you are directly under the merciful hand of Krishna’s mercy and he is just orchestrating your life in such a way to give you every opportunity to surrender. But even with those opportunities, he never interferes with your independence. You can accept the opportunity or reject it.

Q: How do we know of the opportunity sent by Krishna?

AM: It is the will of Krishna to give you the best opportunity. How does he give the opportunity? First, he sends the spiritual master and great sadhus just speak to you, to give you knowledge. That is His gift. And then the next gift is, he puts in a predicament of life, where you have the opportunity to live according to that knowledge. To apply that knowledge to your life. First you hear and then you are put just in a situation, where you apply what you have learned from your spiritual master and hear to his instructions. You can make such great spiritual advancement.

God is very great. We are very small. Always remember this.
