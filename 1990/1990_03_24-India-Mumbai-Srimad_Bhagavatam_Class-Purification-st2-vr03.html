Maharaj reading Translation and Purport

A twice-born brahmana gains his life by the grace of his parents through the process of purifica[break]tion known as garbhādhāna. There are also other processes of purification, until the end of life, when the funeral ceremony [antyeṣṭi-kriyā] is performed. Thus, in due course a qualified brahmana becomes uninterested in materialistic activities and sacrifices, but he offers the sensual sacrifices, in full knowledge, into the working senses, which are illuminated by the fire of knowledge.

Purport by His Divine Grace Srila A.C. BhaktiVedanta Swami Prabhupada

Those interested in materialistic activities remain in the cycle of birth and death. Pravṛtti-mārga, or the inclination to stay in the material world to enjoy varieties of sense gratification, has been explained in the previous verse. Now, in this verse, it is explained that one who has perfect Brahminical knowledge rejects the process of elevation to higher planets and accepts Nivṛtti-mārga. In other words, he prepares himself to go back home, back to Godhead. Those who are not Brahmanas but atheists do not know what is pravṛtti-mārga or nivṛtti-mārga. They simply want to obtain pleasure at any cost. Our Krishna consciousness movement is therefore training devotees to give up the pravṛtti-mārga and accept the nivṛtti-mārga in order to return home, back to Godhead. This is a little difficult to understand, but it is very easy if one takes to Kṛiṣhṇa consciousness seriously and tries to understand Kṛiṣhṇa. A Kṛiṣhṇa conscious person can understand that performing yajna according to the karma-kāṇḍa system is a useless waste of time and that merely giving up the karma-kāṇḍa and accepting the process of speculation is also unfruitful. Therefore, Narottama dāsa Ṭhākura has sung in his Prema-bhakti-candrikā:

karma-kāṇḍa, jṣāna-kāṇḍa, kevala viṣera bhāṇḍa

‘amṛta’ baliyā yebā khāyanānā yoni sadā phire,

kadarya bhakṣaṇa kare, tāra janma adhaḥ-pāte yāya

[song by Narottama Das Thakura]

[The fruitive worker of karma-kanda and the speculations of jnana-kanda are two pots of poison.  If one drinks these two poisons, and then proclaims that they are both as sweet as nectar, he will fall from the human realm and spend a long time wandering in many species of life, eating many abominable things, and enjoying many horrible pleasures.]

A life of karma-kāṇḍa or jṣāna-kāṇḍa is like a poison pot, and one who takes to such a life is doomed. In the karma-kāṇḍa system, one is destined to accept birth and death again and again. Similarly, with jṣāna-kāṇḍa one falls down again to this material world. Only worship of the Supreme Person offers one the safety of going back home, back to Godhead.

Translation

A twice-born brāhmaṇa gains his life by the grace of his parents through the process of purification known as garbhādhāna. There are also other processes of purification, until the end of life, when the funeral ceremony [antyeṣṭi-kriyā] is performed. Thus, in due course a qualified brāhmaṇa becomes uninterested in materialistic activities and sacrifices, but he offers the sensual sacrifices, in full knowledge, into the working senses, which are illuminated by the fire of knowledge.

Start of Lecture by His Holiness Radhanath Swami Maharaja.

Life is a series of process of Purification

It is herein described the system of varnashrama dharma, in which one is gradually elevated to indifference to the object of the senses and complete surrender in the service of Sri Krishna. It is described by Srila Narada Muni, that the brahmana gains his life by the grace of his parents through the process of purification, known as garbhadhana. You see, human form of life, at every stage, according to Vedic culture, the most important aspect of one’s life is purification. Some very foolish materialistic people, they consider that what is the benefit of taking to the purificatory process of spiritual life. When one is young. While one is young, one should enjoy and at the time, when one exhausts all his propensities for sense enjoyment, at the fag end of life, after dedicating one’s activities to one’s family, one’s society, one’s nation, that is the proper time to take to spiritual life. After all, there is a time and place for everything. But here it is described that according to civilized human beings, the process of purification must begin at the ultimately earliest stages of your birth. Which means at the time of your conception. According to the Vedic system your life begins at the time of conception. In fact, it was commonly understood that when you are born, you are one year old. Because you have been living within the womb for approximately one year. Therefore, here it is very, very strongly instructed, that the purification of your consciousness should begin at the very time you are conceived within the womb of your mother. And from that point, until the time of your funeral ceremony, (the antyeshti-kriya), your whole life is a series of processes of purification.

Accept the responsibility of a brahmana

And it is through these the gradual process of purification, that one becomes purified from all the desires, to lord it over the material nature. And one becomes attached to the service of the Lord and his devotees. Here it is specifically describing that this is the Uh life in which every brahmana must accept, to follow all the processes of purification throughout one’s life. Otherwise, one cannot be considered truly a brahmana. It is not simply a question of birth. It is a question of accepting the responsibility of a brahmana. Just like we may use as an example, a person may be born a son of a policeman, but unless that person accepts the responsibilities and the austerities, that are the duties of a policeman, will anyone consider him a policeman? So, a brahmana is one who by his qualities in life and by his acts, he behaves according to the duties of a brahmana and this holds true for all the four varnas and all the four ashramas. But we should understand all of the duties for a brahmana, kshatriya, vaisya, or shudra. For a brahmachari, grihastha, vanaprastha and sannyasa, all of these Vedic injunctions prescribed are exclusively meant for the purpose of purification of the consciousness. To gradually strip one’s heart of all his propensities to enjoy separate from God. Therefore, we should know, very clearly, that this life is not meant for any other purpose but to become purified. This is the duty of every human being and especially for those who are Brahmanas because they are the leaders of society.

Simplicity means no duplicity

In this room today, we do not have any guests. We have only very serious, initiated or aspiring initiated devotees, who very soon, if not already, will accept the sacred thread and Brahmanical initiation. So, this verse is specifically speaking to all of us, that the duty of a brahmana is that, at every stage of life, at every moment of life, he must be struggling and striving for purification. He will accept whatever is purifying and he will reject whatever is un-purifying. The most important quality of a brahmana, is his detachment, his desire to live a simple and austere life. One of the qualities of a brahmana is simplicity. Simplicity is very simple, it means no duplicity. One is only duplicitous, when one is desirous to lord it over material nature, because this is a world of duality and to the degree we become attached to the dualistic principles, that I like this and I do not like this, to the degree we are attached to getting our way, which means to the degree we are attached to lording it over material nature, to that extent our whole consciousness is filled with the dualities of honor and dishonor, pleasure and pain, happiness and distress, gain and loss. Therefore, on the basis of this principle of material contamination, we have to do anything and say anything in order to, as they say, fulfill all our purposes and get ahead in this world. Therefore, this breeds duplicity. So, in order to actually live a simple life, one must live an austere life. In order to have simplicity of mind, one must have a simplicity of lifestyle, which means one must be detached from gain or loss, pleasure or pain, honor or dishonor, happiness or distress. One must simply be seeking purification of consciousness. Therefore, such a person sees no one as his enemy, no one as his friend. He only sees how he can serve Krishna and how I can help every living being. This is simplicity. As soon as we put ourselves first, our selfish interest first, immediately there is duplicity. But when we put Krishna first and when we put those persons, who we are meant to be the servant of, first, which means every living being who is part of Krishna. Then our life becomes very simple. And this is described as a very great quality of a brahmana. Because a brahmana is not interested in getting ahead in this world, but in purifying his consciousness to free himself from the slightest trace of desire to get ahead in this world.

Bharat Maharaj an ideal King(Jada Bharata)

In the Srimad Bhagavatam it describes the story of Bharat Maharaj. In summary, he was a very great king. He was the senior most son of the incarnation of Krishna himself, Rishabhdev and Bharata Maharaj became such a fearless ruler of this world and such a selfless compassionate king, that the entire planet earth, to this day, according to the sastra, is named after him, Bharat Varsha, the planet of King Bharat. It is described that although he ruled the kingdom and dealt with all the situations, all the problems, he had such a simple and pure heart, that internally he was always simply thinking, how can I best serve Krishna. And therefore, when he found that his children were old enough to take over the kingdom, it is described he left his beautiful wife, he left his beautiful throne, he left his royal kingdom, just as a man leaves his stool after he had passed. With utter disgust for all these material conditions, he just walked off into the forest. He understood that as a kshatriya he had to rule the kingdom for his purification. He did not rule the kingdom, in any way, shape or form, for his own enjoyment, or for his own fame. He did it because it was his duty and by performing his duty, he would be purified, and he would help up purify others.

The real motive - purification of Consciousness

I was just reading one letter by Srila Prabhupada, where a devotee was asking Srila Prabhupada, that he had taken second initiation, he had studied the sastras very carefully, he wanted to write literature, Vaishnava literature. And Prabhupada quoted Krishnadasa Kaviraja Gosvami from the Caitanya-caritamrita and there Prabhupada explains, that Krishnadas Kaviraja, in this verse, he is saying that I have no qualification to write this book, Caitanya-caritamrita. I am simply writing it because it is the order of my superiors, for my purification. And then Prabhupada began to describe, that a devotee never does anything for his own interest regarding fame, distinction and wealth. Whatever he does in Krishna Consciousness, he does for his purification. Many people want to write books because they want to become famous authors, but Prabhupada says a devotee never considers. A devotee is not even so concerned if nobody ever reads the book. He writes the book for his purification. Whether it is accepted or rejected by society, does not mean that much. In this way whatever a devotee does is with this motive.

Lives of Bharat Maharaj

So, Maharaja Bharat, he ruled the kingdom for his purification and at the proper time, at the proper stage of life in a kshatriyas existence, he left the kingdom behind to live in a jungle, eating only the raw fruits, berries and herbs that grow. Living with nothing but tree bark, on the bank of Gandaki River. He lived a life of severe penance and tapasya for his purification. And it is described that one day a deer was being chased by a ferocious tiger and the deer was pregnant and she was running away. And she went to jump over the river and as she jumped over the river, the ferocious animal began to roar and out of fear, she had a miscarriage. And the tiny little child fell into the river. And then the mother approaching the other side, seeing her child in such distress and in such a helpless condition, not being able to help her. The natural quality of a parent, they feel so deeply for the child that the mother deer died. So, Bharat Maharaj, he was thinking that there is no one on earth that can save this deer except me. So, he took the deer in his loving hands and protected it. Then he was thinking, now how will this deer eat?'' Does not have a mother. Who will protect him from the dangerous animals in the forest? So, Bharat Maharaj began to care for this deer very carefully. And gradually he became very attached to this deer. He developed the maternal, paternal affection for this deer. He became like the mother and father and he worried about the deer just like a mother or father worries about its child. When the deer would not return home on time, he would be just thinking where is my? The food is prepared, he would go out through the forest, “where are you? Where are you?” He would cry. When the deer would be in its arms, he would caress it with great affection. In this way he passed his days and at the time of death, naturally he thought of this deer. So, he took his next birth as a deer. But you see, because it was a arrangement of Krishna, that it was not for his own selfish purposes that he became attached to the deer, but it was out of a natural sentimental, compassion and because of his sincere desire for self-realization, although Krishna gave him a form of a deer, he did not abandon him. He gave him full awareness of his previous life. So as a deer, Bharat, his only interest was to just go to the places where the saints and sages were reciting the glories of the Lord and he would just hear from them there. And he was always anxious just to associate with great souls and hear their narrations of the Supreme Personality of Godhead form their lotus mouths. And very soon that deer left his body, and he took a birth again in the family of brahmans. And in this third birth he had complete awareness by the grace of Krishna, paramatma, of his previous two births. So, he was very cautious, that in this birth I am not going to entangle myself in any way, shape, or form. In any type of material attachments. In fact, the only way to do that in this world, because this world is so entangling, if I have to completely disguise myself as a mad man. So, although his father was a very, very distinguished and qualified brahmana, he would be constantly trying to train his son, but his son would do everything the opposite. Because he understood full well that the only purpose of this existence is to finish off this material world and purify myself. And he became an Avadhuta. His name is Jada Bharat. His father is. It is described, he would never bathe, he would never wash. His skin was black, his hair was matted, his nails were long and his brahmana thread was black. His father would try to teach him how to pass stool. He would say, now first you pass the stool, then you wash yourself. He would wash himself first, then pass stool. That was it, finished. His father would say my god, how disgusting. He did not know what to do with this boy.

Finally, his brothers, who were qualified brahmanas as well, they thought, the only use of this boy is let’s just use him to keep the crows out of the agricultural fields. And they would have him stand like a scarecrow, just to keep the crows out and he was very happy just to do like that. He had no objection. And while he was out there standing like a scarecrow, some dacoits who wanted to perform a human sacrifice to the goddess kali. They saw him and they thought, this man is a complete mad man but look he has a very beautiful body. He is very strong, and he is very handsome. He will be a very fit offering to our goddess. So, they just picked him up, out of the agricultural field. Jada Bharata did not care. He was completely indifferent. Whatever Krishna wants to do to me, I do not mind, it is all for my purification. Because when a person is accustomed to all sorts of reversals in live, a person just sees Krishna everywhere. His brothers, the whole society, they completely ridiculed him, humiliated him and physically abused him. They thought he was a mad man. But in his heart, he was the most wise, the most sensitive and the most sincere man on earth. He was just accepting all this for his purification. So, when these dacoits took him, he did not mind. He was depending on Krishna. He did not say a word. He did not try to fight to defend himself. And they bathed his body very nicely and they put nice clothes on him and then they put him before the goddess Kali. And then they, “Kalimata, here is the blood of your human sacrifice, to satisfy your quench, your thirst for blood.”

Of course, it is described in the Bhagavatam, that those who are deeply Umm possessed by the modes of ignorance, they worship these ghastly forms of the demigods. So, they took the sacred sword of their puja and they raised it and with all their force they started to come down to sever the head of Jada Bharata. But within the heart of Bharata, there was only pure devotion to Krishna. There was only helpless simple dependence on Krishna’s mercy. He did not have to defend himself, because when one has this simplistic devotion to the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Krishan says in Gita, kaunteya pratijānīhi na me bhaktaḥ praṇaśyati [Bg. 9.31]

Kaali Devi protected Jada Bharat

“To my pure devotee I am the protector. He is never vanquished.” So, in this condition, Kali who happens to be a great Vaishnavi, one of the most glorious servants of Lord Hari. In fact, she is an expansion of the Goddess of fortune Herself, but she takes this thankless position in the service of Narayana, just to give a chance for those in the lower modes of ignorance to make some spiritual advancement. She became so outraged that they were going to commit this offence to her devotee. That the deity herself leaped off of the altar, snatched the sword from the hands of the dacoits and started to scream with a shrilling laughter and began to cut off the heads of each of the gang of the dacoits. Then she took off their heads and hilariously laughed with the shrilling sound vibration, which was thundering throughout all directions. She was playing with their heads and bouncing them and throwing them and cutting them and the dacoits were just being completely massacred, until every single one of them was mutilated. Krishna let your will be done.

Jada Bharat instructed King Rahugana

At that time, Bharat Maharaja went into the jungles and it is described that there was a king named Rahugana and he was going on a royal procession, in the beautiful royal palanquin. And the person who was on one side of the palanquin, there were four carriers, he had some difficulty. And he could no longer carry the palanquin. So, the King’s procession was at a standstill. So, they had to find a very stout and strong man. And they happened to see in the forest, Jada Bharath. So, they asked him to come. So of course, Jada Bharat would not resist anyone, he would do whatever anyone ask him to do. But he would always do it all wrong, purposely. Just so he would not get any credit. [Mj. laughs] He knew, as soon as you get do something good, you become glorified and then you become proud and you become attached. Isn’t that correct? So, it is better, if you do everything completely wrong. Then everyone will condemn you. Then everyone just thinks you are totally useless and then there is no question of becoming materially attached to anything. He would not even talk. He appeared like a man, who was deaf and dumb. Because he was afraid if he began to speak, people would think he was a learned man and then he would get proud. So, they said to him, Oh! “you are a stout young man, you come, you carry the King’s palanquin.” He just went, “hmm, hmm.” So, he agreed and so they took him. They put him on one side of the palanquin. They began to walk. He was very strong, everything was going very smoothly and all of a sudden, the king felt Zuup, very bad jolt. All of a sudden, one side of the palanquin just stopped, and the king almost fell off. “What is this?” Jada Bharat stopped because some ants were on the road. And he was afraid, he might step on the ant. So, he stopped. But everyone else kept going because no one else cared about ants.Uh, So, the king was very disturbed. “Who is doing this?” “It’s him! It’s him!” Jada Bharata did not say anything. King said, “don’t you stop ever again. I am the king, and you are my servant. Now proceed.” They proceeded again. And Jada Bharat was very nicely carrying. He was very strong living in the jungles. And then Haan, another ant. He immediately stopped. And as he stopped the whole palanquin went [Mj. makes phrew sound]. One side almost slipped. Maharaja Rahugana was very angry. He said, “if you ever do this again, you will be killed. This is an offence to the king.” Jada Bharat did not say anything. yes,yes,yes,yes,yes ,yes of course. So, they continued on. And they went for some time and then by the arrangement of God, another ant on the road. Jada Bharat stopped. At this time, the king was so furious, he just began to scream and yell and chastise and blaspheme this poor man. “I am the king; you are the subject. Do you not understand the king is the representative of God? You must die!” And the king was about to order his death. And he was so angry, and Jada Bharat just stood there. He was not disturbed, nor was he elated by the king’s actions. He was simply seeing it as the divine arrangement of Krishna. And then finally, Rahugana Maharaja said, “Say something! Do something! What is your, what is it, what kind of crazy man are you?” And then Jada Bharata, although he was taking the role of an Avadhuta, in his heart he was actually a most compassionate and loving Vaishnava. He saw that here is finally an opportunity to preach to somebody, who will listen. So, he began to speak. He said, “my dear king, you are thinking that you are the king, and you are thinking that I am the servant. But this is all an illusion because in next birth, I may be the king and you may be this servant. So, who are you? This is all your great illusion that you are thinking, you are the body, and you are thinking I am my body. We are not these bodies. This is all a mental concoction, and the greatest enemy is our own mind. In fact, our mind is our only enemy. Because our mind is constantly “sankalpa-vikalpa”, “Accepting one thing, rejecting another thing” on the basis of bodily conception of life for sense gratification. Therefore, the purpose of human life is to become purified of the mind’s tendencies for enjoyment.” And he spoke in this way several chapters of the Shrimad Bhagavatam are the beautiful illustrious instructions of Jada Bharat to king Rahugana.

A devotee seeks opportunity to become purified

And when Maharaja Rahugana heard the piercingly, purifying words of Jada Bharat, he realized that this is the greatest man on earth. And he came down off of his palanquin and he put his head, adorned with his golden crown on the feet of Jada Bharata. And he surrendered. And through this beautiful narration we understand that in every stage of life we should be striving and struggling for purification. We should not be concerned with the external appearance of how things work out for us in this world. We should only be concerned with how we are becoming purified, by whatever circumstance comes into our life. Whether there is failure or success. Whether there is happiness or distress, honor or dishonor, pleasure or pain, a devotee simply sees every situation as an opportunity to become purified. He is not so concerned with how things work out from the sensual perspective. He is only concerned with the purification that takes place in that circumstance and the purification takes place, to the extent, we accept every opportunity as an opportunity to serve Krishna and serve his devotees.

sarvopādhi-vinirmuktaṁ tat-paratvena nirmalam

hṛṣīkeṇa hṛṣīkeśa- sevanaṁ bhaktir ucyate

[SB. 6.16.40, Cc. Madhya 19.170]

[‘Bhakti, or devotional service, means engaging all our senses in the service of the Lord, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, the master of all the senses. When the spirit soul renders service unto the Supreme, there are two side effects. One is freed from all material designations, and one’s senses are purified simply by being employed in the service of the Lord.’]

“That the same mind and senses that are now causing us, due to mental concoction and attachment, so much entanglement. Those same minds and senses, that are engaged in Krishna’s service, Hrishikesh’s service become the very basis of our purification.” So therefore, a devotee’s only purpose in life is to engage in the service of the Lord for his purification. A devotee is not concerned what my occupation is, what my occupation is not. What this person thinks about me or what this person does not think about me. A devotee does not think in terms of, how much money I do have, or how much money I do not have. A devotee is just thinking in terms of, how I can best purify my life. Without purification, even if you are materially successful in this world, your whole life is being wasted. All of your endeavors are simply creating a deeper, more miserable condition of bondage. Uh. Therefore, at every stage of life and in every circumstance of life, from the time of conception, to the time one’s body is put on the fire of cremation, a human being should always be striving and struggling to purify his mind and purify his senses. And he should have really no other objective in life than that.

Think of Krishna in humble service attitude

And the most sublime and powerful means of purification is to always think of Krishna. To always think of Krishna and always do the will of Krishna. The will of Krishna is coming down through the disciplic succession, through the spiritual masters, the great acharyas. When they speak to us, we can understand that they are speaking the will of Krishna. And we learn to always think of Krishna by acting according to their instructions. Sometimes the great souls, they tell us so many duties we must perform or through their representative, they are telling us so many duties to perform. Understand that by performing these duties properly both internally as well as externally we are being trained how to always think of Krishna. And in this life and at the time of death, if we can learn in this proper service attitude to always think of Krishna, then we return back home back to godhead. But you see, unless you have a humble service attitude, you cannot always think of Krishna. Thinking of Krishna is not simply based on your abilities to control your mind. No. Krishna says in Gita.

sarvasya cāhaṁ hṛdi sanniviṣṭo

mattaḥ smṛtir jṣānam apohanaṁ ca

vedaiś ca sarvair aham eva vedyo

vedānta-kṛd veda-vid eva cāham

[Bg. 15.15]

[I am seated in everyone’s heart, and from Me come remembrance, knowledge and forgetfulness. By all the Vedas, I am to be known. Indeed, I am the compiler of Vedanta, and I am the knower of the Vedas.]

That he within the heart, is the source of remembrance, knowledge and forgetfulness. You can only remember Krishna when it is Krishna’s desire to manifests himself within your mind. It is not by your endeavor. And Krishna manifests himself to the humble devotee who is in the proper service attitude. One may be very scholarly and always be talking about Krishna, but if he has proud and he does not have a proper service attitude, at the time of the greatest need, Krishna will not be there. At the time of death when your whole body is just full of pain and full of bewilderment and there are just endless distractions, all around you and within you, it is not possible to think of Krishna, unless Krishna reveals himself to you at that time. And Krishna reveals himself according to the humility of our attitude to serve. So therefore, the spiritual master and his representatives, they are meant to train us, how to think of Krishna. By training us how to attract Krishna to appear in our mind, by being a humble servant.
