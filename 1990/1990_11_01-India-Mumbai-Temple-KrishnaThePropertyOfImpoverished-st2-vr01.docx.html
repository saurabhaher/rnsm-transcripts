kvacit snāto ’nuliptāṅgaḥ

suvāsāḥ sragvy alaṅkṛtaḥ
rathebhāśvaiś care kvāpi
dig-vāsā grahavad vibho

[SB 7.13.41]

[O my lord, sometimes I bathe very nicely, smear sandalwood pulp all over my body, put on a flower garland, and dress in fine garments and ornaments. Then I travel like a king on the back of an elephant or on a chariot or horse. Sometimes, however, I travel naked, like a person haunted by a ghost.]

nāhaṁ ninde ca na staumi
sva-bhāva-viṣamaṁ janam
eteṣāṁ śreya āśāse
utaikātmyaṁ mahātmani

[SB 7.13.42]

[Different people are of different mentalities. Therefore it is not my business either to praise them or to blaspheme them. I only desire their welfare, hoping that they will agree to become one with the Supersoul, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Kṛṣṇa.]

Purport by his Divine Grace, Srila A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada:

Purport

As soon as one comes to the platform of bhakti-yoga, one understands fully that the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Vāsudeva, is the goal of life (vāsudevaḥ sarvam iti sa mahātmā sudurlabhaḥ). This is the instruction of all the Vedic literature (vedaiś ca sarvair aham eva vedyaḥ, sarva dharmān parityajya mām ekaṁ śaraṇaṁ vraja). There is no use in praising someone for material qualifications or blaspheming him for material disqualifications. In the material world, good and bad have no meaning because if one is good he may be elevated to a higher planetary system and if one is bad he may be degraded to the lower planetary systems. People of different mentalities are sometimes elevated and sometimes degraded, but this is not the goal of life. Rather, the goal of life is to become free from elevation and degradation and take to Kṛṣṇa consciousness. Therefore a saintly person does not discriminate between that which is supposedly good and supposedly bad; rather, he desires for everyone to be happy in Kṛṣṇa consciousness, which is the ultimate goal of life.

Lecture transcription

[Different people are of different mentalities. Therefore it is not my business either to praise them or to blaspheme them. I only desire their welfare, hoping that they will agree to become one with the Supersoul, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Kṛṣṇa.]

‘Different people are of different mentalities. Therefore it is not my business either to praise them or to blaspheme them. I only desire their welfare, hoping that they will agree to become one with the Supersoul, the Supreme Personality of Godhead.’

[SB 7.13.42]

A brahmana describes his past lives activities to Prahlada Maharaja

The great brahmana speaking with Prahlada Maharaja is sharing with us his realizations, the realizations by which hearing can enlighten one to detach oneself from the fruitive endeavors of this world and attach oneself only to the lotus feet of those great sadhus who are dedicated in the service to the lord. He is describing here…hu…how the spirit soul in its process of transmigrating through the eight million four hundred thousand species of life, has practically seen and considered everything. He is describing that, “I have been a king, living in palatial palaces, wearing crown, silken robes, fine ornaments, traveling on backs of elephants, or on beautiful chariots and horses.” And he describes that, “Sometimes I find myself travelling like a naked madman, people thinking that I’m haunted by a ghost.”

Indeed, in this world there are so many varieties of matters, of…of ways to enjoy or suffer. But after being through all of them one realizes the futility of material life. In fact, we read in Srimad Bhagavatam how the lord puts the living entity in a situation in this world, where he can see and consider all alternatives, until every last hope is exhausted and one re…and one is convinced that there’s no other shelter than Krishna. Then you have to continue traveling, experiencing newer varieties of methods to try to be happy.

Sri Narada Muni in past life as a maidservant’s son serves maha-bhagavatas

In the story of the young servant’s…um…the maidservant’s son, which is found in the [the] First Canto, it is described that he was just an ordinary maidservant’s boy, but by the grace of God he was given the association of sadhus. They were living in the forest and doing the Chaturmasya. A group of maha-bhagavatas, highly elevated spiritual personalities, happened to stay in the forest for those four months. And he had the opportunity of hearing from them and rendering service to them, even taking the sanctified prasad from their plates.

In fact, hearing from the great souls and rendering service to the great souls is the first and foremost process of purification. Sometimes we preach that the chanting of the holy name is the most important process in Kali-yuga. But factually, preliminary to chanting the holy names is to hear and render service to the great souls. Because it is through their mercy that we learn how to chant the holy names properly. It is said in Bhagavatam,

naṣṭa-prāyeṣv abhadreṣu
nityaṁ bhāgavata-sevayā
bhagavaty uttama-śloke
bhaktir bhavati naiṣṭhikī

[SB 1.2.18]

[By regular attendance in classes on the Bhāgavatam and by rendering of service to the pure devotee, all that is troublesome to the heart is almost completely destroyed, and loving service unto the Personality of Godhead, who is praised with transcendental songs, is established as an irrevocable fact.]

‘That by regularly hearing the Bhagavat philosophy and by rendering service to those persons who are enunciating Bhagavat philosophy by their life and words, then great service is done, and the lord in the heart, taking great pleasure in our activities, purifies us of all material contaminations and reveals himself from within our own heart.’

So this young maidservant’s son, hardly even literate, he was given the great fortune by the gift of God to hear from great souls and to become their menial servant. This is the process and the only process to make proper spiritual advancement - to hear from the great souls and to become their menial servant.

Narada becomes a menial servant and receives blessings

What does it mean to become menial servant? This young boy, he was not thinking himself of any qualification whatsoever. He was just thinking it was by the great mercy of God that he was being allowed to bring them some prasad, to clean up after them, to just ask, “Is there anything I can do for you?”, and do it. That is the perfection of life. No one should ever consider that his service is so great, so important, that it warrants credit. Because if you are thinking like that, that means you are no longer menial servant. And if you are not a menial servant, you have missed the whole point of Krishna consciousness. Menial means ‘I am nothing. Whatever little I can do for the devotees, that is the perfection of my life. That is the greatest opportunity I have in my life, just to do something to please the devotees.’

And because this child was so humble, in his submissive oral reception and his desire to serve, he got the heartfelt blessings from the sadhus. A sadhu cannot give his heartfelt blessings unless you approach him in this way. A sadhu is giving his blessings to everyone. But the heartfelt blessings come from one who actually is rec…a rec…a desiring recipient of that mercy. And it is only through meekness, humility, and meniality in one’s service that the heartfelt blessings of the sadhus comes into one’s life. And without those blessings we cannot approach God.

So therefore, when he received the heart…plus…felt blessings, then Krishna was satisfied with him and Krishna took over his life. Krishna established with him faith, and this child was thinking, ‘My God! I do not want any longer to just sit home and help my mother serve. The sadhus are gone. Now what am I going to do? I want to take to the path of God realization. I want to become a…a…a renunciant, one who is giving up everything for the service of Krishna. That’s what I want. But I’m in this predicament. I have to take care of my aged mother.”

So while he was thinking like that, by the arrangement of Krishna, who is in the heart of every living being,

īśvaraḥ sarva-bhūtānāṁ
hṛd-deśe ’rjuna tiṣṭhati
bhrāmayan sarva-bhūtāni
yantrārūḍhāni māyayā

[Bg 18.61]

Narada Muni sees Krishna’s arrangement even in unfavorable material situations

Krishna is directing everyone from within their heart. According to your faith and according to the arrangement he wants to make, he is directing everyone. That is why a devotee should come to the platform of complete fearlessness. Because the devotee has nothing to fear because he knows Krishna is in everyone’s heart. And if I’m surrendering to Krishna, Krishna will only allow and direct others to do what I need, what needs to be accepted for my purification.

So Krishna in the heart of a serpent struck his mother and killed her. Then this child, he was the only son. He was just a small boy. He had no one to protect him. He had no one to facilitate his livelihood. But he was thinking that, ‘Ah! Krishna is so kind that he has killed my mother. My mother, she’s obviously, because she has served the great souls, she’s going to some higher destination. There is no doubt about that. But now I am free to completely dedicate all my time to self-realization.’

And in this spirit of renunciation, which is actually the highest expression of love, even to his mother - a materialist cannot understand this. A materialist will think, ‘How hard-hearted! His mother is dead and he’s glad? How is this possible?’ You see, a saintly person, his compassion is upon the soul. He was fully aware, he was fully aware that, due to the association of the sadhus, the maha-bhagavatas, his mother would become a great devotee in her next life, if not go back to Godhead. And not only that, but he knew full well, by hearing from the great souls, that whatever spiritual advancement he makes his mother shares as well, wherever her soul may be. She gets the credit. So therefore, ‘If I am free to become completely self-realized, if I become perfectly pure, my mother gets liberation or goes back to Godhead. So therefore, the greatest thing that I can do for my mother is become Krishna conscious. And therefore, now I am completely free to become Krishna conscious.’

So he was feeling actually glad, not only that he was free to serve Krishna without impediments, but he was also feeling glad because he could do the greatest act of love in service as a son to a mother. In Sanskrit the word “putra” is the word for a son. Putra means one who delivers his forefathers from hellish conditions. When a son performs the proper oblations in the service of Lord Hari, when the son becomes purified through devotion to God, then whatever sinful karmic reactions his ancestor…or his forefathers are…are…may be suffering or enjoying, they are purified from all those things, and they become liberated in the state of Krishna consciousness.

The boy embarks on a great spiritual journey fully depending on Krishna

So therefore, this child simply left, and with complete faith in God, he began to travel. And where did he travel? Krishna within his heart wanted to give him a full experience of the world, of the material existence. He was simply watching. He was simply observing, trying to see how people were thinking, how people were living. Trying to see how Krishna was revealing himself in different circumstances within this world. So Krishna led him through the heart to the big metropolis cities. And he travelled and…and searched out all the alternatives within those cities. And then he went to the towns and traveled and saw what was taking place in the towns, looking for Krishna consciousness there. And then he went into the little villages. And from the little villages he traveled into the agricultural fields. From the agricultural fields he traveled into the forests. And from the forests he traveled into the deepest, darkest jungles.

Narada Muni’s realizations about material life

In this way he saw how all classes of men were living. In this way he understood full well through his own realization, that there is no alternative except taking complete shelter of Krishna for relief and happiness in this world. He saw the animals in the jungles, the simple…the simple field workers in the agricultural fields, the very simple life of the village people, the hard working life of the townspeople, and the very passionate people of the city. He saw they were all simply enjoying and suffering the results of their past karma. Therefore he could understand there was really no difference. He could see that the mode of goodness, the mode of passion, and the mode of ignorance are all just temporary situations, which afford different…different varieties of happiness and distress. But in essence it’s all the same. It’s all material. It’s all temporary.

Prahlada Maharaja has said, mitho ’bhipadyeta gṛha-vratānām adānta-gobhir viśatāṁ tamisraṁ punaḥ punaś carvita-carvaṇānām. [SB 7.5.30] That only the most foolish person remains within this human life attached to sense gratification, and he spends his valuable time simply chewing that which has already been chewed.

All the happinesses and distresses in this world, we’ve already had millions of times. And each time we’ve been frustrated by them and we’re still trying to find more. Prahlada Maharaja has described that this whole process of trying to find pleasure in this world is nothing but chewing that which has already been chewed, and chewed and spit out and picked up and chewed again. Therefore, a wise man becomes indifferent to sense gratification, and struggles and strives for the higher realizations of God consciousness.

So this maidservant’s son, he gave up all hope of happiness in this world. He had seen everything. As Narottam Das Thakur has said, “I have seen and considered this whole world, and without you, Krishna, no one and nothing can save me.” That was the realization of this boy.

The boy receives darshan of Lord Krishna and is later reborn as Narada Muni

And thus he sat down in meditation, in prayer, chanting the holy names, and meditating on the lord within his heart. And when Krishna saw that he had honestly and genuinely given up all hope for happiness anywhere except in him, at that time only did Krishna reveal himself. And when Krishna revealed himself, this child, ‘Ah, how beautiful! How wonderful! All my…all my desires are now fulfilled, simply by the darshan of the Supreme Personality of Godhead. I have… there’s nothing to be attained. It has been attained.’ And then the…the vision disappeared from his eyes, and he felt so alone in separation that, ‘Where is that lord of my life, who has so kindly appeared to me?’ And he began to perform the same meditation process again, seeking out the beauty of the lord within his heart, but to no avail.

And then the lord spoke to him. He heard the sweet voice of the lord within the silence of his own heart. And Bhagavan Sri Krishna spoke to him and told him that, “I am the Paramatma and in this life you will not see me again. But because you will have no taste for anything except me, you will be indifferent from all the pleasures and pains of this world, and you will always be longing to see me again. And this longing, this crying to see me, those tears of your longing to see me, will wash away all material contaminations and misconceptions within your heart, and in your next birth you will be my eternal associate. You will never forget me. You will see me everywhere and in everything.”

So that child, for the rest of his life, he simply longed for that vision of Krishna. He was constantly immersed in chanting his name and remembering the lord. And then that child in due course of time left his body and in the next day of Lord Brahma, he became the son of Brahma. And he is celebrated throughout all the Vedic literatures as the great Narada Muni, that pure soul whose only business is to enlighten others in the path of bhakti, devotion to Sri Hari.

Material happiness is hopeless

So note how he had seen and experienced so much of this world, and this avadhuta-brahmana is echoing the same message. He is describing that we have all seen and considers everything of this world, practically speaking. Why do we keep trying to chew that which has already been chewed? Why are we still trying to find relief and happiness through the process of sense gratification? How many times have you had sense gratification in this birth? What to speak in previous births? And how many times you are frustrated? How many times you are put in hopeless conditions? Sense gratification is hopeless. Material attachment is a hopeless condition. To put our hope in what is hopeless is the greatest illusion. Therefore, this great brahmana is describing how, because he has put his only hope in life in God, in the lotus feet of Krishna, and in the service of His devotees. Therefore, he is no longer interested in the phantasmagoria of the glitter of this material existence.

A saintly person sees people with equal vision

And he is describing here that in this world different people are of different mentalities. According to the three modes of material nature every person is unique in his consciousness. And therefore he says, “It is not my business to either praise them or blame them.” Someone who has material qualities, of course one who has advanced in spiritual qualities, all saintly persons, want to praise such a person. But as far as material qualities, he is saying “It is not my business to praise such people.” Because those material qualities, Prabhupada explains here, they’re all just very superficial. Whether one is elevated to a high position, or whether one is degraded to the lowest position, to a saintly person it makes very little difference.

vidyā-vinaya-sampanne
brāhmaṇe gavi hastini
śuni caiva śva-pāke ca
paṇḍitāḥ sama-darśinaḥ

[Bg 5.18]

[The humble sages, by virtue of true knowledge, see with equal vision a learned and gentle brāhmaṇa, a cow, an elephant, a dog and a dog-eater [outcaste].]

‘A saintly person sees a highly learned brahmana, an elephant, a cow, a dog, or the lowest…the lowest of humanity, candala - he sees them all with equal vision.’

He says that their particular position in society is just based on their karma. The brahmana is in the mode of goodness and the candala is in the mode of ignorance. And the various animals are in different degrees of passion and ignorance. He sees the soul in everyone’s heart and therefore, he is the well-wisher of every soul. Therefore, as far as he is concerned, there is no value in material qualifications, so why should I praise someone who has them? And there’s no fault in disqualifications. In fact, saintly persons see more glory in disqualifications than qualifications.

Krishna is easily obtained by the materially impoverished

Queen Kunti was a maha-bhagavata. Queen Kunti was such an elevated soul that she happened to be the very maternal aunt of Lord Sri Krishna. She was the sister of Vasudeva – Krishna’s…ah…paternal father. And therefore, her prayers are so full of devotion and realization that an entire chapter of the Srimad Bhagvatam – the Maha Purana - is dedicated only to her. And great paramhamsas have been chanting her prayers with love for thousands and thousands of years, being inspired by her realizations. And she is describing that, “My dear Lord Krishna, please cut all my attachments.”

As far as I am concerned, Krishna,

janmaiśvarya-śruta-śrībhir
edhamāna-madaḥ pumān
naivārhaty abhidhātuṁ vai
tvām akiṣcana-gocaram

[SB 1.8.26]

[My Lord, Your Lordship can easily be approached, but only by those who are materially exhausted. One who is on the path of [material] progress, trying to improve himself with respectable parentage, great opulence, high education and bodily beauty, cannot approach You with sincere feeling.]

The most fortunate people are those who are impoverished, because they are not proud. People with high education, with high birth, with physical beauty and with lots of money, their tendency is to become very proud. They lose the menial mentality that I am simply the nothing servant of Krishna. The more you know how great is Krishna (Krishna is everything), when you see everything, when you understand everything, in the form of the glory of God, then you realize that you are nothing (so infinitesimally small), that there’s nothing to say about yourself. There is nothing to think about yourself. Just think about Krishna. Think about how great is Krishna! As long as you think you are great you cannot understand Krishna’s greatness. But as re…as soon as you realize that you are nothing, then your heart is completely open to appreciate the glory and greatness of Krishna and the glory of his devotees.

So that is the fact - we are nothing. And if we think we’re something, then we’re more nothing than nothing. We’re less nothing than nothing. And that is the mentality that we should strive to acquire through the service to the lord and his devotees.

So therefore, what is there to praise or blame? Queen Kunti is saying that this false pride is such an impediment in spiritual life that, “I consider that you, my dear Lord Krishna, are akincana-gocaram. You are the property of the impoverished. You are never the property of the proud.” [The] Bible says, “Pride cometh before the fall.”

Even great kings may yet be materially impoverished

So therefore, what about great kings? The Bhagavatam is describing such powerful and distinguished personalities who were kings. In the Twelfth Canto of Srimad Bhagavatam, it describes what qualifications a literature must have to be an authorized Purana. And one of the qualifications is…it must tell great histories of great kings of the past. And we read that Srimad Bhagvatam is constantly talking about Maharaja Priyavrata, Maharaja Agnidro, Maharaja Rishabhdeva, Maharaja Bharata, Maharaja Ambarish, Maharaja Dhruva, Maharaja Prahlada, Maharaja Pariksit, Maharaja Yudhisthira, Maharaja Rantideva, Maharaja Ramachandra, Ugrasena. The list goes on and on and on. The Srimad Bhagavatam is filled with stories of great kings. And they were all, each and every one of these men, practically were pure devotees of Lord Hari.

So why Kunti is saying akincanam-gocaram? These are kings, they’re not impoverished. Krishna is the property only of those who are impoverished, she says. Because in their hearts they were impoverished. Although they were living in palatial buildings with great wealth, incredible intelligence, influence, power, popularity, in their hearts they always felt themselves impoverished, that ‘I am nothing! I am the most menial! I am the most insignificant! But Krishna, by his grace, he is putting me in this position. It’s all his. It’s all by the blessings of the sadhus that I am allowed to do this service, but personally I am nothing.’ This is the way all great devotees consider themselves, whatever situation they’re in.

A devotee should take the position of the menial servant of the servant

And therefore, a devotee only sees the humility in a person’s heart. He doesn’t care anything for anything else about a person. Everything else is superficial, external, and meaningless. A person only sees how one is…is willing to take the humble position of the menial servant of the servant of the servant. And he praises and glorifies that quality in another. And any other quality – it is nothing to praise. It’s all material. And that is what this great brahmana is saying: “I do not praise anyone for what they can do and I do not blame anyone for what they cannot do. This is all karma. This is all meaningless. I only see the surrender of a person.” That is what it means to be Krishna conscious. And a devotee simply sees the heart of another. And when he sees that in the heart, he glorifies, he adores that principle.

And that is what the avadhuta-brahmana is explaining here. He has no business either praising or blaming anyone for their material conditions of life. He only desires the welfare of all. And really, a sadhu must be on this platform. This is very important. Because if a saintly person is attracted by the facilities that others could give him, then he is no longer impartial. Then he’ll be merciful to the wealthy, but he…would, he can, he would, can’t…couldn’t care less about the poor. He’ll be very very anxious to help those are…who have distinction in society. But those who are illiterate and downtrodden, ‘I don’t have time for them.’

A devotee is the welfare worker of everyone

Therefore, a de…a real devotee of the lord, he is the welfare worker of everyone. And the supreme example of this was Lord Nityananda himself. When he would travel through the countryside, teaching people to take to the holy name of Lord Sri Krishna, he did not discriminate. He made no discrimination between who is rich and who is poor; between who is literate and who is illiterate; between who is a man and who is a woman; who is…who is old and who is young. He made no discrimination between who is famous and who is infamous. He did not even make the slightest discrimination between who is sinful and who is pious, who is religious and who is irreligious.

It is difficult to teach people who are proud of their piety

Sometimes today people are very proud of living in India and coming from brahmana families and being very pious and never breaking the principles. And sometimes they’re very difficult to preach to, these people, because they already know it all. Yes, they’ve never eaten meat, they’ve never had illicit sex, they’ve never taken intoxication, they have never…um…gambled. They have been doing the puja since their birth. And they are proud of it. They think that ‘I am very spiritually advanced. And what can you tell me. After all, I am…I am born in this culture.’

Suffering souls yearn for Krishna consciousness

Recently we were in Tompkins Square Park, the place where Prabhupada established Krishna consciousness. A place where practically the entire population is either drug addicts, alcoholics [break in recording at 00:34:22] brahmanas of India, in many ways. Because although they’re addicted to illicit sex, they’re addicted to drugs, they’re addicted to alcohol, they gamble their whole life away, and they…they…they know nothing but meat eating since their birth, since the conception in the womb - they’re basically suffering. They realize they’re downtrodden. They realize that they’re in…in hopeless conditions of life. Material nature is smashing them. And they just want, they…when they hear the message of Krishna consciousness, all…they just want to offer respect. They want to touch your feet. They want to…’We’re so thankful, we’re so grateful that you’re so kindly giving us another way to live.’ And they’ll chant, and they’ll dance. And they’ll read the books and they’ll preach and they’ll help and they…so many of them will surrender their lives.

A devotee is the well-wisher of everyone

So who’s more advanced? Therefore, Lord Nityananda did not see the difference between those who are sinful and those who are not sinful. He saw that every living entity is potentially a pure devotee of Krishna and therefore I have to give everybody the chance. Not that I reject the rich for the poor, nor do I reject the poor for the rich. In fact, he was even merciful to the animals, the lower species. ‘Give them prasad too. They deserve it much as anybody else.’ This is the quality of a saintly person. He’s equal to all. He simply wants to be the welfare servant of every living being. He wants to be the servant of the servant of the servant. He wants to be the humble servant of every creature on earth. He sees that the soul is longing for Krishna within everyone’s heart.

And all material qualifications that people are so busy praising and blaming people for - it is all just an illusion. It’s meaningless. Therefore, if a person comes to kill me, if a person comes to…to…to offer flowers to me, I do not see a difference in that person. I only see my service to that person. This is the way a saintly person thinks. My service is to somehow or other try to bring that person to chant the holy names. What he does to me, what his mentality is – that’s between him and Krishna. My service is to try to get him somehow or other to accept the name of Krishna in his life.

The translation of today’s verse is:

[Different people are of different mentalities. Therefore it is not my business either to praise them or to blaspheme them. I only desire their welfare, hoping that they will agree to become one with the Supersoul, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Kṛṣṇa.]

[SB 7.13.42]

Therefore a devotee is the well-wisher of everyone. He is simply praying for everyone’s welfare. He doesn’t see anyone as his friend or enemy. He sees everyone as his prabhu, his master. That is Krishna consciousness.

Questions and Answers

Is there any questions?

[Maharaja:] The sadhu is not discriminating, the sadhu wants…in the sense that the sadhu wants to see everybody like that. That is the only quality that a sadhu appreciates. Because he knows that is the only quality that is pleasing to Krishna. That is the only quality that really brings one to spiritual advancement. That quality is opulence on the spiritual level. Material opulence to a saintly person, Krishna says, he sees no difference between gold and the dirt on the ground. People are very anxious to decorate themselves with gold rings and gold jewelry. But a sadhu sees that there is no difference between a clump of dirt, which everybody can just pick up and throw away, and the…and the beautiful gold ornaments that people are taking such care to decorate themselves with. There’s no difference. Because he says material…he sees that material qualifications have nothing to do with [the] reality of the soul. They cannot bring you closer to Krishna. The only thing that can bring you closer to Krishna is the humble attitude of service. Therefore he sees that humility, that service attitude, as the most glorious opulence. And he appreciates that. He praises that in another. He doesn’t praise any other quality.

[devotee:] [00:40:00 – 00:40:13 inaudible]

[Maharaja:] The chanting is a prayer to engage us in his service. The service is always there. The service is eternal. There are nine processes of devotional service.
