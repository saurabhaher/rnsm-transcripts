nāsti buddhir ayuktasya
na cāyuktasya bhāvanā
na cābhāvayataḥ śāntir
aśāntasya kutaḥ sukham

[Bg. 2.66]

Translation

One who is not connected with the Supreme [in Kṛṣṇa consciousness] can have neither transcendental intelligence nor a steady mind, without which there is no possibility of peace. And how can there be any happiness without peace?

Purport

Unless one is in Kṛṣṇa consciousness, there is no possibility of peace. So it is confirmed in the Fifth Chapter (5.29) that when one understands that Kṛṣṇa is the only enjoyer of all the good results of sacrifice and penance, that He is the proprietor of all universal manifestations, and that He is the real friend of all living entities, then only can one have real peace. Therefore, if one is not in Kṛṣṇa consciousness, there cannot be a final goal for the mind. Disturbance is due to want of an ultimate goal, and when one is certain that Kṛṣṇa is the enjoyer, proprietor and friend of everyone and everything, then one can, with a steady mind, bring about peace. Therefore, one who is engaged without a relationship with Kṛṣṇa is certainly always in distress and is without peace, however much he may make a show of peace and spiritual advancement in life. Kṛṣṇa consciousness is a self-manifested peaceful condition which can be achieved only in relationship with Kṛṣṇa.

Lecture Transcription

Translation: One who is not connected with the Supreme [in Kṛṣṇa consciousness] can have neither transcendental intelligence nor a steady mind, without which there is no possibility of peace. And how can there be any happiness without peace?

Peace and pleasure

Peace is something that everyone is searching for in this world, in their own way. Pleasure is the motivating force within everyone’s life. If we analyze scrutinizingly whatever we do, the foundation of that motivation is the pursuance of pleasure. The Bhagavad-gita describes that there are three gunas, there are three modes of material nature: sattva guna, rajo guna, tamo guna. Goodness, passion, and ignorance. And according to these three modes of material nature different classes of living beings are seeking pleasure in different ways. Those in the mode of ignorance are seeking pleasure through intoxication, through sleep, through violent and cruel acts upon others. Those in the mode of passion are seeking pleasure through amassing great fortunes, accumulating material prestige, indulging in sensual activities; such as eating, sleeping, having sex life. This is pleasure for those in the mode of passion - name, fame and physical enjoyment. And for those in the mode of goodness, they take pleasure in doing good for others, the feeling that I have given in charity, the feeling that I have uplifted a friend, the feeling that I have lent my helping hand for another’s benefit in life. But in all cases we are looking pleasure.

Importance of Bhagavad-Gita

But Bhagavad-gita is a very great and important scripture in this world because it is explaining, where real pleasure is to be found, where real peace is to be found. And It is Krishna’s only business to come here in this material world to show the way to real peace and pleasure. Therefore, it is said in the fourth chapter by Sri Krishna,

paritrāṇāya sādhūnāṁ
vināśāya ca duṣkṛtām
dharma-saṁsthāpanārthāya
sambhavāmi yuge yuge

[Bg. 4.8]

[To deliver the pious and to annihilate the miscreants, as well as to reestablish the principles of religion, I myself appear, millennium after millennium.]

I come to this world in order to re-establish the principles of religion, protect the pious, and annihilate the miscreants.

yadā yadā hi dharmasya
glānir bhavati bhārata
abhyutthānam adharmasya
tadātmānaṁ sṛjāmy aham

[Bg. 4.7]

[Whenever and wherever there is a decline in religious practice, O descendant of Bharata, and a predominant rise of irreligion – at that time I descend myself.]

And why does he do like this? To establish an opportunity for all to attain peace, real pleasure in life. ahaṁ bīja-pradaḥ pitā [Bg. 14.4] Krishna is the father of all living beings. The father’s greatest concern is the peace, the happiness, the pleasure of his children. Is it not? That is the nature of a father.

Just yesterday I was with one very nice man. He just had a baby child. It was just one-month back baby was born. So, he was explaining what should I know, now I have this baby? What to do with him? This is a little boy. I told him, “What you have to know, is you have to know the responsibility you have now. You did not know what you are getting into when you engaged in whatever you have to do to have this child. Because now you have the most tremendous responsibility you have ever had.” He is saying, “But I am devotee, I can be detached from him.” I said, “What does it mean to be detached from him? That you do not understand.” Sometimes devotees are like this, ‘Ha! Ha! God will protect him. I am a devotee; I am detached’. The materialists, they are attached to their children but devotees are detached. That is true but we have to understand from Bhagavad-gita, what it means to be detached? To be detached means that you are attached to the service, but you are detached from the results for your own personal enjoyment. Arjuna’s duty was, he was a warrior. Krishna told him, “You must fight. You must fight better than anybody else. You must fight with care, with conviction, with all of your intelligence, all of your strength, all of your energy; but fight for me. Do not fight for you. Do not fight according to your whims, fight according to my direction. This is the responsibility that I have given you.” Isn’t that what Krishna is teaching Arjuna in Gita?

Responsibility

So, when you have a child, Krishna is giving you that child and with the child he is giving you the responsibility, pitā na sa syāj jananī na sā syāt [SB 5.5.18] that this child is my child, not yours. You are the secondary father, but I am the primary father. And of the two who has the last word? Who has the ultimate proprietorship? You are only a father on his behalf, but he is the father. And therefore, you have a great responsibility to be a father on his behalf. To be a father on his behalf means to raise that child according to his will, not according to your will, not according to your whims and you must be thoroughly attached to doing that. If you neglect your duty, if you neglect your responsibility, you are not on the path of God consciousness. Your life is in your hands. Your life is in the hands of the bitter cruel laws of nature – karma; action and reaction.

Krishna is the supreme father

Therefore, this is such a responsibility. You must behave in such a way that this child will be the best of all human beings, narottam, best human being. You must give him the best of everything by your example in life. Therefore, if you are attached to any vices, you are ruining his life and you are insincere in your service to God. So, in this way it’s a great responsibility. So, this is the duty of every father. But Krishna is the supreme father and he is such a loving and caring father that he comes to this world just to give us the way by which we can become happy. By his example and by his words, he teaches us the perfect way of life, by which we can find ultimate pleasure. Just like you as a father or you as a mother, must not by your words, but by your example show your children how they can lead a life by which they can attain eternal enjoyment, eternal pleasure. That is the real duty.

Krishna is such a loving and caring father. The only pain that Krishna or his representatives experience is the pain of others. It is said, para-dukha-dukhi. It is not that when you become an advanced devotee, you become hard like stone. You become soft like butter. But what does that mean? That means you don’t care anything about your own whimsical mind’s demands, but you are very much concerned for the welfare of others. You can judge how purified a person is by how much a person is willing to reach out to uplift others spiritually. And of course the supreme most merciful friend is Krishna himself.

Compassion of Lord Chaitanya

I would like to give an example of how Krishna himself demonstrated the supreme manifestation of compassion upon others. When Lord Krishna appeared as Sri Chaitanya Mahaprabhu, it is described that he was surrounded by the most intimate associates living in the land of Navadvipa. His wife was none other than Lakshmi Devi, the Goddess of fortune. He had a sweet loving mother. He was living a very peaceful, beautiful life surrounded by so many loving friends, companions and relatives. But what happened is, he noticed that the people of this world, they were suffering; they were following religious paths without understanding love of God. Therefore, all the religion was simply a waste, also much uselessness. Most of their religion was just to worship God or the Gods for the purpose of material prosperity. And therefore, no peace, no happiness, no pleasure.

And of course, Navadvipa was the high seat of learning at the time. People were very much attached to studying the Vedas, studying the scriptures for the sake of learning, not for the sake of purification. Just like sometimes we have these debates. There’s two types of debaters. One is trying to establish truth for the benefit of others, and one is trying to establish that I have conquered, I have won, I am the best. Doesn’t matter whether you are right or wrong, you are only trying to establish yourself. I will take the right side, I will take the wrong side, it doesn’t matter, the main thing is, I can beat you and therefore that is my goal; to defeat, to conquer. And through time immemorial, in those days there was a Keshava Kashmiri, he was one of these types of debaters. He would just travel from place to place just defeating everyone and getting their signatures, but ultimately, he met defeat at the hands of Nimai pandit.

But one who is really concerned, he studies the shastra for the sake of establishing truth, that I know nothing, I am nothing, I am simply a representative of the truth, a servant of the truth for the purpose of uplifting others. But in Navadvipa and throughout India at that time people were simply studying the scriptures for their own false ego, to be the greatest pandit, to be the greatest grammatical word juggler, to narrate discourses and speeches for their own aggrandizement. People were following tantric arts. Lord Sri Chaitanya was thinking ‘What am I to do?’ And now they are criticizing me. They are thinking I am just an ordinary householder like the rest of them. Therefore, they will not hear what I have to say. I have descended especially to deliver them by convincing them of the glory of the holy name, to give them real peace, real pleasure, but they are not willing to hear. Therefore, he decided that in order for people to take him seriously, he would have to take a very drastic measure in his own life and accept the sannyasa, the renounced order, then he would be separate. People would not think; he is just like us. They would have to offer him obeisances, if not out of love but out of formality and by just offering him obeisances they would become purified.

Lord Chaitanya takes sannyas

So, he made this plan. And it is described, it was on the night of the makara sankrant…sankranta, that night of the auspicious constellation in the month of January or Magha. He decided, he would leave home. So, on that night by his own sweet will, he inspired the devotees from within their hearts to all to come to visit his house. They all came with garlands; they all came with prasad. they did not know he is leaving that night. But they all came, and they would give him garland, he would take the garland off and gave it back to them. In this way they exchanged their love. And he appealed to all of them that if you love me, take the name of Krishna everywhere you go and in everything you do. Whatever you do, do it along with the name of Krishna. The name of Krishna is your constant friend, your constant companion. He is the supreme deliverer from all the miseries and pains in this world. Have faith, have trust in God’s name. Have faith and trust in God’s devotees. Lord Chaitanya told them that, “You chant - hare kṛṣṇa hare kṛṣṇa kṛṣṇa kṛṣṇa hare hare/hare rāma hare rāma rāma rāma hare hare. This is maha mantra. This is not an ordinary mantra. This mantra was especially given by God for the sake of delivering even the most sinful and falling living creatures in this age of Kali-yuga. It is the mantra specially in this age that can uplift the lowest and bring them to the highest. So, always remember the name of Krishna and always chant Hare Krishna! And then he embraced each and every one of them and they were all thinking ‘Oh! how wonderful it is that living right amongst us is this most beautiful and merciful personality.’

Glories of Kolavecha Sridhar

Late that night one devotee, his name was Kolavecha Sridhar. He came very late with a pumpkin and offered it to Lord Chaitanya. Kolavecha Sridhar was very poor man. He was so poor he could hardly feed himself or his family. He lived in utter poverty. But he was always experiencing the highest pleasure by chanting the holy name of Krishna. It is explained that in the night he would just be crying out, hare kṛṣṇa hare kṛṣṇa kṛṣṇa kṛṣṇa hare hare/hare rāma hare rāma rāma rāma hare hare, and he would be just crying out the names all night long in love, in divine ecstasy. And the local people all around who are very materialistic, they would be thinking ‘Why you are keeping us awake with this loud chanting?’ They were thinking ‘He is chanting all night so loudly because he is so hungry, because he is a devotee of Krishna. If he was a devotee of Durga or devotee of Kali, mataji would give him so much wealth and so much nice material happiness. But because he is a devotee of Krishna, Krishna has taken everything away from him therefore he is just howling in night because of hunger pains in his stomach.’ So, they would throw food at him and say, “Here eat and keep your mouth shut and let us sleep.” He wasn’t concerned with food or no food. He was simply concerned with loving Krishna by chanting his name. So, that night Kolavecha came and brought him a pumpkin, which was a great donation and Lord Chaitanya was thinking ‘Throughout most of my life, I have been stealing things from this man, arguing with him, taking things from him and now he is giving me this pumpkin. Such a loving devotee.’ So, he told his mother Sachi, he said, “Please prepare this pumpkin, cook it in milk.” So, she made sweet rice with pumpkin. She took milk and rice and sugar and cooked it with this pumpkin. And Lord Chaitanya ate that, and then he took rest, and everyone was sleeping.

And Sachi mata was thinking in her heart, she was feeling a fear that ‘Just as my previous son had disappeared in the middle of night and taken sannyasa, I fear that Nimai will do the same thing to night.’ And this is her greatest anxiety. Her husband died out of fear of this only. So, she stood guarding the door that night. And at three o’clock in the morning as Vishnupriya, his wife was sleeping. She was only fourteen. He was only twenty-four. He got up from his bed and when he came to the door and saw his mother, he offered his obeisances to his mother and walked out and she was frozen like stone; she knew exactly what her son was doing but she became paralyzed. She could not move. She was stricken with such intense pain of separation.

And after that he went to Ganges, he took his bath in the Ganges and with his wet clothes, he ran to Katwa, the holy city of the great sannyasi, Keshava Bharati. And it was there that he was planning to accept sannyasa, the renounced order of life. He ran and he ran, and he ran. He finally reached Katwa at about nine o’clock in the morning. Meanwhile when the devotees of Navadvipa woke up, like regular they came to the house of Sachi mata in order to greet Lord Chaitanya and offer their obeisances to him and they saw Sachi mata standing at the door, frozen, paralyzed. She told them you take over this house, it’s no longer mine, it is yours. I’ll go off somewhere and disappear from this world. They said, “What do you mean?” She said, “You are his devotees, you take this house. It is his property.” They said, “No! you must take care of his wife. You must take care of things. We need you.” And then gradually they understood that he had gone to Katwa to take sannyasa. It was like a thunder bolt in their heart. We will never see him again. We will never hear from him again. They began to fall in the ground, crying, weeping. Many of them immediately started running as fast as they could, to Katwa.

Meanwhile Lord Chaitanya arrived, Keshava Bharati Maharaja was standing on the bank of the Ganges. And he saw an effulgence coming from the horizon. He was thinking ‘What is this light that is coming?’ And then he saw that coming from the body of this young man was an all-pervading effulgence. He understood that this is Nimai of Navadvipa. And Nimai Pandit approached him and offered his obeisances and he said, “Today I want to accept sannyasa from you.” Keshava Bharati Maharaja, he was thinking ‘Sannyasa? You? You are so young. You are so beautiful. You have the most lovely chaste, the most loving and the most caring wife and the most loving and caring mother and you have so many friends. How can you give all these things up, for what? I’ll not give you sannyasa. It is not possible.’ Lord Chaitanya said, “No, you must, please.” He was appealing to him. Because that day was an auspicious day. Many-many people by the thousands were coming to get bath in Ganges. And when they saw that Lord Chaitanya was appealing to Keshava Kashmiri [Bharati] to give him sannyasa, they were terrified by this idea. They approached Keshava Kashmiri [Bharati] Maharaja, they said, “If you give this man sannyasa, you are condemned. We will not permit it. It is not possible. He has a wife. He has a mother. He is so young. He is so beautiful. Let him enjoy his life. Sannyasa is a life of rigid, umm… tapasya, of poverty. This man is not meant for this. He is meant for enjoyment only. This is irreligious. You cannot do this. If you give this man sannyasa, we will destroy your ashram, we will tear down your temple, we will tear down everything.” There was a revolt. There was revolution amongst all the people.

And meanwhile Nimai was saying, “No, no, no, you must give me sannyasa. It is very important. I have a mission.” And Keshava Kashmiri [Bharati] didn’t know what to do? So, finally he said, “Ha! How can I give you? You are so young. You are so beautiful. First you go to your mother, and you get her permission written and then you comeback, then I’ll give you sannyasa.” Lord Chaitanya immediately started running back to Navadvipa. And Keshava Kashmiri [Bharati] was thinking ‘My God, he is such a powerful personality, he will convince his mother to write the letter. So, why should…he is…he is such a great…he is God. Why should I make him run all the way there and back?’ So, he said “No, no, no, you do not have to run, stay here.” So, there was a great argument. Everyone, hundred percent of the people were totally against it, making threats, with their hearts, with their souls, they were objecting, he cannot. You must not. And Keshava Kashmiri [Bharati] was getting one order from the Lord and threats from everybody else. So, he did not know what to do.

So, at that time Lord Chaitanya seeing the revolution that was just about to take place, he began to chant and to dance, he performed a beautiful kirtan. In this way everybody started to chant and dance with him. By this time, thousands and thousands and thousands of people were gathered around. They were all chanting and dancing with the Lord. And the beauty of his dance and the sweetness of his voice was charming everyone’s hearts and everyone’s anxieties disappeared. And they chanted throughout the entire night in kirtan and the next morning their hearts were so much melted by the sweetness of the Lord’s kirtan, that Lord Chaitanya had no more anybody to object to his desire. So, Keshava Bharati Maharaja said, “Now, first you must shave Your head.”

So, on the bank of Ganges Lord Chaitanya asked, “Who will shave my head?” As soon as people looked at his hair, it was the most beautiful hair in all of creation on the most beautiful head in all of creation. It was utterly impossible for anyone to even conceive of this beautiful hair to be shaved. “No…no it is not possible. It cannot be.” So, Lord Chaitanya understood none of my friends, they will not do. None of my devotees, they cannot do. So, he called for a professional barber. He said, “It is your duty, you must.” He said, “It is my duty.” So, he went with the scissors and he approached and when he saw the beauty of his blackish curling locks of transcendental hair on his most beautiful golden form, he said, “No, no, I cannot touch you. Not possible. Impossible. This cannot be done.” Lord Chaitanya ordered him, Nimai. You must. So, again he approached, and his eyes began to fill with tears, he could not even see. Nimai said, “You must.” And then when he touched his hair and he put his scissors, he fell unconscious. It is not possible. I cannot do this. Please do not make me do this. And meanwhile all the devotees around and all the people around by the thousands they were begging, “Please my Lord, this cannot be done. You cannot do this.” And the Lord ordered the barber, “You must cut.”

So, he began to cut the beautiful hair of the Lord. And as the hair began to fall to the ground, the people around, to think that ‘The supreme Lord, the most beautiful, loving of all personalities, he is going to live such a life; a life of poverty and tapasya. It is beyond our imagination. How is this possible?’ And they began to cry, to wail, to scream. They were falling down unconscious and conscious, then unconscious and conscious. They were crying out, stop! This cannot be done. Others were crying out, screaming out, “Who is so cruel, who has created this sannyasa order of life. For a person, completely abandons his relatives, all his friends, and leaves them helplessly crying, who leaves everything near and dear and accepts a life of a beggar, poverty. This sannyasa order of life should never been created. It is created by someone only who must have a very hard stone like heart. Why must this be?” And meanwhile, as the hair was falling to the ground, when his head was half shaved, Lord Chaitanya maddened in transcendental ecstasy, just leaped in the air and began to dance in kirtan, uncontrollably.

And then he sat down again, and they completed the function. And the barber as he was cutting, his hand was trebling, as he was shaving, his hand was trembling, tears just erupting from his eyes, his heart breaking with every hair that fell from the Lord’s head. Then he made a vow. He said, “With this hand I’ll never again touch another man’s hair, as long as I live.” From that day on, he renounced his profession as a barber, and he became a maker of sweetmeats.

Nimai Pandit as Sri Krishna Chaitanya

And then Lord Chaitanya took his…Nimai Pandit took his bath in the holy Ganges river. And then he went to his Guru Maharaja, Keshava Bharati. They sat under a tree, which is close to the Guru Maharaja’s ashram. And the Lord whispered in his ear that, “Last night I had a dream, and this is the mantra that was told to me in the dream. Is this the mantra that you want to give me to initiate me in sannyasa?” And then Keshava Bharati Maharaja said, “Yes, this is the mantra.” And then he repeated the mantra he had heard from the Lord back into his ear. And from that time this is the mantra of all sannyasis in the line of Sri Chaitanya. And then Keshava Bharati Maharaja in front of everybody, he declared, to everyone’s great surprise that, “Your new sannyasa name will be Sri Krishna Chaitanya.” Now this is very unconventional, because Keshava Kashmiri [Bharati] Maharaja was a sannyasi in the line of Shankaracarya. And the sannyasis in the line of Shankaracarya are very strict. That they have ten names of sannyasis. And the name Chaitanya is the name of a brahmacari servant of a sannyasi. So, why did he give him this name?

Meaning of Krishna Chaitanya

Keshava Bharati Maharaja described, because your great mission my Lord is, you have descended to invoke the consciousness of love of Krishna in everyone. Krishna means, ‘Krishna, the Supreme Personality of Godhead’ and Chaitanya means ‘Consciousness’. So, Krishna Chaitanya means ‘Krishna Consciousness’. This is your mission, this is your life, to give Krishna consciousness to everyone. Therefore, your name will be Sri Krishna Chaitanya. And there was one man that happened to be there. He was the classmate of Nimai Pandit in Navadvipa. And he happened to be on his way to his father in law’s home. And on the way he was passing through Katwa. And he heard that Nimai Pandit is here and today he is taking sannyasa. And he was thinking ‘My God this is the most horrible thing. How could Nimai Pandit take sannyasa? Our life and soul is seeing him enjoy the beauty of his house, the beauty of his wife, the beauty of his mother’s love, the beauty of his friends. How can he do this and leave us. Everything.’ So, he was there. And he was one of those great souls who was crying and weeping and wailing as the hair of the Lord was falling to the ground and when he heard Keshava Kashmiri [Bharati] announce that, “Your new name is Sri Krishna Chaitanya”, these words like a thunderbolt struck his heart. And he just left that place in a day in a maddened state, constantly just chanting, “Sri Krishna Chaitanya, Sri Krishna Chaitanya, Sri Krishna Chaitanya.” And he went back home. And he couldn’t do anything. He was just simply saying, “Sri Krishna Chaitanya, Sri Krishna Chaitanya.” So, they named him ‘Chaitanya Das’. Because he could do nothing but think of Chaitanya’s name. Do nothing but say Chaitanya’s name. And ultimately his son was Srinivas Acharya, who is one of the greatest-greatest proponents of the philosophy of Lord Chaitanya in all of history.

Love of God

So, in this way Lord Chaitanya after this grand and auspicious ceremony, he became so joyful that, “Now I could just give all of my energy simply to uplifting others, to creating the love of God from the hearts of all living beings.” So, Lord Chaitanya and his Guru Maharaja both leaped in the air and began to chant and dance to the holy names of Krishna. So why…why the husband of the Goddess of fortune renounced her? Out of his love for his children. The Lord himself is willing to endure even separation from his own loved ones for the sake of uplifting those who are suffering the pains of material nature. And therefore, this is also the quality of his devotees. They are here on his behalf. They are here simply for the sake of you and me. They are here simply to uplift our painful suffering condition of birth and death. The most painful thing for a transcendentalist to see, is a person who is about to die and that includes a hundred percent of everyone in this world and they are under the illusion that they are happy. Someone who knows that they are suffering, they are not is in…in his painful condition as someone who is enjoying material life. There is no one more fortune…more unfortunate than someone who is enjoying material life and forgetfulness of God. It is better to suffer, because at least in that suffering you can turn to God. Anything that helps us turn to God is the greatest benediction in life. Because there is no truth, there is no reality except that. Everything else is grand illusion which ultimately ends in pain and death. So, this is the love of the Lord.

Separation is intense experience of love

Lord Jesus Christ accepted crucifixion for the upliftment of others. Lord Chaitanya Mahaprabhu accepted this life of an ascetic for the upliftment of others. This is the nature of the Lord and his devotees. Then one may ask, “If he’s come to give love then what about the inhabitants of Navadvipa? His own devotees, his own relatives, he left them.” It seems like a contradiction. He is come to give love, to give happiness, to give pleasure, but those who loved him most, is given them nothing but grief.

Ha! this is another subject matter. Actually, his purpose, the Lord’s purpose is always perfect and complete in whatever he does, especially in relationship to his devotees. There is no deficiency, there is nothing undecided when the Lord acts in relationship to his devotees. For those who are forgetful of the Lord, he accepted the renounced order to reach out to them, to gain their respect and to enlighten them with the transcendental vibration of the holy name of Krishna. And to those who loved him so dearly, his mother, his wife, his friends… Understand that Lord Chaitanya is Krishna himself. He is avatar. He understood that by accepting the renounced order of life, he would increase their love through the process of separation. Separation is the most intense experience of love. And even in this world we can feel this.

When you love someone and they are next to us, we take them for granted. Do we not? We think ‘Yes, yes, I am very happy. I am thinking of you; I am feeling for you.’ But the extent of your love is the intensity of your thought of that person. When that person is away, our hearts are driven to such inner deep meditation on that person, and if our sentiment is love in separation, our love can only grow. Therefore, Lord Chaitanya specially taught us, that we should cultivate the feelings of separation from Krishna. The feelings of meeting with Krishna is not as important as cultivating feelings of separation. Crying out Krishna, when will that day be, when I will be yours. Just like Narottam Das Thakur prays –

viṣaya chāriyā kabe śuddha ha 'be mana
kabe hāma herabo śrī-bṛndābana

[Gauranga Bolite Habe 3]

[When the mind is completely purified, being freed from material anxieties and desires, then I shall be able to understand Vrndavana and the conjugal love of Radha and Krsna, and then my spiritual life will be successful.]

We do not find the great souls saying, “Oh yes, I see Krishna. Krishna is dancing with me at night. Yesterday Krishna was playing his flute for me. The day before Krishna appeared to me and gave me some laddus.” The great souls, they are praying, “When will that day be mine, when I will finish all material desires, then and only then will I see Vrindavan. Krishna, where are you? he rādhe vraja-devīke ca lalite he nanda-sūno kutaḥ [Śrī Ṣaḍ Gosvāmy Aṣṭaka 8]. When will that day be mine? When all of my false ego, all of my pride, all of my selfish desires will be dissolved in the name of Krishna and then and only then will I taste the sweetness of love of God.” This is the prayer of the real great souls of the mature Vaishnavas.

Praying for the dust of the feet of the Vaishnavas

And the greater the devotee, the greater the feeling of separation, because separation is far more intimate. Even in connection and is much safer, because Radha and Krishna’s divine lila is very confidential. To think of meeting Krishna and meeting Radha, it is a very elevated subject matter but to cultivate the feelings of separation, to long for that separation, there is no danger. There is a class of Vaishnavas called sahajiyas, to take things very cheaply. Yes, I’ll dance with Krishna. Yes, I’ll be kissing with Krishna. This, you cannot be thinking like this. This is offensive. How in our contaminated state can we think like this? [break] hard like a rock. Lord Chaitanya himself, what was his mood of love, he said, “My Lord, I do not even have one drop of love for you in my hard heart because I am not seeing you and if I have one drop of love for you, I could not live one moment without seeing you. The fact that I am breathing, the fact that my heart is still beating means I have no love for you.” Crying out, “Please my Lord, give me just one drop of love, one drop of devotion to you. I have nothing.” This is the greatest of the devotees think. So, we should be praying for that. Praying for the dust of the feet of the Vaishnavas, please, this is the most valuable wealth, the most valuable possession there is, because it is only by serving the great souls these feelings of separation from Krishna will awaken in my heart. The price of love of God is your eagerness to want it. Love of God is not something that comes like some business arrangement where you pay the price and you get what you pay for. You do not get what you pay for. In justice in this material world you get punished according to your bad deed and you get rewarded according to your good deed; that is justice. For one, we are talking about the mercy of God, there is no question of justice. The mercy of God is causeless. There is only one price, your eagerness, your willingness to long for it. And how do you express that? Through your service, through your sacrifice in your service. The more we are willing to do anything, we run to the temple to see the Lord. We run to the temple to hear these transcendental glories of the pravachan of the Lord. We run to the temple to hear the kirtan of the name of the Lord. We run to our beads every morning to chant the holy names. We run to our altar to offer some bhoga. We run to accept the divine remnants of prasad. To the degree we are eager for these things, eager to assist the devotees, eager to help the Lord, eager to help his mission, to that degree love of God will be ours. You cannot be complacent.

What is a lukewarm?

Jesus says in the Bible that, “Either you be hot or cold, but if you are lukewarm, I spit you out.” What does it mean to be lukewarm? Ha! I have my religion. Yes, yes, I know Krishna very well. Yes, I know what religion means. You have a little here but then you have everything else and if you have some time for God then you give it. That’s lukewarm! No one can attain love of God through that. You have to be intense; you have to be eager, you have to hanker, you have to long, you have to be willing to give everything for that. If you are not willing to do so, you are not worthy. Even if you are the most sinful of all sinners, the Lord does not even consider those things. He does not care for your piety or impiety; he is only concerned with your eagerness. And in that eagerness, of course, you become transcendental, you become the most pious of all pious persons. Because you will do not…not do anything that will come between you and your devotion to God. So, forget what you have done. Forget what’s been in the past. This world, it is like a bridge. We must cross over it. We shouldn’t make a permanent place of residence on a bridge. Right? A bridge is a place to cross over to go from one side to another, from the land of illusion to the land of ecstasy and sweetness and love, to the land of Krishna. So, whatever comes within our life, we have to accept everything as an opportunity to deepen our love, deepen our faith, deepen our devotion, deepen our eagerness for that feeling of longing and separation for God. This is the great gift that has been given by the Lord and his devotees. And, it is for this reason that the Lord appears in this world. paraṁ dṛṣṭvā nivartate [Bg. 2.59] When you experience the higher taste of love of Krishna then all the pains, all the frustrations, all the anxieties of this material life become insignificant. We should not be so much concerned. Krishna is the supreme controller, he knows what is best, whatever he does is out of love for everyone.

The divine hand of Krishna

Everyone is going where they should be going. If a person is sinful, Krishna is sending that person to a place where that person can ultimately be purified for his sins. That is his love. If a person is pious, Krishna will put that person in a place where he could gradually become purified from his piety. But if a person is a devotee, if a person has the blessings of the great souls, then Krishna will not deal with that person ordinarily. He guarantees, he assures that person will be elevated in all circumstances of life. So therefore, we must see the divine hand of Krishna leading our every step in life and we must have the conviction, the faith that this world is no place for a gentleman. To further implicate myself with the lethargic spirit of complacency in my spiritual life, by just taking my spiritual life as something on the side and all my other responsibilities are something so important that means, we are cheating ourselves royally. We are the fool number ones of this world. A person who has nothing, has nothing to lose. But a person who is given the gift, the jewel of Krishna consciousness and waste it, utilizing his time for the…for the cheap garbage of material life, that person is the greatest fool, the most unfortunate soul in all of creation. So, let us take the advantage of the mercy of the Lord. Let us be eager, more eager and most eager for Krishna consciousness.

And to inspire that eagerness in our heart the Lord has come, and he has come in the form of his name to reciprocate and deliver us back home back to Godhead.

hare kṛṣṇa hare kṛṣṇa kṛṣṇa kṛṣṇa hare hare/hare rāma hare rāma rāma rāma hare hare.

Thank you, very much.

And to the degree that we all come together to share the blessing of the Lord to that degree we could experience real friendship and real love. So, we would like to thank all of you for sharing your love and your kindness by coming. And please come as much as you can and let us perform what is sankirtan. Sankirtan means to come together as a congregation to glorify the Lord, Sri Sri Radha Gopinath.

Hare Krishna!
