Lecture Transcription

Six Characteristics of Pure Devotional Service

Thereis a very beautiful poem, which was written by one of the great acharyas of the Gaudiya Vaishnava-sampradāya. [indistinct] [0:17-0:29 inaudible] His name is Srinivasa Acarya. In this poem, he is glorifying the qualities of the six gosvamis of Vrindavana. There he prays, ‘nānā-śāstra-vicāraṇaika-nipuṇau sad-dharma-saḿsthāpakau’[Śrī Ṣaḍ Gosvāmy Aṣṭaka 3]. He describes that the six gosvamis, they very very scrutinizingly studied all the vedic literatures and they extracted the essence of all the spiritual principles. Indeed, they concern themselves exclusively with the conclusion, the siddhanta of all knowledge. Krishna explains in the Gita,

bahūnāṁ janmanām ante
jṣānavān māṁ prapadyate
vāsudevaḥ sarvam iti
sa mahātmā su-durlabhaḥ

[Bg. 7.19]

[After many births and deaths, he who is actually in knowledge surrenders unto Me, knowing Me to be the cause of all causes and all that is. Such a great soul is very rare.]

That, after many many births and deaths of following the principles of religion, when finally one comes to the platform of the highest knowledge, he surrenders unto Vasudeva, knowing Vasudeva to be the cause of all causes and all that is. Similarly, Krishna concludes the Gita,

sarva-dharmān parityajya
mām ekaṁ śaraṇaṁ vraja
ahaṁ tvāṁ sarva-pāpebhyo
mokṣayiṣyāmi mā śucaḥ

[Bg. 18.66]

[Abandon all varieties of religion and just surrender unto Me. I shall deliver you from all sinful reactions. Do not fear.]

That, Bhagavad-gita explains everything to everyone. It is the complete scripture of life and the summum bonum, the conclusion of the Gita is, ‘Abandon all varieties of religion and just surrender to me. I shall relieve you of all suffering due to sinful reactions, there is nothing to fear.’ Krishna also claims in the Gita, “vedaiś ca sarvair aham eva vedyo / vedānta-kṛd veda-vid eva cāham” [Bg. 15.15] – that, I am the compiler of all the vedas and by all the vedas, I am to be known.

mattaḥ parataraṁ nānyat
kiṣcid asti dhanaṣ-jaya
mayi sarvam idaṁ protaṁ
sūtre maṇi-gaṇā iva

[Bg. 7.7]

[O conqueror of wealth, there is no truth superior to Me. Everything rests upon Me, as pearls are strung on a thread.]

‘Krishna says -There is no truth superior to me. Everything rests on me as pearls are strung on a thread.’

So these six gosvamis of Vrindavana, they examined very carefully because they were the foremost scholars of the time. The Upasinads, the four vedas, the puranas, the Ramayana, the Mahabharat, and they extracted the very very essence. It is described that the vedic literatures are like milk. Sometimes, it is said that the mother.. the vedas are our mother and Krishna or God is our father. Ah? Now, what is the glory of the mother? The mother gives milk to nourish the children. So, the Vedic literatures, they are that milk which nourish human society with direction, knowledge and purpose in life and it is described that the essence of the milk is the cream and Srila Vedavyasa was the author of the vedas. He describes, “dharmaḥ projjhita-kaitavo ’tra paramo nirmatsarāṇāṁ satāṁ” [SB 1.1.2]

He says that the essence, the purest form, the cream of all Vedic literatures is the Srimad-Bhagavatam. Why? Because the Bhagavat Purana is unique in that it does not concern itself with any of the lower principles of religion. Basically, the scriptures of the world are concerned with four things. Teaching you how to approach God for economic development, for religiosity, for sense gratification and for liberation. And although the Vedic literatures throughout are constantly bringing one gradually to that point of surrender to God, the Vedas are like a desire tree. “nigama-kalpa-taror galitaṁ phalaṁ.” [SB 1.1.3]

In that the Vedas give every class of man, according to their desire and excuse to worship God. Whatever you want, approach God for it. Through this process gradually you will become purified of all desires for economic development, religiosity, sense gratification and liberation and ultimately you will come to that platform of “premā pumartho mahān”, unmotivated, uninterrupting desire to love and serve the Supreme Lord and the Srimad-Bhagavatam especially only deals with this principle of pure devotion to God.

Life in pure Devotional service by Srila Rupa Gosvami

So therefore, Srila Rupa Gosvami, who is the leader of these six gosvamis, he elaborated very very beautifully by showing us how in our practical daily life we can live exclusively for this purpose of pure devotion to god. And therefore, he has written the handbook or the guide for all the devotees of Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu. Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu specifically empowered Rupa Gosvami for this purpose. It is described when Lord Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu was on his way to Vrindavana, he stopped in the holy place of Prayaga. The place of the confluence of the Yamuna, the Ganges and the river Saraswati. And at that place Rupa Gosvami, who was a very very Chief minister and at that time, the Muslim emperors, regime, he resigned from all his responsibilities to dedicate all in his life to help Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu. So, he met Lord Caitanya at Prayaga and at the very sacred place called the Daśāśvamedha Ghāṭa, Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu took Rupa Gosvami in confidence and instructed him in the very essence of all knowledge and he explained to Rupa Gosvami for fifteen days throughout the day, how one must live a life in pure devotional service and how step by step gradually from the neophyte stage of devotional service to the highest state of prema bhakti, one can always be secure in his progress in spiritual life. So, after instructing him for fifteen days, Ha!, Lord Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu, he requested Rupa Gosvami, “Now you take, you churn this instruction of mine and from it, I want you to present a glorious literature, which will be the handbook for all my devotees for all time to come.”

So, Srila Rupa Gosvami went to Vrindavana and there he composed the handbook for devotion to God and he entitled that the Bhakti-rasāmṛta-sindhu. Bhakti-rasāmṛta-sindhu is Rupa Gosvami’s manifestation of Lord Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu’s mercy upon him. It is described in Caitanya-caritamrta that one time Lord Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu, he was reciting a very very confidential prayer of his particular mood of separation from Sri Krishna and nobody could understand the deep significance of this prayer. But Rupa Gosvami on a aa, palm leaf, he wrote down a very beautiful verse which explained perfectly for a common man to understand what was the significance in the nature of Lord Caitanya’s feelings of separation and he put it in the roof of his hut in Jagannath Puri and then he went to bathe in the sea. At that time Lord Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu came to that place and when he saw this leaf stuck in the roof, he opened it and read it and he said to his secretary Svarup Damodar Gosvami that how is it possible that Rupa Gosvami could understand the inner most confidential nature of my heart? No one can understand this and Svarup Damodara Gosvami said, “My dear Lord, it is only possible because you have blessed him with your supreme mercy.” Then Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu with tears of love in his eyes, he said, “Yes. Because Rupa Gosvami is a completely pure hearted surrendered servant, I have blessed him with all of my mercy.”

Mercy of Lord Caitanya towards Rupa Gosvami

The next day, Lord Caitanya visited that place with many many of his associates and he requested Rupa Gosvami, “Can you explain to me the particular writings that you are working on at this time?” And Rupa Gosvami began to recite beautiful, beautiful Sanskrit slokas and prayers of the holy name of Krishna. The holy name of Krishna, I cannot imagine, I do not understand, I cannot comprehend how much nectar it produces! When one chants the name of Krishna, one desires hundreds and millions of tongues and when the sound of that name enters into the ears, one desires trillions and trillions of ears. And when the holy name enters into the courtyard of the heart, the heart becomes paralyzed. And when Lord Caitanya and the devotees heard this, in great ecstasy they were crying in love and they all explained that “Never ever we have heard any explanation of the holy name”, so full of bhakti and the devotees understood that ‘One could only accomplish such a glorious realization in life by the mercy of Lord Caitanya’ and then Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu began to praise the qualities of Rupa Gosvami as if he had five mouths. Because he was such a dear and humble servant, Lord Caitanya said, “I have empowered him with all of my blessings. Therefore, I am making him the person that will manifest the proper guidelines for pure devotional service for all meant to come.” And when the devotees heard this their hearts were beating in pleasure. You see the nature of a devotee! Is they love to see someone else getting the mercy of God? The nature of a materialistic person is, if somebody else is getting special mercy, you become very envious. ‘I deserve it. Why not me?’ But a genuine devotee, his greatest happiness is not seeing himself gain the mercy of guru but seeing another gain the mercy of guru.[break]

He told me this personally many times. One time he brought me to see Prabhupad, Srila Prabhupada and as we were on our way [break], pleasure for me to see Prabhupada. But its even greater pleasure, when I can bring someone else to meet Prabhupada. It gives me ever greater happiness to see someone else get his mercy. So, in this way he said, “I am simply aspiring for people to come to New Vrindavana, who will become sincere and humble devotees, very dedicated to the service of my guru and then I can bring those jewels to Prabhupada and see them get his blessings. That is my greatest happiness.” So, this is the quality of the great souls.

Suddha bhakti is the first characteristic of pure devotion

So from this passage of the Caitanya-caritamrta, we can understand that Srila Rupa Gosvami was endowed with supreme blessings of the lord and in his Bhakti-rasāmṛta-sindhu, he begins by explaining the glory of pure devotion to God. He explains, “There are six characteristics of pure devotional service. The first characteristic is that pure devotional service or su aa suddha bhakti, sudhha bhakti, it relieves one of all material distress. This material world is a place of great distress.” Krishna says in Gita, “mām upetya punar janma /duḥkhālayam aśāśvatam” [Bg. 8.15]

That, those who are intelligent, they do not make any of their goals of life in this world because they know that “dukhala, duḥkhālayam aśāśvatam”, that this material world is ultimately a place of misery because everything is temporary, everything is in the process of dying. And no matter what arrangements you make, at any moment those arrangements can fall to pieces right before your eyes.

Their goal is paraṁ padam, Vaikuṇṭha, the place where there are no material miseries, not the place where there is danger at every step.]

Those who are intelligent, they never make their goal of life in any happiness in this world because they know this world is a place where there is danger at every step.

Types of sinful reactions

Man proposes but God disposes. Ah? It is described by Rupa Gosvami in the Bhakti-rasāmṛta-sindhu that suffering is due to the reactions of past sinful activities. You see people today, they want to sin but they do not want to suffer the reaction of sin. So modern technology, modern medicine, they are very nice. They are trying to counteract the reactions of sin, but nobody is concerned with stopping the cause of the suffering, which is sinful activity. Rupa Gosvami explains - There are two types of sinful reactions. One is those which are mature and the other are those which are immature. The mature sinful reactions are the sinful reactions that we are at the time of suffering and the immature sinful reactions are like seeds within our heart. They haven’t even began to fructify but in the future sure enough they will.

Comparison of sinful reactions and Bhakti

Rupa Gosvami quotes Lord Sri Krishna in the eleventh canto of Bhagavatam, where Krishna instructs his friend Uddhava. He says that devotional service, service or bhakti, it is like a blazing fire. And when the seeds of sinful reactions, whether they are mature or immature, when they are consumed by the fire, they are finished forever. So, by engaging in the service of the lord, all sinful reactions are destroyed. Sometimes sinful activities are compared to snakes and devotion to God, bhakti is compared to a forest fire. In the forest fire, the four-legged animals sometimes they can escape, but the snakes are immediately burn to ashes. So, in this same way the power of surrender to God is so great like fire that it completely burns, extinguishes all mature and immature sinful reactions that are within our heart and that is why Krishna says, “sarva-dharmān parityajya mām ekaṁ śaraṇaṁ vraja” [Bg. 18.66]

Abandon all varieties of religion and just surrender unto me. Everyone in this world is either immaterial distress or is potentially in material distress. And nobody likes to be in distress, why? Because the soul by nature is pleasure seeking. The soul hates distress. The soul is looking for ananda or bliss. When we awaken that pure devotion to God, then immediately all material distress, it becomes insignificant. Krishna says in the Gita,

āpūryamāṇam acala-pratiṣṭhaṁ
samudram āpaḥ praviśanti yadvat
tadvat kāmā yaṁ praviśanti sarve
sa śāntim āpnoti na kāma-kāmī

[Bg. 2.70]

[A person who is not disturbed by the incessant flow of desires – that enter like rivers into the ocean, which is ever being filled but is always still – can alone achieve peace, and not the man who strives to satisfy such desires.]

That this material world it is a place, you cannot avoid distress but you can rise above the dualities of material existence. You can rise above the actions or reactions of material life by attaining the spiritual platform.

Rupa Gosvami explains that pure devotional service is the beginning of all auspiciousness. What is auspicious is that which is for the welfare of all living beings. What is auspicious is that which brings prosperity and happiness to all. Therefore, charity is considered very auspicious. Philanthropy, loving your neighbor is yourself, is considered most auspicious. But the greatest, most auspicious form of charity is to awaken a person to his pure consciousness. Therefore, pure devotional service is the most auspicious of all auspicious things. It is the essence of auspiciousness. Because it is through this process that there can be real revolution in the hearts of men. The United Nations has been trying for so many years. Before that the legal nations, to create an auspicious condition within this world, by having so many meetings amongst this ma hmm many many powerful nations of this world, trying to agree upon and create peace in a world of quarrel and hypocrisy. But what is the conclusion? Because there is no change in the hearts of the living beings. No matter how many external superficial arrangements you make to try to create peace, as long as men’s hearts are seeking after selfish interests, they are like cats and dogs, you cannot create peace amongst cats and dogs. Peace can only be understood in the presence of God, who is within our heart. One, who finds God within his own heart, can spread that auspicious peace to all others. Indeed, Prahlada Maharaja, he was such a person, when Lord Narahari offered him any benediction he desired, Prahlada said that I do not even want to go to Vaikuntha, Lord Narashima deva, nor do I want liberation, nor do I want any of the enjoyments of this world. I only want on your behalf to show compassion to the suffering people of this world. It is that consciousness in the heart of the great souls that invokes the greatest auspiciousness within this world. Real auspiciousness is only to be found in the hearts of the pure devotees. And their hearts are like the shining sun. They are emanating the rays of that auspicious quality to everyone, who comes in contact with them.

Srila Rupa Gosvami explains that pure devotional service automatically puts one in transcendental pleasure. Everyone is seeking pleasure. Everyone is seeking love. But the soul is eternal. The temporary objects of love in this world will never satisfy you. And according to the acaryas in sastra, it is the greatest illusion. According to the sadhus and according to the vedic literatures, there is no greater illusion in this world than family attachment. Why? Because more than anything else, if one is not guided properly, family attachment diverts our propensity to love, away from Krishna toward the temporary finite objects of this world, which is all completely an illusion. There is no reality.

Therefore, we find that the great acaryas as well as the scriptures which describe the essence of life, they condemn family attachment. In fact , Lord Rishabha Deva explains, “puṁsaḥ striyā mithunī-bhāvam etaṁ”[SB 5.5.8]

Does family attachments affects devotional service

He says that this attachment between man and wife and then attachment to children and relatives and family members and and household possessions, it is the hard nut of bondage that keeps everyone in illusion. Ah? But Krishna explains in a Gita and he also explains in the Bhagavad - How there can be family life, which is completely enlightening, illuminating to the soul. A source of liberation rather than bondage. Prahlada Maharaja explains in Bhagavad “hitvātma-pātaṁ” un, what is that? Hitvātma he calls he calls Gṛham andha-kūpam”  [SB 7.5.5], that materialistic family life is a deep dark blind well. Ah? When you fall into a deep dark well, you cry for help, but nobody even hears you, you are so deep and so down. Ah! But Bhakti Vinoda Thakur, he explains that ‘When I come home from my work in a day and I come to my home’, he sees, says, “I see my whole family and I see my whole household transform into Goloka Vrindavana. If you do not have wisdom, your family life is nothing but the deep dark well.” The greatest illusion, the greatest condition that diverts our loving propensities away from God toward the temporary objects of the affection of this world, which are called in the Bhagavad, fallible soldiers. They all disappoint us. You know the story of Gajendra, the elephant. At the time of need all of his loved ones were just standing there watching. Ah? So therefore, when we understand that ‘Ha! My wife, my husband - they are not mine. They are Krishna’s. My children - they are not mine. They are Krishna’s. My home is not mine. It is Krishna’s. I have no right to exploit these things for my selfish interest. But I must with all respect and all devotion, I must relate with these objects of my love in the service of God.’ It is said, the family that prays together and stays together in spirit. But it is actually a fortress which protects us from maya.’ So therefore, those who are in the grhasta or the family ashram of life should know that their family attachments, if they are directed toward working together to attach ourselves to the service of Krishna, our family is the cause of our liberation. But if we are simply attached to each other, because of our relationship to one another, it is the greatest illusion in all of creation. Ah? We are all seeking the pleasure of love, and the fulfillment of our love is only one that propensity is directed toward God. That is the greatest happiness. So therefore, it is explained that pure devotional service automatically puts one in transcendental happiness because all of our desires for love are completely fulfilled.

There was one devotee, who had a wife and children and he wanted to live at all to join Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu and take to the renounced order. It is not an illusion. Human life is meant to awaken our love for God.” Queen Kunti prayed ha,

The model ideal mother. Now does that mean she res, relinquished her responsibilities towards children? No. She was the most loving, caring mother that the world has ever seen. If you see your child is god’s child, will you take better care of him than if you see it’s your child? Of course, you will love the child much more. Because the love of God will be within the love of your child. And this is the pleasure that everyone is seeking and until and unless we direct our loving propensities toward Krishna, toward the supreme truth, we will never understand real happiness. We will be simply creating a condition of future sufferings.

The fourth characteristic of pure devotional service

Rupa Gosvami explains the fourth characteristic of pure devotional service is that it is rarely achieved. It is very rare. Krishna says in Gita,

manuṣyāṇāṁ sahasreṣu
kaścid yatati siddhaye
yatatām api siddhānāṁ
kaścin māṁ vetti tattvataḥ

[Bg. 7.3]

[Out of many thousands among men, one may endeavor for perfection, and of those who have achieved perfection, hardly one knows Me in truth.]

‘Out of thousands of men one even endeavors for perfection and of those who endeavor me, hardly one knows me in truth.’

Eight mystic siddhis

Why is that? Pure devotional service is rarely achieved because Krishna himself becomes subordinate to his pure devotee. Krishna will easily give material prosperity. Either through himself or through the demigods or even to the atheist. He will easily give material opulence. He will easily give elevation to the heavenly planets. He will easily give mystic perfections. The yogis do great penance and austerity to attain mystic siddhis, anima siddhi, where you can become smaller than this, you can become so tiny and small, you can enter into stone. There is another siddhi, laghima siddhi. Where you can, a yogi can just connect himself to one ray of the sun and just follow that ray and within minutes go to the sun planet. There is another mystic siddhi where you can walk on water or fly in the sky. There is another mystic siddhi, where one can control people’s minds and lives. Completely subjugate another person. Many yogis, they are, they have their siddhi where by simply speaking and seeing someone, that person just surrenders and that person can manipulate that person’s life, it is like a type of mystic hypnotism. There is another mystic siddhi called prapti siddhi, which is a power of acquisition. Anything you want you can get. If you want some fruit from Afghanistan, you just with your mystic hand, you can reach and get that fruit and bring it right back to Chowpatty and eat it. Ha? If you want some gold from Fort Knox ah, there is nothing the US government can do to protect them from the mystic Yogi. You just take that gold and you sitting right here in Radhagopinath temple. Here you want some gold? [laughter) Prapti siddhi. There is another mystic perfection where one can perform magical feats. [pause] We have seen such mystic yogis. And there is another mystic yogi, where you can, a mystic siddhi, where you can completely contradict the laws of material nature. Ha? So, these are the eight mystic perfections one can achieve. It is described that through penance, through tapasya, through austerity, Krishna easily gives these things and mukti or liberation.

God subordinates himself only for devotees engaging in pure devotional service

Krishna tells Uddhava in the eleventh canto of Bhagavat that ‘I even evenly easily I give mukti to the jnanis. But pure devotional service is rarely achieved because only through pure devotional service does God reveal himself. Only through pure devotional service, does God subordinate himself to that person.’ Krishna was the chariot driver of Arjuna, taking orders from Arjuna. When Nanda Maharaja would come home after being in the cowherd pastures through the day, Krishna would take the wooden shoes of Nanda Maharaja on his head ha! and he would say, Nanda Maharaja, “Father”, he was just a little tiny boy at that time, “Here is your shoes.” Krishna takes the feet of his devotees and places it on his head and there is only one way of achieving pure devotional service. This is described by Prahlada Maharaja in the Bhagavatam. Prahlada says that ‘Ha! Not through any amount of tapasya, not through any amount of chanting of mantras, not through any amount of ha penance, austerity, fasting, not through any amount of giving in charity, not through any amount of cultivation of knowledge in studying of the scripture can one attain pure devotional service. It is only by taking the dust from the feet of the great sadhus and smearing it upon his body that one can attain pure devotional service. There is no other way.’ What does it mean to smear the dust of the lotus feet of the great saints upon your body? It means to humbly engage in their service.

Bhakti is the sixth characteristic of pure devotional service attracting Krishna

Pure devotional service is the exclusive monopoly of the pure saints, the mahatmas and by their blessing, by their mercy, Krishna reveals this rare rare jewel of bhakti. And the sixth characteristic of pure devotional service is that it is the only means of attracting Krishna. Krishna does not need your money. Krishna does not need your intelligence. Krishna does not need your abilities. Do you think Krishna needs a good cook to cook for him? Do you think Krishna needs a business-man to provide funds for him? Do you think Krishna needs a scholar to figure things out for him? Krishna is the possessor of all opulences in full. Krishna is pleased by our humble devotion. That devotion attracts Krishna and how does it attract Krishna? It is described that Krishna is called Madana-mohan. Madana-mohan means he who even attracts the mind of Kamadeva, cupid. Everyone in this world is being mesmerized by cupid, ha? by Kamadeva. Kamadeva, the material energy personified is constantly alluring our minds through our senses to attraction to the objects and everyone is under the spell of cupid. Ha? But cupid is under the spell of Krishna. Therefore, call he is called Madana-mohan. Even thousands and millions of cupids are attracted to Krishna. But Srimati Radharani, she is known as Madana-mohan Mohini which means the attracter of cupid is attracted to Sri Radhika. Why? Because she is the devotional energy of the lord. She is the personification of bhakti, bhakti rupa. She is pure devotional service.. personified. Krishna expands his devotional energy in the form of Srimati Radharani and it is described that her love always keeps Krishna subordinate.

We were describing the other night, and Gaura Purnima, we were explaining the beautiful pastime of holy. Where Srimati Radharani and the gopis, they would have a beautiful ha celebration of colors with Lord Sri Krishna and the Gopas. Ha? It was an exchange of such love. First, they would tease one another and by teasing one another, they would invoke transcendental anger amongst each other and then they would challenge to a fight and the fight is not who can take from the other but who can give to the other the most. Radharani challenged, “I will give more love to you Krishna than you can ever give to me” and Krishna said, “No! No, I will give more love to you and the expression of that love is through giving.” They would give each other sweetmeats, they would give each other, they would throw each others, throw upon each other beautiful flowers of the Vrindavana forest and they would get beautiful bright colors and they would throw them upon each other, as the offering of our love. It is an expression of of devotion. It is an expression of the sentiment of our affection. That this is my love being given to you. And then Krishna, ‘This is my love upon you” and this, they would be throwing the colors on each other. Such a sweet and beautiful ceremony. But it is described that on every occasion, the gopis would always be victorious. Why? Because love always conquers the beloved. Krishna is the supreme object of love, Radharani is the supreme lover. And the lover, the loved is always conquered by the love. Now it is described in Gita,“mahātmānas tu māṁ pārtha daivīṁ prakṛtim āśritāḥ” [Bg. 9.13]

That what is the definition of mahatma according to the Gita? One who is always subordinate to the daivi shakti, the spiritual energy of the lord. The spiritual energy of the lord is Sri Radharani. When you engage in the service of your spiritual master, when you engage in the loving service of the sadhus, you put yourself under the jurisdiction of Srimati Radharani cause they are all her servants. Ha? And therefore, who is under the divine devotional hmm supervision of Srimati Radharani, he is declared by Krishna to be real mahatma. And pure devotional service puts us perfectly and directly within the pure spiritual energy of the lord and thus pure devotional service is the only means of attracting Krishna.

Glories of the book Bhakti Rasamrita Sindhu

A devotee is only concerned with pleasing guru and pleasing Krishna through service and Rupa Gosvami explains the supreme position of Bhakti and therefore, he continues after convincing our intelligence of the glory and in the greatness of the supreme position of pure bhakti and then he explains very very systematically, “How one could make advancement from the most neophyte tender stage?
