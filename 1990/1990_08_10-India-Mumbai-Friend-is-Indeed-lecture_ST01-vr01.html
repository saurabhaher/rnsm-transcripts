1990 - 59 A Friend Indeed Srimad Bhagvatam 09-04-38

Mumbai 10-08-1990

Om Namoh Bhagavate Vasudevaya

sādhavo hṛdayaṁ mahyaṁ

sādhūnāṁ hṛdayaṁ tv aham

mad-anyat te na jānanti

nāhaṁ tebhyo manāg api

The pure devotee is always within the core of my heart and I am always in the heart of the pure devotee. My devotees do not know anything else but Me and I do not know anyone else but them.

Purport by his divine grace AC Bhakti Vedant Swami Prabhupada:

Since Durvāsā Muni wanted to chastise Mahārāja Ambarīṣa, it is to be understood that he wanted to give pain to the heart of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, for the Lord says, sādhavo hṛdayaṁ mahyam: “The pure devotee is always within the core of My heart.” The Lord’s feelings are like those of a father, who feels pain when his child is in pain. Therefore, offenses at the lotus feet of a devotee are serious. Caitanya Mahāprabhu has very strongly recommended that one not commit any offense at the lotus feet of a devotee. Such offenses are compared to a mad elephant because when a mad elephant enters a garden it causes devastation. Therefore one should be extremely careful not to commit offenses at the lotus feet of a pure devotee. Actually Mahārāja Ambarīṣa was not at all at fault; Durvāsā Muni unnecessarily wanted to chastise him on flimsy grounds. Mahārāja Ambarīṣa wanted to complete the Ekādaśī-pāraṇa as part of devotional service to please the Supreme Personality of Godhead, and therefore he drank a little water. But although Durvāsā Muni was a great mystic brāhmaṇa, he did not know what is what. That is the difference between a pure devotee and a so-called learned scholar of Vedic knowledge. The devotees, being always situated in the core of the Lord’s heart, surely get all instructions directly from the Lord, as confirmed by the Lord Himself in Bhagavad-gītā :

“tesam evanukampartham

aham ajnana-jam tamah

nasayamy atma-bhava-stho

jnana-dipena bhasvata”

(Bg.10.11)

A pure vaisnava

“Out of compassion for them, I, dwelling in their hearts, destroy with the shining lamp of knowledge the darkness born of ignorance.” The devotee does not do anything not sanctioned by the Supreme Personality of Godhead. As it is said, vaiṣṇavera kriyā mudrā vijṣeha nā bujhaya. Even the most learned or experienced person cannot understand the movements of a Vaiṣṇava, a pure devotee. No one, therefore, should criticize a pure Vaiṣṇava. A Vaiṣṇava knows his own business; whatever he does is precisely right because he is always guided by the Supreme Personality of Godhead.

Translation: The pure devotee is always within the core of my heart and I am always in the heart of the pure devotees. My devotees do not know anything else but Me and I do not know anyone else but them.

Today I have been given the ninth Canto of the Srimad Bhagvatam and we happen to turn to this page which is one of the very great verses of the Srimad Bhagvatam . It is spoken by Lord Narayan, the Lord of Vaikunta.

Watering the root of a tree

Ambarish Maharaj was instructed by his Guru Maharaj to go to the bank of the Jamuna at the forest of Madhuban and perform the Ekadasi Parana in order to give pleasure to the Supreme Personality of Godhead. Krsna says in the ninth Chapter of the Gita : Yat karosi yad asnasi yaj juhosi dadasi yat, Yat tapasyasi kaunteya, tat kurusva mad arpanam. That all that you do, all that you eat, all austerities or penances you may perform, all charity you may give, it should all be done as an offering to give pleasure to the supreme Lord. Notice that Ambarish Maharaj, he performed a very very difficult sacrifice, a sacrifice where he was depriving his body of all sense enjoyment, he was living in a secluded holy place, his mind was fixed in meditation for one complete year. He was not doing it for any other reason than to please Lord Narayan. He was not doing it for elevation, to heavenly pleasures in life, he was not doing it for fame in this world of names and forms, he was not doing it to gain great powers, but he was performing this great sacrifice simply to please Narayan because he was a king and he was a responsible king. He was fully understanding that if Narayan is pleased by the king’s service, just like watering the root of the tree, Lord Narayan will provide the needs and the wants for everyone within the kingdom.

The foolishness of modern society is people are working very very hard to try to provide the responsibilities they have for their families for their students, politicians for their citizens. But Ambarish was more intelligent, he understood that simply by pleasing Krsna everything would be provided. Therefore although he was the king of the whole planet earth at that time, he took one year simply and exclusively to be in that secluded place to please Krsna knowing that it was the greatest service he could do for his kingdom and for every citizen within.

How to please the Lord

Srimad Bhagvatam explains

ataḥ pumbhir dvija-śreṣṭhā

varṇāśrama-vibhāgaśaḥ

svanuṣṭhitasya dharmasya

saṁsiddhir hari-toṣaṇam

Whether you are a brahmin, a kstriya, a vaishya or a shudra , whatever your occupation is in life, the success of the performance of your duty is only when the supreme Lord is pleased by the sentiment in which you are offering your service.

It is also said in Bhagvatam : śrama eva hi kevalam . That means whatever occupation you have if you do not please Narayan by that service, it is all a useless, futile waste of your life’s time.


The society follows the great men

Arjuna was also a great warrior and he performed his duty well but his consciousness was to please Krsna. So Ambarish Maharaj was setting the perfect example .

yad yad ācarati śreṣṭhas

tat tad evetaro janaḥ

sa yat pramāṇaṁ kurute

lokas tad anuvartate

What the great men do the common men follow. Therefore, the leaders on every level of society must set the proper example of God consciousness for others to follow.

Role of the Leaders towards society

gurur na sa syāt sva-jano na sa syāt

pitā na sa syāj jananī na sā syāt

That is why Lord Rishabdev says whatever position of leadership you take, you must set the example of how to perfect your life. And who are the leaders: the Guru is a leader. If the Guru is not setting proper example of how to perfect ones life, he has no business being a guru. The demigod is a guru, is a leader, plus the demigod is acting in such a way that his followers can follow him properly. He has no right being proper demigod. pitā na sa syāj jananī na sā syāt, the parent, the father and the mother, if you are not, the father and mother is a leader, especially father is a leader , he is the leader of the family , mother is also one of the leaders of the family. If the father and mother are not living exemplary God conscious lives by the authority of God, they have absolutely no right to be a father or mother, they are cheaters.

Responsibility of each one of us

So Maharaj Ambarish is setting by his example. He was a father, he was also a king. It says in Bhagvatam- A King has no right in being a king; a politician has no right leading his people unless he is setting a perfect example of God conscious life for others to follow. This is the first responsibility each one of us have in our life, to set an example for those who are depending on us. This responsibility is more important than feeding the bellies of our dependants, because the animals are doing that too, the atheists are doing that too, that’s not such a great thing. Even the demons are feeding the bellies and clothing and giving nice house to their dependants. These things are required, that is a responsibility but is very much secondary. The first responsibility is our example, our leadership, how we are guiding our dependants, how we are setting an example for the world to be influenced by, our devotion to God. So Ambarish Maharaj was very humbly accepting this responsibility as his life and soul.

If devotee is offended even Lord will not protect him

And of course you read about the great sage Durvasa Muni who became very angry at Ambarish and tried to kill him. At that time Narayan sent his sudarshan chakra to destroy the fiery demon created by Durvasa Muni and then that same sudarshan chakra was chasing after Durvasa Muni. If Krsna wants to kill you, nothing can protect you and if Krsna wants to protect you, nothing can kill you. Krsna wanted to protect Ambarish, he did not even raise a finger to protect himself. And even the most mighty fiery demon that was capable of devouring the entire earth planet could not even touch one hair on his head because Krsna was protecting him. And although Durvasa Muni was the most powerful mystic yogi who simply by the chanting of an incantation can create entire bombs more powerful than any nuclear bomb today, within a second, such a powerful mystic. But when Krsna wanted to kill him, he could not protect himself. Lord Brahma could not protect him, Lord Indra could not protect him, Varuna could not protect him . Even Lord Shankar could not protect him. Finally he fell at the feet of Lord Narayan , then Lord Narayan said “even I cannot protect you because you have offended my devotee”. And then he began to explain his own heart.

We are what we associate with.

How wonderful it is that the Supreme Personality of Godhead reveals His mind to us in the scriptures? How intimately loving the Lord is to all of His children? Just imagine how many friends do you have that can honestly, sincerely, confidentially reveal their minds to you. Not so many is there? People in this world have a tendency for very superficial relationships with one another because it is a superficial society. The natural surroundings that bring about a natural lifestyle are so hard to find. We are living in a society of concrete, oil and plastic and exhaust fumes and so much quarrelling for unnecessary paraphernalia for sense enjoyment. Where do you find natural water. You have to buy it in a bottle, imported from 100 miles away. Where do you find natural air. The basic ingredients of our life natural air natural water, its no where to be found. We have to go great great distances just to breathe properly. And of course we are very much a product of our environment, we become like what we associate with. Therefore we become very artificial, very superficial, very unnatural. And therefore our relationships with one another are of the same quality unfortunately.

So like in America, New York city, I go there often I visit so many people , I know people who lived in apartment building for twenty six years, the same little apartment . They are on a floor in the apartment building with about thirty other apartments and of the thirty other apartments practically everybody has been there for twenty five, twenty six, thirty years and not a single one knows any of the others. They just, they get back from work, they go in the house, they lock the door, they watch television. This is not an unordinary situation. People are afraid of each other. And people are very afraid to open their minds and confidentially speak to another because there is a good chance that eventually that person will use that information against you. He is my friend today but how do I know whether he will be my friend tomorrow. If I reveal my mind in confidence today, tomorrow if he’s not my friend what will he say, he will ruin me. Or even if he is my friend, all friends naturally, they like to gossip, so how can I reveal my mind.

Six exchanges of friendship in Krishna Conciousness

So Srila Rupa Goswami in updeshamrita which is a great guide under fundamental principles of Krsna consciousness he explains there are 6 symptoms of love between devotees , 6 exchanges of love – one is to give a gift, That gift is not necessarily something material but it is a gesture of affection, could be in our words, our encouragement, could be an invitation, could also be a gift. It is not the gift itself but it’s the thought that has meaning. So to give gift and to accept gift, to give Prasad and to accept Prasad, to reveal ones mind in confidence and to hear ones mind in confidence, these are the 6 exchanges of friends in Krsna consciousness. So it is very difficult to find a real friend.

Krishna – the supreme friend

(a friend in need is a friend indeed)

But Krsna says in Gita: suhṛdaṁ sarva-bhūtānāṁ. That I am the supreme friend of all the living beings. He is the supreme friend because he never abandons us. The real quality of a friend is when you are most in need he will not abandon you. That’s when you really know who your friends are, when you are most in need. Most people, they only want you when you assist them in their sense gratification. At the time of death who is there to stand by your side. I’m talking not about standing by the side of your body, but the standby the side of your soul. your mother, your father, your grandmother, your children, your grand children, whatever your age bracket is. You may have so many people standing around the bed crying OH don’t leave us, don’t leave us, don’t leave us. We enjoy you too much please do not leave us. But how many of them are standing by the side of your soul to take your soul to a better place, to save your soul from a worse place. Can they do like that. There is only one person that is your friend at the time of your death – That is Krsna. So therefore, he is the supreme friend and of course any devotee who is acting as a representative of Krsna in your life is also your real friend because he is representing your supreme friend. So as the supreme friend, as the most confidential and intimate friend of the soul of every living being, Krsna reveals his mind in confidence to us in the revealed scriptures. And in our prayers we are meant to reveal our minds to him in confidence with faith with trust. So this loving exchange is meant to take place between the sincere devotee and between the Supreme Personality of Godhead.

Relationship between Krishna and His pure devotees

So here in this beautiful and most illuminating verse Lord Narayan, the supreme friend of all living beings, the master of the universe, the super soul within the heart of every living creature, is confidentially revealing the innermost thoughts of his heart .

sādhavo hṛdayaṁ mahyaṁ

sādhūnāṁ hṛdayaṁ tv aham

mad-anyat te na jānanti

nāhaṁ tebhyo manāg api

The pure devotee is always within the core of my heart and I am always in the heart of the pure devotee. My devotees do not know anything else besides Me and I do not know anyone else but them. Those devotees who sacrifice everything for the pleasure of God, such souls, they are always within the core of the heart of the supreme Lord Sri Hari. Therefore any insult, any pain that we inflict upon his devotee, we are inflicting upon the heart of Lord Narayan. Just try to understand how serious this is. If someone comes and pushes you in the face, you do not like that. If someone comes and kicks you in the leg, it hurts, you don’t like that but if someone comes and stabs your heart what is more worse than that . Therefore great demons who come to attack God, we see, like there are many battles between the Lord and His various incarnations and asuras, where sometimes they are shooting the Lord with arrows and hitting Him with clubs, they are hitting Him on the head , they are shooting arrows in His body, they are insulting Him with words in His ears. But those things; are they as bad? Do those demons actually pierce His heart ? But when you cause pain, when you insult the Lord, when you go against the Lord, you are going against His body, the body of God, the form of God. But Lord Narayan is revealing a very secret piece of information to us. When you cause pain to his devotee, you are directly inflicting pain to the very inner core of his heart and there is nothing that is more painful than that. Therefore one must be very cautious. We should always give the devotee the benefit of doubt because if we are wrong we are defeating the entire purpose of our human existence. The purpose of human life Saṁsiddhir hari-toṣaṇam is to please God.

Mystic powers have no value

patraṁ puṣpaṁ phalaṁ toyaṁ

yo me bhaktyā prayacchati

tad ahaṁ bhakty-upahṛtam

aśnāmi prayatātmanaḥ

. Krsna says offer me a little water, a flower, a fruit. If you offer with love I would be pleased and I will accept it. That is the goal of life to please God. And what is the most contrary of all activities to our purpose in life? To cause pain to God, so therefore by inflicting pain upon His devotee, that defeats the purpose of human life more than anything else we can do, because it is inflicting pain to the core of the heart of Narayan . Therefore Narayan is telling Durvasa Muni aahaa! you think you have caused this pain to my devotee, you have harassed and insulted and tried to harm my devotee and now you are coming to me for forgiveness – impossible. The only possibility is you must go to that devotee and please him, only then will My heart feel relieved of the pain. By coming to Me and offering all your prayers, it's sweet to My ears but my heart is still suffering. If you want to relieve this pain of My heart you must go and you must satisfy My devotee because he is within the core of My heart . So Durvasa Muni ran back to Ambarish Maharaj, understanding the confidential mind of the Lord and with a heart of genuine sentiment he apologized and he said “Ambarish I am your servant”. And then Lord Narayan was happy, then he was pleased.

So try to understand that becoming a great mystic yogi is not a very important thing in life. Durvasa Muni, he was able to perform miraculous activities which contained all the eight mystic siddhis. Durvasa Muni had the power to create a planet simply by chanting mantra. He had the power to destroy planets simply by chanting mantras. He had the power to accumulate massive, massive, massive wealth within a second to enjoy or to give to others simply by chanting mantra. Did you imagine if you had these powers, you would not have to be working 9 to 5, Just chant a mantra you have your home, you have your gardens, you have your money, you have your food, you have everything. He had that prapti siddhi that he could obtain anything from anywhere instantly. He also had the power to read others mind. Sometimes we are thinking what is he thinking, should I trust him should I not trust him. He never had these doubts, he would just go right into your mind and know what exactly your motive was, what you wanted to do or what you didn’t want to do. No anxiety- right? You know exactly who your friends are and who is not your friend. Such powers, But all these powers have no value and Durvasa Muni realized that all these powers have no value.

By pleasing the devotee the Lord is pleased

Because if you are not pleasing Lord Narayan then whatever you do and whatever you have is automatically simply meant for your condemnation, for your defeat in life, for whatever you have or whatever you don’t have, if you are sincere to offer your respects to the devotees of Lord Narayan. Try to understand the opposite of what I have just been explaining. If you give satisfaction to the devotee that means you are giving pleasure to the core of the heart of Narayan. Now in our own life if someone massages our body, little pleasure, very nice, thank you very much. If someone fans you with a peacock fan to relieve you from the heat- very nice, little pleasure. This is nice fan, this is only nice, these fans I don’t know if I like them or not. My body likes them but my ears do not like them. Half is pleasure and half is pain from the point of view of materialistic dualities. From the transcendental platform it's all a matter of perception . So pleasing the body, we like it. If someone gives pleasure to your mind by saying Oh! You know you are a very good man, you’re very great, the whole world should soon learn about this. You’ll think ah! yes yes - such pleasure. But for someone to give pleasure to your heart, is there any greater pleasure than that? Someone who actually gives you love, that pleasure goes right to your heart, that’s beyond the physical or mental pleasure. That is what we are all looking for, that is what we are striving for - the pleasure of the heart. By pleasing the devotee, by pleasing God by offering Him some food, by pleasing God by fanning Him, by pleasing God by offering Him some nice ornaments, you are pleasing his transcendental form. But by pleasing His devotee you are directly giving the highest pleasure to his heart, to the core of His heart.

Dasa anu Das

That is why Krsna says mad-bhakta-pujabhyadhika - If you really really want to be My devotee you have to become the devotee of My devotee and please My devotee. That is why Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu prayed for only one position because Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu being God, in the role of a devotee, knew as the supreme authority how to please God. Who knows better how to please God than God. Anybody? So Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu who is God Himself taking the role of a devotee was showing us according to His supreme authority how to please Krsna. Gopi-bhartur pada-kamalayor dasa-dasa-dasanudasah. My aspiration he said is to become the servant of the servant of the servant of the servant of the servant of the servant- anu, the most tiny insignificant servant of all of other servants of the Lord of the gopis - Sri Krsna .That is how to give pleasure to the core of the heart of Narayan. And this most confidential information is being revealed in the pages of Srimad Bhagvatam and by following this very simple principle we will never have any serious problems in our spiritual life. Even if problems do come they will very quickly be rectified by the Lord.

Humble servant of servants lives in the core of the heart of Lord

In the ninth Chapter of Gita Krsna says

api cet su-durācāro

bhajate mām ananya-bhāk

sādhur eva sa mantavyaḥ

samyag vyavasito hi saḥ

ksipram bhavati dharmatma He says that even if my devotee commits the most abominable activity, if he is rightly situated – rightly situated means humble servant of the servant, I consider him saintly. ksipram bhavati dharmatma which means that very quickly I will rectify his situation because he is under My divine protection. So if we simply keep ourselves as the humble servant of the devotees, if we simply keep ourselves from offending the sadhus, the great souls, and factually according to Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu anyone who is chanting the holy name is a great soul, there will be no serious problems in your spiritual life. Even if there is, Krsna will personally rectify. We should not look for the faults, we should simply try to strive for that position of humble servant of the great souls and then we could know that we will also be living within the core of the heart of Lord Narayan and that is the greatest greatest attainment, that is the greatest benediction, when by our humble service to the Vaishnavas the Lord welcomes us to live within the core of His heart .

Is there any questions?

Janamashtmi – the day Krishna appears in pure hearts

In just a few days from now we will be celebrating Janamashtmi . What does Janamashtmi mean? It means the day that Krsna appears, it means the day that Krsna appears on this earth, it also means the day that Krsna appears within our lives . When does Krsna appear? He appears – but actually does He appear? He’s always everywhere, is He not? He is in your heart right now. isvarah sarva-bhutanam

hrd-dese 'rjuna tisthati Krsna said I am seated in the heart of every living being . He says that a learned soul sees me within the heart of every living creature, even the animals, even the immovable vegetation. It is also said the paramatma who is Krsna is in and between every atom. So what does it mean when Krsna descends into this world when he is everywhere in this world. We cannot see Him in our hearts, we cannot see Him in the hearts of others, we cannot see Him although he is everywhere and in everything. Therefore when our hearts are pure, when they are free from lust, from envy, from greed, from deceit, from pride, when they are free from the desire to lord it over material nature, when their hearts are softened to be the humble servant of the Lord, at that time the Lord reveals His identity to our transcendental vision.

The real celebration of Janamashtmi is to pure our hearts

When the sun rises on the eastern horizon, the sun is not born, the sun simply reveals itself to our eyes. Krsna is everywhere premāṣjana-cchurita-bhakti-vilocanena. But unless our hearts are softened by bhakti, unless our eyes are decorated with the shadow of love, we cannot see Him although He is before us everywhere and in everything. So the real way of celebrating Janamashtmi is not simply celebrating the historical appearance of Krsna five thousands years ago that took place under these particular constellations, but the real celebration of Janamashtmi is making a vow and striving according to that vow, to purify our hearts so that Krsna will take his birth within our life. So that He can appear and reveal Himself in truth within our very life and then we can personally worship Him, adore Him and serve Him through all eternity. We should approach Janamashtmi in this consciousness. And what is the greatest benediction upon all humanity within kalyuga. Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu has given this benediction ‘Vijayate Sri Krsna sankirtanam’. He has given us the simple and sublime means by which we can purify our hearts, by which we can facilitate the invitation by which Krsna reveals Himself and takes birth within our lives and that great benediction comes in the form of Naam sankirtan- the chanting of the holy name. Hare Krsna Hare Krsna , Krsna Krsna Hare Hare, Hare Ram, Hare Ram, Ram Ram Hare Hare .

Not only on this particular day but on everyday, let us invite Krsna to appear within our life everyday by sincerely chanting the holy name and by sincerely accepting the position by which we give pleasure to His devotees.

They will have to suffer but not as much as someone who knowingly with envy does. So its not the offence but the consciousness behind the offence that is considered by Krsna. But if someone maliciously with ill intent deals with the Lord's devotee, the Lord, according to the degree of maliciousness of your heart, to that degree the Lord will reprimand your offense.

Definition of a devotee

What is a definition of a devotee?

Definition of a devotee, Essentially one who accepts the position of the servant of God . One who accepts God as the master of his life and accepts the position of His servant. One who is accepting God as the supreme master of his life and is aspiring honestly to follow his instructions.That is devotee.

Even if you are not so expert if you are honestly striving to follow His instructions, the Lord accepts you as His devotee. It is the honesty and sincerity of our hearts that Krsna accepts. In the Caitanya charitamrita, Prabhupad explains, sometimes demons come in the disguise of devotees. That means maybe accepting the external forms of devotees but within their hearts they do not have that honesty, that sincerity that desire to serve the Lord and his devotees . There are many definitions, By the definition of Krsna, ‘he who calls himself my devotee is not my devotee, but he who calls himself a devotee of my devotee,he is my devotee’. So by this definition which is Gods Definition, one who honestly and sincerely is striving to be the servant of the servant of the Lord, he is a devotee. Even if he is in the most fallen condition of life he still must be considered a devotee.

Principle of serving the devotees

55.28 -- question is not clear..

By striving to please every devotee, whether it is difficult or easy that is your service . You have to learn from a spiritual master. Without a spiritual master you cannot learn, you cannot understand these principles. You must be trained by a bonafide spiritual master, how to be the servant of a servant of a servant. Every devotee you see, every devotee you come in contact with, how can you best help that person’s Krsna consciousness. How can you best assist that person in his Krsna consciousness or even assist that devotee’s service to his Guru maharaj to your Guru maharaj. That should be how you’re thinking throughout the day. Yes it is difficult but nothing great is easy.

Thank you very much. Hare Krsna
