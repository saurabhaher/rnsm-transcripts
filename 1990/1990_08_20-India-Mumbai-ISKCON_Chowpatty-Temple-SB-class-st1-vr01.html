1990-064 Sense Enjoyment Means Spiritual Death SB- 08- 06 36-37 Mumbai - 20-08-90

Om Namoh Bhagavate Vasudevaya

SB 8.6.33

tatas te mandara-girim
ojasotpāṭya durmadāḥ
nadanta udadhiṁ ninyuḥ
śaktāḥ parigha-bāhavaḥ

Translation

Thereafter, with great strength, the demons and demigods, who were all very powerful and who had long, stout arms, uprooted Mandara Mountain. Crying very loudly, they brought it toward the Ocean of Milk.

SB 8.6.34

dūra-bhārodvaha-śrāntāḥ
śakra-vairocanādayaḥ
apārayantas taṁ voḍhuṁ
vivaśā vijahuḥ pathi

Translation

Because of conveying the great mountain for a long distance, King Indra, Mahārāja Bali and the other demigods and demons became fatigued. Being unable to carry the mountain, they left it on the way.

SB 8.6.35

nipatan sa giris tatra
bahūn amara-dānavān
cūrṇayām āsa mahatā
bhāreṇa kanakācalaḥ

Translation

The mountain known as Mandara, which was extremely heavy, being made of gold, fell and smashed many demigods and demons.

Purport

By constitution, gold is heavier than stone. Since Mandara Mountain was made of gold and was therefore heavier than stone, the demigods and demons could not properly carry it to the Ocean of Milk.

SB 8.6.36

tāṁs tathā bhagna-manaso
bhagna-bāhūru-kandharān
vijṣāya bhagavāṁs tatra
babhūva garuḍa-dhvajaḥ

Translation

The demigods and demons were frustrated and disheartened, and their arms, thighs and shoulders were broken. Therefore the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who knows everything, appeared there on the back of His carrier, Garuḍa.

SB 8.6.37

giri-pāta-viniṣpiṣṭān
vilokyāmara-dānavān
īkṣayā jīvayām āsa
nirjarān nirvraṇān yathā

Translation

Observing that most of the demons and the demigods had been crushed by the falling of the mountain, the Lord glanced over them and brought them back to life. Thus they became free from grief, and they even had no bruises on their bodies.

In the Bhagavad Gita Bhagwan Sri Krsna, he tells us that he is the strength of the strong. He is the intelligence of the intelligent. He is the ability in man. Indeed it is said ahaṁ sarvasya prabhavo mattaḥ sarvaṁ pravartate that he is the source of everything, he is sarva karana karnam, he is the cause of all causes. Here in this beautiful story from the Srimad Bhagvatam we find the most powerful personalities in this entire universe. Those persons who are controlling all the affairs of the universe. We are finding the greatest of the demigods and the greatest of the demons. Some of us have some pride of our ability to control. Some of us are even so foolishly proud of our opulences. A rich man in this world may have a few billion dollars even, but you know what that wealth is compared to the wealth of the demigods. What each demigod has proprietorship over is millions of times more valuable than everything on this earth. The heavenly planets are larger in size than this earth planet and they are filled with the greatest opulence, practically everything is made of gold and jewels most precious jewels that are not even to be found on this earth planet. Just like in India the most popular of all fruits is the mango. People are very much satisfied by eating the mangoes. Srila Prabhupada once told us the mango is one fruit which is a heavenly fruit which grows on the earth. But the difference is the mangoes on the heavenly planets it’s just described in the fifth canto of Bhagwatam, that when they fall from a tree and break they create an entire of river of mango juice. It’s very sweet. So we cannot compare the beauty and the opulence of this world compared to the heavenly planets. As far as strength, as far as knowledge, as far as wealth, as far as physical beauty, we have not even a trace of the opulence of the demigods and as far as the power to control, if we can control our family, if we can control a few brahmacharis, if we can control, a wife is always controlling her husband, husband is thinking he is controlling the wife, he becomes very proud, what to speak of some political leaders, like now the president or the dictator whatever he is, of Iraq, he is very proud that he just marched into this tiny little country of Kuwait and seized it – I’m in control and now he wants to walk into another country and seize it and be in control and we’ve seen so many of these foolish petty conquerors in this world. We have seen Napoleon, we’ve seen Hitler, we’ve seen Alexander the great, where are they today. They conquer a few places and then ultimately they are smashed they are crushed and they die and they lose everything. So we are proud of some ability to control, but compared to the demigods they are controlling all the planets and the universes. Indra is in control of the rain of every planet. Vayu is in control of the air and the wind of all planets. Agni is in control of the fire of all planets and as far as the demons, demons are very very proud, but compared to these demons we are reading about in the Srimad Bhagvatam, these demons today are like infant children. When you read about great demons like Hiranyakashpu, my God! He was so powerful. These demons today wreak so much fare amongst men, what are they? They can’t, they cannot even conquer this one little planet. We have not seen a single demon within our modern history that could conquer this one little tiny planet earth and they have all strived to do so, so much. Hitler, his goal was to conquer the earth. Well he conquered a few countries, then he was killed. Napoleon wanted to conquer the earth, aah-killed. Now some of these leaders in the middle eastern countries they want to conquer the earth. Aah they will be squashed like flies. So Hiranyakashpu actually conquered the universe and his great grandson is Bali Maharaj who is more powerful than him. He conquered the universe too. Bali Maharaj with the help of his demon associates literally were able to defeat Indra with his mighty thunderbolt in causing to run in fear of his life and all the other demigods, they’re hiding, hiding from the power and the strength of Bali Maharaj and his great demon forces. But here we find that the demons and the demigods, the most powerful controllers, that we will ever read of, ultimately they will have to turn to Krsna for everything.

This great Mandira mountain is like a lesson for all of them. They were told that you move this Mandira mountain to the ocean of milk, you churn it and you will get nectar. So, yes, yes we will move the mountain. But the mountain is made of gold. Everyone is in anxious of gold. Of course, for a devotee who is in the transcendental platform he sees that there is no difference between gold and the stones in the street. Krsna explains that in Bhagavad Gita. The one who sees gold in pebbles in the street with equal vision, he is a man of real intelligence. He is a man of real detachment. Let the materialists, they are constantly making this distinction, gold is the symbol of wealth. I know in America, perhaps in other countries as well, the whole currency exchange is based on gold. Gold is the supreme symbol of wealth in this world. In the United States of America there’s a place called Fort Knox and in Fort Knox they gathered as much gold as possible through out the land and made it into special bars, gold bars and they had a government seal put on them and there was just tons and tons of gold put into Fort Knox with the highest security possible and the whole wealth of the country was based on this gold and when they wanted to make currency, they would give you a piece of paper which said that this means you have the right to so much gold at Fort Knox and because you have the right to that much gold for this paper, is very much worth something. Gradually they just started making more and more and more and more and more money and then they started making for silver. Silver certificate it says on every dollar bill in the past, silver certificate. Now it doesn’t say anything because they just print, nothing, it’s not worth the paper it’s on. Therefore the economy has to take a dive into disaster because there’s nothing to base the wealth on. So gold is the symbol of wealth. We notice Hiranyakashpu, Hirnayaksha, their great success is that they had, Hiranyakashpu was very famous because he had great gold and Hirnayaksha also he was able to extract so much gold from the earth and the Srimad Bhagvatam describes that kaliyuga when the personification was defeated by Maharaj Parikshat, Maharaj Parikshat said that you can dwell only in four places where there is illicit sex, intoxication, gambling and meat eating and Kali responded that these things cannot exist within your kingdom because you are so powerful as a ruler. You enforce the dharma, where will I be. You have to give me some place I am surrendered to you. So Maharaj Parikshat said Ah! Wherever there is the hoarding of gold, Kaliyuga will be there and the great Acharyas tell us that wherever there is the hoarding of gold, greed for gold inevitably there will be intoxication, illicit sex, gambling and meat eating. All things follow greed. It is also said in the Srimad Bhagvatam that gold is so dangerous because it is so glittery that the glitter of gold attracts the minds of the materialist like nothing else. Therefore a devotee sees gold as nothing but yellow stool. When you have jaundice your stool is yellow. It is stool of a diseased. So a great soul sees gold with that vision, he is not attracted. Even the great devotees of the Lord, when they want to attract the materialist even to devotional service sometimes they use this tendency, but that is good, that is their mercy, in New Vrindavan through Srila Bhaktipad’s very genius idea was let us, let the whole world come to have darshan with Srila Prabhupada, my Guru Maharaj. Let everyone come and fall at his feet and hear his teachings and see the glory of his life. So how to bring them, how to bring a materialistic society that’s mesmerized by wealth. Everyone’s attracted by the glitter of gold. So he took gold leaf which costs, it’s very inexpensive because it’s so thin. If you take a piece of paper, 1/100th. the thickness of a piece of paper is about what the piece of gold leaf is. If you breathe on it, it will just fly to the other side of the room. But even though it is so thin the nature of gold is that it’s such an incredible element that such a thin piece of gold if its put on a building it will last fifty, sixty, a hundred years even through rain and snow. So he covered the domes of Srila Prabhupada’s samadhi with gold and called it Prabhupada’s palace of gold. You think people come to see Prabhpad? People come to see gold. The devotees come to see Prabhupad, but the average man they come to see the gold. A dome covered with gold, such a sense enjoyment to see the glitter of gold sparkling in the sunlight. But when they come they see that just like Srila Bhaktipad used to say, that Srila Prabhupada palace is meant just like a piece of jewelry. You have a gold setting so that you can put within that setting the most valuable jewel. So he created this beautiful golden palace just to set in the centre the most valuable jewel, his divine grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Srila Prabhupada. So when people come to see the gold they also have to see that the gold is a setting and sitting on the altar in the most merciful and benevolent transcendental manner is Srila Prabhupada, the crest jewel of all humanity was descended from Goloka Vrindavan to give us all, love of God.

So in this way, such is the power of attraction of gold. People kill, they steal, they’ll do anything for gold, for wealth and even pious people, they maybe in the mode of goodness but still they are very much attached to wealth, to the glitter of gold. So here we see what gold does to people who are attached. Here is a mountain of gold, what more can you want than a mountain of gold and this mountain is miles and miles and miles high and the demigods and the demons, who are all in the mode of goodness, passion or ignorance but they are all very very much addicted and attached to the glitter of gold and here right in their own hands is more gold that they can possibly ever desire and they are being crushed by that very mountain of gold. The very object of their greatest affection is crushing them, tearing their limbs apart, killing them, bruising them. They are screaming and crying in agony but they can’t let go. This is the nature of this material world. Gold is the symbol of attachment. Any attachments we have in this world, it’s so crazy, because the very thing that we are dreaming, imagining, meditating upon to give us pleasure will ultimately crush us and ultimately cause our spiritual death. Therefore, it is said

ye hi saṁsparśa-jā bhogā
duḥkha-yonaya eva te
ādy-antavantaḥ kaunteya
na teṣu ramate budhaḥ

[Bg 5.22]

[An intelligent person does not take part in the sources of misery, which are due to contact with the material senses. O son of Kuntī, such pleasures have a beginning and an end, and so the wise man does not delight in them.]

An intelligent person does not take part in the sources of misery which are borne of the contact of the senses and their object. Such pleasures have a beginning and an end and so the wise men does not delight in them. Just like a moth is so much attracted to the site of a flame. He’d do anything to get to the flame. If he’s on the other side of a window he’ll just be smashing himself going back and flying as hard as he can into that window to try to break through, because somehow or the other he has this imagination within his mind that if I can be in that fire I’ll be happy I’ll find pleasure. This mental imagination is an illusion. He’s simply meditating the fire, the fire, the fire, the fire. And he goes through great, great lengths and even risks of life and finally when he attains the fire what happens- he’s burnt to ashes and it’s not an easy death. Do you know what it feels like to be, to be roasted by fire. Sometimes devotees when they have difficulty they say I’m fried. But do you really know what it means to be fried. The moth is actually fried, you are not fried. You are in just a little mental anxiety. You really want to know what pain is get fried like the moth and factually anything you are attached to ultimately will fry you like the moth. So similarly the object of everybody’s affection in this world is gold, wealth. Gold as I say is the symbol of all attachment in this material world and we here have the most incredible enjoyers of material nature and here they have the very symbol of ultimate enjoyment right in their hands and it’s crushing them it’s causing them unbearable pain. It’s causing them death. So therefore we should learn from their example that the pleasures of this world are all sources of pain. Sense enjoyment is the cause of spiritual death and spiritual death means no real pleasure, because it is only in spiritual awakening that there is true ananda or bliss and to the degree to which we are attached to the objects of the senses we cannot experience the joy of the soul. The mind as explained in the Gita is the best friend of the conditioned soul if it is attached to Krsna.

bandhur ātmātmanas tasya
yenātmaivātmanā jitaḥ
anātmanas tu śatrutve
vartetātmaiva śatru-vat

[Bg 6.6]

[For him who has conquered the mind, the mind is the best of friends; but for one who has failed to do so, his mind will remain the greatest enemy.]

That the same mind that is attached to sense enjoyments is the worst enemy. The mind is in such illusion. The mind is constantly hankering and lamenting, accepting and rejecting, all on the basis of trying to find happiness where there is no happiness. You spend years and years and years and years hoping, praying, imagining if only I have, if only I can attain this pleasure how much joy it will bring me and then at last when we find it, we are crushed by that very attachment. The nature of maya is to promise us everything and in her promise that she simply cheats us of the valuable time where we could be meditating on Krsna. We’re meditating on some hope for pleasure, some material sense enjoyment. In that very time that we are meditating on material sense gratification, our valuable human life is ticking away. Tick tock tick tock tick tock, one second less, one second less, one second less and our whole life is wasted in this way. In this way maya is cheating us through the enemy of our mind. Therefore the devotee when he reads this story he understands, yes! This is the fact. Why should I contemplate the objects of the senses. Ultimately that dhyāyato viṣayān puṁsaḥ Krsna says while contemplating the objects of the senses you’ll develop attachment for them, from such attachment comes lust. When lust is unfulfilled there is anger krodhād bhavati sammohaḥ from anger we become illusioned. From this illusion we lose of all our memory. Our intelligence is lost and then we engage in crazy abominable activities which simply cause us great suffering. So therefore devotee after reading this beautiful narration understands why should I be contemplating this mountain of gold in the form of whatever material attachments or attractions that I am seeking in my life, when I know in the end it’s simply going to crush me and cause me pain. This is the spirit of the real renunciate. He sees through the fallacies of material nature. Duḥkhālayam aśāśvatam - this place is a place of misery and there’s nothing more to be said than that.

ā-brahma-bhuvanāl lokāḥ
punar āvartino ’rjuna
mām upetya tu kaunteya
punar janma na vidyate

[Bg 8.16]

[From the highest planet in the material world down to the lowest, all are places of misery wherein repeated birth and death take place. But one who attains to My abode, O son of Kuntī, never takes birth again.]

From the highest planet on this material world down to the lowest, they are all places of misery where in repeated birth and death take place. But Krsna says one who attains my abode never takes birth again. So devotee must focus his consciousness on Krsna not be illusioned, not be dissuade by the propaganda of this world which is promising us so many mountains of gold because we know whatever mountain of gold in any form it comes it is simply going to cause us suffering and ultimately crush us and cause us pain. So here the demigods and the demons, the very object of all of their attraction, the thing that they are giving their life and their soul to attain is crushing them, breaking their limbs, smashing their fingers, smashing, tearing their arms out of their sockets. Ultimately many of them are being brutally crushed to death by the very finger giving their life they’re dedicating their life, they’re surrendering their life for. This is material life. In a very, very grand symbolic way the demigods and the demons of the highest order are showing us what material life is all about.

Therefore a devotee understands that the real life is to follow in the footsteps of Queen Kunti. Vishaya, what is that verse she explains that Lord of, Lord of Madhu, Sri Krsna, as the Ganges river flows to the sea without hindrance, let my attraction be forever drawn unto you without being diverted to anything or anyone else. To cultivate this Krsna consciousness this one pointed goal, mām upetya punar janma duḥkhālayam aśāśvatam Those great souls who make Krsna the goal of life they are not interested in this place of misery, the material existence and the pleasures therein. So this must be constantly our target. In the association of sadhus we must constantly be hearing and chanting to make sure our aim is always fixed on Krsna.

vyavasāyātmikā buddhir
ekeha kuru-nandana
bahu-śākhā hy anantāś ca
buddhayo ’vyavasāyinām

[Bg 2.41]

[Those who are on this path are resolute in purpose, and their aim is one. O beloved child of the Kurus, the intelligence of those who are irresolute is many-branched.]

Krsna says in Gita that those who are on this path their aim is one - Krsna , those who are not in this path their aim is many branched, their intelligence is going wherever their senses drag it. So yes our senses are uncontrolled. What to do? We have conditioned ourselves just as time immemorial to be go-das the service of the senses.

tāni sarvāṇi saṁyamya
yukta āsīta mat-paraḥ
vaśe hi yasyendriyāṇi
tasya prajṣā pratiṣṭhitā

[Bg 2.61]

[One who restrains his senses, keeping them under full control, and fixes his consciousness upon Me, is known as a man of steady intelligence.]

The one who restrains his senses and fixes his consciousness upon me is known as a man of steady intelligence. So we have to tolerate the senses. We have to conquer the lower self with the higher self by hearing the transcendental glories of the Lord. It’s very important. We read in Chaitanya Charitamrita in the story of Sarvabhauma Bhattacharya. When Sarvabhauma Bhattacharya saw Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu who was only twenty-four years old at that time and he was very beautiful, he was the most beautiful of all objects in the entire existence, his beautiful golden form and he had just taken sanyas and Sarvabhauma was thinking unless he’s very well versed in Vedanta he cannot maintain his sanyas. Unless you are constantly hearing about the illusion of this material world and you are constantly hearing what is the truth and what to strive for, you cannot maintain a standard of renunciation. So therefore Sarvabhauma was very worried about this young sanyasi and he told Lord Caitanya let me instruct you in Vedanta. Unless you know Vedanta very well, you will not be able to resist the attractions of this world. Your spiritual intelligence which is the gift of God must be so strong that you are always able to discern what is truth and what is illusion and actually Sarvabhauma Bhattacharya on one level we must accept, yes, he was correct. So when we come together to hear the bhakti shastras. To hear about the difference between devotion to Krsna and attachment to material life we are constantly given a good direction.

naṣṭa-prāyeṣv abhadreṣu
nityaṁ bhāgavata-sevayā
bhagavaty uttamaśloke
bhaktir bhavati naiṣṭhikī

[SB 1.2.18]

[By regular attendance in classes on the Bhāgavatam and by rendering of service to the pure devotee, all that is troublesome to the heart is almost completely destroyed, and loving service unto the Personality of Godhead, who is praised with transcendental songs, is established as an irrevocable fact.]

It is said one must hear Bhagwatam daily. If you hear Bhagwatam daily, not just sitting because it’s the formality that I’m supposed to be in class now, but if you are actually hearing with open heart, open ears, open mind like a hungry man eating a nice feast of Prasad. When you’re hungry, when you’re fasting all day and at the end of the day there’s a nice feast do you eat as a ritualistic formality? Aah! You’re just completely absorbed in this Prasad. Right. You’re just being satisfied with every bite. You’re just looking for satisfaction in every particle of that Prasad. This is how we must hear Srimad Bhagvatam. Not just as a ritual, not just O! it’s time for class. Alright from 5.45 to 6.30 I will sit here. Hare Kishna somehow or the other I’ll do it, I’ll make it through this one again, I’ve made it every other day, I’ll make it again. If this is your consciousness what will you hear. But if you really like a starving man relishing each bite of the feast of Krsna Prasad in such a way we should be relishing each word at the narration of Srimad Bhagvatam and if we are like this everyday in the association of sadhus hearing Bhagwat, it will strengthen our intelligence, it will constantly reconfirm our direction in life and we need constant reconfirmation.

In this age of Kaliyuga as soon as you walk outside of the temple, of course here you don’t even have to walk outside of the temple. The outside is coming in constantly. As soon as you just open your ears and your eyes in any direction except Radha Gopinath, you’re just going to be attacked with the propaganda enjoy, enjoy, enjoy. Everyone around you is just trying to enjoy. punaḥ punaś carvita-carvaṇānām they are just chewing the chewed and chewing the chewed and chewing and chewing and chewing and chewing and they are making it look like they’re enjoying like anything but actually what are they enjoying? If you don’t hear everyday the glories of the Bhagwatam, you’ll start thinking they are enjoying, why am I not enjoying. Look at what they do, look at all they have. They’re listening to all this nice music, they get to do anything they want whenever they want. They get to eat whenever they want, whatever they want. They get to have nice associates, girlfriends, boyfriends and they get to drive in nice cars and wear nice clothes and they’re enjoying, look, they are smiling, the brahmacharis are not smiling so much here. They are wearing rags, they are sleeping on the floor. They cannot do anything except they relate to what he says. what is this. The propaganda of the material energy in your own family members is so, what are you doing you crazy fellow, come get married, have nice sex life with a beautiful woman and have nice children that look just like you and they are your obedient servants at all times and have a nice house with nice furniture and nice car and servants and just do what you want and, and think of what you want and be free, be liberated, enjoy your friends, your family members and everyone around you. They are all giving you this propaganda. who are we? No, no. Unless you are constantly fixed in the target of Krsna, as the goal of life. Unless you are constantly fixed with the condition, with the conviction that they are all simply inevitably going to be crushed by mountain, the golden mountain of all their so called attachments. They are all like so many moths, so anxiously flying towards the fire. In due course of time they will all be burnt to ashes. If we do not have this conviction we will inevitably fall down to material life. Therefore it’s very, very essential that we have the association of devotees and we hear the philosophy of Bhagwat daily. This will give us direction. It will give us strength. It will keep us immune from the illusions of the lying propaganda of maya in this most powerful time of kaliyuga. Kaliyuga is the time when maya is supreme in the lives of practically all. This is her age. To resist her illusions we must be very strong. We must be resolute in purpose and our aim is one. Understand that these people who are enjoying a little in this world, they are nothing compared to what the demigods are enjoying and the demons, I’m talking about the pious people, there are so many pious people, they are just in the mode of goodness and enjoying. They think Oh! I don’t have to be a demon. I’ll just be pious and look at how I can enjoy. The demigods are the most pious and we see how they are being crushed by the very object of their love and the demons, these are the greatest demons, the most successful demons they’ve conquered the universe and they’re all being crushed by the very object of their affection and even the foolish ass, he’s being kicked in the face by the very object of his affection. So from the highest to the lowest, everyone is being embarrassed by material nature. So let us keep our intelligence very strong. Let us keep our hearts very pure. Let us keep our direction in life fixed at the lotus feet of Sri Krsna.

samāśritā ye pada-pallava-plavaṁ
mahat-padaṁ puṇya-yaśo murāreḥ
bhavāmbudhir vatsa-padaṁ paraṁ padaṁ
padaṁ padaṁ yad vipadāṁ na teṣām

[SB 1.2.18]

[For those who have accepted the boat of the lotus feet of the Lord, who is the shelter of the cosmic manifestation and is famous as Murāri, the enemy of the Mura demon, the ocean of the material world is like the water contained in a calf’s hoof-print. Their goal is paraṁ padam, Vaikuṇṭha, the place where there are no material miseries, not the place where there is danger at every step.]

The great souls they make their goal in life only Vaikuntha. They never make their goal in life anything in this material world because they know that this is a place where there is danger at every step.

Is there any questions?

Thank you very much. Hare Krsna.
