[SB. 8.6.33]

Thereafter, with great strength, the demons and demigods, who were all very powerful and who had long, stout arms, uprooted Mandara Mountain. Crying very loudly, they brought it toward the Ocean of Milk.

dūra-bhārodvaha-śrāntāḥ

[SB. 8.6.34]

Because of conveying the great mountain for a long distance, King Indra, Mahārāja Bali and the other demigods and demons became fatigued. Being unable to carry the mountain, they left it on the way.

nipatan sa giris tatra
bahūn amara-dānavān
cūrṇayām āsa mahatā
bhāreṇa kanakācalaḥ

[SB. 8.6.35]

The mountain known as Mandara, which was extremely heavy, being made of gold, fell and smashed many demigods and demons.

Purport

By constitution, gold is heavier than stone. Since Mandara Mountain was made of gold and was therefore heavier than stone, the demigods and demons could not properly carry it to the Ocean of Milk.

tāṁs tathā bhagna-manaso
bhagna-bāhūru-kandharān
vijṣāya bhagavāṁs tatra
babhūva garuḍa-dhvajaḥ

[SB. 8.6.36]

The demigods and demons were frustrated and disheartened, and their arms, thighs and shoulders were broken. Therefore the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who knows everything, appeared there on the back of His carrier, Garuḍa.

giri-pāta-viniṣpiṣṭān
vilokyāmara-dānavān
īkṣayā jīvayām āsa
nirjarān nirvraṇān yathā

[SB. 8.6.37]

Observing that most of the demons and the demigods had been crushed by the falling of the mountain, the Lord glanced over them and brought them back to life. Thus they became free from grief, and they even had no bruises on their bodies.

Lecture Transcription

Opulence of the heavenly planets

In the Bhagavad-gita, Bhagavan Sri Krishna, he tells us that he is the strength of the strong, he is the intelligence of the intelligents, he is the ability in man. Indeed, it is said, ahaṁ sarvasya prabhavo mattaḥ sarvaṁ pravartate [Bg. 10.8] that he is the source of everything, he is Sarva-kāraṇa-kāraṇam [Bs. 5.1], he is the cause of all causes. Here in this beautiful story from the Srimad-Bhagavatam, we find the most powerful personalities in this entire universe. Those persons who are controlling all the affairs of the universe. We are finding the greatest of the demigods and the greatest of the demons.

Some of us have some pride of our ability to control. Some of us are even so foolishly proud of our opulences. A rich man in this world may have a few billion dollars even, but you know what that wealth is compared to the wealth of the demigods. What each demigod has proprietorship over is millions of times more valuable than everything on this earth. The heavenly planets are larger in size than this earth planet and they are filled with the greatest opulence, practically everything is made of gold and jewels, most precious jewels that are… that are not even to be found on this earth planet.

Result of pride of controllership

Just like in… Auh… India the most popular of all fruits is the mango. People are very much satisfied by eating the mangoes. Srila Prabhupad once told us that the mango is one fruit which is a heavenly fruit which grows on the earth. But the difference is the mangoes on the heavenly planets, it’s just described in the fifth canto of Bhagavatam, that when they fall from a tree and break, they create an entire of river of mango juice. It’s very sweet. So, we cannot compare the beauty and the opulence of this world compared to the heavenly planets. As far as strength, as far as knowledge, as far as wealth, as far as physical beauty, we have not even a trace of the opulence of the demigods. And as far as the power to control, if we can control our family, if we can control a few brahmacharis, if we can control… Aurr… a wife is always controlling her husband, husband is thinking he is controlling the wife, he becomes very proud.

What to speak of some Aum… political leaders, like now Auh… the president or the dictator whatever he is, of Iraq, he is very proud that he just marched into this tiny little country of Kuwait and seized it ‘ I’m in control.’ And now he wants to walk into another country and seize it and be in control. And we’ve seen so many of these foolish petty conquerors in this world. We have seen Napoleon, we’ve seen Hitler, we’ve seen Alexander the great, where are they today. They conquer a few places and then ultimately they are smashed, and crushed and they die and they lose everything.

Death conquers unconquerable

So, we are proud of some ability to control, but compared to the demigods they are controlling all the planets and the universes. Indra is in control of the rain of every planet. Vayu is control the… in control of the air and the wind of all planets. Aha… Agni is in control of the fire of all planets. And as far as the demons, demons are very very proud, but compared to these demons we are reading about in the Srimad-Bhagavatam, these demons today are like infant children. When you read about great demons like Hiranyakashpu, my God! He was so powerful. These demons today that wreak so much fare amongst men, Auh… what are they? They can’t, they cannot even conquer this one little planet. We have not seen a... a single demon within our modern history that could conquer this one little tiny planet earth and they’ve all strived to do so, so much. Hitler, his goal was to conquer the earth. Auhe… conquered a few countries, then he was killed. Napoleon wanted to conquer the earth, Aah… killed. Now some of these leaders in the middle eastern countries, they want to conquer the earth. Aah… they will be squashed like flies.

So Hiranyakashipu actually conquered the universe and his great grandson is Bali Maharaj who is more powerful than him. He conquered the universe too. Bali Maharaj with the help of his demon associates literally were able to defeat Indra with his mighty thunderbolt in causing to run in fear of his life. And all the other demigods, they’re hiding, hiding from the power and the strength of Bali Maharaj and his great demon forces. But here, we find that the demons and the demigods, the most powerful controllers, that we will ever read of, ultimately, they have to turn to Krishna for everything.

Gold, the symbol of wealth

This great Mandara mountain is like a lesson for all of them. They were told that, “You move this Mandara mountain to the ocean of milk, you churn it and you will get nectar.” So, “Yes, yes we will move the mountain.” But the mountain is made of gold. Everyone is in anxious of gold. Of course, for a devotee who is on the transcendental platform, he sees that there is no difference between gold and the stones in the street. Krishna explains that in Bhagavad-gita, ‘The one who sees gold and pebbles in the street with equal vision, he is a man of real intelligence. He is a man of real detachment.’ But the materialists, they are constantly making this distinction, gold is the symbol of wealth. I know in America, in perhaps in other countries as well, the whole currency exchange is based on gold. Gold is the supreme symbol of wealth in this world.

In the United States of America, there’s a place called Fort Knox. And in Fort Knox they gathered as much gold as possible through out the land and made it into special bars, gold bars and they all had a government seal put on them. And there was just tons and tons of gold put into Fort Knox with the highest security possible. And the whole wealth of the country was based on this gold. And when they wanted to make currency, they would give you a piece of paper which said that, ‘This means you have the right to so much gold on Fort Knox.’ And because you have the right to that much gold for this paper is very much worth something. Gradually, they just started making more and more and more and more and more money and then they started making for silver. Silver certificate it says on every dollar bill, in the past, silver certificate. Now it doesn’t say anything because they just print, nothing, it’s not worth the paper it’s on.

Therefore, the economy has to take a… take… take a dive into disaster because there’s nothing to base the wealth on. So gold is the symbol of wealth. We notice Hiranyakashipu, Hiranyaksha, their great success is that they had, Hiranyakashipu was very famous because he had great gold and Hiranyaksha also, he was able to extract so much gold from the earth. And the Srimad-Bhagavatam describes that Auh… Kaliyuga, when the personification was defeated by Maharaj Parikshit, Maharaj Parikshit said that, “You can dwell only in four places where there is illicit sex, intoxication, gambling and meat eating.” And Kali responded that, “These things cannot exist within your kingdom because you are so powerful as a ruler. You enforce the dharma, where will I… where will I be? You have to give me some place, I am surrendered to you.”

Effect of glittering gold

So Maharaj Parikshat said, “Ah! Wherever there is the holding of gold, Kaliyuga will be there.” And the great acharyas tell us that ‘Wherever there is the holding of gold, greed for gold, inevitably there will be intoxication, illicit sex, gambling and meat eating.’ All things follow greed. It is also said in the Srimad-Bhagavatam that gold is so dangerous because it is so glittering that the glitter of gold attracts the minds of the materialist like nothing else. Therefore, a devotee sees gold as nothing but yellow stool. When you have jaundice, your stool is yellow. It is stool of a diseased.

Medicine in candy

So, a great soul sees gold with that vision, he is not attracted. Even the great devotees of the lord, when they want to attract the materialist, even to devotional service, sometimes they use this tendency, but that is good, that is their mercy. At New Vrindavan, Srila Bhaktipad’s very… Arr… genius idea was ‘Let us, let the whole world come to have darshan with Srila Prabhupad, my guru maharaj. Let everyone come and fall at his feet and hear his teachings and see the glory of his life.’ So how to bring them? How to bring a mam… materialistic society that’s completely mesmerized by wealth. Everyone’s attracted by the glitter of gold.

So he take… he took gold leaf which costs, it’s very inexpensive because it’s so thin. If you take a piece of paper, one-hundredth, the thickness of a piece of paper is about what a piece of gold leaf is. If you breathe on it, it will just fly to the other side of the room. But it is very… even though it is so thin, the nature of gold is that it’s such an incredible element that such a thin piece of gold, if its put on a building, it will last fifty, sixty, hundred years even through rain and snow.

Valuable jewel in gold setting

So, he covered the domes of Srila Prabhupad’s samadhi with gold and called it Prabhupad’s palace of gold. You think people come to see Prabhupad? People come to see gold. The devotees come to see Prabhupad, but the average man, they come to see the gold. A dome covered with gold, such a sense enjoyment to see the glitter of gold sparkling in the sunlight. Ah..um… But when they come, they se… they see that just like Srila Bhaktipad used to say that ‘Srila Prabhupad palace is meant just like a piece of jewelry. You have a gold setting so that you can put within that setting the most valuable jewel.’ Aha… So, he created this beautiful golden palace, just to set in the center, the most valuable jewel, his divine grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupad.

So, when people come to see the gold, they also have to see that the gold is a setting and on sitting on the altar, in the most merciful and benevolent transcendental manner is Srila Prabhupad, the crest jewel of all humanity, was descended from Goloka Vrindavan to give us all love of God.

Crushed by a ‘Crush’

So, in this way, such is the power of the attraction of gold. People kill, they steal, they’ll do anything for gold, for wealth. And even pious people, they may be in the mode of goodness but still they are very much attached to wealth, to the glitter of gold. So here, we see what gold does to people who are attached. Here is a mountain of gold, what more can you want than a mountain of gold and this mountain is miles and miles and miles high. And the demigods and the demons, who are all either in the mode of goodness, passion or ignorance but they are all very very much addicted and attached to the glitter of gold and here right in their own hands is more gold that they can possibly ever desire.

And they are being crushed by that very mountain of gold. Huum… The very object of their greatest affection is crushing them, tearing their limbs apart, killing them, bruising them. They are screaming and crying in agony but they can’t let go. Aha… This is the nature of this material world. Gold is the symbol of attachment. Any attachments we have in this world, it’s so crazy, because the very thing that we are dreaming, imagining, meditating upon to give us pleasure will ultimately crush us and ultimately cause our spiritual death. Auh… Therefore, it is said,

ye hi saṁsparśa-jā bhogā
duḥkha-yonaya eva te
ādy-antavantaḥ kaunteya
na teṣu ramate budhaḥ

[Bg. 5.22]

[An intelligent person does not take part in the sources of misery, which are due to contact with the material senses. O son of Kuntī, such pleasures have a beginning and an end, and so the wise man does not delight in them.]

Flight to site of a flame

‘An intelligent person does not take part in the sources of misery which are borne of the contact of the senses and their object. Such pleasures have a beginning and an end and so the wise men does not delight in them.’ Just like a moth is so much attracted to the site of a flame. He’d do anything to get to the flame. If he’s on the other side of a window, he’ll just… Hhsss… smashing himself going backward, flying as hard as he can into that window to try to break through, because somehow or the other, he has this imagination within his mind that, if I can gain that fire, I’ll be happy, I’ll find pleasure. This mental imagination is an illusion. He’s simply meditating the fire, the fire, the fire, the fire. And he goes through great, great lengths and even risks his life and finally when he attains the fire, what happens? Tus…tus..shhu..pisssu… He’s burnt to ashes and it’s not an easy death. Do you know what it feels like to be, to be roasted by fire? Aha...

Sometimes devotees, when they have difficulty, they say, ‘I’m fried.’ Auh? But do you know really means to be fried. [laughter] The moth is actually fried, you are not fried. You’ve just a little mental anxiety. You really want to know what pain is? Get fried like the moth. And factually anything you are attached to ultimately will fry you like the moth. So similarly, the object of everybody’s affection in this world is gold, wealth. Gold as I say is the symbol of all attachment in this material world. And we here have the most incredible enjoyers of material nature. And here they have the mo… the… the very symbol of the ultimate enjoyment right in their hands and it’s crushing them, it’s causing them unbearable pain, it’s causing them death.

Cheating of mind

So therefore, we should learn from their example that the pleasures of this world are all sources of pain. Sense enjoyment is the cause of spiritual death and spiritual death means no real pleasure. Because it is only in spiritual awakening there is… there is true ananda or bliss. And to the degree to which we are attached to the objects of the senses, we cannot experience the joy of the soul. The mind as explained in Gita is the best friend of the conditioned soul, if it is attached to Krishna.

bandhur ātmātmanas tasya
yenātmaivātmanā jitaḥ
anātmanas tu śatrutve
vartetātmaiva śatru-vat

[Bg. 6.6]

[For him who has conquered the mind, the mind is the best of friends; but for one who has failed to do so, his mind will remain the greatest enemy.]

That the same mind that is attached to sense enjoyments is the worst enemy. The mind is in such illusion. The mind is constantly hankering and lamenting, accepting and rejecting, all on the basis of trying to find happiness where there is no happiness. Wespend years and years and years and years hoping, praying, imagining if only I have, if only I can attain this pleasure how much joy it will bring me. And then when we… alas, when we find it, we are crushed by that very attachment.

Mills of time grinds slow but fine

The nature of Maya is to promise us everything and in her promise that she simply cheats us of the valuable time where we could be meditating on Krishna. We’re meditating on some hope for pleasure from some material sense enjoyment, and that very time that we are meditating on material sense gratification, our valuable human life is ticking away. [Maharaj makes sound of a clock] Tick tock tick tock tick tock, one second less, one second less, one second less and our whole life is wasted in this way. In this way Maya is cheating us through the enemy of our mind.

Therefore, the devotee when he reads this story, he understands, ‘Yes! This is the fact.’ Why should I contemplate the objects of the senses? Ultimately that… Aha… dhyāyato viṣayān puṁsaḥ [Bg. 2.62]. Krishna says when contemplating the objects of the senses, you develop attachment for them, from such attachment comes lust. When lust is unfulfilled there is anger, krodhād bhavati saṁmohaḥ [Bg. 2.63]. From anger we become illusioned. From this illusion, we lose all of our memory. Our intelligence is lost and then we engage in crazy abominable activities which simply cause us great suffering.

History solves mystries

So therefore, a devotee after reading this beautiful narration, understands why should I be contemplating this mountain of gold in the form of whatever material attachments or attractions that I am seeking in my life? When I know in the end, it’s simply going to crush me and cause me pain. Ahh? This is the spirit of the real renunciate. He sees through the fallacies of material nature. duḥkhālayam aśāśvatam [Bg. 8.15]. This place is a place of misery and there is nothing more to be said than that.

ā-brahma-bhuvanāl lokāḥ
punar āvartino ’rjuna
mām upetya tu kaunteya
punar janma na vidyate

[Bg. 8.16]

[From the highest planet in the material world down to the lowest, all are places of misery wherein repeated birth and death take place. But one who attains to My abode, O son of Kuntī, never takes birth again.]

‘From the highest planet on this material world down to the lowest, they are all places of misery where in repeated birth and death take place.’ But Krishna says ‘One who attains my abode never takes birth again.’

False promise turns our life into a mess

So, a devotee must focus his consciousness on Krishna, not be illusioned, not be dissuade by the propaganda of this world which is promising us so many mountains of gold. Because we know whatever mountain of gold in any form it comes, it is simply going to cause us suffering and ultimately crush us and cause us pain. Auh…

So here the demigods and the demons, the very object of all of their attraction, the thing that they are giving their life and their soul to attain is crushing them, breaking their limbs, smashing their fingers, smashing, tearing their arms out of their sockets. Ultimately, many of them are being brutally crushed to death by the very thing they’re giving their life, they’re dedicating their life, they’re surrendering their life for. This is material life. In a very, very grand symbolic way, the demigods and the demons of the highest order are showing us, what material life is all about.

Undiverted flow of attraction

Therefore, devotee understands that the real life is to follow in the footsteps of Queen Kunti. Vishaya… what is that verse? She explains that ‘lord of… lord of Madhu, Sri Krishna, as the Ganges forever flows to the sea without hindrance, let my attraction be forever drawn unto you without being diverted to anything or anyone else.’ To cultivate this Krishna consciousness, this one pointed goal, mām upetya punar janma duḥkhālayam aśāśvatam [Bg. 8.15]. Those great souls who make Krishna their goal of life, they are not interested in this place of misery, the material existence and the pleasures therein. So, this must be constantly our target. In the association of sadhus, we must constantly be hearing and chanting to make sure our… our aim is always fixed on Krishna.

vyavasāyātmikā buddhir
ekeha kuru-nandana
bahu-śākhā hy anantāś ca
buddhayo ’vyavasāyinām

[Bg. 2.41]

Those who are on this path are resolute in purpose, and their aim is one. O beloved child of the Kurus, the intelligence of those who are irresolute is many-branched.

Krishna says in Gita that, ‘Those who are on this path their aim is one, Krishna. Those who are not in this path, their aim is many branched, their intelligence is going wherever their senses drag it.’ So yes, our senses are uncontrolled, What to do? We have conditioned ourselves just as time immemorial to be godas, the service of the senses.

tāni sarvāṇi saṁyamya

[Bg. 2.61]

‘The one who restrains his senses and fixes his consciousness upon me is known as a man of steady intelligence.’ So, we have to tolerate the senses. We have to conquer the lower self with the higher self by hearing the transcendental glories of the lord. It’s very important.

Necessity of scriptures

We read in Chaitanya-caritamrita, in the story of Sarvabhauma Bhattacharya. Hare Krishna. When Sarvabhauma Bhattacharya saw Lord Chaitanya Mahaprabhu who was only twenty-four years old at that time and he was very beautiful. He was the most beautiful of all objects in the entire existence, his beautiful golden form. And he had just taken sannyas and Sarvabhauma was thinking ‘Unless he’s very well versed in Vedanta, he cannot maintain his sannyas. Unless you are constantly hearing about the illusion of this material world and you are constantly hearing what is the truth, and what to strive for, you cannot maintain a standard of renunciation.’

So therefore, Sarvabhauma was very worried about this young sannyasi and he told Lord Chaitanya that, “Let me instruct you in Vedanta. Unless you know Vedanta very well, you will not be able to resist the attractions of this world. You have to… your intelligence… Your spiritual intelligence which is the gift of God, must be so strong that you are always able to discern what is truth, and what is illusion.” And actually, Sarvabhauma Bhattacharya on one level, we must accept, yes, he was correct. So, when we come together to hear the bhakti shastras, to hear about the difference between devotion to Krishna and attachment to material life, we are constantly given a good direction.

naṣṭa-prāyeṣv abhadreṣu

Ritual with right spirit is spiritual

It is said one must hear Bhagavatam daily. If you hear Bhagavatam daily, not just sitting because it’s the formality that I’m supposed to be in class now. But if you are actually hearing with open heart, open ears, open mind like a hungry man eating a nice feast of prasad. Uha… When you’re hungry, when you’re fasting all day and at the end of the day, there’s a nice feast, do you eat as a ritualistic formality? Aah… You’r… you’re just completely absorbed in this prasad. Right. You’re just being satisfied with every bite. Auh… You’re just looking for satisfaction in every… in every particle of that prasad.

This is how we should hear Srimad-Bhagavatam. Not just as a ritual, not just, ‘O! it’s time for class. Alright from… from 5:45 to 6:30, I will sit here. Hare Krishna, somehow or other, I’ll do, I’ll make it through this one again, I’ve made it every other day, I’ll make it again.’ If this is your consciousness, what will you hear? But if you really like a…. like a starving man relishing each bite of a feast of Krishna prasad, in such a way, we should be relishing each word of the narration of Srimad-Bhagavatam. And if we are like this everyday, in the association of sadhus hearing Bhagavat, it will strengthen our intelligence, it will constantly reconfirm our direction in life and we need constant reconfirmation.

Protecting from worldly brain washing

In this age of Kaliyuga, as soon as you walk outside of the temple, of course here, you don’t even have to walk outside of the temple, the outside is coming in constantly. Ah? As soon as you just open your ears and your eyes in any direction except Radha Gopinath, you’re just going to be attacked with the propaganda, ‘Enjoy! Enjoy! Enjoy! Everyone around you is just trying to enjoy. punaḥ punaś carvita-carvaṇānām [SB. 7.5.30]. They are all chewing the chewed, and chewing the chewed, and chewing and chewing and chewing and chewing, and they are making it look like they’re enjoying like anything. But actually what are they enjoying?

If you don’t hear everyday, the glories of the Bhagavatam, you’ll start thinking, ‘They are enjoying, why am I not enjoying? Look at what they do? Look at all they have. They’re listening to all this nice music, they… they... they get to do anything they want, whenever they want. They get to eat whenever they want, whatever they want. They get to have nice, you know associates or girlfriends and boyfriends and… Nhaa… they get to drive in nice cars and they’re wearing nice clothes and they’re enjoying, look, they are smiling. The brahmacharis not smiling so much here, wearing rags, or sleeping on the floor. They cannot do anything except Devamrita, what he says... What is this?’

False propaganda of Maya

The propaganda of the material energy in your… in your own family members would say, “What are you doing, you crazy fellow, come get married, have nice sex life with a beautiful woman and have nice children that look just like you and they… they are just your obedient servants at all times. And have a nice house with nice furniture and nice car and servants and just do what you want and… and think of what you want and be free, be liberated, enjoy!” Your friends, your family members and everyone around you they are all giving you this propaganda. And what are we? We… No, no. Unless you are constantly fixed in the target of Krishna, as the goal of life. Unless you are constantly fixed with the condition, with the conviction that they are all simply inevitably going to be crushed by the mountain, the golden mountain of all their so called attachments. They are all like so many moths, so anxiously flying towards the fire. In due course of time, they will all be burn to ashes. If we have… do not have this conviction, we will inevitably fall down to material life.

Most powerful vaccine dose in Kaliyuga

Therefore, it’s very, very essential that we have the association of devotees and we hear the philosophy of Bhagavat daily. This will give us direction. It will give us strength. It will keep us immune from the illusions of the lying propaganda of Ma… Maya in this… in her most powerful time of Kaliyuga. Kaliyuga is the time where Maya is supreme in the lives of practically all. This is her age. To resist her illusions, we must be very strong. Our… we must be resolute in purpose and our aim is one. Understand that these people who are enjoying a little in this world, they are nothing compared to what the demigods are enjoying. [clears the throat] And the demons, I’m talking about the pious people, there are so many pious people, that are just in the mode of goodness and enjoying. We think ‘Oh! I don’t have to be a demon. I’ll just be pious and look at how I can enjoy.’ The demigods are the most pious and we see how they are being crushed by the very object of their love. Auh… And the demons, these are the greatest demons, the most successful demons, they conquered the universe and they’re all being crushed by the very object of their affection. Auh… And even the foolish ass, he’s being kicked in the face by the very object of his affection.

Vaikunta - the goal of intelligent

So, from the highest to the lowest, everyone is being embarrassed by material nature. So let us keep our intelligence very strong. Let us keep our hearts very pure. Let us keep our direction in life fixed at the lotus feet of Sri Krishna.

samāśritā ye pada-pallava-plavaṁ
mahat-padaṁ puṇya-yaśo murāreḥ
bhavāmbudhir vatsa-padaṁ paraṁ padaṁ
padaṁ padaṁ yad vipadāṁ na teṣām

[SB. 1.2.18]

[For those who have accepted the boat of the lotus feet of the Lord, who is the shelter of the cosmic manifestation and is famous as Murāri, the enemy of the Mura demon, the ocean of the material world is like the water contained in a calf’s hoof-print. Their goal is paraṁ padam, Vaikuṇṭha, the place where there are no material miseries, not the place where there is danger at every step.]

The great souls, they make their goal in life only Vaikunta. They never make their goal in life anything in this material world because they know that this is a place where there is danger at every step.

Is there any questions?

Thank you very much. Hare Krishna.
