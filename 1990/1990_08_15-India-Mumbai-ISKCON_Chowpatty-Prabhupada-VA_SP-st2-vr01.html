Usually, the offering is made after the talks, the verbal offerings, but because we got a late start due to the massive floods in Bombay, we did not want to keep Srila Prabhupada waiting from his normal lunch time. Generally, the way it is done is during the aarti, spiritual master takes the prasad. Just before that while the devotees are offering the flowers and so forth. But this day is for his pleasure, for his convenience, so we should not make his schedule around our lateness. We should make our schedule around the factor of what is most pleasurable to him. In fact, this is the very essence of spiritual life, to please the spiritual master.

yasya prasādād bhagavat-prasādo
yasyāprasādān na gatiḥ kuto ’pi

[Sri Guruvastakam,8]

Vishwanath Chakarvarti Thakur has prayed that if the spiritual master is pleased then Krishna is pleased. But if the spiritual master is not pleased there is no power within all the three worlds that you can acquire, that will please Krishna. ‘Acharya Upasanam’– one who hears from the acharya he knows things as they are. Everyone else, however traditionally religious we may be, or however learned we are in the scriptures, our approach to God is based on mental speculation. Without receiving the divine order of a bonafide spiritual master one cannot understand things as they are.

tad-vijṣānārthaṁ sa gurum eva abhigacchet

[Muṇḍaka Upaniṣad 1.2.12]

The Vedas explain that without hearing from a guru, you cannot understand the Vedas as they are. Bhagavad Gita was translated perhaps over a hundred versions in the English language. Not a single person ever became a devotee of Krishna and the entire Gita is simply saying to become a devotee of Krishna.

man-manā bhava mad-bhakto
mad-yājī māṁ namaskuru

[Bg.9.34]

‘Always think of me, become my devotee, worship me, offer your homage unto me. In this way you will come to me without fail.’

mattaḥ parataraṁ nānyat
kiṣcid asti dhanaṣ-jaya
mayi sarvam idaṁ protaṁ
sūtre maṇi-gaṇā iva

[Bg.7.7]

[O conqueror of wealth, there is no truth superior to Me. Everything rests upon Me, as pearls are strung on a thread.]

‘Krishna says there is no truth superior to me. Everything rests upon me as pearls strung on a thread.’

sarva-dharmān parityajya
mām ekaṁ śaraṇaṁ vraja
ahaṁ tvāṁ sarva-pāpebhyo
mokṣayiṣyāmi mā śucaḥ

[Bg.18.66]

[Abandon all varieties of religion and just surrender unto Me. I shall deliver you from all sinful reactions. Do not fear.]

bhaktyā mām abhijānāti
yāvān yaś cāsmi tattvata

[Bg.18.55]

‘Only by pure devotion can I be understood as I am. The Gita teaches only one thing from the beginning to the end’

paraṁ brahma paraṁ dhāma
pavitraṁ paramaṁ bhavān

….avyayam.

What was Arjunas realization after hearing from Krishna? That Krishna you are the supreme Brahman, you are the supreme purifier, you are the supreme cause of all causes. I surrender to you.

bahūnāṁ janmanām ante
jṣānavān māṁ prapadyate
vāsudevaḥ sarvam iti
sa mahātmā su-durlabhaḥ

[Bg.7.19]

[After many births and deaths, he who is actually in knowledge surrenders unto Me, knowing Me to be the cause of all causes and all that is. Such a great soul is very rare.]

‘After many births and deaths, when one finally comes to the point of knowledge, one surrenders to me Vasudev, knowing me to be the cause of all causes and all that is.

The Gita is teaching to become Krishna’s devotee. To surrender to him. Yesterday we celebrated Janmashtami. Krishna explains the divine nature of His appearance.

janma karma ca me divyam
evaṁ yo vetti tattvataḥ
tyaktvā dehaṁ punar janma
naiti mām eti so ’rjuna

[Bg.4.9]

[One who knows the transcendental nature of My appearance and activities does not, upon leaving the body, take his birth again in this material world, but attains My eternal abode, O Arjuna.]

‘O Arjuna, one who knows the transcendental nature of my appearance in my activities never takes birth again in this material world but attains my abode. ‘

mām upetya punar janma
duḥkhālayam aśāśvatam
nāpnuvanti mahātmānaḥ
saṁsiddhiṁ paramāṁ gatāḥ

[Bg.8.15]

[After attaining Me, the great souls, who are yogīs in devotion, never return to this temporary world, which is full of miseries, because they have attained the highest perfection.]

That the great souls, they surrender to me. They make me the object of the goal of life and thus they never have to suffer the miseries of material existence, but they attain my abode.

So how is it over one hundred translations in the English language of the Bhagavad Gita and the Gita only deals with one subject ultimately – to surrender to Krishna. To become His devotee, to know him as the cause of all causes and not a single person of all the millions of people that studied the Bhagavad Gita, including the greatest most proficient professors and scholars, not a single one even considered becoming a devotee of Krishna.

nāhaṁ prakāśaḥ sarvasya
yoga-māyā-samāvṛtaḥ
mūḍho ’yaṁ nābhijānāti
loko mām ajam avyayam

[[Bg.7.25]

[I am never manifest to the foolish and unintelligent. For them I am covered by My internal potency, and therefore they do not know that I am unborn and infallible.]

The reason is very simple. Although the words of Krishna are right before their eyes, they cannot see it, because Krishna does not reveal the truth even if the scriptures, unless you approach the scriptures according to his command. And what is his command?

tad viddhi praṇipātena
paripraśnena sevayā
upadekṣyanti te jṣānaṁ
jṣāninas tattva-darśinaḥ

[Bg.4.34]

[Just try to learn the truth by approaching a spiritual master. Inquire from him submissively and render service unto him. The self-realized souls can impart knowledge unto you because they have seen the truth.]

‘If you want to know the truth, you must approach a bonafide spiritual master. You must inquire submissively and render service unto him. The self-realized soul can impart knowledge unto you because he has seen the truth’.

Therefore Acharya Upasanam, one who hears from the Acharya understands everything as it is. But whatever you try to understand through your own intellectualization, through the teachings of various scholars or other so called religious reformers, you cannot understand the truth. The truth is only revealed when the truth reveals himself. It is not by your prowess that you can ever conceive of the truth. The truth is a supreme person, and the truth remains hidden until he decides by his sweet independent will to reveal himself to you. Therefore, you must approach the truth according to his command

Tasmād guruṁ prapadyeta jijṣāsuḥ śreya uttamam.[SB.11.3.21]

Bhagvatam says if you want to know truth, you must approach the Guru. So Srila Prabhupada wrote one translation of Bhagavad Gita – ‘Bhagavad Gita as it is’. Why did he name it as it is? From this purely scholarly point of view, there were far superior people writing Bhagavad Gita. After all, Srila Prabhupada was going to college, but he left his college because Gandhi’s non partition, non-participation movement with the British. He decided to become a follower of Gandhi. Therefore, he could not continue college because all the colleges were run by the British. So, from the scholarly point of view, he had no degrees and there are multiple Ph. D’s that[hmm] have translated Gita. Why they cannot give anyone the enlightenment of the truth of the Gita. Srila Prabhupada wrote Bhagavad Gita As It Is, because he was a surrendered devotee of the Lord.

evaṁ paramparā-prāptam
imaṁ rājarṣayo viduḥ

[Bg.4.2]

Krishna says that this knowledge must be received from guru in disciplic succession. Srila Prabhupada used to tell us that I have not written this book. This is being written by all the previous acharyas. I am simply like the instrument, like the pen within their hands. Sometimes Srila Prabhupada would have the devotees read Bhagavad Gita to him. Now from materialistic point of view this would seem to be egoistic. Every night having people read your own books to you, but when Srila Prabhupada would hear Bhagavad Gita, he would not hear his voice, he would hear exclusively the voice of his Guru Maharaj and all the previous acharyas and therefore hearing the sweetness of their teachings brought him ultimate ecstasy to his life. When we would read to him the Nectar of devotion, he would hear the voice of Srimati Radharani speaking- She is the author of that book. It was written by Rupa Goswami, but it is the incarnation of Srimati Radharani. When we would read Krishna book to him, he would say to us I have not written this book, Krishna has written this book. An acharya is one who selflessly surrenders to the previous acharyas. Therefore, he knows things as they are. I remember one time a newspaper reporter was very challenging. They said Bhaktivedanta Swami, I want to know,” Are you perfect?” Srila Prabhupada replied,” I may be perfect, I may not be perfect. In fact, as far as I am concerned, I am not perfect, but I am exclusively repeating the words of my Guru Maharaj, Om Vishnupad Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur and therefore because I am only repeating his words, everything I say is one hundred percent perfect.” And I remember when we were all just very new devotees, we didn’t know hardly anything, we were not this body, Krishna is God, chant hare Krishna, bas that’s all we knew. Srila Prabhupada told us go out and preach. We did not know so many shlokas to defend the absolute truth. He said you simply repeat what you have heard from me, whatever quantity it is in, it is perfect. Don’t speculate, don’t add, don’t subtract, simply repeat what you have heard from me. If you have heard one thing repeat that, if you have heard two things repeat that, if you have heard three things repeat that, but preach and your preaching will be perfect. But he said,” I have written so many books, you read them, and you’ll learn more and more and more and more. And the more you learn, more service you can offer to the world.”

Srila Prabhupada’s Greatest Gift

This is the great gift that Srila Prabhupada has given us. The gift that he has given us we will never understand. It is the gift of divine love. It is the gift of utter sacrifice of his body, his mind, his words, his very life, for the welfare of each and every one of us. Srila Prabhupada explains that it was at that great and famous moment, it took place in Calcutta, the address was no. 1 Ultadanga junction Road. It was an old building and Srila Prabhupada was brought by his friend Narendra to the roof of that building and there seated on a dais, under the shade of a beautiful jackfruit tree, in the evening moonlight, was sitting that great empowered acharya, Om Vishnupad Paramhamsa Parivrajakacharya Srila Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur. He was speaking on the Chaitanya Charitamrita and Srila Prabhupada not knowing who this sadhu was or anything about him, he bowed down as was the tradition just at the feet of Srila Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur and as he was getting up, Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu’s voice echoed through the mouth of Srila Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur. The great command came. You are a very intelligent young man. You should take the teachings of Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu, and you should spread it in the English language throughout all the world. Srila Prabhupada who was then a follower of Gandhi, began to defend his position. But as long as we are dependent on the British, no one will take our philosophy seriously. First, we must gain independence. That’s what we are doing in Gandhi’s movement. Then we can teach Gita and Caitanya Mahaprabhu’s message to the world and then people will listen. And Srila Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur replied-,” No. There is only one urgency in this world and that is Krishna consciousness. There is only one problem in this world and that is a lack of Krishna consciousness”. Political parties will always be fighting. There will always be changes in this relative world. This is no concern to us. The propagation of Krishna consciousness is the absolute necessity, and it must be preached now, otherwise this world is in a hopeless condition. When Srila Prabhupada heard this, he became very happy. He told us many times, my Guru Maharaj defeated me, and I was most happy to be defeated. This is similar to Krishna and Arjuna. Arjuna was presenting arguments, but when he heard Krishna, he was blissfully willing to accept defeat at the feet of his Guru and surrender. This is the qualification of the bonafide disciple. That he humbles himself, pridelessly before his Guru, that I know nothing, you know everything, please instruct me.

]Srila Prabhupada: a perfect master and a perfect disciple

Srila Prabhupada has taught us this example. If we want to be bonafide disciples, we have to be willing to accept defeat before our Guru. We have to come before our Guru with no pride. We have to come before our spiritual master, I know nothing. I am fool number one. Please instruct me. Sanatan Goswami also approached Mahaprabhu Sri Caitanya in this way. I am a scholar in Persian, I am a scholar in Bengali, I am a scholar in Sanskrit. I’m wealthy, I’m learned, I’m handsome. I have everything anyone can possibly want, and I’m considered the greatest spiritual personality, but to tell you the truth, before you, I realize I am nothing and I have nothing. I ‘m fool number one. If you consider yourself fool number two, you cannot hear from your Guru properly. You have to accept the position that I am fool number one. Doesn’t matter how much influence you have, how much money you have, how many scholarships or degrees you have, how many tapasyas you have performed, how many names of God you’ve chanted. These things mean nothing when you come before your guru, you’re fool number one and if you’re not you cannot learn from him. Not properly. The seed of bhakti is being plucked by ego, by pride. So Srila Prabhupada taught us in this way. He came before his Guru and he accepted the position of defeat at his feet and at that moment, within his heart he accepted Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur, as his Guru Maharaj, as his eternal master and savior. The seed of bhakti was planted within his heart at that time and throughout his life his main ambition was to water that seed. He was a grihastha. He would sometimes lament, that all most all of my God brothers are brahmacharis and sanyasis. They get to be with Guru Maharaj all the time, traveling with him, preaching with him. I have a business, I have children, I have wife, so many responsibilities. But he knew this was the will of his Guru, so he accepted it. And it was the will. Whenever Srila Prabhupada would write, Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur had a daily newspaper with spiritual subject matter, was called the Harmonist. When so many people wrote and they had so many editors and most of the things written were not included because of, if there was the slightest tinge of philosophy which wasn’t strictly according to the teachings of the six goswamis of Vrindavan, it would not be included. But when Abhaycharan Dey who was later initiated as Abhay Charanarvinda whenever he would write, Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur said “it is my law, that whatever he writes you print. No question of editing. No question of considering, whatever Abhay writes you print.” Why? Because he knew he would not speak anything that he had not heard from his Guru Maharaj. It was at RadhaKund during the famous Vraj Mandal parikrama of Srila Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur. I am sorry, this particular occasion took place at Koshi. They were camped at Koshi and all the devotees were going on parikrama to go to the Sheshashayee mandir in Vrajdham. But Srila Prabhupada stayed at his feet of his guru. Everyone else was coming in and going out, but he stayed throughout the day, simply hearing from his Guru Maharaj and Srila Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur, Prabhupada tells us, was very pleased with him. He said, Aah! This young man, he said, because he hears so nicely, everything he says will be perfect. So, try to understand that Srila Prabhupada, the supreme master, the paramhamsa guru, he taught us in his life that his qualification was that he was the perfect disciple.

In Bombay, Srila Prabhupada, he had his business here. He was approached by one of his very very dearly beloved God brothers who accepted in his heart with such love and devotion. In fact, he accepted him as his Shiksha Guru. His name is his divine grace Bhakti Rakshak Sridhar Swami Maharaj. And also, his divine grace Srila Bhakti Saranga Maharaj was also here. And they approached Srila Prabhupada and asked him, can you help us to start a centre here, you know so many people. You have so much influence. So Srila Prabhupada practically day and night, he would do whatever was absolutely necessary for his business, and the rest of the time he was simply in kirtan with these great vaishnavas, and they would go and setup various programs at different halls and in people’s homes. Srila Prabhupada would lead the kirtan, Srila Sridhar Dev Maharaj would give the lecture and then Bhakti Saranga Maharaj would give the presentation of the temple to raise funds. So, between the three of them they dedicated their lives to Srila Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur’s mission. And between the three of them they acquired a beautiful place only about ten minutes from here. It is still present the Gaudiya math, and they established a beautiful temple and preaching centre and they called Srila Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur to come to install the deities of Radha and Krishna. When Srila Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur came, Srila Sridhar Maharaj and Srila Bhakti Saranga Maharaj, they were so happy with Srila Prabhupada’s great great devotion and his expert preaching, they appealed to Srila Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur we are sanyasis we have to preach and travel but this Abhaycharan Charanarvinda, he is such a first class man, we think that he should leave his worldly responsibilities and he should be the president of this math and Srila Bhakti Siddhanta Saraswati Thakur replied “No”. He said he is doing just what I want him to do, and he will do everything in due course of time, you just see. So Srila Prabhupada, although he appeared to be a businessman, actually he was simply a surrendered soul to the divine plan of his Guru Maharaj and at the proper time by Krishna’s divine arrangement, he left his home to reside in Vrindavan and there in deep deep contemplation and prayer he was appealing to his Guru Maharaj Srila Rupa Goswami and all the previous Acharyas, to empower him to do the work of his spiritual master. And of course, we know the story, how much faith, how much hope he had on the basis of his guru’s order. He left his home, Vrindavan, seventy years old, no money, no friends, on a cargo ship, across the Atlantic, several heart attacks, when he arrived in New York city he had no place to stay. He was staying with crazy drug addicts and alcoholics. People threatened his life. People kicked him out into the streets in the middle of winter. He left Vrindavan for this. He appealed to so many people for help, but no one wanted to help. He was struggling alone, but he had only one possession, the gift that his guru had given him, infinite compassion for the sufferings of the forgetful living entities in this world. His heart yearned, his heart cried to see his brothers and sisters from Goloka Vrindavan separated from Krishna and suffering the repetition of birth and death. It was unbearable. Therefore, all of his sufferings, all of his struggles, all of his problems were insignificant compared to the heaviness in his heart when he saw the sufferings of others. Therefore, he continued on.

Srila Prabhupada was willing to suffer

What is our position? We have our service, the slightest difficulty and we become disturbed in our faith, we want to give up, we want to go somewhere else, we want to do something else. We become discouraged. This is all due to selfishness. It is because we still think we are the enjoyers but Srila Prabhupada showed us what is a real devotee. Srila Prabhupada was willing to suffer. He was willing to suffer all the tortures of hell if necessary, if that’s what it took to be enjoyed by his Guru and Krishna. In fact, it says in the Bhagvatam, that the topmost service of all services is to accept suffering even if risk of death to show compassion to others. We read about it but when we see it before our eyes, then we can understand. Srila Prabhupada’s sacrifice was something that we’ll never understand. We can only pray that someday we can appreciate even a little.

Hare Krishna Movement begins

When he was in New York city, his greatest joy, his greatest happiness was when anybody would even show the slightest little spark of desire to understand Krishna. One of the first people who became attracted to Prabhupada was a very crazy boy. What was his name? I know him very well. He is…I met him many times. He’s still a very crazy boy. His name is something Nelson, Robert Nelson. This Robert Nelson, he had no material qualifications. He was very slow mentally, physically, everything. He had no money. He was a little bit retarded kinderly. Because of that he was getting money from welfare. Srila Prabhupada was giving lectures at this Dr. Mishra’s institute on riverside drive. And it was mostly old ladies that would come and they just, Dr. Mishra wouldn’t even let him speak because he would always be pounding against mayavad philosophy and Dr. Mishra was a self-professed mayavad. So, he would just let Prabhupada sing because he thought, he sang very nicely. But this Robert Nelson, he told me that he used to go to the back of the room after Prabhupada would chant, while Dr. Mishra was talking, he would ask questions to Prabhupada and Prabhupada saw [aah!] This person is interested to learn. He had no qualifications. He only had one. He had a little tiny interest to learn. Prabhupada gave his whole life for this person. Prabhupada had no money. He said you, Prabhupada had a little apartment, not even an apartment, he was just sharing a little flat with someone else, a crazy drug addict. Prabhupada brought him, he said Aah! Would you like me to cook for you? Prabhupada said this. Would you like me to cook for you? He said yes yes. I would love. I will cook nice Indian foods for you. He said yes. So, Robert Nelson, Prabhupada had no money, so Robert Nelson got some of his welfare money and they went to the vegetable market and Prabhupada started shopping for vegetables. And I don’t know if you know America, in America there’s a set price. Prabhupada wasn’t used to this. Prabhupada would go to the store, they’d say, he’d go to the cash register, they’d say Oh! this is two dollars for this cauliflower. Prabhupada would say I will give you one dollar. Robert Nelson was telling me this story. They’d say Sir we don’t, in this country we do not do things like this. Prabhupada would start arguing with them. Actually, he would go to the produce and he would just, whatever he wanted to pay, ultimately the person gave it to him for that. They broke all the conventions and all the rules. Robert Nelson said I couldn’t believe it; I was arguing with Prabhupada right in front of the man. He said I’ll give you one dollar for this. No, it costs two, Robert Nelson said Prabhupada we don’t do things like this in this country. And the cashier would say Sir we do not do things like this in this country. Prabhupada said well this is for Krishna and it’s going to be prasad. Who’s Krishna, who’s prasad? Anyways just take it. [devotees:] ‘laughter’ Give me a…. every day they would shop it would be like this and Prabhupada said this is Krishna’s money we should not waste. We should be very frugal with Krishna’s money. And then they’d go home to this little flat. Actually sometimes, one-time Prabhupada was kicked out and he would go to Robert Nelsons little flat, just a little apartment and Prabhupada would set up his pots, he had that three tiered, that with rice in one pot, and [ aah] tiffin with holes in it, rice in one, subji in another and dal in another. And he’d make chapattis while that was going on. So, he’d be cooking like this and then after he’d cook, he’d serve it and Robert Nelson, and they’d eat together. They’d take prasad together. First Prabhupada would offer it to his guru maharaj. Very beautiful thing and then after taking prasad together, Prabhupada would wash all the plates because he knew Robert Nelson didn’t know how to get them clean. At first Robert Nelson, but it wasn’t clean so Prabhupada said I’ll wash the plates, I’ll wash the pots. He did everything. He did all the cutting, all the cooking, because this man was too much, not even able to do the simplest things. You notice, most Indian guru’s that come to the west, they’re looking for big, big politicians and millionaires, so that their name becomes very famous. Prabhupada didn’t care who you were. If you had the slightest inclination for Krishna, you were greater than the greatest in his heart. And then Robert Nelson told me that after Prabhupada would cook and after they would take prasad and after Prabhupada would clean, Robert Nelson would say Prabhupada would you like to stay on my bed, Prabhupada would say no, in India we sleep on, Vaishnav’s sleep on floor. So, they would just sleep, lay on the floor together and they would just talk. Prabhupada would be preaching to him lying on the floor next to him, on his left side, for digestion purposes, left side. This is the way sadhus are. They would just…Prabhupada would talk, I asked Robert Nelson, what did he talk about. He said everything. He’d talk about his Guru. He’d talk about the holy name. He’d tell me stories about Lord Chaitanya, stories of Krishna, stories of when he lived in Vrindavan. Weeks and weeks and weeks it was like this, but this boy he wasn’t making any commitment and gradually he just disappeared and then there is the wonderful pastime of when some devotees helped him get a little storefront on twenty sixth, second avenue on the lower east side of New York .And he started holding Bhagavad Gita classes but practically no one was coming and as he was walking down Houston street, which is just a block away from his storefront, he happened to meet Hayagriva whose name was then Howard Wheeler and Hayagriva approached Prabhupada, because he had just come from India with Keith Ham who later became Kirtanananda Swami. And Hayagriva approached him and said aah! Are you coming from India? Prabhupada said,” Are you?” He said,” No no I’m not from India, but are you?”. Prabhupada said,” yes and I’m teaching Bhagavad Gita, please come, tell me if you think this is a proper place”. Then he invited him to come. In this way the Hare Krishna movement began.

Prabhupada knew how to love

Gradually more and more people started coming and Prabhupada would have kirtans at Tompkins Square park and two devotees, later they became Brahmananda and Achyutananda. They used to dance in the kirtans and even though they were not even following the regulative principles or anything, simply because they were dancing in kirtans the way Prabhupada showed them, with their arms raised, Prabhupada was so happy. He was so overjoyed. Even ten years later he used to remember that I remember when Achyutananda and Brahmananda would dance in Tompkins Square park. They had no other qualification, but they decided to dance, and he showered them with his mercy, with his love and later they became his initiated disciples also. So how did Prabhupada bring his people to his movement. Yes, his philosophy was the highest, most developed philosophy on earth. The spiritual realizations he was offering was the most sublime spiritual experiences, the ultimate realization of God, Radha Krishna and Vrindavan. But it is not for this reason these people became devotees. Later on, they understood these things. It was because Prabhupada, more than anyone they ever met, knew how to love them, knew how to love their souls and knew how to express his love for their soul. Prabhupada would say anything or do anything to raise a person’s consciousness.

Later on,…should I continue or is it getting too late? It’s raining so you have nothing else to do anyway[devotees:] ‘laughter’ and one darshan he had a, I personally gave him a rose one day and he was holding this rose and he had a garland of very simple wildflowers and he was explaining ‘achintya bheda abhedha tatva,’ simultaneously one and different. There was only about three devotees there, myself and two others. We were sitting at Prabhupada’s feet. It was the evening darshan. He would give about two hours darshan every night. He was sitting on his asana and he was talking to us and he said just like this rose and these flowers, he said they are both flowers, so they are one but still one is a rose, and one is a flower, the rose is better. This is one in difference. Of course, for myself I certainly am of no significance to discuss my relationship with Prabhupada, but Srila Prabhupada, this was his glory that he would give special mercy even to the most insignificant.

Why I am here in Bombay is no accident. Because in all the world, I traveled throughout the entire world looking for guru, through America, through Europe, through the Middle East. I was traveling throughout India for over a year. The first place I met my guru maharaj was in Bombay and I did not know how it happened, there was no plan. I was just about to leave, there was no plan, I was just about to leave, I was just passing through, on my way somewhere else and I happened to be walking down the street and I saw a big billboard Hare Krishna festival Cross Maidan. I said where is Cross Maidan? They say, if it was more than two blocks away, I never would have gone. They said its two blocks away. [ahh] I might as well go. So, I went, and I talked to some devotees and I was thinking these people are very nice but they’re not the type of sadhus I can relate to. I was living in Himalayas, matted hair, old dhoti and fasting all the time and sleeping on riverbanks, following all the rituals of the sadhus. And I was thinking these people they all have such nice new clothes, and they have tape recorders. This is not the way; what kind of sadhus are these? They drive in cars. Third class train is the only way a sadhu will ever travel …he’s not walking. They wore shoes. I was thinking O! nice but you know...they are Americans, what do you expect. But they told me that Srila Prabhupada is speaking tonight, just stay till tonight. So, all of a sudden about twenty thousand people come into this big tent. [ah] it was a huge crowd, and I was just in the middle of everybody. When I said who is this? I said to one devotee. This is guru maharaj. I was thinking his name is guru maharaj, I’ll wait till he is done and Prabhupada will come on. [devotees:]’laughter’ So, he was sitting on the Vyas aasan and I was thinking why is guru maharaj sitting on Prabhupada’s vyas. Maybe he’ll get off and then Prabhupada will sit. So, then what happened is, I was thinking like this, I was waiting for Prabhupada. In the end Madhav Tirth swami was leading kirtan. Everyone was dancing. One devotee his name is Gurudas, he was just going like this, looking, he was like at the edge of the stage, going like this, for about ten minutes. Then he looked at me and he went like this and I was thinking, he’s not talking to me this, must be somebody else. So, then he came all the way down, grabbed me by the hand and said Prabhupada wants me to come on stage. I said Prabhupada, where is Prabhupada? This was my first meeting with my guru maharaj. So, then I went up on stage and then when I looked at his face I understood, aw my God, this must be Prabhupada, because he was so grave and so, and he smiled at me and he called me over like this. He was sitting on his Vyasasan, he called me over. All the other devotees were dancing, he called me over and I went, and I stood and then he told me to sit down right at his feet. He said you sit, please sit here. So, I sat down, and he patted me up on the head and he said every night you sit here. And then I sat there, and I was so, compared to the other devotees I so dirty looking, my God. They were all clean shaved, tilaks, nice robes, and kurtas and decorated saris and I had this old dhoti that was a year old with holes in it, and it was only up to my knees and it was river stained white, and a little old chaddar with holes in it and hair that hadn’t been cut in 4 years, hadn’t been combed in a year and a half. I hadn’t shaved in a year and a half. Here I am sitting with all these effulgent transcendental Vaishnavas and Prabhupada just, you stay here. And I sat and Prabhupada chanted ‘Jai Radha Madhav’. No, he didn’t chant Jai Radha Madhav, I don’t believe he chanted Radha Madhav, but he gave his lecture, he gave lecture, and I was listening to the lecture and I remember within thirty seconds after he spoke my heart began to cry out, this is your guru maharaj and you must, he’s very old, you must surrender to him immediately. My heart was just crying that out, but my mind was saying no, no, no. This is all sentiment. There are so many gurus. I had met so many dozens and hundreds of gurus and all of them are so great. So, my heart accepted him, but my mind did not. This is within thirty seconds after sitting next to him. And then after he gave his lecture, he got up to walk away and, living in India I was used to touching holy men’s feet. I would go everywhere and touch holy men’s feet. So, I reached down to touch his feet and one devotee, he grabbed my hand, he kicked my hand and said no, nobody touches Prabhupada’s feet. There was a law, no one was allowed to touch Prabhupada’s feet. In fact, devotees would keep everybody away, nobody was allowed to touch his feet. Devotees used to keep people back when he’d walk away so no one could touch him. But then Prabhupada looked at me, first he told the other devotee to go away. He said no no..you go away. He said he can touch my feet. Then when I took the dust from his feet and put it on my head, and he said, you can touch my feet and then he walked away. So why he called me to sit next to him, to this day I do not know. All I can understand is it was his causeless mercy. Of course, I did not accept him as my guru, officially then, I was so mixed up with so many other people that I was learning from. I could say that the seed of my spiritual conception took place in Bombay, but my birth was in Vrindavan, because it was in Vrindavan that I met Prabhupada again, about one year later. No, it’s not a year later it was about four months later. I met him again in Vrindavan and there he showed very very great kindness to me. All the other devotees were against me, they thought I was a sahajya and everything else as I was living in Vrindavan. And I was, but Prabhupada didn’t care. Prabhupada showed his love, his kindness. I did not deserve anything, and he gave me everything and he’s still giving. Although we are so hesitant to accept it, he’s still giving everything, and I have that faith that for the rest of eternity Srila Prabhupada will be giving us everything. But our great unfortunate condition is we are not willing to accept.

So, on this most blessed and auspicious occasion of Srila Prabhupada’s vyas puja, we should remember him. We should remember that it is either directly or indirectly by his infinite mercy that Krishna has come into our lives. So let us offer our deep deep prayers and love to Srila Prabhupada on this day.
