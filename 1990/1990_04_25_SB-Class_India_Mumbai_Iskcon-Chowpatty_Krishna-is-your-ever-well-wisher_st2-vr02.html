Lecture Transcription

[Mangalacharan]

ātmāvāsyam idaṁ viśvaṁ
yat kiṣcij jagatyāṁ jagat
tena tyaktena bhuṣjīthā
mā gṛdhaḥ kasya svid dhanam

[SB 8.1.10]

TRANSLATION

Within this universe, the Supreme Personality of Godhead in His Supersoul feature is present everywhere, wherever there are animate or inanimate beings. Therefore, one should accept only that which is allotted to him; one should not desire to infringe upon the property of others.

PURPORT BY HIS DIVINE GRACE A.C BHAKTIVEDANTA SWAMI PRABHUPADA

Having described the situation of the Supreme Personality of Godhead as transcendental, Svāyambhuva Manu, for the instruction of the sons and grandsons in his dynasty, is now describing all the property of the universe as belonging to the Supreme Personality of Godhead. Manu's instructions are not only for his own sons and grandsons, but for all of human society. The word "man"—or, in Sanskrit, manuṣya—has been derived from the name Manu, for all the members of human society are descendants of the original Manu. Manu is also mentioned in Bhagavad-gita (4.1), where the Lord says:

imaṁ vivasvate yogaṁ
proktavān aham avyayam
vivasvān manave prāha
manur ikṣvākave ’bravīt

[BG 4.1]

[The Personality of Godhead, Lord Śrī Kṛṣṇa, said: I instructed this imperishable science of yoga to the sun-god, Vivasvān, and Vivasvān instructed it to Manu, the father of mankind, and Manu in turn instructed it to Ikṣvāku.]

"I instructed this imperishable science of yoga to the Sun-god, Vivasvān, and Vivasvān instructed it to Manu, the father of mankind, and Manu in turn instructed it to Ikṣvāku." Svāyambhuva Manu and Vaivasvata Manu have similar duties. Vaivasvata Manu was born of the Sun-god, Vivasvān, and his son was Ikṣvāku the King of the earth. Since Manu is understood to be the original father of humanity, human society should follow his instructions.

Svāyambhuva Manu instructs that whatever exists, not only in the spiritual world but even within this material world, is the property of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who is present everywhere as the super-consciousness. As confirmed in Bhagavad-gita (13.3),kṣetra-jṣaṁ cāpi māṁ viddh; sarva-kṣetreṣu bhārata.

In every field—in other words, in every body—the Supreme Lord is existing as the supersoul. The individual soul is given a body in which to live and act according to the instructions of the supreme person, and therefore the supreme person also exists within every body. We should not think that we are independent; rather, we should understand that we are allotted a certain portion of the total property of the Supreme Personality of Godhead.

This understanding will lead to perfect communism. Communists think in terms of their own nations, but the spiritual communism instructed here is not only nationwide but universal. Nothing belongs to any nation or any individual person; everything belongs to the supreme personality of godhead. That is the beginning of this verse and meaning of this verse. Ātmāvāsyam idaṁ viśvam. Whatever exists within this universe is the property of the supreme personality of godhead. The modern communistic theory, and also the idea of the United Nations, can be reformed—indeed, rectified—by the understanding that everything belongs to the supreme personality of godhead. The Lord is not a creation of our intelligence; rather, he has created us. Ātmāvāsyam idaṁ viśvam ; Īśāvāsyam idaṁ sarvam. This universal communism can solve all the problems of the world.

One should learn from the Vedic literature that one's body is also not the property of the individual soul, but is given to the individual soul according to his karma

karmaṇā daiva-netreṇa jantur dehopapattay

[SB 3.31.1]

[The Personality of Godhead said: Under the supervision of the Supreme Lord and according to the result of his work, the living entity, the soul, is made to enter into the womb of a woman through the particle of male semen to assume a particular type of body.]

The 8,400,000 different bodily forms are machines given to the individual soul. This is confirmed in Bhagavat gita

īśvaraḥ sarva-bhūtānāṁ
hṛd-deśe ’rjuna tiṣṭhati
bhrāmayan sarva-bhūtāni
yantrārūḍhāni māyayā

[BG 18.61]

[The Supreme Lord is situated in everyone’s heart, O Arjuna, and is directing the wanderings of all living entities, who are seated as on a machine, made of the material energy.]

The Lord, as the supersoul, sits in everyone's heart and observes the various desires of the individual soul. The Lord is so merciful that he gives the living entity the opportunity to enjoy varieties of desires in suitable bodies which are nothing but machines yantrārūḍhāni māyayā([Bg. 18.61]). These machines are manufactured by the material ingredients of the external energy, and thus the living entity enjoys or suffers according to his desires. This opportunity is given by the Supersoul.

Everything belongs to the supreme, and therefore one should not usurp another's property. We have a tendency to manufacture many things. Especially nowadays, we are building skyscrapers and developing other material facilities. We should know, however, that the ingredients of the skyscrapers and machines cannot be manufactured by anyone but the supreme personality of godhead. The whole world is nothing but a combination of the five material elements tejo-vāri-mṛdāṁ yathā vinimayo. A skyscraper is a transformation of earth, water and fire. Earth and water are combined and burnt into bricks by fire, and a skyscraper is essentially a tall construction of bricks. Although the bricks may be manufactured by man, the ingredients of the bricks are not. Of course, man, as a manufacturer, may accept a salary from the supreme personality of godhead. That is stated here: tena tyaktena bhuṣjīthā [Īśo mantra 1].One may construct a big skyscraper, but neither the constructor, the merchant nor the worker can claim proprietorship. Proprietorship belongs to the person who has spent for the building. The supreme personality of godhead has manufactured water, earth, air, fire and the sky, and one can use these and take a salary (tena tyaktena bhuṣjīthā). However, one cannot claim proprietorship. This is perfect communism. Our tendency to construct great buildings should be used only for constructing of large and valuable temples in which to install the deity of the supreme personality of godhead. Then our desire for construction will be fulfilled.

Since all property belongs to the supreme personality of godhead, everything should be offered to the Lord, and we should take only prasāda. We should not fight among ourselves to take more than we need. As Nārada said to Mahārāja Yudhistira:

yāvad bhriyeta jaṭharaṁ
tāvat svatvaṁ hi dehinām
adhikaṁ yo ’bhimanyeta
sa steno daṇḍam arhati

[SB 7.14.8]

[One may claim proprietorship to as much wealth as required to maintain body and soul together, but one who desires proprietorship over more than that must be considered a thief, and he deserves to be punished by the laws of nature.]

Of course, we need to be maintained in eating, sleeping, defending and mating (Āhāra-nidrā-bhaya-maithunaṁ), but since the supreme lord, the personality of godhead, has provided these necessities of life for the birds and bees, why not for mankind? There is no need for economic development; everything is provided. Therefore, one should understand that everything belongs to Krishna, and with this idea, one may take prasāda. However, if one interferes with the allotments of others, he is a thief. We should not accept more than what we actually need. Therefore, if by chance we get an abundance of money, we should always consider that it belongs to the supreme personality of godhead. In Krishna consciousness we are getting sufficient money, but we should never think that the money belongs to us; it belongs to the supreme personality of godhead and should be equally distributed to the workers, the devotees. No devotee should claim that any money or property belongs to him. If one thinks that any portion of property of this huge universe belongs to anyone, he is to be considered a thief and is punishable by the laws of nature.

daivī hy eṣā guṇa-mayī ;mama māyā duratyayā:

[ BG 7.14].

[This divine energy of Mine, consisting of the three modes of material nature, is difficult to overcome. But those who have surrendered unto Me can easily cross beyond it.]

No one can surpass the vigilance of material nature or hide his intentions from material nature. If human society unlawfully claims that the property of the universe, either partially or wholly, belongs to mankind, all of human society will be cursed as a society of thieves and will be punished by the laws of nature.

LECTURE TRANSCRIPTION

THE PEACE FORMULA

The only means of peace to be established within our life and within our society is to understand this most basic principle of religion, “tena tyaktena bhuṣjīthā” that everything is the property of God and therefore we only have the right to accept what is provided and allotted for us by the laws of God. Krishna also explains in Gita,

bhoktāraṁ yajṣa-tapasāṁ
sarva-loka-maheśvaram
suhṛdaṁ sarva-bhūtānāṁ
jṣātvā māṁ śāntim ṛcchati

[BG 5.29]

[A person in full consciousness of Me, knowing Me to be the ultimate beneficiary of all sacrifices and austerities, the Supreme Lord of all planets and demigods, and the benefactor and well-wisher of all living entities, attains peace from the pangs of material miseries.]

This is the peace formula, that one who understands how Krishna is the supreme proprietor of everything that exists and knows that everything is meant for his enjoyment and trust with faith that Krishna is our friend, only such a person can achieve peace. You see unless we understand that Krishna is our friend. Unless we have faith and trust in the supremacy of God, then it will not be possible for us to utilize everything for his enjoyment.

We see in the lives of great asuras such as Hiraṇyakaśipu and Kamsa, they knew that Krishna is the Supreme Personality of Godhead. They knew that he is the supreme god. It is not that they were in ignorance. They had knowledge. They knew that he is the proprietor of everything. They knew that everything is meant for his enjoyment. But they did not know that he is the friend of all living beings. They considered Krishna or Narayana to be their enemy. Therefore, they wanted to claim proprietorship and compete with their enemy or Vishnu.

Just like there is the story of how Hiranyaksha, he was the younger brother of Hiraṇyakaśipu , in order to please his brother, he wandered throughout the entire universe with his formidable club conquering the demigods, conquering all the planets. He even went to the abode of Varuna and challenged that either you give me your property or fight with me. No one could even stand before him in battle. Then he was told by Hiranyaksha that the supreme Lord Vishnu had incarnated in a wonderful form of a boar. And that, If he really wanted a good fight he should fight with Vishnu. So of course, we know the story. There was a great battle between Hiranyaksha and the supreme Lord Sri Varahadeva and in the end Varahadeva slay this mighty asura. AND The family members were weeping, they were crying in great distress that their beloved son and brother was dead.

In fact, Diti, the mother of both Hiraṇyakaśipu and Hiranyaksha, she was weeping in great distress. Even though she knew that her sons were very unrighteous demons, simply because of her motherly affection, she could not bear the separation. So Hiraṇyakaśipu, he vowed to avenge the death of his brother. He vowed that our enemy is Vishnu and therefore Vishnu must be killed, and therefore we must conquer and usurp everything that is his and we must take the position of God. suhṛdaṁ sarva-bhūtānāṁ . Krishna says if you simply understand that he is your friend, you will find peace. Hiraṇyakaśipu understood everything except that Krishna was his friend, he considered him as his enemy, and therefore he made a very very strategic plan. He decided that let us kill all the brahmanas, let us destroy their sacrifice. If we do like this Vishnu, who is dependent on their oblations, he will die. Because in sacrifice, the brahmanas offer oblations to the Devatas, as well as to Vishnu. He felt that if Vishnu does not get his oblations ,he will die. This is the way we will kill our enemy, indirectly.

So of course they are causing a great havoc and. Hiraṇyakaśipu by his mighty strength, he conquered the entire universe. But how intolerable it was, that his worse, greatest and only enemy in Hiraṇyakaśipu’s life was worshiped by his favourite son Prahlad. Hiraṇyakaśipu was demanding all demigods and all brahmanas and all living beings to worship him as the supreme proprietor of the universe and he was so powerful, he was so extravagant in his success that even great sages like Narada Muni became his humble obedient servant, what to speak of anybody else. There was nobody that would not bow down to his command except one person, his own favourite son Prahlad.

His own favorite son Prahlad was constantly chanting the name of Narayan. He was constantly thinking of Sri Krishna’s beautiful form and his beautiful pastimes. He was always crying out in love for Krishna. And whenever his father would inquire from him of what he was thinking or what the best part of his education was, Prahlad would always tell his father to give up his false ego and surrender to Krishna. Hmm It was too much for Hiraṇyakaśipu to tolerate.

ENVY

When you hate somebody or you are envious of somebody there is nothing that disturbs your mind more than seeing the person you are envious of glorified. Even within our own life we can feel this. Our envy is not very much, but even if we are a little envious of someone, and we hear that person glorified, its very, very difficult to tolerate. We want to either assassinate that person’s character or minimize on or think that the person who is glorifying him is some kind of uninformed fool ah In this way it is very difficult to tolerate.

In fact, it creates a burning sensation within the core of our heart does it not when we hear about a person, we are envious of being glorified, does it not? Just like Sisupala he was so envious of Krishna. He was given a benediction because Krishna was born to kill Sisupala. He was given a benediction that you can blaspheme Krishna up to 100 times. But beyond 100 times Krishna said,” I’ll have to kill to you.”

And when he saw that Krishna was being glorified in such a way in the Agra Puja, of the Rājasūya sacrifice, of Maharaja Yudhistira. This puja is given for the most respected and honoured man in the entire world and this honour was being given to Krishna, such a glorification. Sisupala could not tolerate. He could not contain himself. He just went wild and just started blaspheming as if he had a hundred thousand of mouths. Just blasphemes were coming out. He lost all of his good memory and all of his common sense. Then he went beyond hundred mark and then Krishna very calmly took his Sudarśana Chakra and severed his head.

So, when we are envious of someone, if that person is glorified it is very painful to the heart, and the worst type of glorification is, if someone you love, glorifies that person. If just a stranger glorifies the person, it is intolerable, but somehow or other you can contain yourself ah But if someone you love is glorifying that person, someone you are attached to is glorifying that person, someone who you want to adore you, is glorifying him instead. My god! It is like a death blow to the false ego ah It is such a threat. So imagine Hiraṇyakaśipu’s condition of consciousness. Of all his sons he wanted Prahlad to be the next king, he was his favourite son he was the most virtuous and the most intelligent and he possessed the most munificent qualities. Hiraṇyakaśipu was so proud of his son, but his son was 24 hours a day chanting the glories of his enemy. suhṛdaṁ sarva-bhūtānām .

So in order to properly practice devotional service, you have to have faith that Krishna is your ever well-wishing friend. Otherwise even if you know, he is the proprietor of everything and even if you know everything is meant for his enjoyment, you will not trust giving anything to him. Right!!

THE SPIRITUAL MASTER

The spiritual master is the representative of Krishna. That is why Srila Prabhupada, he would always sign in his letter, “Your ever well-wisher”. What does this mean? Unless we understand this principle, we cannot properly practice our devotional life. Unless we have this faith, unless we have trust that Krishna as well as the spiritual master his representative, they are our ever well-wishers. That means whatever we surrender to them we should have complete faith that it is to our best interest. Whatever they asked us to do they are never trying to cheat us, they are never trying to exploit us, but they are our best friends trying to help us and give us the greatest of all benedictions. We must have that faith. Otherwise, when the test comes, we will fail. The test is when we have to surrender and at that point of surrender there must be trust, there must be faith, that yes, this person is my ever well-wisher.

THIS WORLD- A PLACE OF CHEATERS AND THE CHEATED

Srila Prabhupada used to tell us, many times he told us that “This is the world of the cheaters and the cheated”. Everyone is cheating each other. He even gave us an example. The example he gave was in India, he said that in the villages, sometimes there is the idea that “if I can only marry my daughter to a sadhu then we will get great benediction of God within our family”. So when the sadhus beg door to door ah they they take their daughter and they have the daughter take very good care of the sadhu and trick the sadhu in to becoming attracted and then the sadhu marries the daughter. And they cheat the sadhu in this way, They cheat him from his renunciation.

But, what happens is that unscrupulous, low class, the lowest of mankind, they know ah that in the wealthy family they want to marry their daughter to a sadhu. So they disguise themselves as a sadhu and they came to the home. The daughter takes very good care of him and he says, yes I will give benedictions and I’ll marry her and I’ll become the heir of your property. Very nice, you are the recipient of God’s mercy. But actually they are getting nothing of God’s mercy. They are simply getting a low class criminal as a son in law. This is called the cheaters and the cheated.

You want to cheat but the cheaters who will cheat you. Ultimately Krishna says that he is the greatest cheater. He cheats everyone of everything in due course of time. You cannot cheat Krishna, but his cheating is out of love. He is not cheating us, he is actually our best friend. So this is the world of the cheaters and the cheated and Srila Prabhupada used to tell us that if you want to make proper spiritual advancement you simply have to have faith in one thing, that your guru has not come to cheat you. Everyone else is cheating you. But your guru is not cheating you, he is your friend and he is giving to you only. He is only concerned with your interest. The guru is never exploiting you. The guru is your best well wishing friend.

Lord Kapila Deva explains to Devahuti that there is only one friend and that is Krishna. There is no one else except Krishna who can be your friend because a friend in need is a friend indeed and when are you most in need at the time of death. There are so many incidents in the sashtra. The story of Gajendra, he had so many friends but when he was in need at the time when he was about to be killed, no one could help him. In fact at the time of death, none of your family members, none of your social acquaintances, none of your ah political lords within society, nobody can help you. Only the name of Krishna can help you. Therefore Krishna is our only friend. And one who is here as his servant, one who is his intimate associate, one who is fulfilling his desires, one who is on his mission that person is equally a friend to Krishna. Therefore the only friend is a God conscious representative of the Lord. And if we do not have this faith, there is no question of really being able to surrender.

FAITH

Faith is the most important part of our spiritual life. Faith that the guru is not coming to cheat you. Faith that Krishna is our friend and here is his representative on earth. If we do not have this faith, we cannot make proper spiritual advancement. Our whole spiritual life is basically pseudo and superficial. Of course none of us really have that faith. A neophyte would be very untruthful if he said that I have implicit faith that my guru is my best friend. We have an intellectual understanding and with that intellectual understanding, we must develop this faith.

yasya deve parā bhaktir
yathā deve tathā gurau
tasyaite kathitā hy arthāḥ
prakāśante mahātmanaḥ

[Śvetāśvatara Up. 6.23]

[Only unto those great souls who have implicit faith in both the Lord and the spiritual master are all the imports of Vedic knowledge automatically revealed.]

One who has implicit faith in guru and Krishna that they are our friends and well-wishers, all the imports of Vedic knowledge are automatically revealed. So therefore we must very, very carefully cultivate the seed of faith that is within our heart. Therefore we should never ever, ever, ever, ever in our life tolerate hearing offences which are minimizing the position of Krishna or our spiritual master because these words have a very, very deep affect on the subtle consciousness and create havoc to our faith. Even if in our external consciousness, we think oh “I have heard this so many times, it does not disturb me”. We may think like that. But we do not understand that we may think we are very advanced externally but in our heart’s we are still neophytes. And these words penetrate through the superficial conceptions of our own spiritual advancement that, “yes yes I am strong and stable in my devotional life, that I can hear these things, it will not bother me, it will not disturb me, it will not go on with my service”. But in our subtle stratum of consciousness havoc is taking place to our faith, havoc, chaos and when the real test is there of surrender at that time we will not be able to perceive with our superficial gross consciousness. Our activities will have to be based on the very, very internal subtle realizations of the heart. That’s all we have when the test come. When Krishna really wants to test us, he smashes, he obliterates our superficial promise of Krishna Consciousness and at that time we realize, my god, my faith is weak. What I have heard is actually disturbing me very much. How will I surrender? Where is my trust? So therefore one must be very, very careful by the order of Krishna to protect his spiritual life, by protecting his faith, by maintaining the trust that “guru is our ever well wisher” because Krishna is our ever well wisher.

EVER WELL WISHER

Did you ever think of what that means “ever well wisher?” That means forever. That means he is not only the well-wisher of this life. You may have a mother and father who are on a very, very gross level of consciousness they are our well-wishers. But their well wishing sentiments only survive a few years. At the time they die or at the time we die, they are not any longer our well wisher. In fact they will not even recognize who we are?

So we may have well wishers in this world. But only those great souls who are acting on behalf of God, they are our ever well wishers. They pray to God that I’ll come back birth after birth after birth after birth in this material world, whatever is necessary till I can deliver this disciple to your lotus feet in the spiritual world. So he is your ever well wisher. And if you have that faith, then and then only, you can attain peace. Because then only can you accept that Krishna is the proprietor of everything and trust that yes I can give everything to Krishna, it all belongs to him, i’ll use everything for his enjoyment because by doing such, that is the ultimate service I can do for all living beings and that is the ultimate benefit that I can derive in my own heart in my own life. 33.46

SURRENDER

Sometimes grihastas they have their businesses but they are very afraid very afraid of surrender very afraid of guru. What if he tells me to do like this? My god, I can surrender to somethings but not that. What if he tells me to do like this? What if he …this attachment of mine? What if he wants me to do what he wants me to do with it ? What if he does not want me do what I want to do? Its mine….ah

If you trust, if you have faith that he is your ever well-wisher, there will be no anxiety. You will say ah, whatever you ask, you tell, take it , take it, Do what you want with it. I have faith in you. And if you do not have faith, you are always like this, “upto this borderline guru maharaja, you can give me nice instructions but dont go beyond the dotted line because after that you are fringing upon what is mine”. very dangerous. That means we dont faith. That means we dont have trust.

Krishna told Arjuna “kill your relatives, kill your grandfather, kill Drona, your teacher”. He loved them more dear than his own life. That was a very difficult thing. He was only able to do it because he had complete faith. Sometimes we are tested, but were you ever asked to kill those objects of your our love in this world. Ha!! You see when you have this type of faith, when you have this type of trust you become completely fearless. Because the only fear in life is that we are going to loose something that we are attached to. Ha!! If you have faith that guru is our well wisher and Krishna is our well wisher, everything is happening by the direction of Sri Krishna then what is there to fear? There is nothing to fear because you have faith. Whatever Krishna does is for my good. Whatever condition of life comes ultimately it is for my good, I have faith. I want something but Krishna won’t give it to me. If I dont trust Krishna, I think Krishna why can’t I have what I want? But if you have faith in Krishna, you will think ah Krishna you are so kind, you are actually giving me just what I need, out of your love. So I’ll accept this and I’ll be in peace and I’ll be in happiness because I trust you. What to speak of, when he takes away what you already have and what’s dear to your life. Krishna why are you taking this away from me? How will I live? If you do not have that trust, you become very disturbed. But if you have that trust, with folded hands Krishna it is very painful but it is..let your will be done. That is my faith.

Therefore, a devotee whether he is in life or death, he has nothing more than his faith in Krishna. If you have that faith, you have attained complete peace. And if you have that faith in the spiritual master who is the representative of Krishna, because after all, you can only love Krishna to the extent you will learn how to love your guru. If you love your spiritual master and you have faith and trust in him then there is no fear of Him.

That When you have a boil and you squeeze it, it hurts too much. I am speaking at the present time from immediate realizations, because I have several boils right now. So, if you believe me, for those who do not have boils, if you even touch it, if you even breathe on it, if someone even looks at it, it is painful. What to speak of when you squeeze it? Too much pain. But what happens if you do not have a boil. You take any ordinary healthy part of your skin and squeeze it. You can squeeze it as hard you can and then nothing no pain. So, when the spiritual master or Krishna squeeze you, and if it hurts, it means there is an infection there. It means there is the puss of material attachment inflicting your consciousness. But when the guru squeezes and if it does not hurt, no matter how hard he squeezes, we just say yes, yes, yes I surrender now. This is the song we sing in New Vrindavana every afternoon. I surrender now…. So.. I surrender all that’s it I surrender all… Everyone has to sing that every day, I surrender all. How many people are actually surrendering all? That is the most important thing.

So when you surrender all, let it squeeze, let it squeeze let the pus of our material attachments, let it just flow and flow and flow. But if the guru does not squeeze, what happens? Then the infection remains within our heart. So, in order for us to allow him to squeeze, we must have faith that he is our well-wisher. When we go to the doctor and he squeezes our infection, we must have trust that he is trying to help us. Otherwise, will we allow him to do it? What do you say doctors?..If a patient does not have faith in what they are doing, are they going to allow you to cause pain to them. Ha They have to have complete faith that he is actually helping me. So therefore, the basic foundation of our spiritual advancement and our peace in life is faith in guru and faith in Krishna. And we must cultivate to protect that faith by associating with persons who have faith, by hearing from them and by rendering service. Through the process of hearing submissively and rendering service “paripraśnena sevayā” this nourishes the seed of faith which is within our heart. And then

bhoktāraṁ yajṣa-tapasāṁ
sarva-loka-maheśvaram
suhṛdaṁ sarva-bhūtānāṁ
jṣātvā māṁ śāntim ṛcchati

[BG 5.29]

A person in full consciousness of Me, knowing Me to be the ultimate beneficiary of all sacrifices and austerities, the Supreme Lord of all planets and demigods, and the benefactor and well-wisher of all living entities, attains peace from the pangs of material miseries.

We will be very happy; we will be very glad to accept and admit that Krishna is the proprietor of everything. Everything I have and everything else is meant for His enjoyment and it is my greatest pleasure, it is my greatest purpose of life to simply surrender everything to Krishna and know that this is the only real happiness that I’ll ever experience in my life.

Thank you very much. Hare Krishna

Q&A Transcription

Question :- (inaudible)

Ans: Amongst such ignorant people, who do not know anything, we have no real acquaintance. Their blaspheming is hardly even blaspheming because they do not know anything about anything, they are just talking. If you can see that you can actually change this person’s heart, then you can tolerate it to some extent. If you see that he is simply an atheist and he is not listening, then you should not listen to anything he has to say. You should simply say namaste and walkout. Hare Krishna. Give him some Prasad and leave. Dont waste your time with such crazy minded people. But people who actually are envious who have a personal grudge against Krishna or our guru and who are trying to blaspheme on that level, who are motivated, such persons we cannot listen to, even for a second. These people are people who do not know anything about anyone. They are just talking craziness. That is the difference between someone who is specifically trying to blaspheme a personality. We should not associate with such persons. We should never hear what they have to say under any condition. But in preaching this crazy people of the masses, they do not even know what they are saying. So we just say, yes, yes, yes sir, you are very nice just give me this donation and take this Prasad. Then say alright very nice. We can tolerate that.

Thank you very much.

Hare Krishna.
